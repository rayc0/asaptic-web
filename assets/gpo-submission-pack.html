HealthPRO supplier capability summary

Asaptic (HK) Ltd is a Hong Kong principal-reseller sourcing certified clinical bioimpedance and body-composition analyzers for Canadian health-system buyers. We qualify manufacturers through a document-first workflow covering ISO 13485 quality certification, Health Canada MDALL listing evidence where applicable, TGA ARTG evidence where applicable, CE MDR documentation where available, model-level specifications, clinical validation, sample inspection, landed-cost modelling, and export-readiness review. For hospitals, long-term-care operators, dietitians, renal teams, and allied-health programs, Asaptic assembles buyer-ready packages that connect objective muscle-mass, body-composition, and fluid-status measurement to malnutrition, sarcopenia, renal care, and quality-improvement use cases. Our role is to shorten supplier screening, reduce documentation friction, and present certified device options with clear pricing, lead-time, incoterm, warranty, and training assumptions. We support Canadian procurement teams with responsive evidence collection, bilingual Asia supplier coordination where required, and human-reviewed commercial commitments through engage@asaptic.com and post-award onboarding support. All claims remain subject to buyer verification and current regulatory review.

Mohawk Medbuy LaunchPad pitch

Asaptic (HK) Ltd proposes a LaunchPad evaluation of certified clinical bioimpedance/body-composition analyzers to improve malnutrition and sarcopenia screening, with GLIM criteria as the clinical anchor. Many nutrition and allied-health teams still rely on weight, BMI, intake notes, and manual observation, which can miss clinically important muscle loss or fluid shifts. BIA adds a fast, non-invasive, repeatable measurement layer that helps teams distinguish fat loss, muscle wasting, and hydration change, then document response to nutrition, rehabilitation, renal, and frailty interventions. Asaptic’s contribution is a qualified sourcing and evidence package: model-level regulatory evidence, ISO 13485 certification review, clinical versus consumer-grade screening, validation-paper collection, sample-unit coordination, landed pricing, documentation, and implementation notes. A LaunchPad pilot could screen a defined LTC, rehabilitation, renal, or acute-care cohort; record baseline and follow-up body-composition measures; compare workflow time against current practice; and assess whether objective measurement improves intervention targeting, care-plan documentation, and escalation decisions. The expected outcome is not a standalone device purchase, but a reproducible pathway for earlier nutrition risk detection, better sarcopenia monitoring, and clearer evidence for dietitian and physiotherapy impact, and gives procurement teams a basis for scale, training, support, and contract review. Current LaunchPad eligibility and submission terms must be verified before filing.
