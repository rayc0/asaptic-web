Health Canada MDALL & TGA ARTG — How Buyer-Verifiable Clearance Works

Asaptic acts as principal-reseller — not as the device manufacturer, licence holder, or importer of record. This page explains what the regulatory numbers mean, how you verify them independently, and what obligations sit with each party.

Health Canada — Canada

Medical Device Licence (MDALL) — Class II

  • What it is: A device-specific licence issued to the manufacturer or Canadian importer of record under the Medical Devices Regulations (SOR/98-282).
  • Class II scope: Moderate-risk devices (e.g. bioimpedance analysers, Class II diagnostic equipment). Requires 510(k)-equivalent technical file, quality-system attestation, and Safety & Effectiveness summary.
  • Licence holder: The named entity on the MDALL. This is NOT Asaptic. Asaptic's supplier/partner holds or arranges the licence.
  • Asaptic's role: Sources devices from MDALL-cleared factories; confirms licence status before any purchase order is executed.
  • Relevant licence number: [device-specific MDALL #] — to be provided in the product datasheet for each SKU.
  • Public verification: Health Canada Medical Devices Active Licence Search (online, free, real-time).
TGA — Australia

ARTG Entry — Class IIa

  • What it is: An entry in the Australian Register of Therapeutic Goods (ARTG), maintained by the TGA under the Therapeutic Goods Act 1989.
  • Class IIa scope: Medium-risk medical devices. Sponsor holds the ARTG entry and takes regulatory responsibility for the device in Australia.
  • Sponsor ≠ Manufacturer: The ARTG sponsor is typically the Australian entity responsible for bringing the device to market. This is NOT Asaptic unless explicitly stated in writing for a specific product.
  • Asaptic's role: Sources devices from factories whose products are held by an ARTG-registered sponsor; confirms ARTG status before PO.
  • Relevant ARTG number: [device-specific ARTG #] — to be provided in the product datasheet for each SKU.
  • Public verification: TGA ARTG Public Summary Search (tga.gov.au, free, real-time).

How to Independently Verify Clearance Status

Health Canada MDALL Lookup
URL: canada.ca → Health Canada → Medical Devices → Device Licence Search
Search by: device name, manufacturer name, or MDALL number.
Status to confirm: Active. Check device class and intended use align with your procurement.
TGA ARTG Public Summary Search
URL: tga.gov.au → Find a medical device (ARTG)
Search by: ARTG number, device name, or sponsor name.
Status to confirm: Active. Confirm device type, sponsor, and intended purpose.

Buyer's Due-Diligence Checklist

  • Request device-specific MDALL # or ARTG # from Asaptic before issuing a purchase order.
  • Verify the licence/entry is Active and the device class matches your intended clinical use.
  • Confirm who will act as importer of record in your jurisdiction and ensure they hold or can obtain the appropriate clearance.
  • Review the technical file summary or IFU (Instructions for Use) for compatibility with your facility protocols.
  • Retain copies of all regulatory documentation for your own compliance records.
