Import Logistics Guide — China → Canada & Australia

This page sets out how Asaptic-sourced shipments move from Chinese factories to your destination, including Incoterms options, payment timing, customs broker roles, HS code responsibilities, and indicative delivery timelines. Duty and tax rates are jurisdiction- and HS-code-specific — verify with your licensed customs broker before ordering.

Incoterms — Which Party Bears Which Risk and Cost

Incoterm Factory / Asaptic responsibility ends at… Buyer takes risk & cost from… Best for buyer when…
EXW — Ex Works Factory gate. Goods available for collection. Factory gate — buyer arranges all freight, export clearance, insurance. Buyer has own freight forwarder and wants full control.
FOB — Free On Board Goods loaded on vessel at named Chinese port. Export customs cleared by seller. Once on vessel. Buyer pays ocean freight, insurance, destination customs. Buyer has preferred carrier or consolidates multiple shipments.
CIF — Cost, Insurance & Freight Goods delivered to destination port. Seller pays freight + minimum insurance. Destination port. Buyer pays import duties, taxes, inland delivery. Buyer wants predictable door-to-port pricing. Most common for medical devices.
DAP — Delivered at Place Goods at named destination, unloaded from inbound carrier. Import duties unpaid. Buyer pays import duties/taxes; handles customs clearance. Buyer wants door delivery but controls duty payment for accounting.
DDP — Delivered Duty Paid Full delivery including import duties, taxes, and customs clearance. Nothing — risk fully on seller until delivery. Buyer wants all-in landed cost; seller must be registered/compliant in destination.
Note on DDP for regulated medical devices: DDP requires the seller to act as or arrange an importer of record in Canada/Australia. Asaptic does not hold this role by default. DDP availability must be confirmed per-order in writing.

Payment Timing vs. Shipment Milestones

T0 — PO Confirmed30% deposit invoice issued. Payment triggers production allocation or warehouse pick.
T+[lead time] — Pre-shipment70% balance invoice issued upon pre-shipment inspection pass and B/L draft. Balance due before or against B/L release (TT or LC terms).
T+[transit] — ArrivalCustoms broker files import entry. Duties/taxes assessed. Buyer pays directly to broker or government authority.
T+clearance — DeliveryGoods released for inland delivery to buyer's warehouse or facility.
Lead times vary by product and factory. Indicative: production 15–30 days + ocean transit 12–25 days (CN → CA/AU). Air freight available for urgent orders — indicative 3–7 days transit, higher cost.

Customs Broker Role

  • A licensed customs broker in Canada (CSCB member) or Australia (CBFCA member) files the import entry on the buyer's behalf.
  • The broker classifies goods under the correct HS code, calculates duties/taxes, and submits the entry to CBSA (Canada) or ABF (Australia).
  • Buyer is responsible for nominating their broker and providing accurate product documentation (commercial invoice, packing list, COA, regulatory docs).
  • Asaptic provides: commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin, and regulatory compliance documents (MDALL/ARTG references where applicable).

HS Code Verification

  • The HS code determines the applicable duty rate, GST/HST/GST treatment, and any import permit requirements.
  • Medical device HS codes (Chapter 90) may attract preferential or zero duty under CPTPP or CHAFTA — verify with your broker.

  • CBSA Advance Rulings (Canada) or ATO/ABF tariff advice (Australia) are available for certainty — your broker can apply.

Insurance & Pre-Shipment Inspection

Cargo Insurance: Under CIF, Asaptic arranges minimum cargo insurance (Institute Cargo Clauses C or equivalent). Buyers are strongly advised to arrange All-Risk (ICC A) cover for medical devices given their value and fragility. Insurance cost is typically 0.2–0.5% of CIF value.
Pre-Shipment Inspection (PSI): Asaptic supports buyer-nominated third-party inspection (e.g. SGS, Bureau Veritas, Intertek) at the factory prior to shipment. PSI cost is borne by the buyer. Asaptic recommends PSI for first orders and orders over USD 30,000.

Buyer Responsibilities Summary

  • Nominate a licensed customs broker in Canada or Australia before goods ship.
  • Confirm HS code and applicable duty/tax rates with your broker prior to PO.
  • Arrange and pay import duties, taxes, and any applicable permit fees.
  • Ensure your entity is eligible to act as importer of record for regulated medical devices in your jurisdiction.
  • Retain all shipping, customs, and regulatory documents for your compliance records.
  • For devices requiring specific import permits or TGA/Health Canada notification, ensure these are in place before goods arrive.
