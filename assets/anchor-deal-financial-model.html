Regulatory Gate: Sequencing Before Quote

This changes deal sequencing, not duty math. Canada requires an importer MDEL plus an MDL for a likely Class II clinical BIA device. Australia requires ARTG inclusion through a TGA-registered Australian Sponsor; a new market entry is typically a 6-12 month track.

The immediate anchor path is sub-distribution behind an already-registered brand such as InBody Oceania or InBody Canada. A China clinical device with no confirmed FDA / TGA ARTG / MDALL record should be treated as a 3-5 year, USD $200k-$400k per-jurisdiction registration project, not a near-term close.

The real-margin path is to verify Charder MA601's FDA K-number, then build a private-label CA/AU route around the existing predicate and sponsor/importer partners. The Conmo MDALL record supports only the BCA-381BC consumer/light-clinical scale lane.

Assumptions + Risks

  • Target hardware for this calculator is the medium-term private-label clinical tier, not the immediate sub-distribution lane or Conmo BCA-381BC consumer scale.
  • Buyer must accept a less-known brand in exchange for a large discount; adoption risk remains material.
  • Clinical BIA duty is verified at 0% for Canada and Australia under HS 9018.19; freight, insurance, broker, and local-delivery charges remain quote-dependent.
  • GST is cash-paid at import but recoverable for properly registered B2B importers: Canada 5% GST via ITC; Australia 10% GST for ABN/GST-registered businesses.
  • No reviewed Chinese clinical-tier factory has confirmed FDA / TGA ARTG / MDALL registration. Do not quote China clinical-station hardware as a near-term anchor.
  • TFLN wafers have 0% duty exposure into EU/Canada/Australia, but China-origin US imports face Section 301 exposure of roughly 25%-50%+; route TFLN anchor outreach to non-US Western buyers unless current US tariffs are broker-confirmed.
  • Pricing is illustrative and depends on factory quote validity, payment terms, warranty allocation, and buyer acceptance of specifications.

Bottom Line

One 20-unit private-label clinical order at $9,000 per unit can generate approximately $131,200-$136,000 B2B gross profit, but this is the 6-18 month real-margin path after registration evidence is verified. Immediate revenue should be modeled as lower-margin sub-distribution behind an already-registered brand.

Cash timing: In the private-label case, a 30% buyer deposit covers about 96%-118% of cash landed cost depending on CA/AU lane; a 50% buyer deposit covers about 161%-197%. For the first close, sell behind existing registration.
