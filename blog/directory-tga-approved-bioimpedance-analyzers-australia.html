How buyers verify these

For Australia, use the TGA ARTG public resource at tga.gov.au/resources/artg. Enter the ARTG ID, then confirm the sponsor, device name, class, public summary, and whether the record is still active. Where a record covers a device family, ask the sponsor to identify the exact model and configuration within scope.

For Canada, use Health Canada's MDALL search at mdall-limh.hc-sc.gc.ca. Search by licence number and manufacturer, then confirm the current status, device class, company name, and device description. Put screenshots or exported search results in the procurement file, because registry records can change after supplier qualification.

For device procurement workflows, connect registry verification with the clinical devices sourcing process and the compliance matrix. A registry match is one input; it does not replace intended-use review, ISO 13485 evidence, IEC 60601-1 safety documentation, post-market support checks, or clinical validation review.

Frequently asked questions

Does an ARTG entry prove a body-composition analyser is approved for every clinical use?

No. Buyers should verify the current ARTG record, sponsor, model scope, device class, and intended purpose before procurement. An entry may cover a family of devices or a narrower indication than the buyer's intended clinical workflow.

How do buyers confirm a TGA ARTG record?

Use the TGA ARTG public resource at tga.gov.au/resources/artg and check the exact ARTG ID, sponsor name, device description, class, and status. For the IDs in this directory, confirm each at tga.gov.au/resources/artg/<id>.

How do buyers confirm a Health Canada MDALL record?

Use Health Canada's MDALL search at mdall-limh.hc-sc.gc.ca and confirm the current licence number, manufacturer, device name, class, and active status before using the record in a procurement file.

Are DEXA and BIA body-composition devices interchangeable?

No. DEXA and BIA use different measurement methods and regulatory records should not be treated as interchangeable. This directory flags the Cosmed densitometry body composition entry as DEXA, not BIA.
