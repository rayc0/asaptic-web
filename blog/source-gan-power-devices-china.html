Sourcing GaN Power Devices & Fast Chargers from China: Suppliers, Specs & Export-Control Notes (2026)

By Asaptic Labs 2026-06-14 7 min read

To source China GaN power semiconductors or GaN fast chargers in 2026, separate commercial power devices from GaN RF, start with a supplier shortlist led by Innoscience and qualified charger ODMs, and treat gallium export control as the first compliance gate. Gallium and gallium compounds are China-export-controlled through MOFCOM; Asaptic must verify licence status per shipment before procurement execution. This article is not legal advice.

Key gate: China has export controls on gallium and gallium compounds. A China GaN-device export may require a MOFCOM licence depending on product, destination, end use, exporter, and documentation. On the US side, GaN power devices are generally treated as commercial/EAR99 in many ordinary charger and power-conversion cases, but classification must be confirmed for each SKU and end use.

GaN power, not GaN RF

GaN power devices switch energy in adapters, USB-C fast chargers, server power supplies, solar inverters, motor drives, battery systems, and industrial supplies. The screening focus is normally voltage rating, RDS(on), package thermal path, gate drive, reliability history, certification support, and production capacity.

GaN RF is a different sourcing lane. RF GaN can be used in radar, electronic warfare, satellite, telecom infrastructure, and other high-frequency systems. It can be defense-sensitive and may involve ITAR, military end-use, or other export-control reviews. Asaptic's China GaN power sourcing lane avoids GaN RF and avoids RF technical data exchange unless a separate compliance review clears the work.

Specs buyers should freeze early

  • For GaN FETs: voltage class, continuous and pulsed current, RDS(on), threshold voltage, gate charge, package, thermal resistance, avalanche or short-circuit behavior, JEDEC or automotive qualification target, and reliability report availability.
  • For fast chargers: USB-C PD/PPS profiles, total wattage, port power allocation, standby consumption, thermal rise at full load, EMI margin, safety certification markets, enclosure resin, plug type, cable policy, firmware ownership, and production test plan.
  • For industrial power: isolation, creepage, clearance, surge, lifetime at temperature, derating policy, field-replaceable module strategy, and documentation needed for customer audits.
  • For compliance: exporter, end user, destination, end use, gallium-content documentation, ECCN or EAR99 basis, sanctions screening, and MOFCOM licence evidence when applicable.

Export-control caveat

China has controlled exports of gallium and gallium compounds since 2023 through MOFCOM. Because GaN is a gallium compound semiconductor, a shipment involving GaN devices, wafers, dies, modules, or related material may require licensing review. The practical question is not simply "is this a charger?" but which item is exported, who exports it, where it goes, who uses it, and what documentation supports the declared end use.

On the US side, many GaN power devices used in commercial charging and power conversion are generally handled as ordinary commercial items or EAR99, but buyers should confirm classification, destination rules, sanctions exposure, and end-use restrictions for each transaction. 2026 demand context

  • AI data centres adopting 48 V/800 V HVDC bus architectures need GaN power devices to hit 80 PLUS Titanium-class PSU efficiency targets; silicon MOSFETs are a limiting factor in power-density at these switching frequencies (industry estimate).
  • Data-centre energy-efficiency regulations — EU energy-efficiency reporting and China PUE targets — are accelerating the commercial case for a silicon-to-GaN power-stage transition across server and rack PDU designs.
  • Automotive 800 V platform roll-out is pulling GaN into on-board chargers (OBC) and DC-DC converters, where the switching-speed and thermal envelope advantages over silicon are directly valued in pack weight and thermal management.
  • Solar microinverters are adopting GaN power devices for higher conversion efficiency and reduced magnetic size, with multiple ODM design-ins now in mid-volume production (industry estimate).
  • Scale-up of 300 mm GaN-on-Si manufacturing is moving commercial power GaN toward cost parity with silicon at volume, improving the business case for GaN in cost-sensitive industrial and consumer power applications.

Frequently asked questions

Is gallium export-controlled?

Yes. Gallium and gallium compounds have been subject to China export controls administered through MOFCOM since 2023. A China GaN shipment may require a MOFCOM licence depending on item, destination, end user, end use, and exporter process. This is not legal advice.

What is the difference between GaN power and GaN RF?

GaN power devices are commercial power-conversion parts used in chargers, adapters, inverters, and industrial supplies. GaN RF parts target radio-frequency applications and can be defense-sensitive, ITAR-relevant, or subject to military end-use restrictions. This sourcing page is for GaN power, not RF.

Is Innoscience fabless-friendly?

Innoscience is a leading China GaN power foundry with 8-inch capability and public positioning that supports fabless-style access. Practical access, wafer starts, packages, lead times, and export status still need project-by-project confirmation.

What MOQ should I expect?

MOQ depends on the lane. Engineering samples and evaluation boards can be small; charger ODM production commonly moves into thousands of units; custom packaging, firmware, or modules may require larger commitments. Treat any MOQ number as [UNVERIFIED] until a supplier quotes it against a defined spec.

Who handles the MOFCOM licence?

The China exporter normally leads the MOFCOM licence application if one is required, while the buyer supplies accurate end-use, end-user, destination, and shipment documentation. Asaptic verifies licence status per shipment before execution and escalates to specialist review when required.
