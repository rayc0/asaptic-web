O QUE ESTA PÁGINA RESPONDE

Equipas de compras que obtêm dispositivos de bioimpedância (BIA) da China para o Canadá ou a Austrália enfrentam quadros regulatórios sobrepostos: uma norma de gestão da qualidade (ISO 13485), normas de segurança elétrica (família IEC 60601-1), normas de gestão em serviço (AS/NZS 3551 para a Austrália) e registos obrigatórios de acesso ao mercado (Health Canadá MDALL para o Canadá; TGA ARTG para a Austrália). Esta matriz define o âmbito de cada norma, a sua aplicabilidade de mercado e se se aplica a dispositivos BIA — para que responsáveis de compras confirmem, numa única referência, que documentos precisam de solicitar e verificar.

Apenas informativo. Esta página é uma comparação informativa de interesse público. Não é decisão de certificação, aconselhamento jurídico, avaliação de organismo notificado ou aprovação de acesso ao mercado. Verifique todos os requisitos com a autoridade reguladora relevante (Health Canadá; TGA) antes da compra.

NORMAS DE CONFORMIDADE — COMPARAÇÃO LADO A LADO

Norma Mercado Âmbito Aplica-se a dispositivos BIA? Notas para compras
ISO 13485:2016 Global Quality management system (QMS) for medical device manufacturers and supply chain. Covers design controls, production, traceability, post-market surveillance. Sim — obrigatório Required by both Health Canadá and TGA as evidence of systematic quality control. Certificate scope must explicitly cover the BIA device category — not a generic QMS covering unrelated product lines. Verify currency (expiry date) and certification body via the CB's public register. Included in Asaptic's compliance document package.
IEC 60601-1:2005+AMD1:2012+AMD2:2020 Global General requirements for basic safety and essential performance of medical electrical equipment. Covers insulation, leakage current, mechanical strength, temperature limits, and essential performance under single fault condition. Sim — por design BIA devices are medical electrical equipment; IEC 60601-1 compliance is required by design and must be demonstrated by the manufacturer. The relevant national adoptions (CAN/CSA-C22.2 No.60601-1 for Canadá; AS/NZS 3551 references IEC 60601-1 for base equipment requirements) supersede the base IEC text for market access. Test reports should reference the specific edition and amendment.
AS/NZS 3551:2012 Austrália / Nova Zelândia Management programs for medical equipment in healthcare facilities. Covers acceptance testing, periodic safety checks, maintenance, repair, and decommissioning of in-service medical electrical equipment including BIA devices. Sim — em serviço Applies once the device is deployed in an Austrálian or New Zealand healthcare facility — it governs the facility's management obligations, not the manufacturer's design obligations. Procurement teams should confirm their biomedical engineering department's AS/NZS 3551 acceptance testing protocol before specifying incoming device requirements. Facilities may require a documented test-before-use record.
CAN/CSA-C22.2 No.60601-1 Canadá Canadian national adoption of IEC 60601-1. General requirements for basic safety and essential performance of medical electrical equipment — functionally aligned with the IEC base standard, with any Canadian national deviations noted in the CSA adoption. Sim — obrigatório For devices entering the Canadian market, compliance to CAN/CSA-C22.2 No.60601-1 is the applicable electrical safety requirement. Test reports should explicitly reference the CSA adoption, not only the base IEC standard, for Health Canadá submissions. Factory test reports should list the edition and confirm no outstanding national deviations apply to the BIA device category.
Health Canadá MDALL Canadá Medical Devices Active Licence Listing — mandatory Class II medical device licence for devices sold in Canadá. Covers device identity, manufacturer identity, and device class. Searchable at mdall.hc-sc.gc.ca. Sim — acesso ao mercado Without an active MDALL licence, a Class II BIA device cannot be legally sold in Canadá. The licence is issued to the Canadian importer/distributor (or directly to the foreign manufacturer in some cases). Procurement officers should verify the specific device model and licence number — not just the manufacturer name — in the MDALL public database. Asaptic provides the verified licence number in the compliance document package.
TGA ARTG Austrália Austrálian Register of Therapeutic Goods — Class IIa medical device registration required for devices legally supplied in Austrália. Covers device description, sponsor identity, and ARTG entry number. Searchable at tga.gov.au/resources/artg. Sim — acesso ao mercado Without an ARTG entry, a Class IIa BIA device cannot be legally supplied in Austrália. The ARTG registration is held by the Austrálian sponsor (importer). Procurement officers should verify the ARTG entry number, sponsor name, and device description in the TGA public portal — confirming it matches the shipped product. Asaptic provides the verified ARTG entry number and sponsor details in the compliance document package.
EU CE MDR (EU 2017/745) União Europeia EU Medical Device Regulation — Conformity assessment for medical devices placed on the EU market. Class IIa devices (where BIA typically falls) require notified body involvement. Parcial — apenas UE CE MDR does not confer automatic market access in Canadá or Austrália — Health Canadá and TGA each run independent regulatory pathways. CE MDR technical documentation (PMS plan, clinical evaluation) can support ISO 13485 and factory audit evidence packages but is not a substitute for MDALL or ARTG. Asaptic does not route CA/AU shipments through EU channels.

COMO ISTO SE APLICA A UM DISPOSITIVO BIA FABRICADO NA CHINA

Vender um dispositivo BIA fabricado na China no Canadá e na Austrália — o caminho regulatório.

A pilha de conformidade para uma fábrica chinesa que fornece dispositivos BIA a hospitais canadianos ou instituições australianas de cuidados a idosos envolve quatro camadas independentes de verificação. Todas devem estar implementadas antes de um responsável de compras confirmar conformidade regulatória para uma proposta.

Camada 1 — QMS da fábrica: ISO 13485:2016

The Chinese factory must hold a current ISO 13485:2016 certificate issued by an accredited certification body. The certificate scope must explicitly cover the BIA device category. Verify via the certification body's public register — not from a factory-supplied PDF scan. Asaptic verifies scope and currency before factory engagement.

Camada 2 — segurança elétrica do dispositivo: família IEC 60601-1

The factory must demonstrate the BIA device meets IEC 60601-1 requirements. For Canadá, the relevant adoption is CAN/CSA-C22.2 No.60601-1. For Austrália, the base IEC 60601-1 standard applies to device design, with AS/NZS 3551 governing in-facility management. Test reports should reference the applicable edition and national adoption explicitly.

Camada 3 — acesso ao mercado canadiano: Health Canadá MDALL

The specific BIA device model must appear in the Health Canadá MDALL database with an active Class II licence. Procurement officers verify at mdall.hc-sc.gc.ca using the licence number. The licence is issued to the Canadian importer or the foreign manufacturer directly — the importer chain must be confirmed. Asaptic provides the verified licence number in the document package.

Camada 4 — acesso ao mercado australiano: TGA ARTG

The specific BIA device model must appear in the TGA ARTG as a Class IIa device. Procurement officers verify at tga.gov.au/resources/artg using the ARTG entry number. The ARTG registration is held by the Austrálian sponsor — the sponsor identity and device description must match the shipped product. Asaptic provides the verified ARTG entry number and sponsor details in the document package.

Em serviço (Austrália): AS/NZS 3551

Once the BIA device is deployed in an Austrálian healthcare facility, the facility's biomedical engineering team applies AS/NZS 3551 management protocols: acceptance testing before first clinical use, periodic electrical safety checks, maintenance records, and decommissioning procedures. This is a facility-level obligation — procurement teams should confirm their biomedical engineering department's readiness before device delivery.

O que o CE MDR faz e não faz

CE MDR certification from a Chinese factory is useful as supporting evidence (clinical evaluation, PMS plan, technical file quality) but does not confer Canadian or Austrálian market access. Health Canadá and TGA each operate independent regulatory pathways. MDALL and ARTG listings must be verified separately — CE MDR does not substitute for either.

PERGUNTAS FREQUENTES

Perguntas de conformidade que responsáveis de compras mais fazem.

Does ISO 13485 alone satisfy Health Canadá or TGA?

No. ISO 13485 is a prerequisite QMS standard required by both regulators, but it is not a market-access licence. Health Canadá requires an active MDALL licence for the specific device model. TGA requires an ARTG registration. ISO 13485, the MDALL, and the ARTG are three separate verification steps — all must be confirmed independently.

Can one Chinese factory supply BIA devices to both Canadá and Austrália?

Yes, provided the factory holds both a Health Canadá MDALL licence (for the Canadá pathway) and a TGA ARTG registration (for the Austrália pathway). Many Chinese factories serving export markets hold both for their BIA product lines. Asaptic verifies both database listings — by device model — before engaging any factory.

What is the difference between AS/NZS 3551 and IEC 60601-1?

IEC 60601-1 governs the design and manufacture of medical electrical equipment — it is a factory-level requirement. AS/NZS 3551 governs the in-service management of that equipment once deployed in an Austrálian healthcare facility — it is a facility-level requirement. Both apply to BIA devices in Austrália, at different points in the device lifecycle.

Where do I verify a Health Canadá MDALL or TGA ARTG listing?

Health Canadá MDALL is publicly searchable at mdall.hc-sc.gc.ca — search by manufacturer name, device model, or licence number. TGA ARTG is publicly searchable at tga.gov.au/resources/artg — search by ARTG entry number, sponsor name, or device description. Both databases are authoritative government sources. Asaptic provides the specific licence and ARTG numbers so buyers can verify directly.
