Selling a China-manufactured BIA device into 加拿大与澳大利亚 — the regulatory path.

The compliance stack for a Chinese factory supplying BIA devices to Canadian hospitals or 澳大利亚n aged-care facilities involves four independent verification layers. All four must be in place before a procurement officer can confirm regulatory compliance for a tender submission.

Layer 1 — Factory QMS: ISO 13485:2016

The Chinese factory must hold a current ISO 13485:2016 certificate issued by an accredited certification body. The certificate scope must explicitly cover the BIA device category. Verify via the certification body's public register — not from a factory-supplied PDF scan. Asaptic verifies scope and currency before factory engagement.

Layer 2 — Device Electrical Safety: IEC 60601-1 family

The factory must demonstrate the BIA device meets IEC 60601-1 requirements. For 加拿大, the relevant adoption is CAN/CSA-C22.2 No.60601-1. For 澳大利亚, the base IEC 60601-1 standard applies to device design, with AS/NZS 3551 governing in-facility management. Test reports should reference the applicable edition and national adoption explicitly.

Layer 3 — 加拿大 市场 Access: Health Canada MDALL

The specific BIA device model must appear in the Health Canada MDALL database with an active Class II licence. Procurement officers verify at mdall.hc-sc.gc.ca using the licence number. The licence is issued to the Canadian importer or the foreign manufacturer directly — the importer chain must be confirmed. Asaptic provides the verified licence number in the document package.

Layer 4 — 澳大利亚 市场 Access: TGA ARTG

The specific BIA device model must appear in the TGA ARTG as a Class IIa device. Procurement officers verify at tga.gov.au/resources/artg using the ARTG entry number. The ARTG registration is held by the 澳大利亚n sponsor — the sponsor identity and device description must match the shipped product. Asaptic provides the verified ARTG entry number and sponsor details in the document package.

In-Service (澳大利亚): AS/NZS 3551

Once the BIA device is deployed in an 澳大利亚n healthcare facility, the facility's biomedical engineering team applies AS/NZS 3551 management protocols: acceptance testing before first clinical use, periodic electrical safety checks, maintenance records, and decommissioning procedures. This is a facility-level obligation — procurement teams should confirm their biomedical engineering department's readiness before device delivery.

What CE MDR Does and Does Not Do

CE MDR certification from a Chinese factory is useful as supporting evidence (clinical evaluation, PMS plan, technical file quality) but does not confer Canadian or 澳大利亚n market access. Health Canada and TGA each operate independent regulatory pathways. MDALL and ARTG listings must be verified separately — CE MDR does not substitute for either.

常见问题

采购负责人最常问的合规问题。

Does ISO 13485 alone satisfy Health Canada 或 TGA?

No. ISO 13485 is a prerequisite QMS standard required by both regulators, but it is not a market-access licence. Health Canada requires an active MDALL licence for the specific device model. TGA requires an ARTG registration. ISO 13485, the MDALL, and the ARTG are three separate verification steps — all must be confirmed independently.

Can one Chinese factory supply BIA devices to both 加拿大与澳大利亚?

Yes, provided the factory holds both a Health Canada MDALL licence (for the 加拿大 pathway) and a TGA ARTG registration (for the 澳大利亚 pathway). Many Chinese factories serving export markets hold both for their BIA product lines. Asaptic verifies both database listings — by device model — before engaging any factory.

What is the difference between AS/NZS 3551 and IEC 60601-1?

IEC 60601-1 governs the design and manufacture of medical electrical equipment — it is a factory-level requirement. AS/NZS 3551 governs the in-service management of that equipment once deployed in an 澳大利亚n healthcare facility — it is a facility-level requirement. Both apply to BIA devices in 澳大利亚, at different points in the device lifecycle.

Where do I verify a Health Canada MDALL or TGA ARTG listing?

Health Canada MDALL is publicly searchable at mdall.hc-sc.gc.ca — search by manufacturer name, device model, or licence number. TGA ARTG is publicly searchable at tga.gov.au/resources/artg — search by ARTG entry number, sponsor name, or device description. Both databases are authoritative government sources. Asaptic provides the specific licence and ARTG numbers so buyers can verify directly.
