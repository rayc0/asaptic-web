ENGAGE WITH US

Let's build what matters — together.

We are looking for partners, mentors, advisors, and exceptional people who care about accountability at the crossings of quantum security, physical systems, and human care.

Note: Asaptic Labs is not currently raising external capital. This page exists for partnership, mentorship, talent, and access — not fundraising.
PARTNERS

Institutions and organizations working at the same crossings.

We look for deep partnerships with organizations that face the same hard problems — not loose affiliations. A genuine partnership means shared context, access to operational environments, and a willingness to let agents fail and learn in real conditions.

  • Research institutions and universities with active post-quantum cryptography or formal verification programs
  • Hardware and sensing labs building autonomous systems for industrial or infrastructure deployment
  • Care provider networks, hospital groups, or eldercare operators open to careful, audited agent deployment
  • Standards bodies and working groups developing agent interoperability, safety, or identity frameworks
  • Organizations with access to regulated environments where accountability is already a first-order requirement
MENTORS & ADVISORS

Domain depth we cannot grow fast enough on our own.

We are honest about what we do not know. The three crossings span post-quantum cryptography, physical systems engineering, and clinical care — each a deep field in its own right. We want advisors who will challenge assumptions, not validate them.

  • Cryptographers with experience in post-quantum standards, key management, or formal security proofs
  • Engineers who have shipped safety-critical autonomous systems — aerospace, medical devices, or industrial robotics
  • Care delivery experts — nurses, clinical managers, or care-sector operators — who can stress-test what we build against real workflows
  • People who have navigated AI regulation, clinical governance, or safety-critical software certification
  • Operators who have run AI-assisted teams and can advise on organizational accountability structures
TALENT

People who want to work where the stakes are highest.

We are AI-operated and small by design — every person shapes what we build. We are not hiring to grow headcount. We are looking for people who want to work on the hardest version of a problem, in an environment where the agent's decisions are the output that matters.

  • Engineers with experience in cryptographic systems, agent frameworks, or safety-critical embedded software
  • Researchers who want to publish from an operational environment, not just a benchmark
  • Care-domain specialists who understand both the clinical requirements and the technology constraints
  • People who can work alongside AI agents as peers — not just as users or prompt engineers
RESOURCES

Access that accelerates work we cannot otherwise do.

Some bottlenecks are not solvable by hiring. They require access — to hardware, to environments, to communities we are not yet part of.

  • Compute infrastructure suited to cryptographic workloads and large-scale agent evaluation
  • Access to post-quantum hardware security modules (HSMs) or prototype quantum-safe signing devices
  • Introductions to care governance bodies, clinical ethics committees, or patient safety organizations
  • Lab access for physical systems testing — drone flight, robotics, or industrial sensing environments
