Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire or lamp manufacturer entering the Singapore market must determine whether the product is a Controlled Good under Enterprise Singapore's Consumer Protection (Safety Requirements) Registration Scheme (CPS) — directly-connected mains lamps and certain luminaires are in scope and require registration and the SAFETY Mark, held by a Singapore-incorporated Registered Supplier; demonstrate safety conformity to the applicable SS/IEC standards (SS/IEC 60598 for luminaires, IEC 62560 for self-ballasted lamps, IEC 62471 for photobiological safety) via a Conformity Assessment Body report; register any wireless function (e.g. Bluetooth/Wi-Fi smart lighting) with IMDA before supply; and address NEA energy considerations (the Mandatory Energy Labelling Scheme and Minimum Energy Performance Standards apply to in-scope lamps). Singapore has no horizontal RoHS directive, so restricted-substance control is product/standard-driven and electrical waste is handled through NEA's Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) e-waste scheme — none of which is covered by existing Chinese CCC certification. Verify the current controlled goods list and scheme requirements before shipment.
