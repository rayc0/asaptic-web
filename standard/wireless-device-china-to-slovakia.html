Short answer

Slovakia is an EU member state and Eurozone member — EU Radio Equipment Directive (RED 2014/53/EU) applies in full. ÚRSO (Úrad pre reguláciu elektronických komunikácií a poštových služieb) is the national authority for radio/spectrum enforcement; SOVA (Slovenská obchodná inšpekcia — Slovak Trade Inspection Authority) handles product market surveillance. CE marking with harmonised EN standards (EN 300 328 / EN 301 893 radio; EN 301 489 EMC; EN IEC 62368-1:2020+A11 safety) is mandatory. Chinese SRRC approval and CCC are not recognised. Slovak-specific obligations include mandatory Slovak-language product labelling, separate WEEE national registration with NATUR-PACK or ENVIDOM (zákon č. 79/2015 Z. z.), and EU Authorised Representative appointment for non-EU manufacturers. Slovakia is a major EU automotive and electronics manufacturing hub (Volkswagen, Kia, Samsung SDI), generating high electronics import volumes. RED Art. 3.3 cybersecurity under EN 18031 is mandatory across the EU from 1 August 2025.
