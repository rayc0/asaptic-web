Short answer

For BESS exported from China to Uzbekistan, the first legal-market-access distinction is that Uzbekistan uses the domestic O'zstandart conformity route for regulated electrical products and O'z DSt national standards, many derived from GOST or IEC; it is not an EAEU member, so EAC certification should not be assumed to be the Uzbekistan legal route. The practical gaps are O'zstandart conformity documentation, IEC 62619 / IEC 62133 / IEC 62933-5-2 evidence or O'z DSt/GOST equivalents, National Electric Grid of Uzbekistan or regional energy-company grid technical conditions, fire-authority installation approval, project/lender specifications for ACWA Power, Masdar, IFC/EBRD-backed projects, 220/380 V 50 Hz parameter confirmation, and UN 38.3 transport evidence. China GB/T 36558-2023, GB/T 34120-2023, GB 44240-2024, GB/T 36276-2023, and NB/T 42090-2016 can support mapping but are not automatically accepted as Uzbekistan clearance.
