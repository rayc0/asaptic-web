Short answer

For Chinese furniture exported to Australia, the main gaps are: mandatory ACCC toppling furniture warning and safety-information rules from 4 May 2025 for covered clothing storage units, bookcases, entertainment units, hall tables, display cabinets, buffets and sideboards; mandatory button and coin battery standards for furniture with remotes, lights, memory modules or electronic accessories; no UK-style national mandatory upholstery flammability regime, although general safety duties and buyer specifications still matter; and formaldehyde evidence for wood-based panels is usually managed through voluntary AS/NZS 4266 or buyer limits rather than a furniture-specific national mandatory standard.

QB/T case-furniture stability or strength test reports where used by manufacturers		 The Consumer Goods (Toppling Furniture) Information Standard 2024 is mandatory for covered freestanding household furniture supplied in Australia from 4 May 2025. It applies to clothing storage units and bookcases 686 mm or higher, entertainment units of any height, and hall tables, display cabinets, buffets and sideboards 686 mm or higher. Suppliers must attach permanent warning labels, provide point-of-sale warnings in stores and online, and include toppling-risk information in instruction manuals.
