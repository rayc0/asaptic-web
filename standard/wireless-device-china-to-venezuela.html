Short answer

The main Venezuela compliance gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT device are: CONATEL mandatory homologación — all radio and telecom terminal equipment must obtain a CONATEL homologation certificate before import or sale, with no recognition of FCC, CE, SRRC, or CCC approvals; SENCAMER COVENIN EMC and electrical safety standards (IEC/ISO-adopting); RIF (Registro de Información Fiscal) importer registration; Spanish labelling displaying the CONATEL approval reference; and CONATEL cybersecurity oversight (no mandatory device-level certificate, but advisory scope applies). Venezuela uses 120 V / 60 Hz / Type A/B, making US-spec hardware electrically compatible — but CONATEL homologación is still separately required. Practical note: hyperinflation, USD forex controls, and disrupted formal import channels represent significant operational risks that should be assessed independently.
