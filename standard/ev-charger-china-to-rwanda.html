Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Rwanda, the confirmed practical gaps are: connector hardware redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) / CCS2 (DC), since Rwanda's IEC-aligned infrastructure is physically incompatible with GB/T connectors; IEC 61851-based safety evidence required by RSB and project owners; RSB PVoC (Pre-export Verification of Conformity) import-conformity process for electrical equipment; RURA licensing and site approval for charging infrastructure operators; REG / EUCL grid-connection approval and load-management coordination for grid-tied installations; and OCPP back-office integration for networked chargers. Rwanda operates on 230 V single-phase / 400 V three-phase, 50 Hz — closely aligned with China's 220 V / 380 V baseline, so voltage derating risk is lower than in some other markets, but confirmation of input-range tolerance and tropical-humidity thermal design is still required. An important honest context: Rwanda's most active EV segment as of 2026 is electric motorcycles (e-motos) using battery-swap infrastructure (Ampersand, Spiro), not four-wheel IEC-socket chargers. The formal IEC 62196 four-wheel charging ecosystem is at an early deployment stage; exporters of 4-wheel chargers should confirm active project demand, RURA operator licensing status, and RSB PVoC requirements with local partners before shipment.

Connector Interoperability — GB/T 20234 vs IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 China AC chargers use GB/T 20234.2 couplers and DC fast chargers use GB/T 20234.3 couplers. Although the GB/T 20234.2 AC coupler has a similar overall shape to the IEC 62196 Type 2, they differ in connector gender (GB/T uses male connector at the charger and female vehicle inlet, opposite to Type 2), signaling protocol (CC/CP versus PP/CP), and contact arrangement, making them physically and electrically incompatible. GB/T 20234.3 DC couplers are geometrically different from CCS2 and use a nine-pin configuration with CAN bus via GB/T 27930 communication, incompatible with the CCS2 / IEC 61851-24 communication stack.
