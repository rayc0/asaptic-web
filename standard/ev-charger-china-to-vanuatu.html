Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Vanuatu, the practical gaps are IEC-aligned product evidence, connector conversion from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC, URA-regulated utility or concessionaire project acceptance with UNELCO in Port Vila and VUI in relevant service areas, EMC and power-quality evidence under IEC 61000 practice, and environmental derating for cyclone exposure, salt mist, humidity, corrosion, surge, and island logistics. Vanuatu uses 230 V single-phase / 400 V three-phase at 50 Hz. The frequency is the same as China, but the nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V baseline, so input-voltage range, protection settings, cable ratings, and thermal derating must be confirmed. Vanuatu's EV market is essentially non-existent or very early, and an EVSE-specific national whole-unit certification rule could not be confirmed from official public sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters should verify the current VBSI, URA, utility, customs, HS-code, and project route before shipment.
