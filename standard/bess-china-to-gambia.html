Short answer

Gambia does not have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation. BESS projects in Gambia operate primarily under donor-project specifications (ECOWAS ROGEP, World Bank, UNDP), PURA operating-licence conditions, and NAWEC grid-connection agreements. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Gambia are: (1) grid voltage compatibility — Gambia operates at 230/400 V whereas China is 220/380 V, requiring PCS re-parameterisation and re-testing; (2) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline for donor-funded project acceptance; (3) IEC 62933-5-2 environmental and functional safety evidence for tropical climate conditions (high ambient temperature, high humidity, dust ingress); (4) PURA operating licence and NAWEC grid-connection approval — both are mandatory project gates with no publicly confirmed technical specification as of the dataset date; (5) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory for all lithium battery imports via Port Banjul under IMDG; and (6) TGSB conformity certification — the Gambia Standards Bureau (TGSB) role in BESS product conformity has not been confirmed from official sources. Chinese GB 44240-2024 and GB/T 36276-2023 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC 62619 evidence in donor-project or authority review.
