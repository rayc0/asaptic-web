Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face five major Qatar compliance gaps: (1) electrical safety conformity to GSO IEC 60335-2-24 under the QGOSM conformity regime — CCC/GB 4706.13 certification is not accepted, and units must be rated for the Qatar 240 V / 50 Hz supply; (2) GSO energy-efficiency labelling and MEPS compliance — a GSO/Qatar energy-efficiency label must be earned and affixed, distinct from China's GB 12021.2 grades; (3) EMC compliance to GSO-adopted CISPR/IEC standards — Chinese GB 4343.1 CCC test data cannot substitute without re-issuance to GSO-referenced standards; (4) appointment of a Qatar-registered in-country importer who holds the QGOSM registration and clears goods at Hamad Port; and (5) R-600a refrigerant charge documentation against GSO/IEC 60335-2-24 flammable-refrigerant requirements. R-600a is well-positioned but charge and documentation must be verified to GSO requirements.

Energy Efficiency — GSO MEPS for Household Refrigerators (QGOSM / GSO minimum energy performance) China's mandatory energy-efficiency standard for household refrigerators is GB 12021.2-2015 (Minimum allowable values of energy efficiency and energy-efficiency grades for household refrigerators).
