Short answer

The main Colombia compliance gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT device are: CRC mandatory homologación under IT&C Law 1341/2009 and CRC Resolution 5050/2016 — all radio and telecom terminal equipment requires a CRC homologación certificate before import or sale, with no recognition of CE, CCC, or SRRC approvals; an authorized Colombian importer or representative registered with CRC is required; ICONTEC NTC electrical safety and EMC compliance (NTC standards harmonised with IEC/ISO); product compatibility with Colombia's 110–120 V / 60 Hz power system and Type A/B (US/Canada-style) plugs — distinct from the 220V/50Hz systems prevalent elsewhere in South America; and Spanish language labelling. FCC test reports may serve as a useful starting basis for CRC homologación but do not substitute for it. Cybersecurity certification is currently advisory only under MinTIC policy and Law 1273/2009.
