Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Suriname, the single most critical gap is the grid frequency: Suriname operates at 60 Hz (Americas standard), while Chinese inverters are designed, tested, and certified for 50 Hz grids. This means: (1) anti-islanding protection tested under IEC 62116 at 50 Hz does not satisfy 60 Hz grid requirements — retesting at 60 Hz is required; (2) firmware protection thresholds, reconnection timing, and MPPT parameters validated for Chinese 50 Hz / 220-380 V conditions must be reconfigured and re-documented for Suriname's 60 Hz / 127-220 V grid before installation; (3) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence is required; (4) IEC 62920 EMC evidence must cover the 60 Hz operating configuration; (5) SSB conformity and EBS interconnection approval are the regulatory gates. China GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 are 50 Hz-based and do not satisfy Suriname's IEC-referenced requirements or EBS interconnection conditions.
