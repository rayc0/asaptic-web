Short answer

Kuwait does not have a publicly confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to the EU Battery Regulation or a published utility BESS code. The practical route for China-origin BESS is import conformity under KUCAS/PAI where the product is in scope, then project-specific acceptance with MEW and the project owner, plus Kuwait Fire Force installation approval. The main gaps are: (1) IEC 62619 or applicable GSO-adopted IEC evidence for industrial lithium cells and batteries; (2) IEC 62933-5-2 system safety evidence where required by the project specification; (3) Kuwait 240/415 V, 50 Hz grid settings and MEW connection review; (4) KFF fire-safety design approval for installation, commonly requiring direct local engineering engagement; and (5) UN 38.3 transport test summaries for lithium battery shipment. China GB/T 36558-2023, GB/T 34120-2023, and NB/T 42090 are useful engineering baselines but are not accepted by themselves as Kuwait grid, fire, or import conformity evidence.
