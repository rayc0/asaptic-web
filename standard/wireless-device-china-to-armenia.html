Short answer

Wireless devices entering Armenia require EAC (Eurasian Conformity) marking under TR EAEU 037/2016 (radio equipment) and TR CU 020/2011 (EMC), issued via EAC Declaration of Conformity or EAC Certificate from an accredited EAEU certification body — Armenia is a full EAEU member, so EAC approval obtained in any EAEU member state (Russia, Kazakhstan, Belarus, Kyrgyzstan) is mutually recognised. Mains-powered devices additionally require EAC conformity under TR EAEU 004/2011 (electrical safety, GOST IEC 62368-1). PSRC (Public Services Regulatory Commission) manages spectrum and may require separate registration or notification for radio-frequency products placed on the Armenian market. Electrical grid is 220 V/50 Hz with Type C/F (Schuko-compatible) plugs. Armenian-language (Hayeren) labelling is required. A local importer or authorised distributor registered in Armenia is mandatory. Chinese CCC, SRRC, CE, and FCC documentation are not recognised and do not substitute for EAC conformity. Cybersecurity obligations apply under the Data Security Agency of Armenia and CERT-AM. Note that 5 GHz W52/W53 channels are restricted to indoor use in Armenia.
