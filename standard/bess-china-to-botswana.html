Short answer

Botswana does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation specifically for utility-scale stationary storage systems equivalent to the Saudi SABER/IEC 62619 route or the EU Battery Regulation. The Botswana Bureau of Standards (BOBS) operates the Standards (Compulsory Standards) Regulations (SCSR) scheme — a pre-shipment certificate-of-conformity (CoC) programme covering regulated product categories effective from April 1, 2024 — but no confirmed explicit BESS or large-format lithium battery category within the current regulated product list has been identified from publicly accessible official sources as of the dataset date. BERA (Botswana Energy Regulatory Authority) is the sector regulator and must license generation and storage installations; BPC (Botswana Power Corporation) is the utility and manages grid connection. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Botswana are: (1) BOBS SCSR CoC obligation — verify the current regulated product list with BOBS directly to determine whether large-format lithium BESS cells or systems require a pre-shipment certificate of conformity; (2) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence — the internationally expected baseline for project acceptance, as Chinese GB 44240-2024 and GB/T 36276-2023 are not accepted as substitutes by project owners or international lenders such as the World Bank; (3) IEC 62933 system-level technical requirements where referenced in project specifications or BPC connection agreements; (4) BERA generation and storage licensing — required before commissioning any grid-connected BESS; (5) BPC grid-connection approval — Botswana's grid operates at 230/400 V, 50 Hz (versus China's 220/380 V); PCS must be re-parameterised and validated for Botswana's grid conditions; (6) local fire authority and building-control fire-safety approval — Botswana's fire safety framework references local building codes, BOBS fire standards, and in practice IEC-aligned fire-safety evidence; (7) UN 38.3 transport documentation mandatory under international transport conventions; and (8) Botswana-specific design factors including high ambient temperature (Kalahari desert conditions, peak ambient above 40°C possible), dust ingress (IP rating), and landlocked transport routing via Durban (South Africa) or Walvis Bay (Namibia) with multi-modal road or rail legs. Chinese GB/T 36558-2023, GB/T 34120-2023, NB/T 42090-2016, and GB 44240-2024 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC 62619 evidence in project or authority review.
