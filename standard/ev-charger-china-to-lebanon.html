Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Lebanon, the key practical gaps are LIBNOR conformity review for the exact imported electrical product or package, IEC 61851-1 and IEC 61851-23 safety evidence, IEC 62196 connector hardware for Type 2 AC and CCS2 DC charging, IEC 61000 EMC evidence, and EDL coordination where the installation connects to the public grid. Lebanon's nominal low-voltage supply context is 220/380 V at 50 Hz, but the state grid has been severely constrained; many EV charging deployments are nascent private sites tied to solar-plus-battery systems rather than reliable grid-fed DC fast-charging stations. China GB/T-connector chargers using GB/T 20234 and GB/T 27930 are not plug-compatible with IEC 62196 / CCS2 deployments and require hardware, signaling, and communication-stack redesign. A blanket Lebanon whole-unit mandatory EVSE certification rule could not be confirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters should verify LIBNOR scope, customs treatment, importer obligations, EDL requirements, and project-owner specifications before shipment.
