Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Angola, the key practical gaps are: (1) IANORQ conformity and import-facing standards checks, often using IEC-referenced evidence rather than China-only certificates; (2) IRSEA electricity-sector licensing and project authorization where the project or operator requires regulatory approval; (3) ENDE distribution connection or RNT transmission interface review for grid-tied projects; (4) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 interconnection, and IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC evidence; and (5) Portuguese-language documentation and firmware validation for Angola's 50 Hz, 220/380 V grid. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence is useful background but is not automatically accepted by IANORQ, IRSEA, ENDE, EPC contractors, or lenders without supplementary IEC-referenced testing and project approval.
