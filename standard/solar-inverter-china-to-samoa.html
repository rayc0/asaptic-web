Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Samoa, the practical gaps are: (1) confirming MCIL import and product documentation expectations because Samoa does not appear to have a strong dedicated standards body for PV inverter certification; (2) obtaining IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence expected by EPC projects and financiers; (3) providing IEC 62116 and IEC 61727 grid-interface evidence for OOTR / EPC review; (4) providing IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC evidence; (5) validating firmware for Samoa's 50 Hz / 230 V / 400 V grid, which differs from China's 50 Hz / 220 V / 380 V nominal voltage; and (6) documenting cyclone, humidity, and salt-mist coastal derating plus sea-freight spares planning through Apia. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence is useful background but is not automatically accepted as Samoa IEC or EPC project evidence.
