Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face four major Brazil compliance gaps: (1) electrical safety re-certification to ABNT NBR IEC 60335-2-24 under INMETRO compulsory certification — CCC/GB 4706.13 certification is not accepted and third-party certification by an accredited OCP plus factory inspection is required; (2) Selo PROCEL / PBE energy labelling under the INMETRO + Eletrobras programme, with energy performance recalculated to Brazilian methodology and minimum efficiency levels enforced; (3) an in-country importer or representative holding a CNPJ must hold the INMETRO certification, since certification is issued to a Brazil-resident legal entity; and (4) verification of the R-600a refrigerant transition against IBAMA / Kigali Amendment controls plus ABNT NBR IEC 60335-2-24 flammable-charge limits. Grid voltage (127/220 V, 60 Hz) differs from China (220/380 V, 50 Hz), so electrical re-testing to Brazilian supply conditions is required.
