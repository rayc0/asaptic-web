Short answer

Côte d'Ivoire has no EU-style PPE Regulation. Market access rests on two pillars: (1) CODINORM national (NI) standards, which adopt international references including EN 149 for FFP respirators (FFP1/FFP2/FFP3); and (2) the Ministry of Trade Verification of Conformity (VoC) import-inspection programme, under which consignments are checked against the applicable standard before clearance at Abidjan or San-Pédro. There is no CE-style self-affixed mark and no local Notified-Body equivalent; conformity is demonstrated to the VoC inspection body, documentation must be in French, and a resident Ivorian importer handles clearance. Chinese KN95 (GB 2626) reports are a useful technical baseline but do not by themselves meet EN 149; medical masks are handled through the national health regulator, not the PPE route.

Filtering Facepiece Respirator Safety — EN 149 Performance via CODINORM/NI Adoption China's primary standard for non-powered air-purifying particle respirators is GB 2626-2019, with classes KN90 and KN95. GB 2626-2019 is a mandatory national standard (GB, not GB/T) enforced by SAMR. KN95 requires ≥95% filtration efficiency against NaCl particles at 85 L/min. Key differences from EN 149: GB 2626 uses sodium chloride (NaCl) aerosol only (EN 149 uses both NaCl and paraffin oil); it does not require the practical performance (simulated workplace) test; and it lacks the dolomite clogging resistance test. A CNAS-accredited GB 2626 test report is useful technical evidence but is not, on its own, a demonstration of conformity to the adopted EN 149 standard required by the Ivorian VoC route.GB 2626-2019 — Respiratory protective equipment — Non-powered air-purifying particle respirator (mandatory national standard, SAMR)
