Short answer

Antigua and Barbuda does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to the EU Battery Regulation or a dedicated BESS conformity regime under ABBS. BESS projects operate primarily under project-specific international standards referenced in APUA interconnection agreements and Antigua and Barbuda Fire Service approvals. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Antigua and Barbuda are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline for project acceptance; (2) IEC 62933 system-level technical requirements where referenced in APUA or project-owner specifications; (3) Fire Service installation approval, including evidence aligned with NFPA 855 as the dominant BESS fire-installation code; (4) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory under international transport conventions for sea freight via St John's Port; (5) 230/400 V grid configuration for BESS PCS — CRITICAL: Antigua and Barbuda shares 50 Hz with China, but the nominal voltage differs (Antigua and Barbuda 230/400 V vs China 220/380 V) — PCS must be re-parameterised and re-tested; and (6) hurricane-resilience structural and environmental design for Category 4–5 wind loads and tropical marine salt-mist conditions. Chinese GB 44240-2024 and GB/T 36276-2023 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC 62619 evidence in project or authority review.
