Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Costa Rica, the most critical practical gaps are: (1) the fundamental grid parameter difference — Costa Rica operates at 60 Hz / 120-240 V (single-phase split-phase), whereas China inverters are factory-configured for 50 Hz / 220-380 V; this requires firmware reconfiguration and retesting before any grid interconnection; (2) IEC 62116 anti-islanding compliance validated at 60 Hz — Chinese anti-islanding test results at 50 Hz do not transfer; (3) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence for the inverter model; (4) compliance with ARESEP's generación distribuida para autoconsumo regulatory framework and ICE or relevant distributor interconnection requirements; (5) INTECO conformity where applicable. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 (all 50 Hz-based) do not satisfy Costa Rica ICE/ARESEP interconnection requirements and cannot be substituted without supplementary IEC test evidence validated at 60 Hz.
