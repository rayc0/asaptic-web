Short answer

Exporting EV chargers from China to Luxembourg requires CE marking under LVD 2014/35/EU, EMC Directive 2014/30/EU, and RED 2014/53/EU (if wireless). AFIR Regulation (EU) 2023/1804 mandates IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) and CCS Combo 2 (DC fast) connectors — physically incompatible with Chinese GB/T 20234 connectors, requiring complete hardware redesign. Creos grid-connection approval is required at 230/400 V 50 Hz. OCPP back-office interoperability replaces Chinese GB/T 27930 CAN protocol. An EU Authorised Representative must be appointed before first unit enters Luxembourg market. French and German language documentation is expected.
