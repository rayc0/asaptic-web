Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face four main Rwanda compliance gaps: (1) RSB Pre-Export Verification of Conformity (PVoC) and a Certificate of Conformity (CoC) are mandatory per consignment before shipment — Chinese CCC/GB certification does not by itself produce a Rwanda CoC; (2) electrical safety must be demonstrated against the Rwanda-adopted RS/IEC 60335-2-24 standard (IEC-based), so CCC test reports referencing GB 4706.13 may be re-used only where issued under an IECEE CB Scheme covering the relevant IEC edition; (3) energy performance must meet Rwanda's energy label / MEPS requirements, which use IEC 62552-based test methods and differ from China's GB 12021.2 grade system; (4) products are sold through an in-country importer and shipped via Mombasa or Dar es Salaam (Rwanda is landlocked). Rwanda's grid is 230 V / 50 Hz — same 50 Hz as China but nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V. R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned. Rwanda does not operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS / battery / outdoor-noise regime; those EU requirements have no direct Rwanda equivalent. verified:false throughout — confirm against RSB before relying.
