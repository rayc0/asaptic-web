Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Malawi, the key practical realities are: (1) Malawi's EV market is among the world's least developed — four-wheel EV penetration is negligible and the primary electric mobility opportunity is e-bikes and e-motos, not AC/DC car chargers; (2) Malawi's national grid (ESCOM, 230/400 V 50 Hz) suffers from chronic load-shedding and very low electrification rates outside urban centres, severely constraining any charging infrastructure deployment; (3) the Malawi Bureau of Standards (MBS) administers import conformity requirements, but a dedicated mandatory EV-charger conformity regulation could not be confirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14 — general electrical equipment import rules and MBS compulsory conformity assessment apply and should be verified; (4) the Malawi Energy Regulatory Authority (MERA) is the statutory regulator for energy services and its approval is required for commercial EV charging operations; (5) connector design must shift from China GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) / CCS2 (DC) — the two systems are physically incompatible; (6) IEC 61851 safety evidence and MERA / ESCOM project-acceptance documentation are required gates. Exporters should verify the specific MBS compulsory conformity category, MERA licence requirements, and ESCOM connection approval process before shipment.
