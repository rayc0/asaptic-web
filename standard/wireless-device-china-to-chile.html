Short answer

The main Chile compliance gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT device are: SUBTEL mandatory homologación under Ley de Telecomunicaciones 18.168 — all radio and telecom terminal equipment requires a SUBTEL certificate and TS (Tipo y Subtipo) number on the label before import or sale, with no recognition of FCC, CE, SRRC, or CCC approvals; product adaptation to the unique Chilean type-L plug (3-pin round at 70°) and 220V/50Hz power system; SEC electrical safety certification; SII RUT importer registration for customs clearance; INN/NCh-harmonised EMC compliance; Spanish language labelling; and monitoring obligations under the Ley Marco de Ciberseguridad 2024, which is expanding IoT requirements.
