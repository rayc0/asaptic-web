Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Sierra Leone, the key practical gaps are: (1) SLSB conformity assessment — Sierra Leone Standards Bureau is adopting IEC-referenced standards and may require conformity evidence; Chinese GB/T certificates are not automatically accepted; (2) EWRC licensing — the Electricity and Water Regulatory Commission licenses electricity infrastructure; equipment must meet conditions set in EWRC licences and project approvals; (3) EDSA grid-connection conditions — for grid-tied systems, EDSA connection requires IEC-referenced inverter specifications including IEC 62109-1/-2 safety, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, and 230/400 V 50 Hz firmware settings, not the 220/380 V Chinese GB/T 156 baseline; (4) for off-grid and mini-grid applications (which dominate the Sierra Leone market given approximately 25% electrification), donor-driven and lender-driven projects typically require VeraSol Tier qualification and/or IEC TS 62257 compliance — Chinese domestic approvals do not satisfy these; (5) humidity, coastal salt-air, and Harmattan dust derating must be documented for Sierra Leone's climate zones. China GB/T 37408 and GB/T 19964 baselines do not satisfy IEC-referenced SLSB, EWRC, EDSA, or donor/lender requirements and cannot be substituted without supplementary IEC test evidence.

Chinese off-grid PV inverter documentation commonly references GB/T 37408-2019 (grid-connected inverter technical requirements) or GB/T 20321-2006 (off-grid wind/PV power systems, now superseded). Neither of these Chinese standards satisfies IEC TS 62257 compliance requirements or VeraSol Tier qualification criteria. IEC 62109-1 / IEC 62109-2 Safety Evidence — SLSB Conformity, EWRC Licensing, and Donor Procurement Requirements China's primary voluntary standard for PV grid-connected inverter safety is GB/T 37408-2019 (Technical Requirements for Photovoltaic Grid-Connected Inverters).
