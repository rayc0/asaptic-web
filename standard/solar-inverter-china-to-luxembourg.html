Short answer

Exporting PV inverters from China to Luxembourg requires CE marking under LVD 2014/35/EU and EMC Directive 2014/30/EU, compliance with IEC 62109-1/-2 safety standards, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 utility interface, and IEC 62920/IEC 61000 EMC. Creos requires grid-connection approval per EN 50549-1/-2 and Commission Regulation (EU) 2016/631. Luxembourg operates at 230/400 V 50 Hz (vs China 220/380 V), requiring firmware and protection threshold re-validation. Chinese standards (GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004-2018) are not accepted as EU equivalents. ILNAS administers standards adoption; ILR oversees energy market regulation. French and German language documentation is often expected.
