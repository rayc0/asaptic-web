Short answer

For China-made power tools entering Singapore, the key access question is whether the item is a Controlled Good under the Consumer Protection (Safety Requirements) Registration Scheme administered by Enterprise Singapore; household-type tools in scope need a SAFETY Mark obtained through a Singapore-based Registered Supplier, while industrial tools are generally outside the controlled-goods list. SS/IEC 62841 is the common safety basis, cordless radio-enabled tools require IMDA registration, and Singapore has no equivalent to the EU outdoor-noise marking regime.

China domestic noise declaration practice where applicable		 Singapore does not operate an equivalent to the EU outdoor-noise marking and guaranteed sound-power scheme for equipment used outdoors, so there is no product-level noise marking or declared sound-power obligation on a power tool as a condition of supply. Noise is instead governed at the workplace and environmental level: occupational noise exposure sits under the Workplace Safety and Health framework administered by the Ministry of Manpower, and environmental and construction-site noise sits under National Environment Agency rules.
