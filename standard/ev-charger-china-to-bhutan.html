Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Bhutan, the practical gaps are IEC-oriented safety and EMC evidence, connector redesign from China GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC, BEA / BPC project-level grid-connection acceptance, and high-altitude derating for Himalayan deployment conditions. Bhutan's low-voltage supply should be treated as 230/400 V, 50 Hz: the 50 Hz frequency is shared with China, but the voltage differs from China's 220/380 V baseline, so input-voltage range, protection thresholds, thermal performance, and power-quality settings must be confirmed. Bhutan is a hydro-dominant, India-interconnected market with a nascent EV-charging sector; exporters should verify the current BSB standards route, BEA licensing or tariff requirements, BPC connection process, HS code, charger type, and site specification before shipment.
