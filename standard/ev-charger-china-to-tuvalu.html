Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Tuvalu, the comparison is forward-looking because Tuvalu's EV charging market is essentially pre-nascent. The practical gaps are IEC-based safety and EMC evidence, IEC 62196 Type 2 connector hardware where public AC charging is specified, TEC project and grid-connection acceptance, and salt-mist / humidity derating for atoll installations. Tuvalu uses 240 V single-phase / 415 V three-phase at 50 Hz: the frequency is the same as China, but the nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V baseline. A GB/T-only charger using GB/T 20234 connectors and GB/T 27930 DC communication is not physically or protocol compatible with IEC Type 2 / IEC 61851 deployments without hardware and firmware redesign.
