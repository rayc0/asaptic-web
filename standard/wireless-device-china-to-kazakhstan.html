Short answer

Wireless devices entering Kazakhstan require both OTAU radio type approval (for Kazakhstan-specific spectrum licensing) and EAC conformity marking under TR EAEU 037/2016 (radio equipment) and TR CU 020/2011 (EMC) for the broader Eurasian Economic Union market. Electrical safety testing against IEC 62368-1 / GOST standards is required for the 220 V/50 Hz European-plug (Type C/F) grid. A local importer or authorised dealer is mandatory. Kazakhstan's data localization law and GCERT cybersecurity guidelines add additional obligations for connected devices. Chinese CCC, SRRC, and CE documentation are not recognised and do not substitute for OTAU or EAC approvals.

Local Importer / Authorised Dealer Requirement — Kazakhstan and EAEU For products sold within China, there is generally no requirement for a separate local importer entity as a condition of CCC certification — Chinese manufacturers can obtain CCC certification directly. For exports, Chinese regulations do not impose a symmetric requirement on the export side. However, for products exported to Kazakhstan, the Chinese exporter must partner with a Kazakhstan-registered importer or authorised dealer who will serve as the EAC certificate holder and OTAU type approval applicant.
