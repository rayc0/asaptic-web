Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Rwanda, the key practical gaps are: (1) RSB import conformity — Rwanda operates a pre-export/import conformity verification (PVoC-style) regime under RSB; inverters may require a Certificate of Conformity or pre-shipment inspection depending on product category designation; (2) RURA regulatory licensing — grid-tied PV systems and mini-grid operators require RURA licensing; (3) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence is the expected international baseline for utility and project procurement in Rwanda; (4) IEC 62116 anti-islanding compliance for grid-tied systems; (5) IEC 62920 / IEC 61000-3-2 EMC evidence; and (6) firmware validation against Rwanda's 50 Hz / 230 V / 400 V grid parameters. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 baselines are not automatically accepted and cannot be substituted for IEC-referenced evidence without supplementary test documentation.
