China-to-Rwanda LED Luminaire Compliance Gap Matrix

Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Rwanda Standards Board (RSB) requirements: mandatory Pre-Export Verification of Conformity (PVoC) with a Certificate of Conformity, the S-Mark / import inspection scheme, Rwanda Standards (RS) that adopt IEC 60598 / IEC 62560 / IEC 62471, the national energy-efficiency labelling programme, and RURA radio approval for connected lamps — compared against Chinese GB standards and CCC certification.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Rwanda must clear the Rwanda Standards Board (RSB) Pre-Export Verification of Conformity (PVoC) programme and obtain a Certificate of Conformity per consignment before the goods leave China, because regulated electrical products are inspected at import and must carry the S-Mark / pass RSB import inspection; product safety is assessed against Rwanda Standards (RS) that adopt IEC 60598 (luminaires) and IEC 62560 (self-ballasted LED lamps), photobiological safety against IEC 62471, and energy efficiency against the national energy-labelling programme; connected or radio-enabled lamps additionally need RURA radio approval. Existing Chinese CCC certification and GB / GB-T 7000 and GB 30255 evidence are not automatically accepted — RSB normally relies on IEC / ISO-based test reports (often from ILAC-accredited labs) through the PVoC route. Note Rwanda runs the grid at 230 V / 50 Hz (50 Hz matches China; nominal voltage differs from China's 220 V single-phase / 380 V three-phase), so voltage range and marking must be verified. Rwanda does NOT operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS / hazardous-substance regime for general electricals, so there is no direct local RoHS equivalent to declare against (state this plainly to the importer rather than implying one exists).
