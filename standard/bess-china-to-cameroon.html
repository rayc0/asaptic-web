Short answer

Cameroon does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to a mandatory IEC 62619 certification regime. BESS projects in Cameroon operate primarily under ANOR conformity assessment (including the PECAE pre-shipment import conformity programme for regulated product categories), ARSEL electricity sector licensing, and ENEO or SONATREL grid-connection requirements. Cameroon's grid operates at 230/400 V 50 Hz; China's grid operates at 220/380 V — requiring PCS re-parameterisation. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Cameroon are: (1) ANOR PECAE pre-shipment conformity verification — confirm whether BESS cells, modules, or systems fall within the current PECAE regulated-product scope and obtain the required Certificate of Conformity before shipment; (2) IEC 62619 and IEC 62133 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline in donor-financed and IPP project specifications; (3) IEC 62933-5-2 system-level safety evidence where referenced in ARSEL licensing or project-owner specifications; (4) ARSEL electricity sector licensing for operators and project developers; (5) ENEO or SONATREL grid-connection approval with bilingual (French and English) technical documentation; (6) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory under international transport conventions; and (7) compliance with local fire and building authority installation requirements, as no national BESS-specific fire code exists in Cameroon. Chinese GB/T 36558-2023, GB/T 34120-2023, and NB/T 42090-2016 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC standards in donor, ARSEL, or project-owner review.

UN 38.3 Transport Safety Testing — Mandatory for Lithium Battery Imports to Cameroon via Douala or Kribi Chinese BESS cell and module manufacturers are required to comply with UN 38.3 for export shipments under international transport conventions. Chinese manufacturers typically hold UN 38.3 test reports and test summaries from CNAS-accredited testing laboratories such as UL, SGS, Bureau Veritas, TÜV, or CAICT. The UN 38.3 Test Summary (required since January 1, 2020) must cover the specific cell or battery type being shipped. A Chinese-origin UN 38.3 test summary from an accredited laboratory is acceptable for Cameroon imports via Douala or Kribi — the key gap is ensuring the test summary covers the specific cell model, chemistry, capacity, and configuration of the BESS units being shipped, and that it is maintained current with any cell design changes.
