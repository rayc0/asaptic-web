Short answer

The main Afghanistan gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular, or IoT wireless device are: (1) ATRA mandatory type approval — the Afghanistan Telecom Regulatory Authority (اداره تنظیم مخابرات افغانستان) requires type approval for all radio and telecom terminal equipment sold or imported into Afghanistan under the Telecommunications Law 2012; (2) EMC and radio parameter testing to ITU-referenced standards as part of the ATRA application dossier, assessed by ANSA (Afghanistan National Standards Authority); (3) electrical safety compliance for 220 V / 50 Hz with plug Type C and Type F (European Schuko / round-pin) — Afghanistan shares China's 220 V / 50 Hz voltage but uses European-style plug types instead of the Chinese Type I or Type A; (4) a local Afghan authorized importer is required; (5) Dari and/or Pashto documentation is preferred for user-facing materials; and (6) export compliance must include comprehensive US, EU, and UN sanctions due diligence — sanctions apply to various entities in Afghanistan and must be verified before any commercial transaction. CE and FCC approvals are not recognized by ATRA as substitutes. Active Afghan telecom operators (MTN Afghanistan, Roshan, Etisalat/PWRR, AWCC) continue to procure and operate Chinese-made equipment, and Chinese equipment has significant market presence in Afghanistan.

CCC mandatory certification (GB 4943.1 scope)		 Afghanistan operates on a 220 V / 50 Hz power supply. Wall outlets accept Type C (Europlug, 2-round-pin) and Type F (Schuko, 2-round-pin with side grounding clips) plugs — the European standard. Although Afghanistan shares China mains voltage (220 V) and frequency (50 Hz), Afghanistan uses European-style plug types (C/F) rather than Chinese plug type I or type A. Wireless devices with mains power supply (chargers, adapters, AC-connected base stations) must comply with Afghan electrical safety requirements and must be supplied with Type C or Type F compatible plugs or adapters.
