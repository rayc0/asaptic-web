Short answer

The main Bolivia compliance gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT device are: ATT mandatory homologación — all radio and telecom terminal equipment must obtain an ATT homologation certificate under Ley No. 164 (2011) and display the approval number on a Spanish-language label before import or sale, with no recognition of FCC, CE, SRRC, or CCC approvals; IBNORCA NB standards for EMC (CISPR/IEC-aligned) and electrical safety (IEC 62368-1-aligned) at 220 V / 50 Hz; SIN NIT importer registration; and AGETIC cybersecurity policy (advisory, not a mandatory product certification). Bolivia uses 220 V / 50 Hz with a mixed Type A/C plug environment — Chinese hardware requires a power supply rated for 220 V / 50 Hz and may need a bundled plug adapter. Bolivia is landlocked and most imports transit through Chilean (Arica/Iquique) or Peruvian (Ilo) ports.
