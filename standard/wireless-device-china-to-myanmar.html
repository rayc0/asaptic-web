Short answer

The main Myanmar (MCIT / PTD) compliance gaps for a China-made wireless, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular, LoRa, or IoT device are: (1) mandatory PTD (Posts and Telecommunications Department, under MCIT) type approval for all radio and telecom terminal equipment — SRRC, FCC, CE, and CCC approvals are not recognised; a PTD approval sticker on the product is required; (2) PTD Radio Frequency Administration compliance for 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz Wi-Fi bands and other regulated frequencies; (3) EMC compliance — Myanmar has no comprehensive independent EMC standard framework; IEC/CISPR-based test documentation is advisable as supporting evidence; (4) electrical safety compliance for 230 V / 50 Hz supply with mixed plug types (A/B/C/D/F/G); (5) appointment of a verified local Myanmar importer or agent — the entity must be confirmed as not a sanctioned person or organisation; and (6) CRITICAL export-compliance obligation: mandatory sanctions screening against US OFAC, EU, UK, Canada, and Australia regimes before shipment — military-affiliated importers or end-users are prohibited for most exporters. Burmese (Myanmar) language labelling is required for consumer products. CCC, CE, and FCC provide no equivalence under Myanmar PTD. Internet shutdown and operational risk should also be assessed.
