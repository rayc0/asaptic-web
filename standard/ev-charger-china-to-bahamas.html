Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering the Bahamas, the fundamental gaps are 120/240 V 60 Hz electrical revalidation against China's 220/380 V 50 Hz domestic baseline, hardware conversion from GB/T 20234 to SAE J1772 for AC and CCS1 for DC, UL-direction EVSE safety evidence such as UL 2594 or UL 2202, FCC Part 15 / IEC 61000 EMC evidence for electronic and networked equipment, BPL site connection approval, and hurricane-prone island installation resilience. A Bahamas-specific mandatory EVSE certification route could not be confirmed from the provided sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters should verify the current BBSQ, URCA, BPL, customs, and project requirements before shipment.
