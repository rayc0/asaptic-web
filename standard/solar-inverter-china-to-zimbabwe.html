Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Zimbabwe, the key practical gaps are: (1) SAZ conformity and Zimbabwe-adopted IEC standards evidence rather than assuming China GB/T files are accepted; (2) ZERA electricity licensing and net-metering requirements where the project exports, sells, or connects generation above applicable thresholds; (3) ZETDC distribution-network connection review and commissioning conditions; (4) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence; (5) IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 utility-interface evidence; and (6) IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC and power-quality evidence for the actual 230/400 V, 50 Hz Zimbabwe grid profile. China GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 are useful baselines but are not automatically accepted as SAZ, ZERA, or ZETDC approval evidence.
