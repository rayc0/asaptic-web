Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Suriname, the confirmed practical gaps are: (1) connector hardware redesign — GB/T 20234 connectors are physically incompatible with the SAE J1772 (Type 1) AC and CCS1 DC connectors common to the Americas region; exporters must confirm the connector type required by EAS, EBS, and the specific project before quoting, as no single published Suriname national EV-charger connector standard was confirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14; (2) grid adaptation — Suriname operates at 127 V / 220 V single-phase and 60 Hz, requiring input-voltage and frequency confirmation different from China's 220 V / 380 V 50 Hz domestic baseline; (3) safety evidence aligned with IEC 61851 as referenced by Suriname's IEC-affiliate standards framework (SSB is an IEC Affiliate Member); (4) Dutch-language documentation required for the Suriname market; (5) EAS regulatory coordination and EBS grid-connection approval for any grid-connected installation. Suriname's EV market is nascent and formal mandatory charger certification rules had not been confirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters must engage EAS and EBS directly to confirm current requirements.

GB/T 18487.1-2023 — Electric vehicle conductive charging system — Part 1: General requirements		 Suriname is geographically in the Americas region and operates a 60 Hz electrical grid supplied by EBS, making it part of the Americas connector ecosystem.
