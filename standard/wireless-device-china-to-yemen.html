Short answer

The main Yemen gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular, or IoT wireless device are: (1) MTIT/TPSA mandatory type approval — the Ministry of Telecommunications and Information Technology and its subordinate Telecom and Posts & Savings Authority require type approval for all radio and telecom terminal equipment sold or imported into Yemen; (2) EMC and radio parameter testing to ITU-referenced standards as part of the MTIT/TPSA application dossier; (3) electrical safety compliance for 220–240 V / 50 Hz with Type A (US-style 2-flat-pin) and Type G (UK 3-pin) — both plugs are in common use in Yemen due to historical influences; (4) a local Yemeni importer or authorized agent is required; (5) product labelling in Arabic is required; and (6) sanctions and conflict-zone compliance — Yemen is subject to UN Security Council arms embargo (UNSCR 2216) and US/EU sanctions on Houthi-linked entities; dual-authority divided territory means enforcement and procedures differ between Houthi-controlled (north/Sanaa) and internationally recognized government (south/Aden) areas, and Chinese equipment is widely imported through informal channels in practice. CE, FCC, and CCC are not formally recognized by MTIT/TPSA. SRRC approval is not a substitute.
