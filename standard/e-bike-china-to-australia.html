Short answer

For Australia, the critical China-to-AU gap is classification: a road-legal e-bike is generally either a pedalec complying with EN 15194 and ≤250W continuous rated power, or a power-assisted pedal cycle with auxiliary motor output ≤200W under state road rules. Chinese GB 17761 permits up to 400W, so 251-400W models commonly fall outside the bicycle pathway and may be treated as registrable motor vehicles; charger, battery, RCM/electrical safety, ACCC product safety, AS/NZS evidence, and UN 38.3 documents must be handled separately.
