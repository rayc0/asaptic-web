Short answer

Tuvalu does not yet have a comprehensive standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation. BESS projects operate under a nascent, project-led regime: Tuvalu Department of Energy policy oversight, Tuvalu Electricity Corporation (TEC) interconnection review, and donor-funded ADB / World Bank / EU technical specifications that reference IEC standards. As a Pacific SIDS atoll nation with solar-plus-storage needs, Tuvalu relies on IEC and project-owner requirements in practice. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Tuvalu are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence required by project specifications — China GB 38031 / GB/T 36276 are not accepted substitutes; (2) IEC 62933 system-level performance and safety requirements referenced in TEC interconnection and donor-funded project approvals; (3) IEC 63056 where refurbished or second-life cells are used; (4) NFPA 855 fire-safety installation compliance where specified for building and installation approvals; (5) UN 38.3 and IEC 62281 transport documentation mandatory under international dangerous-goods conventions for sea freight via Funafuti; (6) voltage configuration: Tuvalu grid is 240/415 V 50 Hz while China is 220/380 V 50 Hz — nominal voltage differs and PCS must be configured accordingly; and (7) atoll environmental derating for salt-mist, heat, and humidity conditions. Chinese GB/T 36276, GB/T 34131, GB 38031, and GB/T 36558 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC evidence in DoE, TEC, or donor-funded project review.
