Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Algeria, the practical compliance gaps are IEC-route evidence and grid acceptance rather than China domestic approvals. Algeria's standards system is anchored by IANOR, while electricity regulation and grid operation involve CREG and Sonelgaz; Algeria operates a 230 V / 400 V, 50 Hz system. EV chargers should be checked against IEC 61851-1 for general conductive charging safety, IEC 61851-23 for DC stations, IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC connectors, and IEC 61000 / IEC 61851-21-2 for EMC. China GB/T 20234 connectors are physically incompatible with IEC 62196 vehicles and infrastructure, and GB/T 27930 DC communication does not replace CCS2 / IEC communication or OCPP back-office requirements. IANOR conformity, import documentation, Sonelgaz grid coordination, and any CREG or project-owner approval must be confirmed for the specific charger and site before shipment.
