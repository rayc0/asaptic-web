Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Cape Verde, the practical gaps are IEC/EU-style documentation rather than China-only GB/T evidence, Type 2 / CCS2 connector compatibility instead of GB/T 20234, 230/400 V 50 Hz grid ratings rather than China's 220/380 V 50 Hz baseline, IEC 61851 safety evidence, IEC 61000 EMC evidence, and island/coastal derating for salt mist, heat, wind-blown dust, logistics, and maintenance. Cape Verde's EV market is nascent, so project specifications from IGQPI, ARME, ELECTRA, public tenders, importers, and site owners should be confirmed before shipment.
