Short answer

For China-made electric or cordless power tools entering Japan, the mandatory baseline is DENAN (Electrical Appliances and Materials Safety Act) PSE conformity for in-scope corded tools and AC chargers/adaptors, with a Japan-resident business notifier; JIS C 62841 (= IEC 62841) is the common safety route, VCCI covers EMC, and Japan has no EU-style mandatory outdoor product-noise marking. Cordless lithium packs follow Japan's battery and DENAN rules.

Noise: No EU-Style Outdoor Product Marking in Japan China GB 3883 safety evidence focuses on electrical and mechanical hazards and typically does not include a product noise label. China handles noise via workplace and environmental rules at the place of use rather than a mandatory product-affixed sound-power marking. This is structurally similar to Japan's use-site approach.GB 3883 series
China workplace and environmental noise rules where applicable (use-site, not a product mark)		 Unlike the EU, Japan does not impose an EU-style mandatory outdoor-equipment noise-emission marking (there is no Japanese equivalent of Directive 2000/14/EC requiring a guaranteed sound-power level label on power tools). Noise in Japan is handled mainly as an environmental and occupational matter: the Noise Regulation Law governs construction-site and factory noise at the place of use, and the Industrial Safety and Health Act with related occupational-noise guidance addresses worker exposure. These are use-site and employer obligations, not a product label affixed by the manufacturer or importer. JIS sound-measurement methods may be cited voluntarily for declared noise data.Noise Regulation Law (騒音規制法) [environmental, use-site obligation, not a product mark]
Industrial Safety and Health Act (労働安全衛生法) and occupational-noise guidance [employer obligation]
JIS acoustic sound-power/measurement standards [voluntary technical route for declared data]		 There is effectively no product-marking gap to close for Japan on this point, because Japan has no EU-style outdoor product-noise label. Exporters should not assume an EU 2000/14/EC noise plate is required for Japan; instead, noise data is supplied voluntarily, and the operative Japanese obligations (Noise Regulation Law, occupational-noise) sit with the user/employer at the site of use. The real action is to avoid over-engineering an unnecessary Japan noise label and to keep any declared noise data measured by a recognized JIS method.[INFORMATIONAL] No EU-style outdoor product-noise marking is required for Japan, so there is no product-label compliance step here; exporters should treat Japanese noise obligations as use-site and occupational duties and supply any declared noise data on a voluntary, JIS-measured basis rather than replicating an EU 2000/14/EC plate.
