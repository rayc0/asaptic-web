Short answer

The main Uruguay compliance gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT device are: URSEC mandatory homologación under Ley 17.296 — all radio and telecom terminal equipment requires a URSEC certificate and URSEC mark/number on the label before import or sale, with no recognition of CCC or SRRC approvals (CE and FCC reports may be accepted as a basis for the technical file); an authorized Uruguayan importer with RUT/DGI registration is required; UNIT electrical safety and EMC compliance (standards harmonised with IEC/ISO and Mercosur NM); product compatibility with Uruguay's 220 V / 50 Hz power system and Type C/F/L plugs; and Spanish language labelling. Cybersecurity obligations are currently advisory under Ley 18.331 (personal data protection, AGESIC oversight) and Decreto 93/018 (national cybersecurity framework), but should be monitored as they evolve.
