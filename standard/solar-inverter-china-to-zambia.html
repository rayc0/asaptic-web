Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Zambia, the key practical gaps are: (1) ZABS conformity assessment — Zambia requires an import conformity certificate for regulated products; verify whether PV inverters fall under the current ZABS mandatory certification scheme; (2) ERB licensing — grid-tied PV installations require ERB licensing as an electricity generator; equipment must satisfy ERB technical conditions; (3) ZESCO grid-connection — grid-tied inverters must meet ZESCO network connection requirements including IEC 62116 anti-islanding, 50 Hz / 230 V / 400 V parameter validation, and protection settings; (4) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence is required by project specifications for utility-scale, C&I, and mining solar; (5) IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC evidence. China GB/T 37408-2021 and GB/T 19964-2024 are not automatically accepted as ZABS, ERB, or ZESCO compliance evidence and cannot substitute IEC-referenced test reports without supplementary evidence.
