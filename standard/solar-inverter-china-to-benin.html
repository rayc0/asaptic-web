Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Benin, the key practical points are: (1) Benin and China share the same 220/380 V, 50 Hz nominal grid voltage — eliminating the voltage re-parameterisation gap that exists for markets like Qatar (240/415 V); (2) however, ARE licensing, SBEE/CEB grid-connection approval, and IEC-based compliance evidence remain mandatory gates that Chinese GB/T documents cannot satisfy alone; (3) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence from an ILAC-accredited laboratory is required for ANM conformity and donor-funded project acceptance; (4) IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 interface compliance evidence are required for ARE/SBEE grid-connection approval; (5) IEC 62920 and IEC 61000-3-2 EMC evidence is required under ANM's IEC-aligned framework and donor procurement specifications; (6) West Africa's high ambient temperature (35–50°C), dust, and humidity impose environmental qualification requirements beyond what standard Chinese laboratory testing covers. Chinese GB/T 37408 and GB/T 19964 baselines do not satisfy Benin's IEC-referenced requirements and cannot be substituted without supplementary IEC test evidence.
