Short answer

Sao Tome and Principe has no clearly confirmed strong national standards body or EVSE-specific mandatory certification route as of 2026-06-14, so exporters should treat this as a project-by-project IEC evidence market. The practical gaps for China-made EV chargers are EMAE grid acceptance on 230/400 V 50 Hz, IEC 61851 safety evidence, IEC 62196 Type 2 AC and CCS2 DC connector design, IEC 61000 EMC documentation, OCPP readiness where networked operation is specified, coastal salt-mist and humidity derating, and sea-freight spare-parts planning. China domestic GB/T 20234 connectors and GB/T 27930 DC communication are not accepted substitutes for Type 2 / CCS2 deployments.
