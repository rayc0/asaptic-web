Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to the Maldives, the key practical gaps are: (1) project-level acceptance by STELCO in Greater Male or FENAKA in outer atolls; (2) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence; (3) IEC 62116 and IEC 61727 grid-interface and anti-islanding evidence; (4) IEC 61000-series or IEC 62920 EMC evidence; and (5) engineering validation for a 230/400 V, 50 Hz island grid with extreme heat, humidity, salt mist, and space-constrained atoll installations. Maldives uses the same 50 Hz frequency as China, but the nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V baseline; Chinese GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 evidence should not be treated as automatically accepted.
