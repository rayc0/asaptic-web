Short answer

CITC (Communications, Space and Technology Commission) type approval is mandatory for all wireless and telecommunications terminal equipment imported or sold in Saudi Arabia; the CITC mark must appear on the product label in Arabic and English. SASO/SALEEM conformity assessment is required for EMC (SASO IEC/CISPR 32 and IEC 61000-4 series) and electrical safety (SASO IEC 62368-1). Saudi Arabia's dual-voltage environment (127V/60Hz and 220V/50Hz) requires universal power supply design or Arabic-language voltage labelling. A Saudi-registered in-country authorized agent is mandatory for foreign manufacturers registering with CITC and SASO. Arabic labelling is mandatory on product labels, packaging, and instructions. Chinese SRRC, CCC, and MIIT approvals are not recognized by CITC; separate CITC type approval is always required. NCA IoT cybersecurity controls are mandatory for critical sectors and expanding to consumer devices.
