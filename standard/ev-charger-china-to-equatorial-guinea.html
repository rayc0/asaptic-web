Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Equatorial Guinea, the practical gaps are not a mature national EVSE certification scheme but basic IEC-aligned engineering validation: confirm SEGESA grid-connection acceptance, validate the charger for Equatorial Guinea's 230/400 V, 50 Hz supply rather than China's 220/380 V baseline, redesign GB/T connectors to IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2, prepare IEC 61851-1 and IEC 61851-23 safety evidence, provide IEC 61000 EMC and power-quality evidence, and document tropical humidity and corrosion derating. Equatorial Guinea has no strong publicly confirmed national standards body for EV chargers as of 2026-06-14, so exporters should verify project requirements directly with SEGESA, the importer, customs broker, and the site owner before shipment.
