Short answer

Norway is an EEA member — not an EU member — but the EU Radio Equipment Directive (RED 2014/53/EU) applies in Norway via the EEA Agreement, and CE marking is mandatory, enforced by Nkom (Norwegian Communications Authority). Key gaps versus Chinese SRRC/CCC include: radio performance testing to EN 300 328 / EN 301 893 (with mandatory 5 GHz DFS — Nkom actively enforces DFS violations), EMC to EN 301 489 series, electrical safety to EN IEC 62368-1:2020+A11 (230 V/50 Hz, plug type C/F Schuko, DSB oversight), cybersecurity under RED Art. 3.3(d)–(f) via EN 18031 (mandatory from 1 August 2025), EEA/EU Authorised Representative (an EU AR must also cover Norway or a separate Norway EEA AR must be appointed), Norwegian-language labelling for consumer products, WEEE registration via Retur-El, and Norwegian customs clearance via Tolldirektoratet despite EEA membership. CCC and FCC are not recognised in Norway.

The EU Radio Equipment Directive (RED) 2014/53/EU was adopted into Norwegian law via the EEA Agreement and implemented through Norwegian regulation (FOR-2016-07-01-836 — Forskrift om radioutstyr, the Radio Equipment Regulation). CE marking is mandatory for all radio equipment placed on the Norwegian market, including Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and cellular IoT devices, on identical terms to the EU. The conformity assessment pathway for most Wi-Fi/Bluetooth products is the self-declaration pathway (Module A — internal production control), referencing harmonised standards EN 300 328 / EN 301 893 (radio), EN 301 489-1 + EN 301 489-17 (EMC), and EN IEC 62368-1:2020+A11 (safety), and drawing up an EU Declaration of Conformity (DoC) with CE marking affixed. Nkom (Nasjonal kommunikasjonsmyndighet — Norwegian Communications Authority) is the NRA enforcing RED in Norway through spectrum monitoring, market surveillance, and product checks. Nkom is a documented active enforcer of 5 GHz DFS requirements; devices operating on channels 52–140 without compliant DFS are an enforcement priority. DSB (Direktoratet for samfunnssikkerhet og beredskap — Directorate for Civil Protection) oversees electrical safety and product safety aspects under the equivalent of LVD. Key practical Norway-specific difference: despite EEA membership, goods entering Norway from EU or China are subject to Norwegian customs clearance via Tolldirektoratet (Norwegian Customs) and Norwegian VAT (MVA, 25%).
