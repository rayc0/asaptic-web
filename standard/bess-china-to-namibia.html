Short answer

Namibia does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to the EU Battery Regulation or a Saudi-style mandatory pre-shipment conformity route. The Namibian Standards Institution (NSI) adopts IEC standards as the basis for Namibian standards (NS series), and conformity assessment is the expected route for regulated electrical products. The Electricity Control Board (ECB) licences electricity supply, generation, transmission, and distribution activities under the Electricity Act 2007; all grid-connected BESS projects require ECB licence or authorisation. NamPower (the national utility) and the Regional Electricity Distributors (REDs) set grid-connection technical requirements for their respective networks. Namibia's grid operates at 220/380 V, 50 Hz — the same nominal voltages as China's grid, which reduces hardware reconfiguration risk, but NamPower and RED protection relay settings and connection agreement terms govern and must be verified project by project. The practical compliance gates for Chinese BESS exported to Namibia are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline for project acceptance under NSI/IEC-aligned specifications; (2) IEC 62933 system-level technical requirements where referenced in NamPower, RED, or project-owner specifications; (3) ECB licence or project authorisation under the Electricity Act 2007 — a mandatory regulatory gate; (4) NamPower or RED grid-connection agreement technical compliance, including high-temperature derating validation given Namibia's high ambient temperatures; (5) local fire and building authority installation approval — specific reference to a national BESS fire code has not been confirmed; (6) UN 38.3 transport test documentation, mandatory under international conventions for sea freight via Walvis Bay (Namibia's principal commercial port on the Atlantic coast) or road and air freight. Chinese GB/T 36558-2023, GB/T 34120-2023, and NB/T 42090-2016 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC evidence in project or authority review.
