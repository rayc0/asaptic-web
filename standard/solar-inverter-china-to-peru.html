Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Peru, the key requirements are: (1) NTP-IEC 62109-1:2024 and NTP-IEC 62109-2:2024 safety conformity adopted by INACAL Directoral Resolution No. 035-2024-INACAL/DN; (2) grid-connection settings revalidated for Peru's 60 Hz, 220/380 V system under NTCSE (DS 020-97-EM) and COES/SEIN technical requirements; (3) MINEM/Osinergmin registration or technical approval for projects under the RER renewable energy concession regime. China-market GB/T 19964 and NB/T 32004 documents do not cover Peru's 60 Hz settings or INACAL NTP safety scope.

NB/T 32004		 Peru operates a 60 Hz electricity grid. The Norma Técnica de Calidad de los Servicios Eléctricos (NTCSE), approved by DS 020-97-EM and supervised by Osinergmin, sets quality-of-service requirements including voltage and frequency tolerances. PV inverter firmware, protection settings, anti-islanding parameters, reconnection timing, power-factor capability, and nameplate values must match Peru's 60 Hz, 220 V single-phase / 380 V three-phase grid conditions. COES (Comité de Operación Económica del Sistema Interconectado Nacional — COES/SEIN) technical requirements apply to grid-connected generation.
