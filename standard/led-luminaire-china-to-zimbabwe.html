Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Zimbabwe market requirements — SAZ conformity and ZWS standards adopting IEC 60598 / IEC 62560 / IEC 62471, energy labelling programmes, and POTRAZ radio type approval for smart luminaires — versus Chinese GB standards and CCC certification. Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Zimbabwe should align product safety to SAZ / ZWS standards that adopt IEC 60598-1 (luminaires) and IEC 62560 (self-ballasted LED lamps), confirm with the in-country importer whether SAZ conformity assessment or import inspection applies to the specific product, meet 230 V / 50 Hz grid requirements (50 Hz matches China but the 230 V nominal differs from China 220/380 V), support any applicable Zimbabwe energy-efficiency labelling programme, and obtain POTRAZ radio type approval for any smart / wireless-enabled luminaire. Zimbabwe has no EU-style horizontal RoHS, REACH, battery, or outdoor-noise regime — substance restrictions are not a standalone Zimbabwe market-access barrier, though customers or tenders may still ask for IEC 62321 / RoHS evidence. None of these are covered by existing China CCC certification or GB-standard test reports, which are not mutually recognised by SAZ.
