Short answer

Micronesia presents a fundamentally different technical baseline from China for BESS exports. The most critical gap is the grid mismatch: FSM operations use a 120/240 V, 60 Hz US-aligned low-voltage grid context — versus China’s 220/380 V 50 Hz system. This means BESS power-conversion systems (PCS) designed and validated for China’s 50 Hz grid cannot be directly deployed in FSM; full 60 Hz revalidation is required. The practical compliance gates for Chinese BESS exported to FSM are: (1) UL 1973 (batteries for stationary applications) and UL 9540 / UL 9540A (energy storage system and fire test) as expected US/UL-direction evidence in the FSM project context; (2) IEEE 1547 and UL 1741 interconnection expectations for distributed energy resources including storage in 120/240 V / 60 Hz systems; (3) NFPA 855 fire-safety installation expectations for indoor and outdoor stationary BESS spacing and suppression evidence; (4) DRD and state utility conformity and installation oversight — verify the current regulated-product and project list directly with DRD and the state utility corporations, as no unified national BESS technical regulation was publicly identified in the provided baseline; (5) UN 38.3 transport documentation mandatory for all lithium battery sea freight via Pohnpei and onward inter-island movement; and (6) typhoon-resilience design because FSM is a tropical, cyclone-prone archipelago and many projects are on coastal sites. Chinese GB/T 36276, GB/T 34131, GB 38031, and GB/T 36558 are the Chinese manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for UL or IEEE evidence in project or authority review.
