Short answer

The main Malaysia gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT wireless device are: (1) mandatory MCMC type approval under the Communications and Multimedia Act 1998, with the MCMC approval number on the product or packaging label before import or sale; (2) EMC testing to MS CISPR-based standards at SIRIM-accredited laboratories; (3) ST electrical safety approval for power adapters and chargers under the Electrical Supply Act 1990; (4) MCMC importer/dealer registration; and (5) no mutual recognition with FCC, CE, or CCC — each Chinese approval document must be re-assessed for Malaysian requirements. CyberSecurity Malaysia advisory guidance is not yet a mandatory certification.
