Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Cape Verde, the key practical gaps are: (1) IGQPI standards and conformity expectations, commonly framed around IEC/EU or Portuguese-language technical evidence; (2) ARME energy-sector approval and project authorisation where generation or grid connection requires regulatory review; (3) ELECTRA distribution or island-grid connection review using Cape Verde's 50 Hz, 230/400 V grid parameters; (4) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 interconnection, and IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC evidence; and (5) coastal salt-mist, humidity, logistics, and island derating documentation. China GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 evidence is useful background but is not automatically accepted without supplementary IEC-referenced testing and local project approval. Frequency is the same 50 Hz as China, but voltage differs: Cape Verde is 230/400 V while China is 220/380 V.
