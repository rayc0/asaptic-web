Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Tanzania must route regulated electrical products through the TBS (Tanzania Bureau of Standards) PVoC (Pre-shipment Verification of Conformity) programme, obtaining a Certificate of Conformity (CoC) per shipment and the right to bear the TBS mark; demonstrate electrical safety against TZS standards adopting IEC 60598 (luminaires) and IEC 60598-2 / IEC 62560 (self-ballasted lamps), with the 230 V / 50 Hz Tanzanian grid in mind (same 50 Hz as China but nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V); meet TBS / TZS EMC limits adopting CISPR 15; address photobiological safety via TZS / IEC 62471 where applied; comply with the Tanzanian energy-labelling requirement administered under the national energy-efficiency framework; and obtain TCRA (Tanzania Communications Regulatory Authority) type-approval for any luminaire with wireless functionality. Tanzania has no EU-style horizontal RoHS / battery / SVHC regime — those obligations do not apply, though substances may still be addressed via product-specific TZS standards. Goods clear mainly through the port of Dar es Salaam and an in-country registered importer is required. None of this is covered by existing Chinese CCC certification or GB compliance.
