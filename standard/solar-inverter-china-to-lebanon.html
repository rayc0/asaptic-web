Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Lebanon, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirming whether the exact inverter, bundled electrical accessories, or import package is subject to LIBNOR conformity or Lebanese standard adoption, often through NL standards aligned with IEC; (2) keeping model-specific IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence instead of relying only on China GB/T 37408 or NB/T 32004 files; (3) documenting IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 grid-interface evidence for any grid-interactive or net-metered EDL/LCEC project; (4) supplying IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC evidence; and (5) validating 220/380 V, 50 Hz settings. Because Lebanon's state grid supply has been highly constrained, many rooftop systems are private off-grid or hybrid solar-plus-battery installations rather than formal EDL net-metering projects. In those cases, importer / installer due diligence, LIBNOR conformity status, insurance, and project-owner requirements dominate, while China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence should be treated as China-side baseline evidence only.
