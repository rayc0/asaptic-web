Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Mozambique, the confirmed practical gaps are: (1) connector hardware redesign is required — GB/T 20234 connectors are physically incompatible with IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) and CCS2 (DC) couplers that Mozambique's internationally-aligned charging infrastructure targets; (2) IEC 61851-1 and IEC 61851-23 safety evidence replaces GB/T 18487 as the applicable baseline, and INNOQ conformity assessment applies to electrical equipment imports; (3) Mozambique operates on 220/380 V 50 Hz, matching China's domestic baseline voltage but requiring grid-connection coordination with utility EDM, which has limited grid reach outside major cities; (4) ARENE is the energy sector regulator and any charging-infrastructure project must be coordinated with ARENE and EDM; (5) OCPP back-office interoperability applies for networked public chargers. Critically, Mozambique's EV market is very nascent — national EV charger deployment is early-stage, grid electrification rates outside Maputo and major cities are low, and import volumes are small. Exporters should verify the current INNOQ conformity route, applicable technical regulations, EDM project-acceptance requirements, and ARENE licensing scope with local agents before shipment, as official online documentation from these bodies is limited.

Connector Interoperability — GB/T 20234 vs IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 China AC chargers use GB/T 20234.2 couplers and DC fast chargers use GB/T 20234.3 couplers. Although the GB/T 20234.2 AC coupler has a similar overall shape to the IEC 62196 Type 2, they differ in connector gender (GB/T uses male connector at the charger and female vehicle inlet, opposite to Type 2), signaling protocol (CC/CP versus PP/CP), and contact arrangement, making them physically and electrically incompatible.
