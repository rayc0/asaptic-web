Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face five main Ecuador compliance gaps: (1) a mandatory Certificate of Conformity (INEN-1 / recognized accredited body) against the applicable RTE INEN technical regulation must be obtained before customs clearance at Guayaquil — Chinese CCC/GB 4706.13 certification is not accepted; (2) electrical safety must be demonstrated to NTE INEN / IEC 60335-2-24, and the appliance must be rated for Ecuador's 120/220 V 60 Hz grid, which differs from China's 220/380 V 50 Hz; (3) energy-efficiency labelling under the INEN/MEER (Ministry of Energy) scheme using NTE INEN test methods is mandatory; (4) the R-600a refrigerant charge and flammability documentation must align with IEC 60335-2-24 Annex requirements; and (5) an in-country Ecuadorian importer/registrant is required as there is no foreign self-declaration route. Ecuador has no EU-style horizontal RoHS, battery, or outdoor-noise regime for refrigerators.
