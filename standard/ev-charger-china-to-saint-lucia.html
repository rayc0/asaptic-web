Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Saint Lucia, treat the opportunity as an early-stage OECS / CARICOM tourism-island deployment rather than a mature EV market. Saint Lucia uses a 230 V single-phase / 400 V three-phase, 50 Hz grid, while China uses 220 V / 380 V at the same 50 Hz, so voltage differs and input range must be confirmed. The practical gaps are: IEC 61851-1 / IEC 61851-23 safety evidence instead of GB/T 18487 alone; connector redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC; IEC 61000 EMC and power-quality evidence; LUCELEC grid-connection review under NURC-regulated electricity service; SLBS standards and conformity documentation; tropical coastal protection against hurricane wind, rain ingress, salt mist, corrosion, heat, and shipping via Port Castries; and OCPP back-office support for any networked charger. Saint Lucia's EV fleet and charging market remain essentially non-existent, so exporters should frame proposals as pilots for hotels, fleets, public sites, or renewable-energy projects rather than assume broad retail demand.
