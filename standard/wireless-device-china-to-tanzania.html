Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and IoT device documentation against Tanzania Communications Regulatory Authority (TCRA) mandatory type approval under the Electronic and Postal Communications Act (EPOCA) 2010, TZS/EAC standards for EMC emissions and immunity, electrical safety requirements for 230V/50Hz Type-G British plug, local Tanzanian importer/agent requirements, and general alignment with Tanzania's Computer Misuse Act 2015 on cybersecurity obligations.

Short answer

The main Tanzania gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT device are: mandatory TCRA type approval under EPOCA 2010 (CE, FCC, and CCC are not recognised by TCRA), TZS/EAC standards for EMC emissions and immunity, electrical safety compliance for Tanzania's 230V/50Hz Type-G British plug environment, appointment of an authorised Tanzanian importer or agent registered with BRELA and recognised by TCRA, and general awareness of the Computer Misuse Act 2015 (no mandatory IoT device certification, but data-security obligations apply). EAC radio harmonisation is underway but wireless device type approval remains nationally enforced via TCRA.
