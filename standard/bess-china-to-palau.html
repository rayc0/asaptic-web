Short answer

The Palau presents a fundamentally different technical baseline from China for BESS exports. The most critical gap is the grid frequency and voltage mismatch: the Palau operates on a 60 Hz / 120–240 V (single-phase) / 208–240 V (three-phase) US-heritage grid — versus China's 50 Hz / 220–380 V system. This means BESS power conversion systems (PCS) designed and validated for China's 50 Hz grid cannot be directly deployed in the Palau; full 60 Hz revalidation is required. The practical compliance gates for Chinese BESS exported to the Palau are: (1) UL 1973 (batteries for stationary applications) and UL 9540 / UL 9540A (energy storage system and fire test) — the US-lineage standards referenced by MPIC and project owners in place of IEC 62619 alone; (2) IEEE 1547-2018 grid-interconnection standard as expected by MPIC and PPUC for distributed energy resources including storage; (3) NFPA 855 fire-safety installation code as the internationally dominant stationary BESS fire standard; (4) MPIC conformity assessment — verify the current regulated-product list directly with MPIC, as no confirmed mandatory BESS-specific Technical Regulation has been publicly identified; (5) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory for all lithium battery sea freight transiting Nassau or Freeport; and (6) hurricane-resilience design — the Palau lies in a high-cyclone-risk zone and PPUC / project owners may require wind (up to 155 mph design wind speed) and flood derating specifications for outdoor BESS enclosures. Chinese GB/T 36276, GB/T 34131, GB 38031, and GB/T 36558 are the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for UL or IEEE evidence in project or authority review.

UN 38.3 and IEC 62281 Transport Safety — Mandatory for Lithium Battery Sea Freight to Nassau and Freeport (Archipelago Sea-Freight-Only Market) Chinese BESS cell and module manufacturers are required to comply with UN 38.3 for export shipments under international transport conventions.
