Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Peru should: meet the energy-efficiency labelling requirements administered by MINEM (Etiquetado de Eficiencia Energetica) where the product falls within a regulated category; comply with the electrical safety and performance content of Peruvian NTP standards, which adopt IEC 60598 (luminaire safety), IEC 62560 (self-ballasted LED lamps), IEC 61347-2-13 (LED control gear), and IEC 62471 (photobiological safety); design for the Peruvian grid of 220 V, 60 Hz (similar nominal voltage to China but 60 Hz vs China's 50 Hz, which affects control gear and any mains-frequency-dependent components); obtain MTC (Ministerio de Transportes y Comunicaciones) homologation for any smart luminaire with wireless functionality; and work through an in-country importer for INDECOPI/PRODUCE technical-regulation conformity and Callao port clearance. Peru does NOT operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS regime, a REACH-style SVHC supply-chain notification duty, or an EU-style outdoor-noise rule — those EU obligations have no direct Peruvian counterpart. None of these Peruvian requirements are satisfied by existing Chinese CCC certification or GB-standard test reports.
