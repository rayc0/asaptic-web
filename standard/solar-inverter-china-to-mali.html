Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Mali, the key practical gaps are: (1) AMANORM conformity — Mali's standards body adopts IEC standards, and Chinese GB/T certificates are not automatically accepted; (2) CREE licensing — grid-tied projects above a threshold capacity require a CREE generation licence or concession, and inverter equipment must meet CREE and EDM-SA technical requirements; (3) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence from an ILAC-accredited laboratory, as Chinese GB/T 37408 is not accepted as equivalent by Mali/donor project owners; (4) IEC 62116 anti-islanding compliance for grid-tied inverters; (5) IEC 62920 / IEC 61000-3-2 EMC evidence; (6) for off-grid and mini-grid applications serving AMADER rural electrification programmes, IEC TS 62257 series quality standards and donor/lender (World Bank, AfDB, EU) procurement specifications are typically binding; and (7) Sahel environmental derating — extreme ambient temperatures (up to 45–50 °C), dust (IEC 60721-3 class 4S), and humidity require verified IP and thermal performance beyond typical Chinese domestic test conditions. China GB/T 37408, GB/T 19964, and NB/T 32004 baselines do not satisfy AMANORM/CREE/EDM-SA or donor procurement requirements and cannot be substituted without supplementary IEC test evidence.

GB/T 37408 is a Chinese domestic recommended national standard (GB/T designation), assessed at CNAS-accredited Chinese laboratories. AMANORM, CREE, EDM-SA, and donor/lender procurement specifications for Mali solar projects do not accept Chinese GB/T 37408 as equivalent to IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence.
