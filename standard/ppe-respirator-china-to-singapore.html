Short answer

Filtering respirators (KN95 / FFP-type masks) are NOT Controlled Goods under Singapore's Consumer Protection (Safety Requirements) Registration Scheme, so there is no mandatory pre-market respirator certification and no SAFETY Mark for them. Singapore therefore has no government gate equivalent to EU CE marking for these masks. Instead, occupational use is governed by the Workplace Safety and Health Act (Ministry of Manpower) and the SS 548 code of practice for respiratory protection, while medical and surgical masks are regulated separately by HSA as medical devices. For a Chinese KN95 exporter the practical gap is not a missing certificate but the absence of a recognised mark plus the need to meet buyer and WSH expectations (e.g. SS 548 / equivalence to recognised classes).
