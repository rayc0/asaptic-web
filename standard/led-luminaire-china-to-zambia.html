Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Zambia must meet Zambia Bureau of Standards (ZABS) conformity and import-inspection requirements, which adopt IEC-based Zambian Standards (ZS/IEC 60598 for luminaire safety, ZS/IEC 62560 for self-ballasted LED lamps, ZS/CISPR 15 for EMC); ensure the product is rated for Zambia's 230 V / 50 Hz grid (50 Hz matches China, but nominal voltage differs from China's 220 V / 380 V); satisfy any applicable energy-efficiency labelling programme administered for lighting; obtain ZICTA type approval if the luminaire incorporates radio functionality (Wi-Fi / Bluetooth smart lighting); and route the import through an in-country importer with the required ZABS documentation. Zambia does not operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS / REACH / energy-label regime, so substance restrictions, photobiological-class labelling, and supply-chain SVHC notification are not mandated the way they are in the EU — but China's CCC and GB-standard evidence do not by themselves satisfy ZABS conformity, and re-testing or conformity assessment to the applicable ZS/IEC standards is generally needed.
