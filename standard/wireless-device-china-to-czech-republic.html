Short answer

Czech Republic is an EU member state — EU Radio Equipment Directive (RED 2014/53/EU) applies in full. ČTÚ (Český telekomunikační úřad) is the national authority for radio/spectrum enforcement; ČOI (Czech Trade Inspection Authority) handles product market surveillance. CE marking with harmonised EN standards (EN 300 328 / EN 301 893 radio; EN 301 489 EMC; EN IEC 62368-1:2020+A11 safety) is mandatory. Chinese SRRC approval and CCC are not recognised. Czech-specific obligations include mandatory Czech-language product labelling, separate WEEE zpětný odběr national registration (zákon č. 542/2020 Sb.), and EU Authorised Representative appointment for non-EU manufacturers. RED Art. 3.3 cybersecurity under EN 18031 is mandatory across the EU from 1 August 2025.
