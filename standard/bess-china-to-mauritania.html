Short answer

Mauritania does not have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to the EU Battery Regulation or a formal IEC 62619 pre-shipment conformity scheme. BESS projects in Mauritania — predominantly donor/lender-driven (World Bank, AfDB, EU, and bilateral financiers supporting the Aman, Nour, and green hydrogen projects) — operate primarily under project-specific international standards required by project owners and financiers. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Mauritania are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence, as the internationally expected baseline required by donor/lender project specifications (IFC Performance Standards and lender E&S requirements typically mandate IEC compliance); (2) IEC 62933-5-2 grid-scale BESS safety where referenced in ARE licensing or project-owner specifications; (3) ARE licensing approval — the Mauritanian energy regulator controls grid-connected generation and storage; (4) SOMELEC grid-connection approval and technical compliance with 220/380 V 50 Hz grid requirements (same voltage as China, but SOMELEC interconnection agreement terms must be satisfied); (5) Environmental design ratings for Mauritania's extreme operating conditions — Sahara Desert heat (ambient up to 50°C), severe sand and dust ingress (IP65+ enclosures required), and coastal salt fog at Nouakchott and Nouadhibou; (6) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory for sea freight via Nouakchott or Nouadhibou under IMDG Code; (7) DNPQ import conformity requirements — Mauritania's national standardisation body DNPQ (Direction Nationale de la Promotion de la Qualité) may impose conformity obligations; these have not been confirmed from authoritative public sources as of the dataset date. Chinese GB/T 36558-2023 and NB/T 42090-2016 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC 62619 evidence in donor/lender or ARE project review.
