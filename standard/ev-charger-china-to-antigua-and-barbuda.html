Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Antigua and Barbuda, the practical gaps are IEC-based safety and EMC evidence, connector redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 for the UK/IEC-legacy Caribbean environment, APUA grid and site acceptance for 230/400 V 50 Hz service, and environmental hardening for hurricane exposure, salt mist, heat, humidity, and sea-freight handling through St John's. Antigua and Barbuda's nominal 230/400 V grid differs from China's 220/380 V domestic baseline even though both use 50 Hz, so input-voltage range and thermal derating must be confirmed. The local EV market remains very small and a single Antigua and Barbuda whole-unit mandatory EVSE certification rule could not be confirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters should verify the ABBS standard route, APUA project requirements, HS code, charger type, and any radio or smart-metering approvals before shipment.
