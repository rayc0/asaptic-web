Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Mali, the confirmed practical gaps are: (1) connector hardware redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) / CCS2 (DC), as Mali's Francophone IEC alignment means GB/T connectors are physically incompatible with any IEC 62196 vehicle inlet; (2) IEC 61851-1 safety evidence and IEC 61851-23 evidence for DC stations, required by AMANORM-aligned technical specifications and any EDM-SA or donor-funded project; (3) EDM-SA grid coordination for grid-connected installations — Mali operates 220/380 V, 50 Hz, but suffers chronic load shedding and supply instability that affects charger design and derating requirements; (4) EMC evidence per IEC 61000 series; (5) high-temperature and dust ingress protection (IP65 recommended for outdoor equipment in Mali's Sahelian climate); (6) AMANORM conformity assessment, coordinated through CREE; (7) French-language documentation throughout. Critically, exporters must understand that Mali's EV passenger-car market is extremely nascent, grid electrification is below 50% nationally (well below in rural areas), and 2/3-wheel electric vehicles are far more commercially relevant than four-wheel EV chargers. Feasibility is driven primarily by NGO, UNDP, or development-bank-funded pilot projects rather than commercial retail channels.
