Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Cameroon, the key practical gates are: (1) ANOR PECAE pre-shipment conformity assessment — Cameroon's Programme d'Évaluation de la Conformité Avant Embarquement requires a Certificate of Conformity before export, with IEC-based technical evidence; (2) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence from an ILAC-accredited laboratory — Chinese GB/T 37408 is not accepted as equivalent; (3) IEC 62116 anti-islanding compliance validated for Cameroon's 230/400 V, 50 Hz grid — distinct from China's 220/380 V baseline; (4) IEC 62920 EMC evidence, which Chinese GB/T certificates do not substitute; (5) ARSEL sector licensing for grid-tied installations; (6) ENEO grid-connection approval for distribution-level projects; and (7) donor/lender (AfDB, World Bank, EU) procurement technical specifications for publicly financed solar and mini-grid projects, which commonly mandate IEC certification and may specify ILAC-accredited test reports. China GB/T 37408-2021 and GB/T 19964-2024 baselines do not satisfy any of these Cameroon-side requirements without supplementary IEC evidence.
