Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers exporting to Tanzania face four main compliance gaps: (1) TBS mandatory conformity via the Pre-Export Verification of Conformity (PVoC) programme — every regulated consignment needs a Certificate of Conformity (CoC) issued before shipment, and Chinese CCC certification does not replace it; (2) testing to TZS standards that adopt IEC 60335-2-24 (electrical safety) and the relevant IEC EMC standards — GB 4706.13/GB 4343.1 CCC reports are not automatically accepted and an IECEE CB Scheme report with a Tanzanian CoC is the usual route; (3) Tanzanian energy labelling / MEPS for refrigerators where applicable, plus the TBS mark on the product; and (4) an in-country importer of record to clear the goods at Dar es Salaam. Tanzania uses 230 V / 50 Hz (same 50 Hz as China, different nominal voltage from China's 220/380 V). R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned, but charge and documentation should be confirmed against the IEC 60335-2-24 basis adopted by the relevant TZS standard. Tanzania has no EU-style horizontal RoHS, battery, or outdoor-noise regimes.
