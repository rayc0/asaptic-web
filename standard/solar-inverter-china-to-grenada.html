Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Grenada, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirming the current GDBS, PURC, and GRENLEC approval path for the project; (2) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence; (3) IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 grid-interface evidence; (4) IEC 62920 and IEC 61000-series EMC evidence; (5) firmware validation against Grenada's 50 Hz / 230 V / 400 V grid, which shares frequency with China but differs in nominal voltage from China's 50 Hz / 220 V / 380 V baseline; and (6) environmental derating for hurricane exposure, heat, humidity, and salt-mist coastal service. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence is useful as background but is not automatically accepted for Grenada grid connection.
