Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face four main Chilean compliance gaps: (1) electrical safety must be SEC-certified to NCh IEC 60335-2-24 through an SEC-authorised certifier — Chinese CCC / GB 4706.13 certification is not accepted; (2) energy efficiency labelling under the Ministry of Energy / SEC programme requires a Chilean efficiency-class label distinct from China's GB 12021.2 grade; (3) Chile relies on a designated local importer as the responsible party who registers products and holds SEC approvals — there is no EU-style authorised-representative statute but a Chilean RUT-registered importer is required in practice; and (4) R-600a refrigerant is widely accepted in Chile, so this is largely a documentation rather than a technology gap. Chile shares China's 50 Hz / 220 V single-phase nominal grid, which eases voltage-related re-design but does not remove the SEC certification obligation. Chile has no EU-style horizontal RoHS, battery, or outdoor-noise regime for refrigerators.
