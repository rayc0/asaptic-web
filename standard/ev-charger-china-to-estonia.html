Short answer

For EV chargers exported from China to Estonia, the practical gaps are: mandatory connector replacement (IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC; CCS Combo 2 for DC public fast charging — mandated by AFIR Regulation (EU) 2023/1804 from 13 April 2024); CE conformity under LVD 2014/35/EU, EMC Directive 2014/30/EU, and RED 2014/53/EU for any wireless modules; OCPP 1.6 or 2.0.1 back-office integration; Elering / Elektrilevi OÜ DSO grid-connection documentation; Estonian Communications Board (Sideamet) radio-module type approval; and cold-climate performance validation (outdoor operation down to −30 °C including cable flexibility, connector locking, and RCD performance at low temperatures). China GB/T 20234.2 (AC) and GB/T 20234.3 (DC) connectors are incompatible with European vehicles. Estonian-language end-user documentation is expected. Payment must support contactless bank card and RFID — Alipay and WeChat Pay are not standard in Estonia.
