Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Mauritius, the practical gaps are MSB conformity and standards review, CEB grid-connection coordination on the 230/400 V 50 Hz network, URA and utility project approvals where the installation affects regulated electricity services, IEC 61851-1 / IEC 61851-23 safety evidence, IEC 62196 Type 2 AC and CCS2 DC interoperability for new public deployments, OCPP and IEC 61000 EMC evidence for networked chargers, and coastal cyclone / salt-mist derating. Mauritius has a mixed installed EV fleet, including used right-hand-drive imports from Japan and the UK, so CHAdeMO and Type 1 legacy vehicles may exist; however, new public charging specifications should be checked against the IEC Type 2 / CCS2 direction. China GB/T 18487.1-2023, GB/T 20234, and GB/T 27930 evidence is not a direct substitute for Mauritius IEC-aligned acceptance.
