Short answer

Cape Verde does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to the EU Battery Regulation or a Saudi-style SABER route. BESS projects in Cape Verde operate primarily under IEC international standards referenced in ARME connection agreements and ELECTRA utility interconnection rules, within a Portuguese-language regulatory environment shaped by the country's EU/IEC technical legacy and IGQPI conformity framework. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Cape Verde are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence — Chinese GB 38031 and GB/T 36276 are not accepted; (2) IEC 62933 system-level requirements as referenced by ARME and project specifications; (3) a critical voltage gap — China uses 220/380 V whereas Cape Verde uses 230/400 V (both 50 Hz, but voltage levels differ, requiring PCS re-parameterisation); (4) NFPA 855 fire-safety alignment for container BESS in island installations; (5) UN 38.3 and IEC 62281 transport testing for sea-freight delivery via Porto Grande (Mindelo) or Praia ports; and (6) IGQPI conformity mark plus ARME grid-connection approval plus ELECTRA interconnection sign-off as the three-gate approval chain. Coastal salt-mist, tropical humidity, and island microgrid derating impose additional environmental requirements not tested under Chinese GB standards.
