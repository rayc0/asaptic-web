Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Guinea, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirming the applicable Guinea national conformity route through IGNM or the competent ministry, because a public inverter-specific compulsory list could not be confirmed; (2) obtaining EDG and project-owner acceptance for grid-tied plants, including 50 Hz / 220 V / 380 V settings and protection coordination; (3) preparing IEC 62109-1/-2 safety, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 interconnection, IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC and harmonic evidence where donor, lender, EPC, or utility specifications require IEC documentation; and (4) validating tropical humidity, corrosion, dust, and Harmattan derating assumptions for Conakry and inland sites. China GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 baselines do not automatically satisfy Guinea national conformity, EDG connection, or donor/lender acceptance without supplementary IEC and project-specific evidence.
