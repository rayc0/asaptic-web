Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator exporters to Angola face four practical gaps: (1) electrical safety must align with Angola national standards (NA) that adopt IEC 60335-2-24 — Chinese CCC/GB 4706.13 reports map to the same IEC base but are not automatically accepted, so an IEC/CB-Scheme report path is recommended; (2) regulated imports undergo IANORQ-supervised import conformity inspection at entry (Luanda, Lobito), distinct from China CCC; (3) product and safety documentation, instructions, and labels must be in Portuguese, and an in-country importer of record is required; (4) energy labelling / MEPS expectations differ from China's GB 12021.2 China Energy Label and should be verified with IANORQ. R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned (220 V / 50 Hz grid matches single-phase China nominal), but charge and flammability documentation against IEC 60335-2-24 should be confirmed. Angola has no EU-style horizontal RoHS / battery / outdoor-noise regime.
