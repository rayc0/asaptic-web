Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Namibia, the practical gaps are not a confirmed single national EVSE type-approval rule, but a project-specific chain: NSI standards and conformity treatment where Namibian standards adopt or reference IEC standards, ECB electricity-sector licensing or regulatory treatment where the charging project affects generation, supply, distribution, resale, or network operation, and NamPower or the relevant Regional Electricity Distributor connection approval before energisation. Namibia's low-voltage context is commonly treated as 220/380 V, 50 Hz, so China 220/380 V hardware may fit nominal voltage, but protection settings, power quality, metering, load management, site wiring, and high-temperature derating still require Namibian project validation. Namibia sits in the Southern Africa IEC connector ecosystem: Type 2 is the expected AC connector and CCS2 the expected DC connector where specified. GB/T 20234 connector chargers are physically incompatible and need hardware redesign to IEC 62196; GB/T 27930 DC communication is not a CCS2/OCPP substitute. The EV market is nascent, long driving distances and strong solar resources make solar-charging attractive, and exporters should verify the exact NSI, ECB, NamPower/RED, importer, and site-owner route before shipment.
