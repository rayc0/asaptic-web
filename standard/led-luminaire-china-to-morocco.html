Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Moroccan requirements: IMANOR NM standards (adopting IEC 60598 / 62560 / 62471), mandatory import conformity / attestation administered by the Ministry of Industry, appliance energy labelling, and ANRT type approval for wireless products, versus Chinese GB standards and CCC certification.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Morocco must: meet the safety requirements of the applicable IMANOR NM standard (which adopts IEC 60598-1 for general luminaires and IEC 62560 / 62471 for light sources and photobiological safety); clear mandatory import conformity / pre-shipment attestation administered by the Ministry of Industry for regulated electrical products (an in-country importer is required); comply with Morocco's appliance energy-efficiency labelling where the product is in scope (Morocco uses an energy label rather than an EU-style Ecodesign minimum-performance regime, so map EU 'Ecodesign' to the Moroccan energy label, not to a binding efficacy floor); obtain ANRT type approval if the luminaire has wireless functionality; and note that Morocco has no EU-style horizontal RoHS / REACH-SVHC regime — restricted-substance control runs through general chemical and product-safety law plus contractual buyer requirements, so RoHS must be mapped honestly as 'no direct local equivalent.' None of these are covered by existing CCC certification or China Energy Label registration.
