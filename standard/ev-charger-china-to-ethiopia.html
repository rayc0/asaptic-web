Short answer

For EV chargers exported from China to Ethiopia, the critical gaps are Ethiopia EVCS Directive No. 1034/2024 issued by the Petroleum and Energy Authority, which mandates IEC 61851 charging modes and requires EV importers and assemblers to establish charging infrastructure as a market-entry condition; connector compatibility between the China GB/T 20234 standard and the IEC 62196 connectors common among non-Chinese EVs deployed in Ethiopia; and EEU 50 Hz grid settings. Ethiopia's January 2024 ban on ICE vehicle imports and fast-growing EV market make charger-connector interoperability a live commercial risk. China GB/T 18487 provides a useful baseline but its connector and communication protocols differ from IEC 61851 and IEC 62196.

GB/T 20234 series — Connection set for conductive charging of electric vehicles		 The Ethiopian Petroleum and Energy Authority issued EVCS Directive No. 1034/2024 in December 2024, establishing a comprehensive legal framework for electric vehicle charging infrastructure in Ethiopia. The directive requires EV importers and assemblers to establish charging stations as a mandatory market-entry condition. Fast-charging stations must be available every 50 km (approximately 31 miles), and heavy-vehicle charging points every 120 km (approximately 75 miles). The directive references IEC 61851 for EV charging modes, specifying that charging shall comply with the four conductive charging modes defined in IEC 61851. Connector Interoperability Gap: GB/T 20234 vs IEC 62196 in the Ethiopian EV Market Chinese EV chargers manufactured for the domestic market use GB/T 20234.2 (AC Type 2 equivalent with a different pin layout) and GB/T 20234.3 (DC, different from CCS2). These connectors are incompatible with IEC 62196 Type 2 and CCS2 without an adapter. Chinese manufacturers who produce multi-standard chargers with both GB/T 20234 and IEC 62196 / CCS2 outlets can address the Ethiopia interoperability requirement.GB/T 20234.2 (AC charging coupler — Chinese standard)
GB/T 20234.3 (DC charging coupler — Chinese standard)		 Ethiopia's vehicle fleet is diversifying rapidly following the January 2024 ban on ICE vehicle imports and growing EV adoption. EVs entering Ethiopia from multiple origins carry different connector standards: European-standard EVs use IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) and CCS2 (DC); Chinese-standard EVs use GB/T 20234.2 (AC) and GB/T 20234.3 (DC). The GB/T 20234 and IEC 62196 connectors are physically and electrically incompatible without an adapter. A Chinese charger with only GB/T 20234 sockets will not charge IEC 62196-type vehicles, and vice versa.
