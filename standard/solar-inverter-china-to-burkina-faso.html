Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Burkina Faso, the key practical gaps are: (1) ABNORM conformity assessment — Burkina Faso adopts IEC standards via ABNORM; Chinese GB/T certificates are not automatically recognised and IEC-referenced type-test evidence is the expected baseline; (2) ARSE licensing — grid-tied PV generation equipment requires ARSE regulatory authorisation as part of project permitting; (3) SONABEL grid-connection — inverters must comply with SONABEL's connection requirements for 50 Hz, 220/380 V grid (aligned with West African standard CEDEAO/ECOWAS grid parameters); (4) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence is required by donor/lender procurement specifications (World Bank, AFD, AfDB) and by IEC 61727 grid-interface requirements; (5) high-temperature and dust derating — Sahel ambient temperatures routinely reach ≥45 °C; inverter datasheets must demonstrate rated performance at elevated temperature and IP protection appropriate for dusty environments; (6) off-grid and mini-grid projects additionally require IEC TS 62257 series compliance where ABER/FDE specifications apply. Chinese GB/T 37408 and GB/T 19964 baselines do not satisfy these Burkina Faso gates and cannot be substituted without supplementary IEC test evidence.
