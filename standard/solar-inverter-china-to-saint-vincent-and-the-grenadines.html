Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, the key practical gaps are: (1) Saint Vincent and the Grenadines uses 230/400 V at 50 Hz, so frequency matches China but voltage differs from China's 220/380 V baseline; (2) VINLEC grid-tied projects should be validated against Saint Vincent and the Grenadines utility protection settings, including IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 utility-interface evidence; (3) SVGBS and project owners may expect IEC / UK-legacy documentation rather than China-only GB/T certificates; (4) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety and IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC evidence should be supplied for the exact inverter model; (5) coastal hurricane exposure, salt-mist corrosion, thermal derating, spares, and sea freight through Kingstown should be documented. China GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 are not automatically accepted for Saint Vincent and the Grenadines projects.
