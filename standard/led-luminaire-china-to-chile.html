Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Chilean SEC (Superintendencia de Electricidad y Combustibles) mandatory certification, INN NCh / IEC 60598 / 62560 / 62471 safety standards, Ministry of Energy efficiency labelling, and SUBTEL radio approval for smart luminaires, versus Chinese GB standards and CCC certification.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Chile must obtain SEC (Superintendencia de Electricidad y Combustibles) mandatory product certification and the SEC seal through an SEC-authorized certification body before the product can be marketed or sold; demonstrate electrical safety against NCh/IEC 60598 (luminaires) and IEC 62560 (self-ballasted LED lamps) adopted as Chilean NCh standards by INN; comply with the Ministry of Energy energy efficiency labelling regime for lighting (etiqueta de eficiencia energetica) where the product class is in scope; and obtain SUBTEL (Subsecretaria de Telecomunicaciones) equipment approval for any luminaire with wireless functionality (Wi-Fi / Bluetooth / Zigbee). Chile operates on a 220 V, 50 Hz single-phase domestic grid (same 50 Hz frequency as China; similar 220 V single-phase nominal, whereas China three-phase is 380 V). Chile has no horizontal RoHS-style substance-restriction regime and no REACH-style supply-chain notification duty, so EU-style substance documentation has no direct Chilean legal counterpart — none of these Chilean obligations are satisfied by existing Chinese CCC certification or GB test reports.
