Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face four major Jordan (JSMO) compliance gaps: (1) a JSMO conformity certificate (Certificate of Conformity) issued against Jordanian technical regulation is required for customs clearance at Aqaba — Chinese CCC certification is not directly accepted; (2) electrical safety must be demonstrated to JS standards adopting IEC 60335-2-24, with appliances rated for Jordan's 230 V / 50 Hz grid (the 50 Hz frequency matches China, but the nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V); (3) a Jordanian energy-efficiency label and minimum energy performance standards (MEPS) apply — Chinese GB 12021.2 energy grades do not transfer; and (4) an in-country importer of record established in Jordan is required to clear and place the product. R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned but charge documentation against the adopted IEC 60335-2-24 flammable-refrigerant requirements must be verified. Jordan does not operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS / battery / outdoor-noise regime.
