Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Bhutan, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirming the current Bhutan Standards Bureau adoption of IEC-based inverter standards; (2) obtaining IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence; (3) documenting IEC 62116 and IEC 61727 grid-interface and anti-islanding evidence acceptable to the Bhutan Electricity Authority, Bhutan Power Corporation, and project owner; (4) providing IEC 62920 and IEC 61000-series EMC evidence; (5) validating firmware for Bhutan's 50 Hz / 230 V / 400 V grid. Bhutan uses the same 50 Hz frequency as China, but its nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V; Chinese GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 evidence is not automatically accepted. High-altitude thermal and power derating should be checked for Himalayan sites.
