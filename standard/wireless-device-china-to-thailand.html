Short answer

The main Thailand (NBTC) gaps for a China-made wireless, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular, LoRa, or IoT device are: (1) mandatory NBTC type approval under the Radio Communications Act BE 2498 and Telecommunications Business Act BE 2544 — SRRC, FCC, and CE approvals are not recognised; (2) Thai-language label displaying the NBTC type approval certificate number on the product or packaging; (3) appointment of an in-country Thai importer or authorised agent for the NBTC application; (4) EMC compliance to TISI TIS standards (TIS 2366 / CISPR 32 for emissions; CISPR 35 / IEC 61000-4 series for immunity); and (5) electrical safety compliance to applicable TISI TIS standards (adopting IEC 60950-1 or IEC 62368-1) where mandatory TISI certification applies. Cybersecurity requirements are advisory only (ETDA guidelines) as of 2026-06-17. CCC is not recognised in Thailand.
