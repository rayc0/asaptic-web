Short answer

Lesotho's electricity supply is regulated by LEWA, served by LEC, and supported by imports from South Africa plus Muela hydro. For Chinese solar PV inverters, the rare favorable point is genuine nominal grid alignment: Lesotho uses 220/380 V at 50 Hz, matching China's 220/380 V 50 Hz base parameters. That match does not make Chinese GB/T evidence automatically acceptable. Expect project-level review by LEC and LEWA, likely reference to South African NRS interconnection practice, and a need for IEC 62109 safety, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 utility-interface, IEC 61000-series harmonic/EMC, and IEC 62920 PV inverter EMC evidence. GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 documents should be treated as China baseline evidence only, not substitutes for Lesotho project acceptance.
