Short answer

The Solomon Islands does not have a national standards body with mandatory BESS product certification requirements comparable to those in developed markets. Solomon Power (the national utility, also known as SIEA — Solomon Islands Electricity Authority) is the primary technical gatekeeper for grid-connected BESS projects. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to the Solomon Islands are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence — internationally expected by project owners, donors (World Bank, ADB, SPC, JICA), and Solomon Power in project specifications; (2) IEC 62933 system-level requirements where referenced in Solomon Power or donor-funded project agreements; (3) NFPA 855-aligned fire-safety design documentation, as the Solomon Islands follows a broadly IEC/Australian-influenced legacy with NFPA referenced in international project practice; (4) UN 38.3 transport documentation, mandatory under international conventions for sea freight via Honiara port; (5) grid voltage difference — Solomon Islands operates at 230/400 V 50 Hz whereas China's nominal is 220/380 V 50 Hz; PCS must be re-parameterised for 230/400 V despite the shared 50 Hz frequency; and (6) tropical derating — high humidity, cyclone risk, and salt-mist corrosion in the Solomon Islands demand enclosure ingress protection, anti-corrosion materials, and environmental derating beyond Chinese standard specifications. Chinese GB/T 36276, GB/T 34131, GB 38031, and GB/T 36558 certificates are not accepted as substitutes for IEC 62619 evidence in project or donor review.
