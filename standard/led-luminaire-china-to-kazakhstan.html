Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer entering the Kazakhstan (EAEU) market must obtain the EAC conformity mark through certification or declaration under the Customs Union / EAEU technical regulations: TR CU 004/2011 (low-voltage electrical safety, on a GOST IEC 60598 / 62560 basis), TR CU 020/2011 (electromagnetic compatibility), and TR EAEU 037/2016 (restriction of hazardous substances in electrical and electronic equipment — a RoHS-style regime that DOES apply, not an absence); comply with EAEU energy-efficiency requirements and labelling; document photobiological safety on a GOST IEC 62471 basis; appoint an in-country (Kazakhstan / EAEU) importer or authorised representative to hold the conformity documents; and have testing and certification performed by an EAEU-accredited body — none of which is satisfied by existing Chinese CCC certification or GB-standard reports. Kazakhstan's 220/380 V 50 Hz grid matches China's nominal voltage and frequency, so no electrical re-rating is needed on that basis.
