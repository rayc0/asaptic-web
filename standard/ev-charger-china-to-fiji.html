Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Fiji, the practical gaps are IEC-aligned product evidence, connector conversion from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC, EFL project-level connection acceptance, EMC and power-quality evidence under IEC 61000 practice, and environmental derating for coastal salt mist, high humidity, cyclones, heat, and outdoor corrosion. Fiji uses 240 V single-phase / 415 V three-phase at 50 Hz. Fiji has the same 50 Hz frequency as China, but the nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V baseline, so input-voltage range, protection settings, cable ratings, and thermal derating must be confirmed. Fiji's EV market is nascent and an EVSE-specific national certification rule could not be confirmed from official public sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters should verify the current DNTMS/FCCC import route, EFL connection requirements, HS code, and project specification before shipment.
