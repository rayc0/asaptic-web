Short answer

Iceland is an EEA member — not an EU member — but the EU Radio Equipment Directive (RED 2014/53/EU) applies in Iceland via the EEA Agreement, and CE marking is mandatory, enforced by PFS (Post og Fjarskipti — Icelandic Post and Telecom, the National Regulatory Authority). Key gaps versus Chinese SRRC/CCC include: radio performance testing to EN 300 328 / EN 301 893 (with 5 GHz DFS requirements — PFS is responsible for spectrum monitoring and enforcement), EMC to EN 301 489 series, electrical safety to EN IEC 62368-1:2020+A11 (230 V/50 Hz, plug type C/F, Neytendastofa oversight), cybersecurity under RED Art. 3.3(d)–(f) via EN 18031 (mandatory from 1 August 2025), EEA/EU Authorised Representative (an EU AR covering only EU member states does NOT automatically cover Iceland — EEA coverage must be explicit or a separate Iceland/EEA AR must be appointed), Icelandic-language labelling for consumer products, WEEE registration via Íslenski rafgeymirinn, and Icelandic customs clearance via Tollstjóri and VSK (24%) despite EEA membership. CCC and FCC are not recognised in Iceland.
