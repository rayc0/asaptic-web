Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Papua New Guinea, the practical gaps are IEC-oriented safety, connector, EMC, and project documentation rather than a confirmed charger-specific whole-unit national certification route. Papua New Guinea uses 240/415 V, 50 Hz supply through PNG Power Ltd and other project power systems, so the frequency matches China but the nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V baseline. Public and fleet deployments should expect IEC 62196 Type 2 AC and CCS2 DC direction, not North American J1772. GB/T 20234 and GB/T 27930 chargers need hardware, signaling, and communication redesign before use with IEC 62196 / CCS2 vehicles or networks.
