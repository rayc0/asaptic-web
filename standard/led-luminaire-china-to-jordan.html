Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Jordan requirements administered by JSMO (Jordan Standards and Metrology Organization) — JS standards adopting IEC/GSO lighting standards (JS/IEC 60598, 62560, 62471), energy-efficiency labelling, and TRC radio approval for smart luminaires — versus Chinese GB standards and CCC certification. Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Jordan must meet JSMO (Jordan Standards and Metrology Organization) technical-regulation and conformity-certificate requirements for regulated lighting products, based on JS standards that adopt IEC/GSO lighting standards (JS/IEC 60598-1 for luminaire safety, JS/IEC 62560/62471 for self-ballasted lamps and photobiological safety); apply an energy-efficiency label under the Jordanian labelling scheme; obtain TRC (Telecommunications Regulatory Commission) approval for smart luminaires with wireless functions; verify the product to Jordan's 230 V, 50 Hz grid (50 Hz matches China, but nominal voltage differs from China's 220 V); and ship through a registered in-country Jordanian importer (port of entry typically Aqaba). Jordan has no EU-style horizontal RoHS, no horizontal SVHC supply-chain regime, and no EU-style ecodesign minimum-efficacy regulation — instead an energy-label scheme applies. Existing CCC certification and GB test reports are not directly accepted; conformity is verified against the Jordanian/JS references.
