Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against South African market-access requirements: NRCS Letter of Authority (LOA) under the lighting Compulsory Specification, SANS/IEC safety standards (SANS IEC 60598, SANS IEC 62560, SANS IEC 62471), mandatory energy-efficiency labelling under SANS 941, and ICASA type approval for wireless models, versus Chinese GB standards and CCC certification.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to South Africa must obtain an NRCS Letter of Authority (LOA) under the lighting Compulsory Specification before the product can be sold or imported; the LOA is typically applied for by a South Africa-based importer. Products must meet the relevant SANS/IEC safety standards (SANS IEC 60598 for luminaires, SANS IEC 62560 for self-ballasted LED lamps, SANS IEC 62471 for photobiological safety), and electrical products must satisfy SANS 941 mandatory energy-efficiency labelling. Wireless-enabled luminaires additionally require ICASA type approval. There is no horizontal RoHS-style substance restriction in South Africa; restricted-substance and e-waste obligations are partial and primarily addressed through producer/EPR registration under the National Environmental Management: Waste Act. None of these are covered by existing CCC certification or China GB compliance — verify all current requirements before shipment.
