Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Belize, the key practical gaps are: (1) Belize's 110/220 V, 60 Hz grid and US-heritage NEMA distribution differ fundamentally from China's 220/380 V, 50 Hz grid; (2) grid-tie review should be treated as an Americas direction using IEEE 1547 and UL 1741, not EN 50549 or VDE framing; (3) Chinese GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence is 50 Hz China-grid evidence and is not automatically accepted by BBS, PUC, or BEL; (4) anti-islanding, ride-through, reconnection, EMC, and power-quality evidence must be validated at 60 Hz for the specific inverter model and firmware configuration; and (5) BEL and PUC should be engaged before procurement because public PV-inverter interconnection details may be project-specific.
