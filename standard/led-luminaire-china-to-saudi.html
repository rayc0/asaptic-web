Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer entering the Saudi market must register the product on the SABER platform and obtain a Product Certificate of Conformity (PCoC) from a SASO-approved Conformity Assessment Body, then a Shipment Certificate of Conformity (SCoC) for each consignment; demonstrate electrical safety to SASO-adopted IEC 60598-1 (luminaires) and IEC 62560 (self-ballasted LED lamps); meet SASO 2902 lighting energy-efficiency / MEPS minimum efficacy thresholds and affix the SEEC (Saudi Energy Efficiency Centre) energy label registered through SASO; meet EMC requirements under the SASO EMC technical regulation (CISPR 15 / IEC 61547 basis); document photobiological safety to IEC 62471; design for 230/400 V, 60 Hz mains; and appoint a Saudi-based importer of record (clearance at Jeddah Islamic Port or Dammam). IECEE CB test certificates and reports are widely accepted by Saudi CABs as the basis for PCoC issuance. Note: Saudi Arabia has no horizontal RoHS-style substance directive — substance control is handled through the SABER/PCoC technical-file process and product-specific SASO regulations, not a standalone 10-substance restriction. None of these are covered by existing Chinese CCC certification.
