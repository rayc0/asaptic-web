Short answer

ANRT mandatory homologation (type approval) under Moroccan Telecom Law 24-96 is the primary gap for any wireless or telecom terminal device exported from China to Morocco. CE mark, CCC, and SRRC approval are not recognised by ANRT; a separate Moroccan homologation process with test reports from ANRT-approved or ILAC/MRA-accredited laboratories is required, along with an ANRT homologation certificate and ANRT label displayed on the product. Additional gaps include IMANOR NM-aligned EMC standards (EN/IEC-harmonized), 220–240 V / 50 Hz electrical safety with Type C/E plug, a registered Moroccan authorized importer, French-language product documentation and labelling, DGSSI cybersecurity requirements under Law 05-20, and a 5 GHz indoor-only frequency restriction.
