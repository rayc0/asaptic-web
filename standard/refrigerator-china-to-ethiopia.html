Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator exporters to Ethiopia face four main compliance themes: (1) electrical safety to Ethiopian Standards adopting IEC 60335-2-24 (ES IEC 60335-2-24) overseen by the Ethiopian Standards Agency (ESA) — GB 4706.13/CCC is technically close (both derive from IEC 60335-2-24) but is not automatically accepted, and conformity is demonstrated through CACEA/Ministry of Trade import inspection rather than China's CCC mark; (2) energy efficiency label/MEPS — Ethiopia operates an energy-efficiency programme for appliances, so EU-style EPREL pre-registration does not exist but local label/MEPS requirements should be checked with the importer; (3) EMC — Ethiopia has no EU-style mandatory horizontal EMC regime for household appliances, so GB 4343.1 test data generally has no Ethiopian counterpart obligation; (4) refrigerant — R-600a is well-positioned, with charge documentation against IEC 60335-2-24 Annex AA to be verified. Ethiopia is landlocked; goods route via Djibouti, and an in-country importer handles conformity and inspection. Grid is 220 V / 50 Hz (same 50 Hz as China; China's domestic three-phase is 380 V).
