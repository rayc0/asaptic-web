Short answer

The main Paraguay compliance gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT device are: CONATEL mandatory homologación under Ley 642/95 de Telecomunicaciones — all radio and telecom terminal equipment requires a CONATEL homologación certificate and the CONATEL seal on the product before import or sale, with no recognition of CCC or SRRC approvals; CE or FCC test reports may be accepted as a technical basis to support the CONATEL dossier but do not replace the homologación certificate; an authorized Paraguayan importer with valid RUC/SET registration is required; INTN NP electrical safety and EMC compliance (NP standards harmonised with IEC/ISO and Mercosur NM norms); product compatibility with Paraguay's 220 V / 50 Hz power system and Type C (European 2-pin) plugs, with the Mercosur NBR 14136 plug type also present; and Spanish language labelling. Cybersecurity requirements are currently advisory under Ley 4439/2011 (cybercrime framework). Paraguay switched diplomatic recognition to the PRC in 2024, and China-Paraguay trade is normalising.
