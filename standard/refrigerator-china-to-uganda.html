Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face four major Uganda compliance gaps: (1) mandatory Pre-Export Verification of Conformity (PVoC) and a Certificate of Conformity (CoC) issued by a UNBS-appointed inspection agency before shipment — Chinese CCC certification does not substitute and a per-consignment or registered/licensed CoC route is required at the load port; (2) electrical safety to UNBS-adopted IEC 60335-2-24 (IEC/US-style basis) rather than GB 4706.13, with charge documentation for R-600a; (3) energy-efficiency label and MEPS conformity to the applicable Uganda/EAC standard — Chinese GB 12021.2 grades do not map directly; and (4) appointment of an in-country importer of record for clearance, UNBS import inspection, and Q-Mark/market surveillance. Uganda uses 240 V / 50 Hz (same 50 Hz as China, different nominal voltage from China's 220/380 V), and as a landlocked country goods route via Mombasa or Dar es Salaam.
