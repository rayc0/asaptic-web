Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Tanzania, the main practical gaps are TBS PVoC or destination inspection where the goods are in scope; EWURA's 2026 e-mobility charging-station and battery-swapping guidance for public and private charging infrastructure; IEC 61851 safety evidence; connector interoperability because EWURA recommends IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS while also accepting GB/T 20234 and IEC 62196-3 for DC use; TANESCO or DNO load, protection, metering, and completion-certificate review; and a clear mapping from China GB/T 18487 / GB/T 20234 files to Tanzania's IEC-oriented expectations. A blanket mandatory whole-unit EV charger product certificate was not confirmed from official sources; exact HS code, charger type, installation route, public/private use, and site load should be verified.
