Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face several Mozambique market-access gaps: (1) electrical safety must be demonstrated against NM/IEC 60335-2-24 (Mozambique adopts IEC via INNOQ NM standards) — CCC/GB 4706.13 certification is not automatically recognised; (2) an in-country importer of record is required and import consignments may be subject to INNOQ conformity assessment / import inspection for regulated products; (3) energy labelling / MEPS programmes for refrigerators are emerging and should be verified before shipment; (4) Portuguese-language product documentation, instructions, and markings are required. R-600a refrigerant aligns with IEC 60335-2-24 flammable-refrigerant practice, but charge documentation must be verified. The China-Mozambique grid is closely aligned (both 50 Hz; Mozambique 220 V single-phase nominal), which simplifies electrical adaptation versus markets with different frequency.
