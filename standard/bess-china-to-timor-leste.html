Short answer

Timor-Leste does not yet have a publicly confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to a full market-wide certification regime. The market is developing, with ANE serving as the likely national standard route, EDTL handling electricity-sector interconnection interfaces, and the energy ministry referenced in project approvals. A practical export matrix for China-to-Timor-Leste BESS is therefore: (1) IEC 62619 evidence for cell and module safety where procurement documents require it; (2) IEC 62933 project-level evidence for system safety and planning where specified; (3) EN/IEC-aligned fire-safety installation controls as required by project package; (4) UN 38.3 transport documentation for lithium-cell logistics; (5) confirmation of Timor-Leste project connection settings on a 220/380 V 50 Hz grid. Timor-Leste and China nominally share 220/380 V 50 Hz, which simplifies electrical parameter transfer relative to non-matching countries. EV and charging ecosystems are nascent, and market access for charging remains project-specific with IEC connector expectations only where those specifications apply.
