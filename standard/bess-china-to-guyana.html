Short answer

Guyana does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to the EU Battery Regulation or a Saudi SABER-type mandatory conformity route. BESS projects in Guyana operate primarily under project-specific standards referenced in GPL grid-connection agreements, GEA renewable energy policy, and Guyana Fire Service / GNBS building fire-safety requirements. A critical hardware consideration unique to Guyana is its mixed-frequency grid: the coastal Demerara-Berbice Interconnected System (DBIS) — where utility-scale BESS projects are concentrated — operates predominantly at 60 Hz with 120/240 V (single-phase) and 208/240 V (three-phase), while some legacy inland areas retain 50 Hz supply. Chinese BESS power conversion systems (PCS) are designed and validated for China's 50 Hz / 220–380 V grid; installation on Guyana's 60 Hz / 120–240 V coastal grid requires PCS reconfiguration, re-validation, and re-parameterisation — this must be addressed explicitly in project specifications. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Guyana are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence, the internationally expected baseline for project acceptance; (2) IEC 62933 system-level technical requirements where referenced in GPL or project-owner specifications; (3) GPL grid-connection approval under the GPL Grid Code and Interim Interconnection Requirements, with reference to IEEE 1547 and NEC 2014; (4) Guyana Fire Service / GNBS building fire-safety compliance, including evidence aligned with NFPA 855 where adopted for stationary BESS; (5) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory under international transport conventions for shipment via Georgetown (Guyana's main port); and (6) 60 Hz / 120–240 V PCS reconfiguration for the coastal DBIS grid. Chinese GB 44240-2024, GB/T 36276-2023, and GB/T 36558-2023 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC 62619 or IEC 62933 evidence in project or authority review.
