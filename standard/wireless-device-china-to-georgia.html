Short answer

Wireless devices entering Georgia require GNCC (Georgian National Communications Commission) type approval and a conformity certificate or registration for all radio and telecommunications terminal equipment under the Electronic Communications Law of Georgia. GEOSTM (Georgian Standards and Metrology Institute) GS standards — many adopted from ISO/IEC/ETSI and increasingly aligned with EU EN standards under the DCFTA Association Agreement — provide the technical basis for EMC and radio conformity assessment. Electrical safety testing for the 220 V/50 Hz European-plug (Type C/F) grid is required. A local importer or authorised distributor registered in Georgia is mandatory. Georgia is not an EAEU member and operates independently of EAC conformity marking; EU Association Agreement regulatory convergence with RED is underway but GNCC type approval remains a separate national process. Chinese CCC, SRRC, and CE documentation are not recognised and do not substitute for GNCC approvals. Cybersecurity obligations under the Georgia Cybersecurity Law (2012) and CERT.GOV.GE apply to connected devices.
