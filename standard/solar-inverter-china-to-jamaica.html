Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Jamaica, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirming BSJ standards adoption and any import conformity route for the specific inverter; (2) preparing IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence; (3) preparing IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 utility-interface evidence for JPS interconnection review; (4) preparing IEC 62920 and IEC 61000 EMC and harmonic evidence; and (5) validating firmware and protection settings against Jamaica's 110/220 V, 50 Hz grid conditions. Jamaica uses the same 50 Hz frequency as China, but its US-influenced 110 V single-phase and 220 V split-phase service is materially different from China's 220/380 V baseline. China GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 evidence should not be treated as automatically accepted in Jamaica.
