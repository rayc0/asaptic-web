Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Papua New Guinea, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirming applicable NISIT-adopted or project-specified IEC standards for the specific project; (2) PNG Power Ltd grid-connection review for grid-tied systems; (3) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence; (4) IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 grid-interface evidence; (5) IEC 62920 and IEC 61000-series EMC evidence; and (6) firmware validation for Papua New Guinea's 50 Hz / 240 V / 415 V grid. Papua New Guinea shares China's 50 Hz frequency, but the nominal voltage differs: China is 220/380 V, while Papua New Guinea is 240/415 V. China GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 baselines are not automatically accepted as Papua New Guinea compliance evidence.
