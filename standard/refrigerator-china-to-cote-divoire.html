Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers exporting to Côte d'Ivoire face four practical compliance gaps: (1) electrical safety conformity to NI standards adopting IEC 60335-2-24 — CCC/GB 4706.13 certification is not automatically recognised, and refrigerators are typically regulated products subject to the Verification of Conformity (VoC) import programme; (2) a Certificate of Conformity (CoC) issued under the Ministry of Trade VoC scheme is generally required at the port (Abidjan or San-Pedro) before customs clearance, on a per-shipment or registered-product basis; (3) energy labelling/MEPS expectations are developing rather than an EU-style EPREL database, so requirements must be confirmed with CODINORM and the Ministry; and (4) French-language product documentation, instructions, and markings plus a locally established importer/responsible party are needed. R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned and accepted, but charge and flammability documentation should reference IEC 60335-2-24. Côte d'Ivoire does not operate EU-style horizontal RoHS, battery, or outdoor-noise regimes, so those EU-specific obligations do not apply.
