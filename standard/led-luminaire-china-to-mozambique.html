Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Mozambique should expect: INNOQ-administered conformity for regulated electrical products, built on NM (Norma Mocambicana) standards that adopt IEC 60598-1 / 60598-series for luminaire safety, IEC 62560 for self-ballasted lamps, IEC 61347-2-13 for LED control gear, CISPR 15 / IEC 61547 for EMC, and IEC 62471 for photobiological safety; a 220 V / 50 Hz supply (the 50 Hz frequency matches China, but Chinese three-phase 380 V product must be checked against the 220 V single-phase nominal); an energy-efficiency / labelling expectation for lighting products; INCM (Instituto Nacional das Comunicacoes de Mocambique) type-approval for any luminaire with wireless functionality; Portuguese-language documentation, instructions, and markings; and an in-country importer for customs clearance through Maputo, Beira, or Nacala. Mozambique does NOT operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS / restricted-substances or REACH-style chemical-notification regime, so there is no direct counterpart to the EU 'ecodesign' efficacy regulation (energy labelling is the nearest lever) and no horizontal RoHS to satisfy — but the importer should confirm current INNOQ regulated-product lists and any sector energy programme. Chinese CCC certification and GB test reports are not automatically recognised; IEC-based (IECEE CB) test reports are the practical bridge.
