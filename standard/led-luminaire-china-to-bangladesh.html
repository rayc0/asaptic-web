Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Bangladesh market-access requirements — BSTI mandatory certification for lamps, BDS/IEC 60598/62560/62471 safety and photobiological standards, SREDA energy efficiency labelling, and BTRC radio-equipment approval for smart luminaires — versus Chinese GB standards (GB 30255 energy efficiency, GB 7000 luminaire safety).

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Bangladesh must obtain BSTI (Bangladesh Standards and Testing Institution) mandatory certification for lamps and luminaires against the relevant BDS standards (which adopt IEC 60598 for luminaire safety and IEC 60598/62560 for lamps); demonstrate electrical safety to BDS/IEC 60598 and, for self-ballasted lamps, IEC 60560/62560; address photobiological safety to IEC 62471 where required; comply with SREDA (Sustainable and Renewable Energy Development Authority) energy efficiency and energy-labelling rules where the product falls in a regulated category; obtain BTRC (Bangladesh Telecommunication Regulatory Commission) approval for any smart/wireless luminaire with Wi-Fi or Bluetooth; and import through an in-country importer with the BSTI mark and proper marking via the ports of Chattogram or Mongla. Bangladesh has no EU-style horizontal RoHS, battery, or outdoor-noise regime, so EU RoHS substance restrictions do not have a direct Bangladesh legal counterpart. None of these Bangladesh requirements are covered by China's GB standards (GB 30255 energy efficiency, GB 7000 luminaire safety) or CCC certification.
