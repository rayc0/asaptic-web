Short answer

For power tools exported from China to the Philippines, do not assume Chinese GB/T evidence or a CCC mark carries over. The defensible starting point is to confirm whether the specific tool falls under a Philippine mandatory product certification scheme administered by the Bureau of Philippine Standards under DTI; where it does, obtain a PS licence for local manufacture or an Import Commodity Clearance for imports against the relevant PNS, usually adopting IEC 62841. Treat 230 V 60 Hz mains compatibility, EMC, NTC type approval for any cordless radio module, an in-country importer, and Philippine labelling as separate obligations.

Chinese workplace occupational noise management rules		 The Philippines does not operate an outdoor equipment noise-emission marking scheme equivalent to the EU guaranteed sound power label, so there is no mandatory LWA noise mark on the tool for placement on the Philippine market.
