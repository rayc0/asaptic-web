Short answer

The main Uganda gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT device are: mandatory UCC type approval under the Communications Act 2013 (CE, FCC, and CCC are not recognised by UCC), UNBS standards for EMC emissions and immunity (IEC/CISPR based), electrical safety compliance for Uganda's 240V/50Hz Type-G British plug environment, appointment of a local Ugandan authorised dealer or importer, and awareness of the Computer Misuse Act 2011 and Data Protection and Privacy Act 2019 (no mandatory IoT device cybersecurity certification, but data-security obligations apply). A 5 GHz indoor-use restriction may apply. EAC harmonised type approval is developing but wireless device type approval currently remains nationally enforced via UCC.
