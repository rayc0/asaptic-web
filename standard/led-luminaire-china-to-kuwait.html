Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Kuwait market-access requirements: the mandatory KUCAS (Kuwait Conformity Assurance Scheme) run by PAI (Public Authority for Industry), GSO lighting standards (GSO IEC 60598 / 62560 / 62471), the GSO/Kuwait energy-efficiency label, MEW grid requirements (240 V, 50 Hz), and CITRA radio approval for smart luminaires, versus Chinese GB standards (GB 30255, GB 7000) and CCC certification.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Kuwait must obtain a KUCAS (Kuwait Conformity Assurance Scheme) conformity certificate administered by PAI (Public Authority for Industry) — typically a Technical Evaluation Report plus a per-shipment Certificate of Conformity issued before goods clear Shuwaikh or Shuaiba port; demonstrate electrical safety to GSO IEC 60598-1 (luminaires) and the relevant GSO IEC component standards, rated for the Kuwait grid of 240 V, 50 Hz; meet GSO/Kuwait energy-efficiency requirements and affix the GSO/Kuwait energy-efficiency label where applicable; document photobiological safety per GSO IEC 62471; appoint an in-country Kuwaiti importer / registered agent as the legal entry party; and, for smart/wireless luminaires, obtain CITRA (Communication and Information Technology Regulatory Authority) radio type approval. China RoHS (GB/T 26572) is not mirrored by any horizontal Kuwait RoHS, so there is no Kuwait RoHS step — but it also gives no Kuwait credit. CCC certification and Chinese GB test reports do not by themselves satisfy KUCAS.
