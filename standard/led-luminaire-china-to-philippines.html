Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to the Philippines must obtain a BPS Import Commodity Clearance (ICC) — or a PS Quality Certification Mark for local manufacture — for products covered by the lighting technical regulation, with safety demonstrated to the relevant PNS IEC standards (PNS IEC 60598 for luminaires, PNS IEC 62560 for self-ballasted LED lamps, PNS IEC 62471 for photobiological safety); secure DOE energy labelling and meet any applicable MEPS for the product class; obtain NTC type approval for any smart luminaire with radio functions (Wi-Fi / Bluetooth); design to the 230 V 60 Hz Philippine grid rather than China 220/380 V 50 Hz; appoint an in-country importer of record; and address restricted-substance and e-waste obligations even though the Philippines has no single horizontal RoHS-style directive — none of which is covered by existing China CCC certification or GB compliance.

BPS ICC vs PS Mark and In-Country Importer of Record vs CCC / CQC In China, the primary mandatory certification for luminaires sold in the residential market is CCC (China Compulsory Certification), administered by CNCA and certified by CNCA-authorised bodies such as CQC (China Quality Certification Centre); voluntary CQC certification exists for products outside mandatory CCC. CCC is a manufacturer/product certification held directly by the producer, with factory inspection and ongoing surveillance. The Chinese CCC scheme and the Philippine BPS ICC / PS scheme are separate and non-mutual: CCC does not authorise import into the Philippines, and the Philippine clearance must be held by an in-country importer (ICC) or local manufacturer (PS), which is a different applicant model from China's direct-manufacturer CCC certificate.CNCA-C10-01 — CCC certification rules for luminaires (CNCA / CQC)

The applicant must be an in-country entity — a Philippine-registered importer of record (for ICC) or local manufacturer (for PS); a foreign manufacturer cannot hold the clearance directly. The technical file supporting ICC / PS includes safety test reports to the applicable PNS IEC standards (PNS IEC 60598 luminaires, PNS IEC 62560 self-ballasted lamps), performance/energy evidence for the DOE label, and EMC where in scope.
