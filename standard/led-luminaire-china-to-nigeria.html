Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Nigeria must clear the mandatory SONCAP (SON Conformity Assessment Programme): obtain a Product Certificate (PC) for the model family based on NIS-adopted IEC standards, then a per-shipment SONCAP Certificate (SC) issued before vessel loading and required for customs clearance at Lagos (Apapa/Tin Can) or Onne. Safety is assessed against NIS IEC 60598-1 (luminaires) and NIS IEC 60598-2 part standards plus NIS IEC 62560 for self-ballasted lamps; photobiological safety against NIS IEC 62471; energy efficiency must meet the SON appliance energy-performance and labelling requirement; smart luminaires with wireless functions additionally require NCC (Nigerian Communications Commission) type approval. An in-country importer of record is mandatory. Nigeria has no horizontal RoHS-equivalent substance restriction, so EU-style RoHS testing is not a SONCAP entry gate — but it remains good practice and may apply to specific buyer or re-export requirements. CN CCC certification is not recognised for SONCAP; testing must be done to the NIS/IEC editions accepted by SON.
