Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Sri Lanka should expect SLSI (Sri Lanka Standards Institution) mandatory certification and import inspection for regulated lamps and luminaires, with the SLS mark applied on conformity; comply with SLS standards that adopt the IEC base series (IEC 60598 for luminaire safety, IEC 62560 for self-ballasted LED lamps, IEC 62471 for photobiological safety); meet SLSEA (Sri Lanka Sustainable Energy Authority) energy-efficiency labelling for in-scope lamps; obtain TRCSL (Telecommunications Regulatory Commission of Sri Lanka) type approval for smart/wireless luminaires; design for a 230 V / 50 Hz grid (50 Hz matches China but nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V); and appoint an in-country importer to clear customs and hold SLSI registration. Sri Lanka has no EU-style horizontal RoHS, REACH-SVHC, or Ecodesign-efficacy regime, so those EU obligations have no direct Sri Lankan equivalent — Chinese GB / CCC documentation does not by itself satisfy the SLSI scheme.
