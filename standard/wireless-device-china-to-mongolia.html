Short answer

Wireless devices entering Mongolia require CITA (Communications and Information Technology Authority) type approval for all radio and telecommunications terminal equipment — this is a Mongolia-specific national requirement with no EAEU equivalence, as Mongolia is not an EAEU member. Electrical safety testing is required for the 220 V/50 Hz Soviet-legacy Type C/E grid. A local importer or authorised distributor registered in Mongolia is mandatory. Mongolia's Information and Cybersecurity Law 2021 and CITA/Ministry of Digital Development and Communications oversight impose additional obligations for connected devices. Chinese CCC, SRRC, and MIIT NAL documentation are not recognised by CITA and do not substitute for Mongolian type approval. Mongolian Cyrillic-script labelling is preferred; regulatory requirements are still developing.
