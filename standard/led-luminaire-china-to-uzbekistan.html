Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Uzbekistan must obtain O'zStandart conformity certification or declaration of conformity (per the national technical-regulation regime) demonstrating compliance with applicable O'zDSt standards aligned to IEC 60598 (luminaire safety), IEC 62560 (self-ballasted LED lamps), and IEC 62471 (photobiological safety); meet EMC requirements coordinated with the national radio/communications regulator; comply with applicable national energy-efficiency programme requirements for lighting (the local analogue to energy/ecodesign rules, though structured differently from the EU); and work through an in-country importer who typically holds the certification or declaration. Uzbekistan's grid is 220/380 V, 50 Hz — a genuine voltage and frequency match with China's 220/380 V 50 Hz nominal, so design re-rating is generally not required. Uzbekistan has NO horizontal RoHS-type restricted-substance regime (it is not an EAEU member), so the EU/China hazardous-substance restriction model does not map directly — substance restrictions, if any, arise only through specific product or chemical rules. None of these are covered by existing Chinese CCC / GB certification, which is not recognised by O'zStandart.
