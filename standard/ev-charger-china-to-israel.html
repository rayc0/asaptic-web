Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Israel, the confirmed practical gaps are SII-mandatory SI standards (adopting IEC 61851) required for safety compliance, connector redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 since Israel's IEC-aligned infrastructure is physically incompatible with GB/T connectors, Israel Electricity Authority and Israel Electric Corporation grid-connection approval for all grid-tied installations, IEC 61000 series EMC compliance, and OCPP 1.6 / 2.0.1 back-office interoperability for networked chargers. Israel operates on 230 V single-phase / 400 V three-phase, 50 Hz, closely matching China's 220 V / 50 Hz domestic baseline, but exporters must still confirm rated input-voltage range and derating. GB/T 27930 DC communication and GB/T 18487.1-2023 alone are not accepted by SII or the Electricity Authority as compliance evidence. Exporters should confirm the current SII certification scope, applicable SI standard edition, and Israel Electric Corporation connection requirements for the specific charger type before shipment.
