Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Vanuatu, the key practical gaps are: (1) Vanuatu has limited publicly confirmed PV-inverter-specific national standards, so acceptance is usually project-, utility-, regulator-, or owner-led; (2) URA-regulated utility connection review with UNELCO in Port Vila or VUI on other concession networks must be confirmed before shipment; (3) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence should be prepared for the exact inverter model or family; (4) IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 utility-interface evidence should be prepared for grid-tied projects; (5) IEC 62920 and IEC 61000-series EMC evidence should be available; and (6) firmware and protection settings must be validated for Vanuatu's 230/400 V, 50 Hz network. Vanuatu shares China's 50 Hz frequency, but the nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V baseline. Cyclone exposure, high humidity, salt-mist corrosion, thermal derating, and sea-freight routing through Port Vila should be addressed in the project technical file.
