Short answer

Luxembourg is an EU member state: CE marking under RED 2014/53/EU is mandatory and enforced by ILR (Institut Luxembourgeois de Regulation). Radio performance (EN 300 328 / EN 301 893), EMC (EN 301 489), electrical safety (EN IEC 62368-1:2020+A11), and cybersecurity under RED Art. 3.3(d)-(f) mandatory from 1 August 2025 (EN 18031) are the major gaps versus Chinese SRRC/CCC. Luxembourg-specific requirements include the Type C/F plug (230 V/50 Hz), mandatory bilingual product labelling in French and German, EU Authorised Representative, ecotrel WEEE registration, and awareness of Luxembourg's role as the EU headquarters of Amazon and a major cross-border e-commerce logistics hub.

The Chinese standard GB 4943.1-2022 aligns with the IEC 62368-1 second edition, while the EU harmonised standard EN IEC 62368-1:2020+A11:2021 is derived from the third edition (IEC 62368-1:2018/AMD1:2020).
