Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Belize, the fundamental practical gap is electrical and connector redesign for a US-heritage 110/220 V, 60 Hz market. China domestic EVSE is commonly designed around 220/380 V, 50 Hz and GB/T connectors. Belize installations should be treated as requiring 60 Hz input revalidation, 110/220 V class configuration, BEL grid and site coordination, and SAE J1772 Type 1 AC plus CCS1 DC connector hardware, not IEC Type 2 / CCS2 and not GB/T. Safety evidence should be prepared in the US-direction UL 2202 / UL 2594 framework where requested, with IEC 61851 and IEC 61000 evidence as supporting references. Exporters should verify the current BBS conformity route, PUC/BEL project requirements, HS code, and site specification before shipment.
