Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering El Salvador, the practical gaps are 120/240 V, 60 Hz input validation; SIGET and distributor coordination for grid-connected installations; OSARTEC / OSN conformity review where a Salvadoran technical regulation or adopted standard applies; and connector hardware conversion away from China GB/T 20234. El Salvador is an Americas-style 60 Hz market, so SAE J1772 Type 1 AC and CCS1 DC are the likely practical connector ecosystem, but exporters should confirm the required connector with SIGET, the distributor, site owner, tender, or charge-point operator before quoting. GB/T connectors are physically incompatible with J1772 / CCS1 inlets, and GB/T 27930 DC communication is not a substitute for CCS communication or OCPP network requirements.
