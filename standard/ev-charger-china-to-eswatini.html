Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Eswatini, the practical gates are SWASA conformity review, ESERA and Eswatini Electricity Company coordination for grid-connected installations, IEC-family safety and EMC evidence, and IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 connector compatibility. Eswatini uses a 230/400 V, 50 Hz supply and is a landlocked Southern African SADC and SACU market that imports power from South Africa; charging infrastructure is likely to follow South-African IEC Type 2 / CCS2 practice. China-only GB/T 20234 connectors and GB/T 27930 DC communication are physically and protocol-incompatible with that IEC ecosystem.
