Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Egypt must ensure the product conforms to Egyptian EOS (ES) standards that adopt IEC 60598 (luminaire safety), IEC 62560 (self-ballasted LED lamp safety), and IEC 62471 (photobiological safety); the Egyptian importer must be registered with GOEIC and the foreign factory must be enrolled in the GOEIC importer/factory registry under Decree 43/2016 before goods can clear customs; energy-using luminaires must carry the Egyptian appliance energy-efficiency label where the relevant ES energy-label standard applies; smart luminaires with Wi-Fi or Bluetooth require NTRA radio type approval. The Egyptian grid is 220 V single-phase at 50 Hz — the same 50 Hz as China and a similar 220 V single-phase nominal, so electrical re-rating is usually minimal. Egypt has no horizontal RoHS-style hazardous-substance restriction equivalent to China RoHS or EU RoHS as of 2026; substance control is addressed only indirectly through product safety standards. CCC certification is not recognised for Egyptian market access.
