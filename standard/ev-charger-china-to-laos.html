Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Laos, the key practical considerations are: Laos operates on 230 V single-phase / 400 V three-phase, 50 Hz supplied by EDL, which is electrically compatible with China's 220 V / 380 V baseline within standard input-voltage tolerance ranges, but derating at tropical ambient temperatures must be confirmed. DOSM (Department of Standards and Metrology) adopts IEC-based standards for conformity, and MEM / EDL govern grid-connection approval and project licensing. Laos' formal connector direction aligns with IEC 62196 (Type 2 AC / CCS2 DC), but the ground reality is that Chinese-brand EVs (BYD, SAIC-MG, and others) entering Laos via the China-Laos Railway and BRI have brought GB/T hardware physically onto Lao roads — creating a genuine mixed-fleet, mixed-connector environment. For formal DOSM/EDL approval and IEC 62196-compatible charging network deployment, GB/T connectors are not interoperable with IEC 62196 Type 2 vehicle inlets. Exporters should: (1) confirm IEC 61851-family safety evidence acceptable to DOSM; (2) clarify which connector type the specific Lao project or site operator requires; (3) confirm EDL grid-connection and metering approval; (4) address OCPP back-office where networked charging is involved. A blanket Laos-wide mandatory EVSE certification regulation through DOSM could not be confirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters should verify the current conformity assessment route with DOSM or a qualified local agent before shipment.

Connector Interoperability — IEC 62196 Formal Direction vs. GB/T On-Ground Reality in Laos China AC chargers use GB/T 20234.2 couplers and DC fast chargers use GB/T 20234.3 couplers. Although the GB/T 20234.2 AC coupler has a similar overall shape to the IEC 62196 Type 2, they differ in connector gender (GB/T uses male at the charger side, female at the vehicle inlet — opposite to IEC 62196 Type 2), signaling protocol (CC/CP versus PP/CP), and contact pin arrangement, making them physically and electrically incompatible. GB/T 20234.3 DC couplers use a nine-pin geometric configuration with CAN bus communication via GB/T 27930, which is geometrically and electrically incompatible with CCS2 / IEC 61851-24 DC fast charging. On-the-ground in Laos, a number of existing charging stations — particularly those established to serve Chinese-brand EVs entering via BRI — operate GB/T hardware and serve the Chinese-brand EV fleet.
