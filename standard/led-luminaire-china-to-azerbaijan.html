Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Azerbaijan AZSTAND conformity (AZS standards adopting IEC 60598 / 62560 / 62471), national energy programmes, and importer obligations versus Chinese GB standards and CCC certification. Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Azerbaijan must obtain AZSTAND conformity for regulated lighting products; demonstrate electrical safety against AZS standards adopting IEC 60598-1 (luminaires) and the LED control-gear / module standards; meet EMC requirements via AZS standards adopting CISPR 15 / IEC 61547; provide photobiological-safety documentation where the adopted AZS/IEC 62471 framework applies; satisfy any national energy-programme / efficiency requirement (Azerbaijan operates national energy programmes rather than an EU-style mandatory energy label); and appoint or work through an in-country importer for conformity and market access. Azerbaijan has no horizontal RoHS-style hazardous-substance restriction and is not an EAEU member, so EAEU EAC marking does not apply. The Azerbaijan grid is 220/380 V, 50 Hz — matching China's 220/380 V, 50 Hz nominal — so no nominal voltage/frequency redesign is required. CCC certification and Chinese GB test reports are not automatically accepted; conformity is assessed under the Azerbaijan national regime.
