Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Austria, practical gaps are: confirm the applicable ASI route and project-level approval path via E-Control / APG; validate that IEC 62109-1/-2 safety testing, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 utility-interface evidence, and IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC documentation are accepted for Austrian installations; and prove 230/400 V 50 Hz performance, protection thresholds, and reconnection timing for APG-connected projects. OVE (Österreichischer Verband für Elektrotechnik) electrotechnical rules apply alongside EU directives, and German-language documentation is commonly required. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 documents are useful but not automatically interchangeable with Austrian evidence.
