Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Angola, the practical gaps are not a mature, published EVSE-specific rulebook but the market-access and project gates around IANORQ conformity/import review, IEC-based safety and EMC evidence, IRSEA electricity-sector oversight, ENDE distribution-grid coordination, and connector conversion from China GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 where Angola projects follow the international IEC direction. Angola operates on a 220 / 380 V, 50 Hz grid, close to China's nominal 220 / 380 V, 50 Hz baseline, but project voltage tolerance, earthing, protection, metering, load capacity, and commissioning still require local confirmation. GB/T-only chargers are physically incompatible with IEC 62196 vehicle inlets and need hardware, cable, labeling, and communication-stack changes before they can be treated as Angola-ready.
