Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Malaysia, the confirmed practical gaps are: IEC-based safety evidence required under MS IEC 61851-1 and ST equipment guidelines; connector hardware redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) and CCS2 (DC), which are physically incompatible with GB/T connectors; a Suruhanjaya Tenaga (ST) Certificate of Approval (CoA) for EV charging equipment before installation; SIRIM certification for electrical equipment imports; and OCPP back-office interoperability for networked public chargers. Malaysia operates on 240 V single-phase / 415 V three-phase, 50 Hz — the same nominal grid frequency as China but at a higher single-phase voltage than China's domestic 220 V baseline, requiring input-range confirmation. GB/T 27930 DC communication is incompatible with the IEC 61851-24 / CCS2 communication stack used in Malaysia's IEC-aligned charging ecosystem. Exporters should confirm the exact ST CoA requirement, product scope, and any MyHIJAU or ChargEV network integration requirements at the project stage before shipment.
