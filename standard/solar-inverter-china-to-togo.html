Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Togo, the key practical points are: (1) Togo uses the same 50 Hz 220/380 V nominal grid voltage as China, so the voltage-setting gap is smaller than in 240/415 V markets; (2) this does not make Chinese GB/T evidence automatically acceptable — ARSE authorization, CEET/CEB connection approval, national conformity review, and project-owner or donor/lender specifications remain separate gates; (3) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence, IEC 62116 anti-islanding evidence, IEC 61727 utility-interface evidence, and IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC evidence are the practical evidence package for grid-tied PV inverter projects; (4) Togo's solar push, including Blitta and rural electrification programmes such as CIZO, often sits in donor/lender procurement environments where IEC reports are expected; (5) high ambient temperature, dust, humidity, and derating should be checked for the actual Togo site. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 baselines should be treated as domestic China evidence, not a substitute for Togo project acceptance.
