Short answer

Malawi does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety technical regulation equivalent to a formal type-approval or pre-shipment certification scheme. BESS projects in Malawi operate under a combination of: (1) MBS import conformity — mandatory pre-shipment conformity assessment (PVoC-style Certificate of Conformity) for regulated products; (2) MERA licensing — the Malawi Energy Regulatory Authority must approve all electricity generation and storage projects; (3) ESCOM grid-connection — the Electricity Supply Corporation of Malawi sets technical requirements for grid-connected assets on the 230/400 V 50 Hz Malawi national grid; (4) donor/lender specifications — World Bank, MCC, and other development financiers backing Malawi's power sector typically mandate IEC 62619, IEC 62933-5-2, and UN 38.3 compliance in project technical specifications. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Malawi are: (a) MBS conformity assessment — confirm whether BESS cells, modules, or systems fall under MBS mandatory product list and obtain any required Certificate of Conformity before shipment; (b) IEC 62619 and IEC 62933-5-2 evidence as the internationally expected baseline for project and donor acceptance; (c) MERA project licensing and ESCOM grid-connection approval, with PCS configured for Malawi's 230/400 V 50 Hz grid (versus China's 220/380 V); (d) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory under international transport conventions; and (e) landlocked routing risk — BESS must transit via Mozambique (Nacala or Beira port corridors) or Tanzania (Dar es Salaam corridor), each adding transit-country dangerous-goods compliance requirements. Chinese GB/T 36558-2023, GB/T 34120-2023, and GB 44240-2024 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC 62619 evidence in donor project review or MERA/ESCOM technical evaluation.
