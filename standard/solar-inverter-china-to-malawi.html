Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Malawi, the key practical gaps are: (1) MBS import conformity assessment (a PVoC-style pre-shipment or destination inspection scheme) — Chinese GB/T certificates are not automatically accepted; (2) MERA licensing for the project or supply activity, which may specify IEC-based equipment standards; (3) ESCOM grid-connection conditions for grid-tied inverters, which reference IEC standards (IEC 62109-1/-2, IEC 62116) and Malawi's 230/400 V 50 Hz grid — not the 220/380 V parameters common in China; (4) donor and lender procurement rules (World Bank, MCC, AfDB) for utility-scale or donor-funded projects, which typically require third-party IEC type-test certificates from ILAC-accredited laboratories; and (5) for off-grid and mini-grid solar home systems, VeraSol / IEC TS 62257 quality verification as increasingly required by development-finance buyers. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 baselines do not satisfy Malawi's IEC-referenced requirements and cannot be substituted without supplementary IEC test evidence.
