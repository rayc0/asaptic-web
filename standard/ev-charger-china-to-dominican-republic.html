Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering the Dominican Republic, the practical gaps are 120/240 V, 60 Hz grid input and distributor coordination; INDOCAL conformity and NORDOM / IEC evidence where applicable; SIE and EDENORTE / EDESUR / EDEESTE project coordination for grid-connected installations; connector hardware conversion away from GB/T 20234 to the locally required connector. Because the Dominican Republic is an Americas-style 60 Hz market, J1772 Type 1 AC and CCS1 DC are likely practical expectations, but exporters should confirm the required connector with SIE, the distributor, site owner, or charge-point operator before quoting. GB/T connectors are physically incompatible and GB/T 27930 DC communication is not a substitute for CCS communication or network OCPP requirements.
