Short answer

Filtering respirators entering Pakistan are governed primarily by the Pakistan Standards and Quality Control Authority (PSQCA), which administers Pakistan Standards (PS) and a Conformity Assessment Scheme for items on its mandatory list. Pakistan's respirator standard adopts the EN 149 filtering-facepiece performance benchmark, so a Chinese KN95 (GB 2626) test report does not automatically satisfy the PS/EN 149 specification. Occupational use sits within the provincial labour-law / factories-act framework rather than a single EU-style horizontal PPE regulation. Importation requires an in-country importer of record. Medical / surgical masks follow a separate route through the Drug Regulatory Authority of Pakistan (DRAP), not PSQCA. Pakistan does NOT operate EU-style horizontal regimes such as RoHS, a battery regulation, or an outdoor-noise directive for this product.

Filtering Facepiece Respirator Safety — Pakistan Standard (PS) adopting EN 149 Performance Requirements China's primary standard for non-powered air-purifying particle respirators is GB 2626-2019, with classes KN90 and KN95. It is a mandatory national standard (GB, not GB/T) enforced by SAMR; KN95 requires ≥95% filtration efficiency against NaCl particles at 85 L/min. Key differences from EN 149 (the method Pakistan's PS adopts): GB 2626 uses sodium chloride (NaCl) aerosol only (EN 149 uses both NaCl and paraffin oil), does not require the practical performance / simulated workplace test, and lacks the dolomite clogging resistance test. A Chinese CNAS-accredited GB 2626 test report therefore does not directly satisfy the EN 149-based Pakistan Standard.GB 2626-2019 — Respiratory protective equipment — Non-powered air-purifying particle respirator (mandatory national standard, SAMR)
