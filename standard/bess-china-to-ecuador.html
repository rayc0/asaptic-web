Short answer

Ecuador does not yet have a comprehensive standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to the EU Battery Regulation or Saudi SABER/IEC 62619 route. BESS projects in Ecuador operate under ARCERNNR energy storage authorization requirements, INEN conformity certification for regulated products, and distribution company (CNEL EP, EEQ, etc.) grid-connection technical approval. The critical and non-negotiable technical gap for Chinese BESS exported to Ecuador is the grid frequency and voltage difference: Ecuador operates at 60 Hz and 120/240 V (residential) / 120/208 V or 277/480 V (commercial/industrial three-phase), whereas all Chinese BESS standards — GB/T 36558-2023, GB/T 34120-2023, NB/T 42090-2016 — are based on China's 50 Hz, 220/380 V grid. PCS firmware, protection relay settings, anti-islanding parameters, and ride-through curves configured for China's 50 Hz grid cannot be directly deployed in Ecuador; full reconfiguration and independent retesting are required. Additional practical gaps are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline; (2) IEC 62933 system-level technical requirements; (3) ARCERNNR storage project authorization; (4) distribution company grid-connection technical approval; (5) RTE INEN conformity certificate if BESS falls within a regulated product category; (6) Cuerpo de Bomberos fire-safety permit; and (7) UN 38.3 transport test documentation. Chinese GB standards are the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC-based evidence by INEN, ARCERNNR, or project reviewers.

The PCS (energy storage converter) is assessed under NB/T 42090-2016 (Technical Code for Testing of Energy Storage Converters). Cell and Module Safety — IEC 62619 and INEN RTE Conformity Requirements for Ecuador BESS China's primary mandatory standard for BESS cells from August 2025 is GB 44240-2024 (Secondary Lithium Cells and Batteries Used in Electrical Energy Storage Systems — Safety Requirements), which replaces the prior GB/T 36276 series as the mandatory safety baseline for large-format BESS batteries over 100 kWh.
