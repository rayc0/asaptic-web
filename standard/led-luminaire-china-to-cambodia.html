Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Cambodian market-access requirements administered by the Institute of Standards of Cambodia (ISC) — adopted CS/IEC luminaire safety standards (CS/IEC 60598 series), lamp safety (IEC 62560), photobiological safety (IEC 62471), conformity and import inspection for regulated products, energy programmes, and Telecommunication Regulator of Cambodia (TRC) type approval for wireless smart lighting — versus Chinese GB 30255 / GB 7000 standards and CCC certification. Cambodia operates a 230 V / 50 Hz grid.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Cambodia should expect: product conformity against ISC-adopted Cambodian Standards (CS), which are typically adopted from IEC — luminaire safety to the CS/IEC 60598 series and self-ballasted lamp safety to IEC 62560; conformity assessment and import inspection for regulated products coordinated through the Institute of Standards of Cambodia (ISC) and the in-country importer; photobiological safety assessment to IEC 62471 where applicable; alignment with any applicable Cambodian energy programmes / minimum energy performance initiatives (Cambodia has no EU-style mandatory Ecodesign efficacy regulation or A-G energy label, so the China-export 'ecodesign' lane maps onto these energy programmes rather than a binding efficacy floor); Telecommunication Regulator of Cambodia (TRC) type approval for wireless/smart lighting; and design for the 230 V / 50 Hz grid (50 Hz matches China, but nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V). Cambodia has no horizontal RoHS, REACH-style SVHC, or battery/outdoor-noise regime equivalent to the EU — those obligations are largely absent and are flagged as such. CCC certification and Chinese GB test reports are not automatically recognised; an in-country importer and ISC conformity route are needed.
