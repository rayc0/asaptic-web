Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Zimbabwe, the practical gaps are IEC-based safety, EMC, and connector evidence through SAZ-aligned conformity expectations; hardware conversion from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC because GB/T connectors are physically incompatible; ZERA / ZETDC coordination for grid-connected sites; and confirmation that the charger can operate on Zimbabwe's 230/400 V, 50 Hz supply under local reliability constraints. Zimbabwe's EV charging market is still very nascent, and chronic load-shedding means many viable deployments will need solar-plus-battery or other backup-power design. A blanket Zimbabwe whole-unit mandatory EVSE certification rule could not be confirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters should verify the current SAZ conformity route, HS code, charger type, ZERA requirements, and ZETDC connection path before shipment or installation.
