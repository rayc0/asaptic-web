Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Trinidad and Tobago, the fundamental gaps are grid and connector localisation. Trinidad and Tobago uses a 115/230 V, 60 Hz US-heritage electrical environment, while China domestic chargers are commonly designed around 220/380 V, 50 Hz. Both voltage and frequency differ, so input stages, protection, magnetics, PCS or inverter firmware, metering, thermal ratings, and power-quality behaviour must be revalidated for 60 Hz operation. The applicable Americas connector ecosystem is SAE J1772 for AC charging and CCS1 for DC charging. GB/T 20234 connectors and GB/T 27930 DC communication are physically and protocol incompatible and require hardware and firmware redesign. The EV market is nascent; exporters should confirm TTBS conformity, RIC regulatory expectations, and T&TEC grid-connection requirements for the specific product and site before shipment.
