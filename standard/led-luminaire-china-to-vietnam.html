Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Vietnam must obtain certification of conformity to QCVN 19:2019/BKHCN (the mandatory national technical regulation for lighting equipment EMC and safety) and affix the CR conformity mark via a designated certification body before customs clearance; test and document conformity to the relevant TCVN IEC standards (TCVN IEC 60598 for luminaire safety, TCVN IEC 62560 for self-ballasted LED lamps, TCVN IEC 62471 for photobiological safety); meet MOIT minimum energy performance standards (MEPS) and apply the Vietnam MOIT energy label after MOIT registration; obtain MIC type approval and conformity for any luminaire with integrated radio/wireless functionality (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Zigbee); observe Circular 30/2011/TT-BCT RoHS-like restrictions on hazardous substances in electrical and electronic equipment and the Decision 16/2015/QD-TTg producer take-back obligation for end-of-life products; and act through an in-country importer that bears registration and labelling responsibility — none of which is covered by existing Chinese CCC certification or GB standards alone.

Minimum Energy Performance Standards (MEPS) for LED Lighting — MOIT / TCVN China's equivalent is GB 30255-2019 (Energy efficiency requirements for LED room luminaires).
