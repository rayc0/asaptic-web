Short answer

Romania is a full EU member state: CE marking under RED 2014/53/EU is mandatory, enforced nationally by ANCOM. Radio performance (EN 300 328 / EN 301 893), EMC (EN 301 489), electrical safety (EN IEC 62368-1:2020+A11), and cybersecurity under RED Art. 3.3(d)-(f) mandatory from 1 August 2025 (EN 18031) are the major gaps versus Chinese SRRC and CCC. An EU Authorised Representative, Romanian-language labelling, RoHS 2, and WEEE registration are additional hard requirements with no Chinese equivalent. CCC and SRRC approvals are not recognised in Romania.

All wireless and radio equipment placed on the Romanian market must bear the CE marking, backed by a signed EU Declaration of Conformity (DoC). The DoC must reference all applicable directives and harmonised standards (RED Art. 3.1(a) safety, Art. 3.1(b) EMC, Art. 3.2 radio performance, and Art. 3.3(d)-(f) cybersecurity where in scope). ANCOM (Autoritatea Nationala pentru Administrare si Reglementare in Comunicatii) is Romania's national market surveillance authority for RED; it conducts market checks, can issue withdrawal orders, and reports non-compliant products to the EU RAPEX/Safety Gate system. ANAF (tax authority) and customs enforce import-stage requirements. CE marking must be affixed to the product or its packaging before placement; minimum marking height is 5 mm. SRRC and CCC are not recognised and cannot substitute. Key Romania-specific points beyond the standard EU RED gap: (1) ANCOM actively enforces RED through market surveillance and product withdrawals — import-stage customs checks are supported by ANAF; (2) Romanian-language labelling is mandatory (product name, manufacturer/EU AR name and address, model identifier, intended use where not self-evident); (3) Plug type C/F (Schuko) and 230 V/50 Hz must be confirmed for mains-powered devices; (4) WEEE registration with Romania's national WEEE registry (RoRecycling or equivalent producer compliance scheme) is required before market placement of EEE.
