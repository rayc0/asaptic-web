Short answer

Romania is a full EU member state: CE marking under RED 2014/53/EU is mandatory, enforced nationally by ANCOM. Radio performance (EN 300 328 / EN 301 893), EMC (EN 301 489), electrical safety (EN IEC 62368-1:2020+A11), and cybersecurity under RED Art. 3.3(d)-(f) mandatory from 1 August 2025 (EN 18031) are the major gaps versus Chinese SRRC and CCC. An EU Authorised Representative, Romanian-language labelling, RoHS 2, and WEEE registration are additional hard requirements with no Chinese equivalent. CCC and SRRC approvals are not recognised in Romania.

Chinese manufacturers exporting to Romania do not need to appoint any representative within China for EU regulatory purposes; however, they must appoint an EU-established entity (EU AR or importer) before the product enters any EU member state market.
