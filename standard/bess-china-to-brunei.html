Short answer

Brunei does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation. The storage sector in Brunei is nascent, and published BESS-specific technical codes are limited. Grid-connected BESS installations require approval from the Department of Electrical Services (DES), which is the main electricity authority and grid connection regulator. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Brunei are: (1) DES grid-connection technical approval — no publicly confirmed BESS-specific technical specification exists; direct DES engagement is mandatory before equipment procurement; (2) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline for project acceptance, noting that Chinese GB standards are not accepted as substitutes; (3) tropical climate derating — Brunei's high-temperature, high-humidity environment requires verified thermal management performance beyond Chinese standard conditions; (4) Fire and Rescue Department Brunei fire-safety installation approval, with applicable code reference to be confirmed directly with the authority; (5) UN 38.3 transport documentation for import via Muara Port. Chinese GB/T 36558-2023 and NB/T 42090-2016 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC 62619 or DES technical approval.
