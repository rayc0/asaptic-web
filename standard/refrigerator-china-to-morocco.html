Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face four main Morocco compliance gaps: (1) electrical safety conformity to the Moroccan NM standard adopting IEC 60335-2-24 (NM IEC 60335-2-24), evidenced through IMANOR certification or a conformity attestation — CCC/GB 4706.13 is not automatically accepted; (2) mandatory import conformity assessment for regulated electrical products run by the Ministry of Industry and Trade (attestation/CMIM-type verification of conformity at import), which has no direct Chinese export equivalent; (3) appliance energy-efficiency labelling under Moroccan energy-efficiency rules administered by AMEE/Ministry — distinct from the China Energy Label; and (4) an in-country importer (importateur) of record, since Morocco channels regulated imports through a resident responsible party. R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned (Morocco follows IEC 60335-2-24 flammable-refrigerant charge limits), but Morocco has no EU-style horizontal RoHS / F-Gas / outdoor-noise marking regime — those map to electrical-safety and import-conformity instruments instead.
