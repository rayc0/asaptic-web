Short answer

FFP respirators entering Rwanda are subject to RSB market control: imported consignments must hold a Certificate of Conformity (CoC) issued under the Rwanda Pre-Export Verification of Conformity (PVoC) programme, against the applicable Rwandan Standard (RS) — Rwanda commonly adopts the EN 149 series as RS/EN 149 (FFP1/FFP2/FFP3). Chinese KN95 (GB 2626) test reports do not by themselves satisfy the RS/EN 149 route. A critical boundary: medical/surgical masks are regulated as medical products by the national health regulator (Rwanda FDA), a separate route from PPE respirators. Rwanda is landlocked — goods route via Mombasa (Kenya) or Dar es Salaam (Tanzania).

CRITICAL BOUNDARY: PPE Respirator vs. Medical Mask — Which Rwandan Regulator Applies (RSB vs. Rwanda FDA)? China uses a three-track classification system for respiratory masks: (1) INDUSTRIAL / NON-MEDICAL: GB 2626-2019 (KN90/KN95) — particle respirators for occupational use, mandatory CCC, administered by SAMR; (2) MEDICAL-PROTECTIVE: GB 19083-2010 (≥95% filtration, medical-grade) — for highly infectious environments, administered by NMPA as a medical device; (3) SURGICAL MASK: YY 0469-2011 (Medical surgical mask) — for surgical use, NMPA medical device. This three-track system maps onto Rwanda two-regulator split: GB 2626 industrial respirators map to the RSB / RS/EN 149 PPE route, while GB 19083 and YY 0469 medical/surgical masks map to the Rwanda FDA health-product route.
