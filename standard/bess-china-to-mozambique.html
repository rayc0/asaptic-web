Short answer

Mozambique does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation. INNOQ (Instituto Nacional de Normalização e Qualidade) is the national standards body and adopts IEC standards for conformity assessment and import certification; however, specific BESS conformity certification routes through INNOQ have not been confirmed from official sources as of the dataset date. ARENE (Autoridade Reguladora de Energia) is the energy regulator and EDM (Electricidade de Moçambique) is the national utility responsible for grid connection approvals. Mozambique's BESS market is primarily driven by donor-funded solar-plus-storage projects (FUNAE mini-grids, World Bank, AfDB), IPP utility-scale projects, and C&I demand from mining and Cabo Delgado LNG sectors. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Mozambique are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline in donor and project-owner specifications — Chinese GB/T standards are not accepted as substitutes; (2) INNOQ conformity and import certification — the specific route must be confirmed directly with INNOQ; (3) ARENE energy licence and project approval; (4) EDM grid-connection approval and any applicable IEC 62933 system-level evidence; (5) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory under international conventions for shipments through Mozambican ports (Maputo, Beira, Nacala); and (6) 50 Hz grid configuration — while Mozambique's nominal grid voltage (220/380 V, 50 Hz) is the same as China, project-specific voltage and protection settings must be confirmed with EDM. Note: Mozambique BESS regulation is at an early and thin stage; multiple requirements listed above are emerging, project-specific, or unconfirmed.
