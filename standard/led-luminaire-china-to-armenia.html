Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Armenia's EAEU / Customs Union requirements: the EAC conformity mark under TR CU 004/2011 (Low Voltage), TR CU 020/2011 (EMC), and TR EAEU 037/2016 (restriction of hazardous substances in EEE, RoHS-style), on a GOST/IEC 60598 / 62560 / 62471 basis, versus Chinese GB standards and CCC certification.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Armenia must place the product under the EAEU / Customs Union framework with the EAC conformity mark: electrical safety under TR CU 004/2011 (Low Voltage) on a GOST IEC 60598 / 60598-1 basis; electromagnetic compatibility under TR CU 020/2011 (EMC) on a GOST CISPR 15 basis; and restriction of hazardous substances under TR EAEU 037/2016 (RoHS-style, EAC-marked, with 10 restricted substances broadly mirroring EU RoHS). Conformity is demonstrated via a certificate or declaration of conformity issued through an accredited EAEU body, with a registered in-country importer; SARM (Armenia's standards body) administers the GOST/IEC standards base. Armenia's grid is 220/380 V, 50 Hz — a genuine match to China's 220/380 V 50 Hz nominal, so no voltage/frequency redesign is needed. None of these EAC obligations are covered by existing Chinese CCC certification, and the four EU-added RoHS phthalates fall in scope under TR EAEU 037/2016 even though they are not yet in China's GB/T 26572 mandatory list.
