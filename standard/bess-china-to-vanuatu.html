Short answer

Vanuatu does not yet have a comprehensive mandatory BESS product safety regulation framework. The country has a limited national standards body infrastructure, and several regulatory requirements for BESS remain unconfirmed from publicly accessible official sources — these gaps are flagged honestly throughout this dataset. BESS projects in Vanuatu operate primarily under project-specific international standards referenced in URA licences, UNELCO-VUI connection agreements, and development-finance institution (ADB, World Bank, IRENA) procurement requirements. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Vanuatu are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline for project acceptance; (2) IEC 62933 and IEC 63056 system-level and stationary-use technical requirements where referenced in utility or project-owner specifications; (3) Vanuatu Fire Service (VFS) fire-safety installation approval, with NFPA 855 as the best-available reference standard — formal VFS adoption of NFPA 855 for BESS has not been confirmed; (4) cyclone-resilience structural hardening for Category 5 wind loads — a critical Vanuatu-specific requirement not addressed by Chinese domestic BESS certification; (5) tropical environmental certification (salt-mist, high humidity per IEC 60068) given Vanuatu's Pacific SIDS coastal conditions; (6) UN 38.3 and IMDG Code transport compliance for sea freight via Port Vila, with attention to long-duration Pacific sea voyage conditions; and (7) BESS PCS re-parameterisation for Vanuatu's 230/400 V 50 Hz grid — voltage differs from China's 220/380 V despite shared 50 Hz frequency. Chinese GB/T 36276-2023, GB 44240-2024, and GB/T 36558-2023 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC evidence in project or authority review.
