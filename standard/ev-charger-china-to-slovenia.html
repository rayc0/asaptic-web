Short answer

For Slovenia-bound EV chargers, the most critical gaps are Type 2/CCS2 connector alignment under IEC 62196, compatibility with AFIR obligations as implemented through member-state practice, CE conformity evidence under LVD/EMC/RED where in scope, RCD/RDC protection, and Slovenia/ELES/Agen-E installation and grid-operator coordination. 230/400 V 50 Hz parameters are required for project readiness and Port Koper is the primary entry port. Slovenian-language documentation is commonly expected.
