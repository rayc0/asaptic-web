Short answer

Iraq's primary conformity gate for imported products is COSQC (Central Organization for Standardization and Quality Control), which issues Iraqi Standards (IQS) and operates a pre-shipment conformity inspection regime. Iraq does not have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to a full IEC 62619 mandatory certification scheme; however, COSQC conformity inspection and IEC-based standards are the practical gates for market entry. The Ministry of Electricity (MoE) governs grid connection and power project approvals. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Iraq are: (1) COSQC conformity inspection and certificate — the pre-shipment gate that requires products to align with applicable IQS or accepted IEC standards; (2) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline where project lenders or MoE specifications reference IEC standards; (3) IEC 62933 system-level technical requirements referenced in utility and project-owner specifications, particularly for TotalEnergies, PowerChina, and Masdar solar-plus-storage projects; (4) Iraqi Civil Defence Directorate fire-safety installation approval for BESS facilities; (5) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory under international transport conventions; and (6) grid voltage adaptation — Iraq operates at 230/400 V, 50 Hz versus China's 220/380 V, requiring PCS re-parameterisation. Chinese GB/T 36558-2023, GB/T 34120-2023, and NB/T 42090-2016 are the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC evidence in COSQC inspection, project, or MoE review.
