Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Sao Tome and Principe, the key practical gaps are: (1) no confirmed strong national standards body route for inverter product certification, so acceptance is normally project-, utility-, customs-, or donor/lender-led; (2) EMAE, Empresa de Agua e Electricidade, should be treated as the practical grid-interface gate for connected projects; (3) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 utility-interface, IEC 62920 EMC, and IEC 61000 evidence should be prepared because China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 are not automatically accepted; (4) firmware must be validated for Sao Tome and Principe's 50 Hz, 230/400 V grid, which shares China's 50 Hz frequency but differs from China's 220/380 V nominal voltage; and (5) coastal salt-mist, high humidity, storage integration, Portuguese-language documentation, and sea-freight-only logistics should be addressed for donor-backed island solar projects.
