Short answer

For PV inverters exported from China to Timor-Leste, the practical gates are: (1) domestic ANE/EDTL interconnection review where project-specific scope documents reference ANE and utility interfaces; (2) IEC 62109-1 / IEC 62109-2 evidence for baseline inverter safety; (3) IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 utility-interface evidence; (4) IEC 62920 and IEC 61000 EMC and power-quality evidence; and (5) confirmation of project-specific protection, commissioning, and communication assumptions for 220/380 V 50 Hz operation. Timor-Leste and China are nominally aligned on 220/380 V 50 Hz. China GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 are useful engineering baselines, but they are not sufficient by themselves for Timor-Leste project acceptance.
