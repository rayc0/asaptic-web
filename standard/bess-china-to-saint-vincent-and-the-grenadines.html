Short answer

Saint Vincent and the Grenadines does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation. BESS projects in Saint Vincent and the Grenadines operate primarily under IEC international standards referenced in VINLEC interconnection agreements, energy-portfolio ministry oversight, and NFPA-referenced fire-safety requirements. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Saint Vincent and the Grenadines are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline for VINLEC project acceptance — Chinese GB 44240-2024 alone is not sufficient; (2) IEC 63056 system-level battery safety requirements where referenced in project specifications; (3) VINLEC grid-connection approval and energy-portfolio ministry oversight including re-parameterisation and retesting of PCS for Saint Vincent and the Grenadines 230/400 V 50 Hz grid — note that although China and Saint Vincent and the Grenadines both use 50 Hz, the voltage differs (China is 220/380 V); (4) fire-safety installation approval from the Saint Vincent and the Grenadines Building Authority under an NFPA 855-aligned framework, combined with hurricane wind-load and tropical salt-mist corrosion design requirements; (5) UN 38.3 transport test documentation and IEC 62281 compliance mandatory for sea freight via Kingstown under the IMDG Code; and (6) tropical maritime environment derating for salt mist, high humidity, and sustained high ambient temperature. Chinese GB 44240-2024 and GB/T 36276-2023 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC evidence in VINLEC, ministry, or project-owner review.

The UN 38.3 Test Summary (required since January 1, 2020) must cover the specific cell or battery type being shipped. A Chinese-origin UN 38.3 test summary from an accredited laboratory is acceptable for Saint Vincent and the Grenadines-bound imports under the IMDG Code — the key gap is ensuring the test summary covers the specific cell model, chemistry, capacity, and configuration of the BESS units being shipped, and that it is maintained current with any cell design changes.
