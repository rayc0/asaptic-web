Short answer

CAM type approval (mandatory for all radio and telecom terminal equipment under Telecommunications Act 2/2016), MNSO-aligned EMC compliance, 230 V / 50 Hz Type D/G electrical safety, designation of a local Maldivian authorised importer/dealer, and awareness of Electronic Transactions Act / Data Protection Act cybersecurity obligations are the major Maldives compliance gaps compared with Chinese MIIT/SRRC type approval, CCC, and GB-standard documentation. CE and FCC reports may be submitted as supporting evidence but do not substitute for CAM type approval.

Local Authorised Importer / Dealer Requirement China does not require a local Maldivian importer for export. Chinese exporters typically work through trading companies or distributors. China Customs regulations on export		 A local Maldivian authorised importer or dealer is required to import and sell radio and telecom terminal equipment in the Maldives under the CAM type approval framework. The importer/dealer must be a legally registered entity in the Maldives and is responsible for ensuring the product holds a valid CAM type approval certificate, maintaining records, and handling post-sale compliance obligations. Chinese exporters without a local Maldivian entity must appoint an authorised local representative before market entry.
