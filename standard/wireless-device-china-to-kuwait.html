Short answer

The main Kuwait gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular, or IoT wireless device are: (1) CITRA mandatory type approval under the Kuwait Telecommunications Law — the CITRA type approval certificate and certificate number must be obtained before import or sale; (2) EMC testing to KSO/ETSI EN 301 489 series standards submitted as part of the CITRA type approval application; (3) electrical safety to IEC 62368-1 (240 V / 50 Hz / Type G British plug) — Kuwait's voltage and plug standard differs from China's 220 V / Type A/I; (4) appointment of a local Kuwait authorized agent or dealer — MOCI import clearance requires a registered local entity; (5) Arabic and English dual-language labelling on device and packaging; and (6) awareness of CITRA/CSA IoT cybersecurity advisory guidelines (currently advisory, not mandatory product certification). Chinese SRRC approval, CCC certification, and FCC/CE marks are not recognized by CITRA as substitutes for Kuwait type approval. The GCC Mutual Recognition Arrangement (GCC MRA) exists but CITRA enforces national type approval in practice.
