Short answer

The main Kuwait gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular, or IoT wireless device are: (1) CITRA mandatory type approval — the CITRA approval number must appear on the device label and packaging before customs clearance or sale; (2) EMC testing to GSO-adopted standards (aligned to ETSI EN 301 489 series) submitted as part of the CITRA type approval application; (3) GCC/GSO GMARK product conformity requirements for regulated consumer electronics categories; (4) electrical safety to IEC 62368-1 at 240 V / 50 Hz / Type G (BS 1363) plug — Kuwait uses British-style 3-pin plugs, unlike most Gulf states which have migrated to Type C/F; (5) a locally registered Kuwaiti importer or authorized distributor (must be a Kuwaiti company or 51% Kuwaiti-owned entity under the Commercial Companies Law); and (6) awareness of Kuwait cybercrime law (Law 20/2014) applicable to connected products handling user data. Chinese SRRC approval and CCC certification are not recognized by CITRA. The GCC Mutual Recognition Arrangement (GCC MRA) may enable a CITRA certificate to be recognized by TRA Bahrain, TRA UAE, MCIT Qatar, and other GCC members under bilateral arrangements, supporting dual-market strategies.
