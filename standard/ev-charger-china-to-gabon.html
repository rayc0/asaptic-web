Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Gabon, the practical gaps are AGANOR conformity review, ARSEE and SEEG coordination for any grid-connected installation, IEC-family safety and EMC evidence, connector redesign from China GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC, French documentation, and thermal/corrosion derating for Gabon's hot, humid equatorial environment. Gabon's grid is commonly referenced as 220/380 V, 50 Hz, close to China's 220/380 V, 50 Hz baseline, but site voltage quality, protection coordination, earthing, load capacity, and commissioning still need SEEG confirmation. A published Gabon EVSE-specific mandatory certification scheme could not be confirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14. The EV market and public charging infrastructure appear extremely nascent, so exporters should treat opportunities as project-specific pilots rather than a mature commercial rollout.
