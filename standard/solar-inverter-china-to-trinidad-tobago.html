Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Trinidad and Tobago, the key practical gap is fundamental grid mismatch: Trinidad and Tobago is a 60 Hz, US-heritage NEMA-market system with common 115/230 V service, while China evidence is normally built around 50 Hz / 220-380 V operation. This means Chinese GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence is not automatically accepted. Exporters should plan for 60 Hz anti-islanding and ride-through validation, firmware and protection setting documentation for T&TEC conditions, IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence, IEC 62920 EMC evidence, and likely IEEE 1547 / UL 1741 interconnection direction rather than a 50 Hz Europe-style EN 50549 or VDE framing.
