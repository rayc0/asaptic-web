Short answer

Equatorial Guinea has no strong national standards body and no confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety or conformity regime as of the dataset date. The electricity utility is SEGESA (Sociedad de Electricidad de Guinea Ecuatorial). The grid operates at 230/400 V, 50 Hz — sharing frequency with China but differing in voltage (China is 220/380 V). The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Equatorial Guinea are: (1) absence of a published national conformity assessment framework — the lack of a clear conformity regime is itself a primary market-access risk requiring direct SEGESA engagement; (2) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline for project and financier acceptance in the absence of a national standard; (3) IEC 62933 system-level technical requirements where referenced in SEGESA or project-owner specifications; (4) NFPA 855 or equivalent fire-safety installation design, expected by international project developers operating in the market; (5) UN 38.3 and IEC 62281 transport test documentation mandatory under international transport conventions; and (6) voltage re-parameterisation — PCS configured for China's 220/380 V must be reconfigured for Equatorial Guinea's 230/400 V at 50 Hz. Chinese GB/T 36276, GB/T 34131, GB 38031, and GB/T 36558 are not accepted as substitutes for IEC evidence in project or financier review. Tropical humidity derating applies and should be reflected in equipment specifications. All goods enter via Malabo or Bata ports.
