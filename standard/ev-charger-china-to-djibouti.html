Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Djibouti, the practical gaps are grid voltage, connector ecosystem, IEC evidence, and project acceptance. Djibouti is a 230/400 V, 50 Hz market served by Electricite de Djibouti, while China domestic chargers are commonly validated around 220/380 V, 50 Hz; frequency aligns, but voltage differs and protection thresholds, metering, leakage devices, power electronics, and thermal derating must be checked for the Djibouti site. Djibouti has limited publicly accessible EVSE-specific national standards, so exporters should not assume a China GB/T file is accepted. French/IEC legacy and regional vehicle imports point to IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC, not Americas J1772/CCS1 and not China GB/T 20234. Extreme desert heat makes derating and enclosure evidence commercially important.
