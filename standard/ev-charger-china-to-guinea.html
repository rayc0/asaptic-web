Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Guinea, the practical question is less a mature EVSE certification pathway and more project feasibility: Guinea's EV market appears extremely limited, while grid deficits, low electrification, mining-load priorities, humidity, dust, and site power quality make public charging difficult without EDG coordination and project-specific engineering. Guinea uses a 220 / 380 V, 50 Hz electricity context, broadly closer to China's 220 / 380 V, 50 Hz baseline than Qatar, but exporters still need input-range, protection, earthing, surge, EMC, and derating evidence for the actual site. The likely connector direction for any IEC / Francophone tender is IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC; China GB/T 20234 connectors and GB/T 27930 DC communication are physically and protocol-incompatible and require hardware and firmware redesign. A Guinea-wide mandatory EV charger certification rule could not be confirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14; verify the route with INGM, EDG, customs, the importer, and the project owner before shipment.
