Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to the Philippines, the main gaps are not a single whole-unit BPS mark by default. Exporters should confirm whether the exact inverter model or bundled electrical components are in BPS mandatory scope, prepare IEC 62109 / PNS evidence where requested, satisfy ERC / DOE net-metering and distributed-generation documentation, re-validate grid settings for the Philippine 60 Hz system, and show EMC and China GB/T baseline evidence as supporting technical documentation.

60 Hz Grid Re-Validation for Philippine Deployment China domestic PV inverter tests are typically built around 50 Hz grid operation. A China market certificate, datasheet, or GB/T declaration may not show that the same hardware, firmware, relays, magnetics, thermal design, and grid-profile lock have been validated for 60 Hz Philippine settings.GB/T 19964

Philippine Distribution Code Grid-Connection Requirements China grid-code documentation commonly references GB/T 19964 and GB/T 37408 for photovoltaic generation and inverter behavior. These are useful engineering baselines, but the Philippine distribution utility will evaluate the actual project against local distribution-code settings and procedures.GB/T 19964
GB/T 37408		 Solar PV inverter installations connected to a Philippine distribution system must satisfy the distribution utility's technical review under the Philippine Distribution Code framework. For inverter suppliers, this means documenting protection functions, voltage and frequency operating windows, disconnection behavior, grounding interface, harmonic contribution, power quality, and commissioning checks in a format usable by the utility or project engineer.Philippine Distribution Code
Distribution utility interconnection requirements		 The key gap is settings translation. Exporters should provide editable grid-code parameter sheets, test evidence for voltage / frequency ride-through or trip behavior where relevant, harmonic and flicker evidence, anti-islanding certificates, and commissioning instructions aligned to the Philippine utility's requested format.[INFORMATIONAL] Distribution Code acceptance is site and utility specific. China GB/T grid documents are useful inputs, but the inverter settings package must be reworked for the Philippine distribution utility review. ERC / DOE Net-Metering and Distributed Renewable Energy Connection China grid-connected PV inverter documentation commonly includes GB/T grid-support and anti-islanding evidence, Chinese nameplate data, and 50 Hz grid settings. That documentation helps technically, but it must be translated into the Philippine project context and cannot replace the customer-side net-metering agreement, distribution-utility approval, or ERC / DOE documentation route.GB/T 19964
GB/T 37408		 The Renewable Energy Act establishes net-metering for qualified end-users and requires distribution utilities to enter into net-metering agreements with qualified customers under rules issued by the Energy Regulatory Commission. For inverter exports, the compliance issue is not only product safety: the project package must support the customer's ERC / DOE / distribution-utility interconnection review, metering arrangement, anti-islanding evidence, protection settings, single-line diagram, and commissioning records.Republic Act No. 9513, Renewable Energy Act of 2008
ERC net-metering rules for qualified end-users
DOE renewable energy net-metering implementation guidance		 The exporter should provide Philippine-ready grid-interconnection documents, not just a factory test report. Typical gaps include local 60 Hz settings, anti-islanding trip data, export-limit functions where requested, wiring and protection drawings, utility application data, and commissioning evidence acceptable to the distribution utility and the qualified end-user's net-metering process.[INFORMATIONAL] Philippine net-metering is a project and customer-utility pathway. A China factory certificate does not close ERC / DOE / distribution-utility interconnection review unless the inverter evidence is packaged for the Philippine net-metering file.
