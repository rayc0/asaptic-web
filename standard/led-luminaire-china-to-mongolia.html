Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Mongolia generally works through a registered in-country importer and obtains MASM (Mongolian Agency for Standardization and Metrology) conformity certification for regulated products against Mongolian national standards (MNS), many of which adopt IEC standards such as MNS/IEC 60598 (luminaire safety), MNS/IEC 62560 (self-ballasted LED lamps), and MNS/IEC 62471 (photobiological safety). Mongolia's grid is 220/380 V, 50 Hz — the same nominal voltage and frequency as China, so no electrical re-design is needed for voltage compatibility. Energy efficiency may be addressed through Mongolian energy-efficiency programmes rather than a single binding minimum-efficacy regulation; verify current programme scope. There is no horizontal RoHS-type hazardous-substance restriction equivalent to the EU in Mongolia (and Mongolia is NOT an EAEU member, so EAEU TR rules do not apply); China's GB/T 26572 and China RoHS 2 disclosure labelling are the manufacturer's existing baseline. Wireless/smart luminaires require radio type approval from the Communications Regulatory Commission (CRC). CCC certification obtained for China is not recognised for Mongolian market access — separate MASM conformity assessment applies. Confirm the exact certified-product list, certification route, and importer obligations with MASM before shipment.
