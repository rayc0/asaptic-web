Short answer

Belize does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to a national certification scheme. BESS projects in Belize operate under PUC interconnection approval and BEL connection agreements, with project-level technical requirements driven by international development finance conditions (IDB, CDB, IFC) and North American/Americas engineering standards. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Belize are: (1) a fundamental 60 Hz vs 50 Hz frequency mismatch AND a 110/220 V split-phase vs 220/380 V three-phase voltage mismatch — both require PCS hardware re-engineering and full revalidation, not just software reconfiguration; (2) UL 1973 cell and module safety evidence (or IEC 62619 in internationally financed projects) as the technically expected baseline for project acceptance in the Americas/Caribbean direction — Chinese GB 44240-2024 and GB/T 36276-2023 are not accepted as substitutes; (3) PUC interconnection approval and BEL connection agreement — IEEE 1547-2018 is the applicable interconnection standard direction, not Chinese GB/T; (4) BNFS fire-safety installation approval, with NFPA 855 as the expected fire code and UL 9540A fire propagation test data increasingly required; (5) UN 38.3 transport test documentation with Belize-specific tropical packaging requirements for Port of Belize City's high-humidity coastal environment; and (6) BBS conformity assessment — verify current BBS mandatory scope with BBS directly before shipment.
