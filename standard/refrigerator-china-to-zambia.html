Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers exporting to Zambia face several compliance gaps: (1) mandatory ZABS conformity assessment and import inspection — products must meet Zambian standards (ZS, which adopt IEC) and clear ZABS verification before customs release, and the ZABS mark applies to regulated products; (2) electrical safety to ZS/IEC 60335-2-24 — Chinese CCC/GB 4706.13 certification is not automatically accepted, though IEC-based CB Scheme reports ease acceptance because Zambian standards adopt IEC; (3) energy-efficiency labelling/MEPS obligations distinct from the China Energy Label; (4) an in-country importer or representative is needed for customs and conformity; (5) for smart/connected models, ZICTA type approval of the radio module. R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned. Note: Zambia uses 230 V / 50 Hz (same 50 Hz as China; nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V) and is landlocked, with imports routed via Dar es Salaam or Durban. Zambia does not operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS, battery, or outdoor-noise regime.
