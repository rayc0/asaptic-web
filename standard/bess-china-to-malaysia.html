Short answer

Malaysia does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to the EU Battery Regulation or Saudi SABER/IEC 62619 route. BESS projects in Malaysia operate under a layered framework: (1) Suruhanjaya Tenaga (ST) licensing under the Electricity Supply Act 1990 and ST grid-code requirements for grid-connected systems; (2) SIRIM product certification for electrical equipment, which may require IEC 62619 or IEC 62133 compliance evidence; (3) Jabatan Bomba dan Penyelamat Malaysia (Bomba) fire-safety installation approval, which is a mandatory project gate referencing UBBL 1984 and potentially NFPA codes; (4) Tenaga Nasional Berhad (TNB) grid-connection technical specifications for distribution-connected assets; and (5) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory under international transport conventions. China's GB 44240-2024, GB/T 36276-2023, and GB/T 36558-2023 represent the manufacturing and grid-connection baselines in China but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC-based evidence in Malaysia project or authority review. Malaysia utility-scale BESS is emerging — TNB has conducted BESS tenders, but project-specific ST, TNB, SIRIM, and Bomba requirements must be confirmed directly for each project.
