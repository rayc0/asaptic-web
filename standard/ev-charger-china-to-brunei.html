Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Brunei, the confirmed practical gaps are connector redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) and CCS2 (DC), since Brunei follows the IEC 62196 ecosystem and GB/T connectors are physically incompatible; IEC 61851-based safety evidence accepted by DES (Department of Electrical Services, Ministry of Development), as no specific published Brunei EV-charger code has been confirmed as of 2026-06; IP54 minimum enclosure protection for outdoor units in Brunei's tropical climate (27–32°C, high humidity year-round); OCPP back-office interoperability, as Brunei has no confirmed mandatory protocol but IEC / OCPP-aligned infrastructure is the expected path; and DES electrical installation coordination and approval for grid-connected units. Brunei operates on 230/400 V 50 Hz, close to China's 220/380 V 50 Hz, so voltage-range confirmation is straightforward but still required. No confirmed EV-charger-specific mandatory conformity marking scheme under NSC has been found as of 2026-06-14; exporters should coordinate directly with DES and the local importer before shipment. The ASEAN AEMTS (ASEAN EV MRA Technical Specifications) framework is under development and is not yet binding.
