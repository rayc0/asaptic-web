Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Cambodia, the confirmed practical gaps are: connector hardware redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) and CCS2 (DC), since Cambodia's nascent IEC-aligned infrastructure is physically incompatible with GB/T connectors; IEC 61851-1 / IEC 61851-23 safety evidence required or anticipated by EAC (Electricity Authority of Cambodia) coordination and project owners, as ISC adopts IEC standards; EDC grid-connection approval for installations connected to Electricité du Cambodge supply; IEC 61000-series EMC evidence; and OCPP-based back-office interoperability for networked public chargers. Cambodia operates on 230/400 V, 50 Hz — closely aligned with China's 220/380 V, 50 Hz baseline, but input-voltage range and thermal design for tropical ambient conditions should be confirmed. The Cambodia EV market is nascent and centred on Phnom Penh; government EV roadmap initiatives are progressing but detailed mandatory conformity assessment routes for EVSE had not been confirmed from single official published regulations as of 2026-06-14. Exporters must verify current ISC conformity requirements, EAC coordination procedures, and EDC grid-connection rules with qualified professionals before shipment.
