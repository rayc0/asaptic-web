IRA Section 45V Clean Hydrogen Tax Credit and Buy America Requirements (Commercial Demand Drivers) China does not have a direct equivalent to the IRA 45V tax credit. Pressure Vessel Design and Certification China regulates pressure vessels under the TSG (Technical Supervision Guideline) regime administered by SAMR (State Administration for Market Regulation). The primary standard is GB 150 (Pressure Vessels), covering design, fabrication, inspection, and testing. Manufacturers must hold a special-equipment manufacturing license issued by a provincial-level market supervision authority. Inspection is carried out by SAMR-designated special-equipment inspection institutes. Chinese TSG certificates and GB 150 conformance are not recognized by US Authorities Having Jurisdiction (AHJs).TSG 21-2016 (Special Equipment Safety Technical Regulation for Pressure Vessels)

SAMR special-equipment manufacturing license		 Pressure vessels (electrolytic cells, separators, storage vessels) must be designed, fabricated, and inspected per ASME Boiler and Pressure Vessel Code (BPVC) Section VIII, Division 1 or 2. Vessels must bear the ASME 'U' stamp (or 'UM' for miniature) issued by an ASME-authorized inspection agency (AIA). The stamp certifies conformance to ASME BPVC and is required by most US state and local jurisdictions as a condition of installation and operation.ASME BPVC Section VIII, Division 1 (Pressure Vessels)

ASME U / UM Stamp (Authorized Inspection Agency certification)		 A hard regulatory gate: the ASME 'U' stamp is a US-specific certification issued only by ASME-accredited manufacturers using ASME-authorized inspectors. Chinese manufacturers holding TSG licenses and GB 150 certification must separately pursue ASME accreditation, re-engineer documentation to ASME BPVC requirements, and engage a US-recognized AIA for third-party inspection of every vessel. There is no mutual recognition or equivalency pathway between TSG/GB 150 and ASME BPVC. This typically requires 6-18 months and significant engineering rework for first-time exporters.[INFORMATIONAL] Chinese-manufactured pressure vessels bearing only TSG/GB 150 certification cannot legally be installed in most US jurisdictions. Exporters must obtain ASME BPVC Section VIII accreditation and the 'U' stamp before shipment. This is a design, manufacturing, and inspection process change — not a paperwork re-label. Hydrogen Safety: Installation, Ventilation, and Gas Detection China regulates hydrogen safety for electrolyzers primarily through GB/T 29729 (Safety Requirements for Hydrogen Systems), GB 50516 (Code for Design of Hydrogen Fueling Stations, referenced for production-side safety), and GB 4962 (Safe Use of Hydrogen). The Ministry of Emergency Management (MEM) oversees hazardous chemical safety; hydrogen is classified as a hazardous chemical under the Regulations on Safety Management of Hazardous Chemicals. Electrical equipment in explosive atmospheres must comply with the GB 3836 series. Regulations on Safety Management of Hazardous Chemicals (State Council Order No. 591)		 NFPA 2 (Hydrogen Technologies Code) is the primary US installation code for gaseous and liquid hydrogen systems including electrolyzers. It governs site selection, separation distances, ventilation, leak detection, emergency shutoff, and signage. NFPA 55 (Compressed Gases and Cryogenic Fluids Code) applies to storage and handling of compressed hydrogen. Both codes are adopted by reference in many US state and local fire codes. Electrical equipment in hydrogen-classified areas must additionally comply with NFPA 70 (NEC) Articles 500/501 for classified locations or Article 505 for Zone classification. NRTL listing (UL, CSA, Intertek, or equivalent)		 NFPA 2 and NFPA 55 are US-specific codes with no mutual recognition pathway to Chinese GB standards. NRTL listing for electrical components (hazardous-location equipment, power electronics, controls) is a US-specific market-access gate: Chinese CCC, CE, or CB certificates are not accepted as substitutes by US AHJs or insurers. Exporters must submit representative electrical components to a USDOL-recognized NRTL for testing and listing. Additionally, NFPA 2 contains prescriptive requirements (e.g., separation distances, ventilation rates, detection setpoints) that may require hardware redesign beyond what GB/T 29729 mandates. OSHA 29 CFR 1910.103 adds federal workplace safety requirements independent of local code adoption.[INFORMATIONAL] Chinese electrolyzer manufacturers exporting to the US face a mandatory dual-track compliance requirement: (1) ASME 'U' stamp for all pressure-bearing vessels, and (2) NFPA 2/55-compliant installation design plus NRTL listing for all electrical components in hydrogen-classified areas. Neither Chinese TSG/GB 150 pressure certification nor Chinese CCC electrical certification satisfies these US requirements.
