Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Georgia, the key practical gaps are: (1) GEOSTM conformity assessment — Georgia adopts IEC/EN standards under its DCFTA EU-alignment trajectory; Chinese GB/T documents are not automatically accepted; (2) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence from an ILAC-accredited laboratory, as these are the applicable safety standards under GEOSTM/IEC alignment; (3) IEC 62116 anti-islanding compliance for GNERC-regulated grid connection; (4) IEC 62920 / IEC 61000-series EMC evidence; and (5) firmware validation against Georgia's 220/380 V, 50 Hz grid (CIS legacy parameters). Georgia is NOT an EAEU member — the EAC/TR CU certification route does not apply. The applicable route is IEC/EN conformity recognized by GEOSTM, plus GNERC and GSE/distribution operator grid-connection approval. Chinese GB/T 37408 and GB/T 19964 baselines do not satisfy Georgia's IEC-aligned conformity requirements and cannot be substituted without supplementary IEC test evidence.
