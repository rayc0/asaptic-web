Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Singapore, the confirmed practical gaps are: (1) connector hardware — GB/T 20234 connectors are physically incompatible with Singapore's IEC 62196 Type 2 AC and CCS2 DC infrastructure, requiring hardware redesign; (2) safety standard evidence — Singapore references TR 25 / SS EN IEC 61851 and requires IEC-accredited test reports, not GB/T 18487 reports alone; (3) electrical installation — all EVSE must be installed by a Licensed Electrical Worker (LEW) under EMA licensing rules; (4) LTA EV Common Charger Grant registration — chargers installed under LTA's programme must meet LTA's technical specifications; (5) Enterprise Singapore CPSR — consumer-facing charging equipment may be subject to Consumer Protection (Safety Requirements) registration; (6) EMC under IEC 61000 — Singapore's grid is 230/400 V 50 Hz, same frequency as China's domestic 50 Hz but different voltage from China's 220/380 V baseline, requiring voltage-range and EMC confirmation. A blanket mandatory whole-unit certification rule for all EV charger types through a single Singapore authority could not be confirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters should verify the current conformity assessment route, LEW installation requirement, LTA grant eligibility, and CPSR scope before shipment.
