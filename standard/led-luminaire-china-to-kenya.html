Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Kenya must route the consignment through the KEBS Pre-Export Verification of Conformity (PVoC) programme to obtain a Certificate of Conformity (CoC) before shipment; comply with the relevant Kenya Standards, which adopt the IEC series (KS IEC 60598 for luminaire safety, KS IEC 62560 / 62471 for lamps and photobiological safety, KS CISPR 15 for EMC emissions); affix the KEBS Standardization Mark / Import Standardization Mark; meet EPRA / KEBS energy efficiency and labelling requirements where the product falls within the regulated lighting category; obtain Communications Authority (CA) type approval for any wireless smart-lighting functionality; and operate through an in-country importer for clearance at the Port of Mombasa. Kenya does not operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS, REACH-SVHC, or energy-labelling regime identical to the EU — substance restriction and energy-efficiency obligations are enforced through KS standards and KEBS / EPRA instruments, not a single horizontal directive — so existing CCC certification and CN GB test reports do not by themselves satisfy the Kenyan PVoC route.
