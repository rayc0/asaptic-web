Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Solomon Islands, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirm the applicable SISO, Solomon Power / SIEA, donor-project, and IEC or AS/NZS-derived requirements for the specific project; (2) provide IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence for the exact inverter model or family; (3) provide IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 utility-interface evidence for grid-tied systems; (4) provide IEC 62920 and IEC 61000-series EMC evidence; (5) validate firmware and protection settings for Solomon Islands' 230/400 V, 50 Hz network. Solomon Islands has the same 50 Hz frequency as China, but the nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V baseline. Humidity, cyclone exposure, salt-mist corrosion, thermal derating, and sea-freight via Honiara should be addressed in the project technical file.
