Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering South Sudan, the practical gaps should be framed honestly as an early-market, project-led compliance question. South Sudan's formal standards capacity through SSNBS is nascent, electricity supply is limited and often diesel or off-grid, and SSEC or the site power owner will be central to any grid or microgrid connection. The technical baseline is 230/400 V at 50 Hz: frequency aligns with China, but nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V, so input range, protection thresholds, metering, thermal design, and derating must be validated. IEC Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC should be treated as the IEC-aligned direction, not Americas J1772 / CCS1. GB/T 20234 and GB/T 27930 China-only charger designs are not connector or protocol substitutes.
