Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Ecuador, the key practical gaps are: (1) a fundamental grid incompatibility — Ecuador's distribution grid operates at 60 Hz and 120/240 V split-phase (LV), whereas Chinese inverters are validated for 50 Hz and 220/380 V; firmware grid-protection settings, anti-islanding thresholds, and reconnection parameters must be fully reconfigured and retested for Ecuador's grid; (2) INEN conformity — NTE INEN technical standards and RTE INEN technical regulations require a certificate of conformity before market entry; (3) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence as required for ARCERNNR and distribution-company grid connection; (4) IEC 62116 anti-islanding compliance validated at 60 Hz; (5) IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC evidence. China GB/T 37408 and GB/T 19964 baselines are 50 Hz-based and do not satisfy Ecuador INEN/ARCERNNR requirements; the 60 Hz frequency difference is a genuine engineering reconfiguration gate, not a paperwork gap.

IEC 62109-1 / IEC 62109-2 Safety Evidence — INEN Conformity and ARCERNNR Grid-Connection Requirements for Ecuador China's primary voluntary standard for PV grid-connected inverter safety is GB/T 37408-2021 (Technical Requirements for Photovoltaic Grid-Connected Inverters), which covers classification, environmental conditions, safety, electrical performance, EMC, and markings. GB/T 37408 is a China domestic standard assessed by CNAS-accredited Chinese laboratories, based on 50 Hz / 220/380 V grid conditions. It shares conceptual safety intent with IEC 62109-1/-2 but is not harmonised with IEC 62109 and is not accepted by INEN or ARCERNNR as equivalent to IEC 62109 type-test evidence.
