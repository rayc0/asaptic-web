Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Ethiopia, the practical gaps are the ECAE PVoC Certificate of Conformity required for regulated imports, model-level safety evidence typically mapped to IEC 62109-1 and IEC 62109-2, Ethiopian Energy Authority and EEU approval for grid-connected projects, Ethiopia 50 Hz grid parameter settings, and the Ethiopia National Transmission and Distribution Grid Codes. China GB/T evidence provides useful technical support but is not a substitute for Ethiopia import clearance or grid approval.

IEC 62109-1 / IEC 62109-2 (where held — supports PVoC application)		 The Ethiopian Conformity Assessment Enterprise (ECAE) administers the Pre-Export Verification of Conformity (PVoC) programme, which requires regulated products to be inspected, tested, and certified in the country of export before shipment to Ethiopia. A Certificate of Conformity (CoC) issued by an ECAE-authorised inspection body is required for customs clearance of in-scope product categories. The programme was expanded in May 2024 under Regulation No. 549/2024 to cover additional product categories beyond the initial solar focus. Stand-alone solar products ≤ 350 W have been covered since February 2022; exporters of grid-connected inverters should verify whether their product category and power rating fall within the current PVoC scope with ECAE or an authorised inspection body before shipment.Ethiopian Conformity Assessment Enterprise establishment and PVoC programme

IEC 62109 Safety Evidence for Solar PV Inverters Chinese PV inverters are commonly certified to NB/T 32004 (inverter technical specification) and carry CGC or TÜV test reports. Where the manufacturer also holds an IEC 62109-1 / IEC 62109-2 report from an accredited body, that report directly supports the Ethiopia PVoC application. NB/T 32004 alone is not treated as equivalent to IEC 62109 by ECAE-authorised inspection bodies.NB/T 32004
IEC 62109-1 / IEC 62109-2 (where held by the Chinese manufacturer)		 IEC 62109-1 (general safety requirements) and IEC 62109-2 (particular requirements for inverters) are the internationally recognised safety standards for solar PV power converters. These standards are referenced by ECAE authorised inspection bodies for PVoC conformity assessment of solar inverter products imported into Ethiopia. Holding a valid third-party test report or certificate to IEC 62109-1 and IEC 62109-2 from an accredited laboratory materially strengthens a PVoC application and is commonly required by project owners and EPC contractors for grid-connected installations.IEC 62109-1:2010 — Safety of power converters for use in photovoltaic power systems — Part 1: General requirements
