Short answer

Dominica does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation. BESS projects in Dominica operate primarily under IEC international standards referenced in DOMLEC interconnection agreements, IRC oversight, and NFPA-referenced fire-safety requirements. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Dominica are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline for DOMLEC project acceptance — Chinese GB 44240-2024 alone is not sufficient; (2) IEC 63056 system-level battery safety requirements where referenced in project specifications; (3) DOMLEC and IRC grid-connection approval including re-parameterisation and retesting of PCS for Dominica's 230/400 V 50 Hz grid — note that although China and Dominica both use 50 Hz, the voltage differs (China is 220/380 V); (4) fire-safety installation approval from the Dominica Building Authority under an NFPA 855-aligned framework, combined with hurricane wind-load and tropical salt-mist corrosion design requirements; (5) UN 38.3 transport test documentation and IEC 62281 compliance mandatory for sea freight via Roseau / Woodbridge Bay under the IMDG Code; and (6) tropical maritime environment derating for salt mist, high humidity, and sustained high ambient temperature. Chinese GB 44240-2024 and GB/T 36276-2023 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC evidence in DOMLEC, IRC, or project-owner review.

The prior voluntary standard GB/T 36276-2023 (Lithium-Ion Batteries for Electrical Energy Storage) provides the technical framework for cells, modules, and battery clusters used in EES. These Chinese standards are not harmonised with IEC 62619 or IEC 63056 and are not accepted as equivalents in Dominica project specifications or DOMLEC/IRC requirements.
