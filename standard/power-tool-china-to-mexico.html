Short answer

For China-made electric or cordless power tools entering Mexico, the mandatory baseline is NOM electrical safety certification under NOM-003-SCFI with the NOM mark issued through an accredited body such as NYCE or ANCE, an in-country importer with a valid RFC, and IFT homologation for cordless tools that contain a radio transmitter; NMX standards aligned with IEC 62841 are commonly used to demonstrate product safety, and there is no EU-style outdoor-noise marking in Mexico.
