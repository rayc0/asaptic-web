Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Ecuador, the confirmed practical gaps are: (1) grid voltage and frequency — Ecuador operates on 120 V single-phase / 60 Hz (not 220–380 V / 50 Hz), requiring hardware input-range redesign and derating confirmation; (2) connector physical incompatibility — GB/T 20234 connectors are physically and electrically incompatible with IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) and CCS2 (DC) couplers, which are the IEC-aligned connectors currently used by private charging networks in Ecuador, though Ecuador has not mandated a single national connector standard as of 2026-06-14; (3) INEN conformity — imported electrical equipment requires an INEN Certificate of Conformity (INEN-1) from an accredited body before customs clearance; whether EV chargers are currently subject to a specific RTE INEN mandatory technical regulation could not be confirmed from official sources and should be verified with INEN directly; (4) ARCERNNR / ARCONEL grid-code compliance — grid-connected installations must satisfy Ecuadorian grid-code requirements (ref. ARCERNNR-002/20); (5) Ecuador's EV charging market is nascent and has experienced serious electricity supply disruptions (rolling blackouts in 2024 due to hydroelectric drought); exporters should evaluate operational risk and local utility coordination with CNEL EP or the relevant distribution company before deployment.
