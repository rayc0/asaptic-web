Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face four major Saudi compliance gaps: (1) electrical safety testing to IEC 60335-2-24 accepted under the SASO/SABER regime — CCC/GB 4706.13 certification is not accepted, and the importer must obtain a Product Certificate of Conformity (PCoC); (2) energy efficiency to SASO 2663 / SASO 2892 (among the world's strictest refrigerator MEPS, designed for high-ambient Gulf conditions) with mandatory SEEC energy label registration — Chinese GB 12021.2 grades do not transfer; (3) per-shipment Shipment Certificate of Conformity (SCoC) issued via the SABER platform before customs clearance at Jeddah / Dammam ports; and (4) a Saudi-resident importer of record who registers the product on SABER — there is no manufacturer self-declaration route. R600a refrigerant is well-positioned but charge documentation against IEC 60335-2-24 Annex AA must be verified for high-ambient (T-class) operation.
