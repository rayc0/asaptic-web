Short answer

KEBS mandatory Pre-Export Verification of Conformity (PVOC) at origin is the critical pre-shipment gate — goods without a valid PVOC certificate will be detained at Nairobi port. Additional gaps include EPRA energy efficiency labelling and MEPS compliance, KS IEC 60335-2-40 safety testing, NEMA ODS refrigerant controls, and CA type-approval for wireless models. CCC is not recognised in Kenya.

GB 9237		 The Kenya Bureau of Standards (KEBS) requires a mandatory Pre-Export Verification of Conformity (PVOC) for regulated products, including heat pumps and air conditioners, before shipment to Kenya. A KEBS-approved inspection body must inspect and test the goods at the origin country and issue a Certificate of Conformity (CoC). Goods arriving at Kenyan ports without a valid PVOC CoC will be detained, re-inspected at the importer's cost, or refused entry. The PVOC inspection verifies conformity to the applicable Kenya Standard (KS), including KS IEC 60335-2-40 for safety. The KEBS Diamond Mark of Quality may be required for locally marketed products. CCC certification from China is not recognised as equivalent to PVOC.Kenya Standards Act, Cap. 496
