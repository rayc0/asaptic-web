Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face four major Kenya compliance gaps: (1) every regulated shipment must clear KEBS Pre-Export Verification of Conformity (PVoC) and obtain a Certificate of Conformity (CoC) per consignment before loading at the Chinese port — CCC certification does not exempt this; (2) electrical safety must be demonstrated to KS IEC 60335-2-24 (Kenya adopts IEC, so the underlying technical basis is close to China's GB 4706.13, but conformity is assessed by a KEBS-appointed PVoC agent, not by CCC); (3) energy efficiency must meet the EPRA/KEBS minimum energy performance standard and carry the local energy efficiency label — Kenya has its own label, distinct from the China Energy Label; and (4) an in-country (Kenyan) importer is required to receive goods and present them for clearance at Mombasa. R-600a refrigerant is broadly accepted; Kenya has no EU-style horizontal RoHS/F-Gas regime, so refrigerant control sits inside the product safety standard rather than a standalone law.
