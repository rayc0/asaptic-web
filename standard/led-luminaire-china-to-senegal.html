Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Senegal should expect ASN/NS standards that adopt the IEC base (NS/IEC 60598-1 for luminaire safety, IEC 62560 for self-ballasted lamps, IEC 62471 for photobiological safety) and a Ministry of Trade conformity / pre-export verification of conformity (PEC/VoC) pathway for regulated imports, rather than the EU CE self-declaration model. Practical points: the grid is 230 V / 50 Hz (50 Hz matches China, but nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V), so rating and marking must suit 230 V; documentation must be in French and routed through an in-country importer; smart/wireless luminaires need ARTP radio approval; energy-efficiency labelling may apply under national energy programmes. Senegal does not operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS, REACH-SVHC, or battery regime, so map the EU 'ecodesign' lane to local energy labelling and the 'rohs' lane honestly — there is no horizontal RoHS substance-restriction law in Senegal as of this review; verify the current PEC/VoC scope and energy programme with ASN and the importer. China CCC and GB/GB-T evidence do not automatically satisfy Senegalese conformity verification.
