Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Maldives, the practical gaps are IEC-based safety and EMC evidence, connector redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 where IEC-aligned infrastructure is specified, site-level grid approval with STELCO in Greater Male or FENAKA in outer atolls, and tropical marine derating for heat, humidity, corrosion, salt mist, and space-constrained island installations. Maldives operates at 230/400 V, 50 Hz. The frequency is the same as China, but the nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V baseline, so input-voltage range and protection settings must be confirmed. A strong dedicated Maldives national standards body or EVSE whole-unit certification rule could not be confirmed from the provided project facts as of 2026-06-14; exporters should verify the current MED, utility, customs, and project requirements before shipment.
