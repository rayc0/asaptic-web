Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Nicaragua, the practical gaps are grid adaptation to Nicaragua's Americas-style 120/240 V, 60 Hz supply, confirmation of site connection and distribution requirements with INE, ENATREL, DISNORTE or DISSUR as applicable, NTON / DGCN-MIFIC conformity routing, IEC 61851 / IEC 61851-23 safety evidence, IEC 61000 EMC evidence, and hardware conversion away from GB/T 20234 connectors. The Americas EV ecosystem commonly uses SAE J1772 (Type 1) for AC and CCS1 for DC, but an official Nicaragua-wide connector mandate could not be confirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters should confirm connector expectations with MEM / INE, the utility, project owner, and charge-point operator before quoting or shipping.
