Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face several Mongolia market-access points: (1) MASM (Mongolian Agency for Standardization and Metrology) conformity certification for regulated electrical products, referencing MNS standards that adopt IEC 60335-2-24 — China's CCC/GB 4706.13 certification is not automatically recognised but the underlying IEC alignment eases re-testing; (2) energy efficiency declaration under Mongolian national energy programmes; (3) an in-country importer or local representative to clear customs (Mongolia is landlocked, served by rail/road, and is NOT an EAEU member — it operates a national regime); and (4) R600a refrigerant documentation against MNS/IEC 60335-2-24 charge requirements. A genuine advantage: Mongolia's 220/380 V 50 Hz grid matches China's 220/380 V 50 Hz nominal, so no electrical re-rating is needed.
