Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Bolivia, the confirmed practical gaps are: IEC-based safety evidence required under IEC 61851 (Bolivia's regulatory framework references IEC standards via IBNORCA NB adoptions); connector redesign from GB/T 20234 to an IEC 62196-compatible connector — the specific connector standard mandated in Bolivia is unconfirmed as of 2026-06-14 and must be verified directly with AE (Autoridad de Electricidad) and IBNORCA, since the Americas region is a mixed IEC/SAE landscape and Bolivia's 50 Hz grid does not automatically resolve to J1772/CCS1; IBNORCA conformity assessment and AE/distributor coordination for grid-connected installations; high-altitude thermal and dielectric derating for the Bolivian Altiplano (La Paz/El Alto at 3,600–4,100 m above sea level, Potosí at ~4,000 m); Bolivia operates on 230 V single-phase / 400 V three-phase, 50 Hz — not 60 Hz — which differs from China's 220 V / 380 V, 50 Hz baseline and requires voltage-range confirmation. The Bolivia EV charging market is extremely nascent; state EV ambitions are anchored in Bolivia's lithium resource strategy (Bolivia holds the world's largest known lithium reserves), but public charging deployment is minimal as of 2026-06-14. Exporters should verify the current conformity assessment route, AE approval, connector type, and ENDE/distributor connection requirements before shipment.
