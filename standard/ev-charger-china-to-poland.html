Short answer

For Poland-bound EV chargers, the most critical gaps are Type 2/CCS2 connector alignment under IEC 62196, compatibility with AFIR obligations as implemented through member-state practice, CE conformity evidence under LVD/EMC/RED where in scope, RCD/RDC protection, UDT technical inspection for certain installation categories, and Polish/URE installation and grid-operator coordination. 230/400 V 50 Hz parameters are required for project readiness and Gdansk/Gdynia are the primary entry ports. Poland's rapidly growing EV market means active enforcement by URE and local DSOs.
