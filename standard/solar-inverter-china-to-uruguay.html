Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Uruguay, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirming UNIT-recognised conformity evidence and the applicable IEC editions for the exact inverter model; (2) URSEA and UTE distributed-generation or microgeneration connection requirements, including project registration, metering, and commissioning; (3) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence; (4) IEC 62116 and IEC 61727 grid-interface and anti-islanding evidence; (5) IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC evidence; and (6) firmware validation for Uruguay's 50 Hz / 230 V / 400 V grid and UTE-specific protection settings. Uruguay's 50 Hz system is closer to China on frequency, but China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence is not automatically accepted as UNIT/URSEA/UTE conformity or connection evidence.
