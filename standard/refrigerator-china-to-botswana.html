Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face several Botswana market-access gaps: (1) electrical safety conformity to BOS/IEC 60335-2-24 demonstrated under the BOBS compulsory standards and BOS import-inspection / mark scheme — Chinese CCC / GB 4706.13 certification is not automatically accepted, though both derive from IEC 60335-2-24 which can shorten testing; (2) demonstrating EMC performance to the IEC/CISPR 14 basis that BOBS standards adopt; (3) an in-country importer (and BOBS import permit / inspection clearance) is required — Botswana is landlocked, so goods route via Durban or Walvis Bay; (4) energy labelling / MEPS obligations where Botswana operates an energy-efficiency programme, with values that differ from China's GB 12021.2 grades; (5) R-600a (isobutane) is well-positioned because BOS adopts IEC 60335-2-24 charge/flammability limits. Botswana does not operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS / battery / outdoor-noise regime; those EU-specific gaps do not apply. Voltage is 230 V / 50 Hz (same 50 Hz as China, nominal voltage differs from China's 220 V).
