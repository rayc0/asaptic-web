Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face several Zimbabwe market-access gaps: (1) electrical safety must be demonstrated against ZWS / IEC 60335-2-24 (adopted via SAZ from IEC, often aligned with SANS) — Chinese CCC / GB 4706.13 certification is not automatically accepted and import conformity inspection may apply; (2) energy-efficiency labelling / MEPS programmes are developing in Zimbabwe (SAZ / ZERA), so exporters should confirm the current label and minimum-performance requirements rather than relying on the China Energy Label; (3) market access runs through SAZ conformity assessment plus an in-country importer, with goods routed via Durban or Beira as Zimbabwe is landlocked; (4) the grid is 230 V / 50 Hz — the 50 Hz frequency matches China but the nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V, so rating-plate and tolerance verification is needed. R-600a refrigerant is well-suited; charge and documentation should be checked against IEC 60335-2-24 Annex. Zimbabwe has no EU-style horizontal RoHS / battery / outdoor-noise regime.
