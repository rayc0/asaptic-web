Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Zambia, the confirmed practical gaps are: connector hardware redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) / CCS2 (DC), since Zambia's IEC-aligned direction is physically incompatible with GB/T connectors; IEC 61851-1 / IEC 61851-23 safety evidence required under IEC-based conformity (ZABS adopts IEC standards); ZABS import conformity assessment and ERB licensing as the regulatory gates; ZESCO grid-connection approval for any charger installation drawing from the national grid; and EMC evidence under IEC 61000. Zambia's grid operates at 230 V single-phase / 400 V three-phase, 50 Hz — close to but distinct from China's 220 V / 380 V domestic baseline, and voltage-range confirmation is required. Critically, Zambia's EV market is very nascent and load-shedding / grid-reliability constraints are a real operational factor that exporters must factor into charger design (e.g. wide input-voltage range, battery buffer, or off-grid capability). A definitive mandatory EVSE-specific product certification rule under ZABS could not be confirmed from official published sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters should verify the current ZABS import conformity route, ERB licensing scope, HS code, and ZESCO connection process before shipment.

Connector Interoperability — GB/T 20234 vs IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 China AC chargers use GB/T 20234.2 couplers and DC fast chargers use GB/T 20234.3 couplers.
