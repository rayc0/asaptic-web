Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face four major South Korea compliance gaps: (1) electrical safety re-certification to KS C IEC 60335-2-24 under the Electrical Appliances and Consumer Products Safety Control Act — refrigerators fall under KC Safety Confirmation (안전확인) and CCC/GB 4706.13 certification is not accepted; (2) energy efficiency grade re-rating to the Korean 1-to-5 grade methodology and mandatory registration with the Korea Energy Agency (KEA/KEMCO) before sale; (3) KC-EMC re-testing under the same Act and RRA rules — Chinese GB 4343.1 CCC test data cannot substitute, and the 60 Hz grid differs from China's 50 Hz; and (4) a local responsible party in Korea (importer or KC certificate holder) is required to apply for KC and hold the technical documentation. R600a refrigerant is well-positioned, but charge documentation against KS C IEC 60335-2-24 and Clean Air Conservation Act handling rules must be verified.
