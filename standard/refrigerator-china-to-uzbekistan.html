Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers exporting to Uzbekistan face a national (non-EAEU) regulatory regime centred on O'zStandart: (1) mandatory conformity certification or declaration must be obtained from O'zStandart or an accredited Uzbek conformity-assessment body before customs clearance — Chinese CCC is not directly recognised; (2) electrical safety must be demonstrated against O'zDSt standards that adopt IEC 60335-2-24, and because Uzbekistan's 220/380 V 50 Hz grid matches China's nominal voltage and frequency, no voltage/frequency redesign is required (a genuine match — only documentary/test re-issuance applies); (3) energy efficiency is governed by national energy-saving programmes rather than the EU EEI/EPREL system; (4) R-600a refrigerant appliances are well-positioned but charge and documentation must align with the adopted IEC 60335-2-24 flammable-refrigerant provisions; and (5) an in-country importer/representative is required to hold registration and certificates.
