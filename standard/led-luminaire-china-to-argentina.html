Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Argentina's mandatory electrical-safety certification with the S-safety mark (Resolucion 169/2018 et seq., via IRAM / recognized certifiers), IRAM/IEC-based safety and performance standards (IEC 60598 / 62560 / 62471), mandatory energy-efficiency labelling, and ENACOM radio approval for smart products, versus Chinese GB standards and CCC certification. Argentina runs on 220 V, 50 Hz single-phase.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer entering the Argentine market must obtain mandatory electrical-safety certification with the S-safety mark (Resolucion 169/2018 et seq., administered by the Secretaria de Comercio) through IRAM or another recognized certifier, testing to IRAM standards that adopt IEC 60598 (luminaires), IEC 62560 (self-ballasted LED lamps), and IEC 62471 (photobiological safety); affix the mandatory energy-efficiency label for in-scope lighting; design for 220 V, 50 Hz single-phase (same 50 Hz as China, but China three-phase is 380 V); obtain ENACOM type approval (homologacion) for any smart luminaire with wireless functionality; and appoint an in-country importer of record holding a CUIT. Argentina has no horizontal RoHS-style restricted-substance regime equivalent to the EU — none of these Argentine obligations are covered by existing Chinese CCC certification.
