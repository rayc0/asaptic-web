Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Nepal, the confirmed practical gaps are NBSM conformity review as Nepal's national standards authority, NEA coordination for grid-connected installations on Nepal's 230/400 V 50 Hz system, IEC 61851-1 and IEC 61851-23 safety evidence, IEC 61000 EMC and power-quality evidence, and connector alignment with the Nepal project specification. Nepal is a real South Asian EV growth market: electric four-wheeler imports have surged, electricity is hydro-dominant, and policy has favored EV adoption through comparatively favorable import duties. Charging infrastructure is expanding but still uneven, so site-level NEA capacity and installation review remain important. The connector reality is mixed: Type 2 AC and CCS2 DC are the IEC-aligned direction, while GB/T connectors appear because Chinese-brand EVs and chargers are common. A GB/T 20234-only charger is physically incompatible with IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 deployments and needs hardware and communication redesign. A blanket Nepal charger-specific mandatory certification rule could not be confirmed from official public sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters should verify current NBSM import/conformity scope, NEA connection requirements, HS code, charger type, and project specifications before shipment.
