Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Nepal NBSM (Nepal Bureau of Standards and Metrology) NS certification, NS/IEC safety standards (NS/IEC 60598, 62560, 62471), photobiological safety, energy efficiency programmes, and NTA radio type approval for smart luminaires, versus Chinese GB 30255 / GB 7000 standards and CCC certification.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Nepal works through an in-country importer of record and must align with NBSM (Nepal Bureau of Standards and Metrology), which operates the NS certification / conformity scheme and adopts NS standards that mirror IEC. Core items: electrical safety to NS/IEC 60598-1 (and NS/IEC 62560 for self-ballasted lamps); LED driver safety to NS/IEC 61347-2-13; photobiological safety to NS/IEC 62471 where applied; EMC broadly per CISPR 15 where adopted; and energy efficiency handled through Nepal's energy programmes rather than an EU-style binding Ecodesign regulation. Smart luminaires with wireless functions need NTA (Nepal Telecommunications Authority) radio type approval. Nepal is landlocked, so goods route via Kolkata / Vizag in India; grid is 230 V, 50 Hz. Importantly, Nepal has no EU-style horizontal RoHS / REACH-SVHC regime — substance restriction is not a standalone mandatory market-access gate the way it is in the EU. None of these Nepal obligations are covered by existing Chinese CCC certification, which is not recognised for the Nepali market.
