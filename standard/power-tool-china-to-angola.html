Short answer

For China-made electric or cordless power tools entering Angola, the practical baseline is conformity to IANORQ national standards (commonly adopting IEC 62841 safety and IEC EMC standards), import inspection and conformity controls, Portuguese-language documentation, and INACOM radio approval for cordless tools that contain radio modules. Angola uses 220 V at 50 Hz; the 50 Hz frequency matches China but voltage configuration differs from China 220/380 V networks. There is no EU-style outdoor-noise product marking scheme, so noise should be handled honestly as a general safety and IEC-test matter rather than an Angola-specific product label.
