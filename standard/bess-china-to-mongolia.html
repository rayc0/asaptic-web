Short answer

Mongolia does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to a full IEC 62619-based certification regime. BESS projects in Mongolia operate primarily under MASM conformity certification using MNS national standards (GOST/IEC-influenced), project-specific international standards referenced in NDC grid-connection agreements, and Energy Regulatory Commission (ERC) licensing. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Mongolia are: (1) MASM conformity certification — Chinese GB certificates are not accepted as equivalents; MASM conformity assessment (MNS standards, IEC 62619, IEC 62933-5-2 as referenced) is the primary product-entry gate; (2) NDC grid-connection approval under the Central Energy System (CES) — with specific SCADA/communication protocol requirements; (3) NEMA fire-safety installation approval for stationary BESS; (4) critical cold-climate design compliance — Mongolia winters reach -40°C, making thermal management, low-temperature battery performance, and heating system validation a non-negotiable design gate that Chinese standard-temperature BESS products commonly fail to address; (5) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory under international transport conventions; and (6) cold-climate derating verification — documented capacity and power derating at low temperatures must be provided to NDC and project owners. Chinese GB/T 36558-2023, GB/T 34120-2023, and NB/T 42090-2016 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for MASM conformity or IEC evidence in Mongolia project or authority review.
