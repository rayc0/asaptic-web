Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers exporting to Nepal face a market-access structure centred on the Nepal Bureau of Standards and Metrology (NBSM): (1) electrical safety should follow NS/IEC 60335-2-24 — Nepal adopts IEC text as NS standards, so Chinese GB 4706.13 (also IEC 60335-2-24-derived) is technically close but CCC certificates are not accepted; NBSM NS conformity is required where the product is regulated; (2) energy performance is addressed through Nepal energy programmes rather than an EU-style Ecodesign/EPREL regime, and Chinese GB 12021.2 grades do not map onto Nepal labels; (3) R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned, governed at appliance level via the adopted IEC 60335-2-24 flammable-refrigerant annex rather than an EU-style F-Gas Regulation; (4) connected models need NTA type approval for radio; and (5) an in-country Nepali importer/representative is required, with goods routed via Indian ports (Kolkata/Vizag) as Nepal is landlocked. Nepal does not operate EU-style horizontal RoHS, battery, or outdoor-noise regimes.
