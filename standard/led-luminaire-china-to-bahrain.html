Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Bahrain must meet BSMD (Bahrain Standards and Metrology Directorate, under MOIC) conformity requirements for regulated products: electrical safety to GSO-adopted IEC standards (GSO IEC 60598-1 for luminaires, GSO IEC 62560 for self-ballasted lamps, plus control-gear safety); EMC to the relevant GSO/CISPR-based standard; photobiological safety to GSO IEC 62471; and compliance with the GSO energy-efficiency labelling scheme for lighting (the Gulf low-energy lamp/lighting label). Smart or wireless luminaires additionally require TRA (Telecommunications Regulatory Authority) type approval. Products are typically registered through an in-country importer and cleared at Khalifa Bin Salman Port. Bahrain runs at 230 V, 50 Hz — the 50 Hz frequency matches China but the nominal voltage differs from China's 220 V single-phase / 380 V three-phase. Existing Chinese CCC certification and China Energy Label registration are not recognised for Bahrain market access. Note: unlike the EU, Bahrain has no broad horizontal RoHS-style restriction-of-hazardous-substances regulation for general EEE; substance control is addressed mainly through specific GSO standards and chemical rules rather than a single 10-substance directive.
