Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to The Gambia, the practical gaps are: (1) confirming TGSB conformity or standards-mark expectations for the exact product route; (2) confirming PURA licensing or approval implications for grid-connected renewable-energy generation; (3) obtaining NAWEC project-specific grid-connection acceptance for 50 Hz 230/400 V conditions; (4) supplementing China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence with IEC 62109-1/-2, IEC 62116, IEC 61727, IEC 62920, and IEC 61000 evidence where required; and (5) documenting humid, dusty West-African operating assumptions, derating, ingress protection, corrosion protection, and donor or lender procurement specifications. Chinese domestic certificates are useful background but should not be treated as automatically accepted in The Gambia.
