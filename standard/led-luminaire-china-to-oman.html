Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Oman market requirements administered by MOCIIP and DGSM (Directorate General for Standards and Metrology): GSO-adopted lighting safety standards (GSO IEC 60598 / 62560 / 62471), GSO energy-efficiency labelling for lighting, and TRA radio approval for smart luminaires, versus Chinese GB / GB-T standards and CCC certification. Oman grid is 240 V 50 Hz.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Oman must meet MOCIIP / DGSM (Directorate General for Standards and Metrology) conformity and registration requirements via an in-country registered importer; comply with GSO-adopted lighting safety standards (GSO IEC 60598-1 for luminaires, GSO IEC 62560 for self-ballasted lamps, GSO IEC 61347 for control gear); demonstrate electromagnetic compatibility consistent with GSO/CISPR 15 emission requirements; carry a GSO energy-efficiency label for lighting where the product is in scope; document photobiological safety per GSO IEC 62471; obtain TRA (Telecommunications Regulatory Authority) type approval for any smart / wireless luminaire; and design for a 240 V 50 Hz grid (same 50 Hz as China, but nominal voltage differs from China's 220 V). Oman applies GSO standards that adopt IEC, so CN test reports built on IEC-aligned GB standards can reduce re-testing, but the conformity, labelling, and importer-registration steps are separate from China's CCC scheme. There is no horizontal RoHS-type substance restriction in Oman equivalent to China's GB/T 26572 disclosure regime.
