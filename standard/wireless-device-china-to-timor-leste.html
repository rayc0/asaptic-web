Short answer

The main Timor-Leste gaps for a China-made wireless or IoT device are: mandatory ANACOM type approval for radio and telecom terminal equipment under Law No. 3/2021 on Electronic Communications — CE, FCC, and Chinese CCC/SRRC certifications are not recognised; ILAC/MRA-accredited laboratory test reports required for the technical file; Portuguese-language documentation may be required alongside Tetum; electrical supply is 220–240 V / 50 Hz with Type C and Type I (Australian) plugs both common; a locally registered Timorese authorised importer must be appointed; and there is no formal cybersecurity device approval framework, though international best practices are advisable. Timor-Leste is a very small market (~1.3 M population) with limited regulatory enforcement capacity; enforcement may be less systematic than in larger ASEAN economies, but ANACOM type approval remains the legal requirement.
