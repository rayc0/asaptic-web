Short answer

The main Ecuador compliance gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT device are: ARCOTEL mandatory homologación under Ley Orgánica de Telecomunicaciones 2015 and ARCOTEL resolutions — all radio and telecom terminal equipment requires an ARCOTEL homologación certificate before import or sale, with no recognition of CE, CCC, or SRRC approvals (FCC test reports may assist as supporting data but do not replace the certificate); an authorized Ecuadorian importer registered with RUC/SRI is required; INEN NTE and RTE INEN electrical safety (IEC 62368-1 aligned) and EMC compliance (IEC CISPR aligned); product compatibility with Ecuador's 120 V / 60 Hz power system and Type A/B (US-style) plugs; Spanish language labelling with the ARCOTEL certificate number on the product label; and advisory cybersecurity expectations under LORC and MINTEL policy — no mandatory IoT hardware security certification exists yet in Ecuador.
