Short answer

The main Ecuador compliance gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT device are: ARCOTEL mandatory homologación — all radio and telecom terminal equipment must obtain an ARCOTEL homologation certificate and display the approval number on the Spanish-language label before import or sale, with no recognition of FCC, CE, SRRC, or CCC approvals; INEN NTE INEN EMC standards (IEC CISPR-aligned) and electrical safety standards (IEC 62368-1-aligned); RUC importer registration with the SRI; and ARCOTEL/SNAP cybersecurity advisory guidelines for connected IoT devices. Ecuador uses 120 V / 60 Hz / Type A/B plugs — US-spec hardware is electrically compatible, giving Chinese exporters with US-market products an advantage — but ARCOTEL homologación is still separately required.
