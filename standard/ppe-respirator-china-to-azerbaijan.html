Short answer

Azerbaijan is not an EAEU member — TR CU 019/2011 (EAEU PPE Technical Regulation) does NOT apply. Respiratory PPE for the Azerbaijan market is regulated under the national AZSTAND/AzStandard regime using GOST-adopted standards (e.g. GOST 12.4.191 / AZS EN 149 equivalents). Conformity certification from the national certification body is required; self-declaration is generally not accepted for regulated PPE categories. Chinese GB 2626 (KN95) test reports do not automatically satisfy AZS national standard requirements — separate testing against the applicable AZS standard is needed. There is no horizontal RoHS-type restriction under Azerbaijan's national PPE regime. Grid: 220/380 V, 50 Hz — matches China, no electrical adaptation required for passive PPE.

Performance Requirements — Filtration Efficiency and Inward Leakage under AZS/GOST Standards Chinese industrial respirators (GB 2626-2019, KN90/KN95) are tested for: (1) filtration efficiency — KN90 requires ≥90% NaCl particle filtration; KN95 requires ≥95% NaCl particle filtration (using a 0.075 µm median diameter NaCl aerosol at 85 L/min flow); (2) total inward leakage; (3) inhalation and exhalation resistance; (4) dead space CO2 content. The GB 2626 test method uses NaCl aerosol, which differs from GOST 12.4.191 and EN 149 in both challenge agent specification and flow rate protocols.
