Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Singapore, the key practical gaps are: (1) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence required under Singapore's IEC-aligned standards framework; (2) IEC 62116 anti-islanding compliance validated for Singapore's 230/400 V, 50 Hz grid as specified in EMA's Handbook for Solar PV Systems; (3) IEC 62920 EMC evidence; (4) firmware validation against Singapore's 230/400 V, 50 Hz grid parameters — not China's 220/380 V baseline; (5) for household-type inverters, potential Consumer Protection (Safety Requirements) Registration (CPSR) with Enterprise Singapore; and (6) installation by a Licensed Electrical Worker (LEW) as required by EMA. China GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 are not accepted as Singapore evidence and cannot be substituted without supplementary IEC test evidence.

IEC 62109-1 / IEC 62109-2 Safety Evidence — Singapore EMA and Enterprise Singapore CPSR Framework China's primary voluntary standard for PV grid-connected inverter safety is GB/T 37408-2021 (Technical Requirements for Photovoltaic Grid-Connected Inverters), which covers classification, environmental conditions, safety, electrical performance, EMC, and markings.
