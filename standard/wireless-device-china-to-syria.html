Short answer

The main Syria gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular, or IoT wireless device are: (1) TRC mandatory type approval — the Telecommunications Regulatory Commission (هيئة تنظيم قطاع الاتصالات) requires type approval for telecom and radio terminal equipment before import or sale in Syria; (2) SCFA / SASMO technical standards compliance — the Syrian Company for Fixed and Automatic Telephone (SCFA) manages telecom infrastructure and SASMO (Syrian Arab Organization for Standardization and Metrology) sets national technical standards; (3) EMC compliance referenced to ITU and SASMO standards as part of the TRC application dossier; (4) electrical safety compliance for 220 V / 50 Hz with plug Type C (Europlug, widely used) and Type L (Italian 3-pin, also common) — both are in use in Syria; (5) a local Syrian importer or authorized agent is required; (6) Arabic documentation and labelling is required; and (7) CRITICAL: Syria has been under extensive US (OFAC), EU, and UK sanctions since 2011 — exporters must complete thorough export-control and sanctions due diligence, potentially including licence applications, before any shipment. Chinese SRRC, CCC, CE, and FCC approvals are not recognized by TRC as substitutes. Chinese trade with Syria has continued through non-sanctioned channels, but each transaction requires independent legal and compliance review.
