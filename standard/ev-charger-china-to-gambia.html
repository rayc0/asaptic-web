Short answer

Gambia has no publicly confirmed standalone EV charger product regulation or mass public EV charging programme as of 2026-06-14. The practical route is project-specific: confirm TGSB conformity expectations, obtain PURA and NAWEC coordination for any grid-connected installation, design to Gambia's 230/400 V 50 Hz grid, use IEC 61851-1 and IEC 61851-23 safety evidence, specify IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC where an IEC-aligned charger is required, and provide IEC 61000 EMC and power-quality data. China GB/T-only chargers are not plug-compatible with IEC 62196 and GB/T 27930 DC communication is not a CCS2/OCPP substitute. Given Gambia's small market, grid deficits, low electrification constraints, humidity, dust, and limited public EV fleet, exporters should treat shipments as engineered pilots or donor/project procurements rather than routine catalogue exports.
