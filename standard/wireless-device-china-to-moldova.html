Short answer

ANRCETI radio type approval plus a DS (Declaration of Conformity under Moldovan technical regulations) are mandatory for wireless devices sold in Moldova; CE documentation from the EU is partially accepted as technical evidence for the DS filing but does not replace it. CCC and FCC are not recognised. SM standards (mirroring EN/ETSI) apply for radio and EMC testing via ATIC market surveillance. Electrical safety requirements follow 220–230 V / 50 Hz / Type C/F (SM equivalents of IEC 62368-1). An importer or authorised representative registered with ANRCETI is required. Moldova's Law 133/2011 on Personal Data Protection (a GDPR-equivalent) applies to IoT devices that collect or process personal data. These are the major compliance gaps compared with Chinese SRRC radio type approval, CCC certification, and GB standards.
