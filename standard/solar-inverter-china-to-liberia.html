Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Liberia, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirming the current conformity-assessment gate with Liberia's standards authority, noting that recent official MOCI pages refer to the Liberia Standards Authority (LiSA) rather than only NSL; (2) LERC licensing or permit review for generation, distribution, micro-utility, and electrical-professional activities where applicable; (3) LEC or local utility connection approval for grid-tied systems; (4) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 grid-interface, and IEC 62920/IEC 61000 EMC evidence; and (5) reconfiguring and retesting Chinese inverter protection settings for Liberia's commonly reported 120/240 V, 60 Hz supply environment, rather than China's 220/380 V, 50 Hz baseline. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence is useful background but should not be treated as automatically accepted in Liberia, especially for 60 Hz anti-islanding and humid tropical operation.
