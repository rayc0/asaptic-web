Short answer

Radio performance (EN 300 328 / EN 301 893), EMC (EN 301 489), electrical safety (EN IEC 62368-1:2020+A11), and cybersecurity under RED Art. 3.3(d)-(f) mandatory from 1 August 2025 (EN 18031) are the major gaps compared with Chinese SRRC approval and CCC certification. Slovenia-specific requirements include: AKOS (Agencija za komunikacijska omrežja in storitve) as the national radio authority enforcing RED; Slovenian-language (slovenščina) product documentation, labelling, and Declaration of Conformity; Type C (Europlug) and Type F (Schuko CEE 7/4) plug compatibility at 230 V/50 Hz; WEEE registration under Slovenian national law (Uredba o odpadni električni in elektronski opremi, coordinated via ZEOS); and an EU Authorised Representative mandatory for non-EU manufacturers. Slovenia's geographic position bordering Italy, Austria, Croatia, and Hungary and its Port of Koper (Luka Koper) make it a significant trans-Alpine transit hub for Chinese electronics shipments into Central and Eastern Europe. CCC and FCC approvals are not recognised in Slovenia.
