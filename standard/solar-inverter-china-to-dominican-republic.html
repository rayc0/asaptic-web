Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to the Dominican Republic, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirming the applicable INDOCAL/NORDOM conformity path and whether IEC-based evidence is accepted for the specific inverter and project; (2) obtaining IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence; (3) documenting IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 grid-interface evidence at 60 Hz; (4) preparing IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC and harmonic evidence; and (5) reconfiguring and validating firmware for the Dominican Republic's 120/240 V, 60 Hz grid and the relevant SIE/distributor interconnection settings. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence is China-side support only and should not be treated as automatically accepted by INDOCAL, SIE, CNE, or EDENORTE/EDESUR/EDEESTE.
