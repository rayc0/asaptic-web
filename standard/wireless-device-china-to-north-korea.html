Short answer

INFORMATIONAL ONLY — SANCTIONS APPLY. Exporting wireless devices from China to North Korea is subject to comprehensive UN, US OFAC, EU, and other multilateral sanctions that prohibit or severely restrict most electronic device trade. For devices that may be permitted under narrow humanitarian or special-category exemptions, North Korean domestic regulation is administered by MIC (Ministry of Information Industry — 정보산업성) for telecom and radio type approval, and OSIM (Office of Standards, Inspection and Metrology — 국가품질감독위원회) for product safety standards. Electrical infrastructure uses 220 V / 50 Hz with Type C/F plugs. Chinese SRRC, MIIT, and CCC documentation are not recognised by DPRK authorities. Any export activity requires explicit sanctions authorisation prior to any transaction.
