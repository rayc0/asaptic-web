Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Pakistan should expect to: obtain PSQCA conformity assessment and the PS conformity/quality mark where lamps fall under PSQCA's mandatory list; demonstrate electrical safety to Pakistan-adopted PS/IEC standards (PS/IEC 60598 for luminaires, PS/IEC 62560 for self-ballasted lamps, plus controlgear standards) for 230 V / 50 Hz operation; address photobiological safety via PS/IEC 62471 where required; meet NEECA energy efficiency and labelling requirements where the product is covered; and obtain PTA type approval for any luminaire with embedded wireless (Wi-Fi/Bluetooth) functionality. Pakistan does NOT operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS regime, a REACH SVHC supply-chain notification duty, or an EU-style Ecodesign minimum-efficacy regulation — energy efficiency is handled through NEECA labelling rather than a binding minimum-performance gate. Chinese CCC certification and GB-standard test reports are not automatically recognised; a Pakistan-resident importer is generally required to handle clearance and conformity. Verify the current PSQCA Conformity Assessment list, NEECA scope, and PTA requirements with the importer before shipment.
