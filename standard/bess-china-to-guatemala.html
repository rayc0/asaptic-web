Short answer

Guatemala does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to a dedicated IEC 62619 certification regime. BESS projects in Guatemala operate under CNEE (Comisión Nacional de Energía Eléctrica) authorization, COGUANOR conformity norms (which adopt IEC standards), AMM (Administrador del Mercado Mayorista) interconnection rules, and local building and fire-safety authority approvals. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Guatemala are: (1) Guatemala's grid operates at 60 Hz and 120/240 V single-phase or 120/208 V three-phase — fundamentally different from China's 50 Hz / 220/380 V; BESS power conversion systems (PCS) require complete firmware reconfiguration and must be validated for 60 Hz operation before any grid-connection application; (2) CNEE authorization is required for all grid-connected energy storage projects; specific technical requirements are defined in CNEE resolutions and distributor/transmission operator connection agreements; (3) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence is expected as the international technical baseline in project specifications; (4) IEC 62933 system-level technical requirements where referenced in CNEE or project-owner specifications; (5) COGUANOR conformity assessment may apply where Guatemala has adopted an IEC standard as a mandatory national norm (NGO); (6) local fire-safety installation approval from Bomberos Voluntarios or Bomberos Municipales and the relevant building authority; and (7) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory under international transport conventions, with sea entry via Puerto Quetzal (Pacific) or Puerto Barrios / Santo Tomás de Castilla (Caribbean). Chinese GB/T 36558-2023, GB/T 34120-2023, GB 44240-2024, and NB/T 42090-2016 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC or CNEE requirements in project or authority review.

BESS units from Chinese factories are most commonly shipped via the Pacific route through Puerto Quetzal. Guatemala's Superintendencia de Administración Tributaria (SAT) and customs at both ports apply IMDG Code requirements for dangerous goods, including UN 38.3 documentation for lithium battery shipments. There is no Guatemala-specific exemption from UN 38.3 requirements — the requirement applies universally to all lithium battery imports by sea, air, or road.
