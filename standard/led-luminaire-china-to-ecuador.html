Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Ecuador's mandatory RTE INEN lighting technical regulations, NTE INEN / IEC 60598 / 62560 / 62471 standards, Certificate of Conformity (INEN-1) requirements, energy-efficiency labelling, and ARCOTEL approval for smart luminaires, versus Chinese GB / GB/T standards and CCC certification.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Ecuador must comply with the applicable mandatory RTE INEN technical regulation for lighting products and obtain a Certificate of Conformity (INEN-1 or recognized conformity-assessment route) before customs clearance and market placement; meet electrical safety per NTE INEN / IEC 60598-1 and lamp standards such as IEC 62560; design for Ecuador's 120/220 V 60 Hz grid rather than China's 220/380 V 50 Hz; provide the required energy-efficiency label; account for photobiological safety per IEC/EN 62471 where applicable; obtain ARCOTEL approval for any smart luminaire with wireless functionality; and work through an in-country importer. Ecuador does not operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS regime, an EU-style Ecodesign efficacy regulation, or a REACH-style SVHC supply-chain regime — so EU ecodesign and RoHS obligations map only partially. Existing Chinese CCC certification is not recognized for the Ecuadorian Certificate of Conformity.
