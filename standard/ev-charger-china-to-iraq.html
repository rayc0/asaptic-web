Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Iraq, the confirmed practical gaps are: IEC-based safety and EMC evidence required for COSQC conformity and Ministry of Electricity (MoEL) coordination (IEC 61851-1, IEC 61851-23 for DC); connector hardware redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) and CCS2 (DC), since Iraq's IEC-aligned direction is physically incompatible with GB/T connectors; COSQC conformity assessment and pre-shipment inspection confirmation; and grid instability planning since Iraq's 230/400 V 50 Hz network suffers frequent outages requiring hybrid or backup power provisions in deployment design. Iraq's EV market is very nascent and there is no single confirmed mandatory national EV charger certification programme published from official sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters must confirm the current COSQC conformity route, MoEL coordination requirements, HS code, charger type, and project approval path before shipment.
