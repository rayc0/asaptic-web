Short answer

Portugal is a full EU member state: CE marking under RED 2014/53/EU is mandatory, enforced nationally by ANACOM. Radio performance (EN 300 328 / EN 301 893), EMC (EN 301 489), electrical safety (EN IEC 62368-1:2020+A11), and cybersecurity under RED Art. 3.3(d)-(f) mandatory from 1 August 2025 (EN 18031) are the major gaps versus Chinese SRRC and CCC. An EU Authorised Representative, Portuguese-language labelling, RoHS 2, and WEEE registration in Portugal are additional hard requirements with no Chinese equivalent. CCC and SRRC approvals are not recognised in Portugal. Note: Portugal and Brazil share the Portuguese language but CE marking does not confer Anatel approval — Brazilian market entry requires separate compliance.

All wireless and radio equipment placed on the Portuguese market must bear the CE marking, backed by a signed EU Declaration of Conformity (DoC). The DoC must reference all applicable directives and harmonised standards (RED Art. 3.1(a) safety, Art. 3.1(b) EMC, Art. 3.2 radio performance, and Art. 3.3(d)-(f) cybersecurity where in scope). ANACOM (Autoridade Nacional de Comunicações) is Portugal's national regulatory authority for electronic communications and primary market surveillance authority for RED; it conducts market checks, can issue withdrawal orders, and reports non-compliant products to the EU RAPEX/Safety Gate system.
