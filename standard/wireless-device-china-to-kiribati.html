Short answer

The main Kiribati gaps for a China-made wireless or IoT device are: (1) MICT (Ministry of Information, Communication and Transport) mandatory type approval for radio and telecom terminal equipment — Kiribati has no dedicated telecom regulator, MICT handles all licensing, and SRRC and MIIT NAL are not recognised; (2) electrical compliance for 240 V / 50 Hz and Type I (Australian) plug — AS/NZS test reports are accepted but CCC is not recognised; (3) appointment of a local Kiribati importer or agent — China has maintained diplomatic relations with Kiribati since 2019; (4) AS/NZS-aligned EMC documentation — Chinese GB standards are not recognised; and (5) limited formal cybersecurity obligations — Kiribati has no dedicated cybersecurity law equivalent to China MLPS, but general compliance with international norms is expected. CE, FCC, and CCC marks are not recognised in Kiribati.
