Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Uzbekistan, the main baseline is O'zstandart mandatory conformity certification for regulated electrical products, using O'z DSt national standards (many derived from GOST or IEC). The real gaps between Chinese and Uzbekistan requirements are: obtaining O'zstandart conformity certification; bridging GOST/O'z DSt versus IEC test-report acceptance; meeting National Electric Grid of Uzbekistan (Uzenergo/regional energy companies) grid-connection technical conditions; and, for internationally-financed projects (ACWA Power, Masdar, IFC/EBRD-backed), satisfying lender technical specifications that may demand IEC 62109 and IEC 62116 test reports regardless of local minimums. The nominal voltage gap is small (China and Uzbekistan both use 220/380 V, 50 Hz), but grid-code settings, O'zstandart certification, and lender requirements must be confirmed separately.
