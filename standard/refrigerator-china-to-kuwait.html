Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face four major Kuwait compliance gaps: (1) the mandatory KUCAS (Kuwait Conformity Assurance Scheme) run by PAI — a Technical Evaluation Report plus a per-shipment Certificate of Conformity is required, and Chinese CCC certification is not accepted as a substitute; (2) GSO energy-efficiency labelling and MEPS — the appliance must meet the GSO/Kuwait minimum energy-performance threshold and carry the GSO energy label, which is calculated differently from China's GB 12021.2 grades; (3) safety re-evaluation to GSO IEC 60335-2-24 with electrical ratings declared for the Kuwait 240 V / 50 Hz grid; and (4) appointment of an in-country (Kuwait) importer of record to clear goods at Shuwaikh/Shuaiba and hold the KUCAS file. R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned, but charge documentation against GSO/IEC 60335-2-24 must be verified.
