Short answer

China-baseline certificates — GB/T 36276, GB 44240, GB/T 36558, GB/T 34120 — do not satisfy Belgian or EU requirements. CE marking under LVD 2014/35/EU is mandatory; EN IEC 62619, EN IEC 63056, and IEC 62933-5-2 are the key harmonised battery-safety standards. UN 38.3 transport testing is required for every cell type shipped via Antwerp or Zeebrugge. Battery Regulation (EU) 2023/1542 adds lifecycle and due-diligence duties from 2027. Voluntary reference, not legal advice.
