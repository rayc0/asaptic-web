Short answer

Cameroon requires ANOR/NC alignment for regulated imports where in scope, import inspection through PECAE-like procedures, ART handling of radio-enabled models, UN 38.3 transport evidence, and a cameroonian in-country importer. Importantly, Cameroon does not impose EU-style battery regulation duties such as EU battery passports, RoHS horizontal restrictions, carbon-footprint labels, or outdoor-noise regime obligations. For electrical safety, the operative technical anchor is NC/IEC 62133-like conformity, not the EU framework. CN baseline evidence (GB/GB-T and CCC where applicable) helps at model level but does not satisfy Cameroon paperwork and import-conformity steps alone.
