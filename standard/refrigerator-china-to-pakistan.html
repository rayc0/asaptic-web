Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face four main Pakistan compliance gaps: (1) PSQCA conformity marking — for items under PSQCA's Conformity Assessment list, a PS Mark/import conformity certificate is required and the relevant PS standard (which adopts IEC 60335-2-24) must be met; Chinese CCC/GB 4706.13 certification is not accepted as equivalent; (2) NEECA energy-efficiency label and MEPS — refrigerators must meet Pakistan's minimum energy performance standards and carry the NEECA energy label; Chinese GB 12021.2 energy grades are not directly transferable; (3) an in-country importer of record handling PSQCA clearance and customs at Karachi/Port Qasim is required, since Chinese exporters cannot self-clear; (4) R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned, but charge documentation against the adopted PS/IEC 60335-2-24 must be confirmed. Pakistan's grid is 230 V / 50 Hz (50 Hz matches China; nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V). Pakistan has no EU-style horizontal RoHS, battery, or outdoor-noise regime for refrigerators.
