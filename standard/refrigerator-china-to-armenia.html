Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers exporting to Armenia face four main EAEU compliance gaps: (1) electrical safety conformity to TR CU 004/2011 (Low Voltage) tested against GOST IEC 60335-2-24 — CCC / GB 4706.13 certification is not accepted; (2) EMC conformity to TR CU 020/2011 — Chinese GB 4343.1 CCC test data cannot substitute; (3) EAC conformity marking via an accredited EAEU body (certification or declaration of conformity), which requires an in-country importer or applicant established in the EAEU; and (4) the EAEU energy-efficiency label, whose grade and methodology differ from China's GB 12021.2 China Energy Label. Armenia's grid is 220/380 V, 50 Hz — a genuine match to China's 220/380 V 50 Hz nominal — and R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned, but charge documentation and the EAC route still require verification.
