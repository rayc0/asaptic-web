Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Kuwait, the practical gaps are KUCAS / Public Authority for Industry conformity documentation for imported electrical equipment, MEW review for electricity connection and site installation, IEC 61851 safety and IEC 61000 EMC evidence, connector redesign from China GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC where GCC-aligned infrastructure is specified, and confirmation that the charger supports Kuwait's 240 V single-phase / 415 V three-phase, 50 Hz supply. Kuwait's public EV charging infrastructure remains nascent, and a Kuwait-specific public EV-charger technical specification could not be confirmed from official public sources as of 2026-06-14. Exporters should engage the Kuwait importer, PAI / KUCAS conformity body, MEW, and the project owner before shipment or installation.
