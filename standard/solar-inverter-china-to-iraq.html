Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Iraq, the key practical gaps are: (1) COSQC pre-shipment inspection and IQS conformity — Iraq's import regime requires conformity assessment against applicable Iraqi Standards (IQS) or accepted IEC equivalents before clearance; (2) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence, which large-scale MoE and project-tendered installations (TotalEnergies, PowerChina, Masdar) reference in their IEC-aligned procurement specifications; (3) IEC 62116 anti-islanding compliance validated for Iraq's 50 Hz / 230 V / 400 V grid parameters; (4) IEC 61000/62920 EMC evidence; and (5) hybrid and off-grid inverter suitability — Iraq's chronic grid instability means many installations integrate diesel/generator backup, requiring inverter specifications to cover hybrid operating modes. China GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 baselines are not automatically accepted under COSQC conformity requirements and must be mapped to IQS/IEC gates.
