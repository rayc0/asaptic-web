Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face four major Ghana compliance gaps: (1) electrical safety conformity to GS/IEC 60335-2-24 under GSA's mandatory conformity assessment — CCC/GB 4706.13 certification is not directly accepted, and a GSA Certificate of Conformity / destination inspection is required at Tema or Takoradi; (2) a notably strict Energy Commission MEPS and mandatory star-rating energy label for refrigerators — units below the minimum efficiency level are prohibited from import, and EEI must be recalculated to the Ghanaian/IEC test method rather than the China GB 12021.2 metric; (3) a registered in-country importer must be the regulatory counterparty for clearance and labelling; and (4) NCA type-approval applies only if the unit contains a radio module (e.g. Wi-Fi smart fridges). R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned but charge documentation against IEC 60335-2-24 must be verified. Ghana has no EU-style horizontal RoHS, battery, or outdoor-noise regime.

GB/T compliance test methods underpinning GB 12021.2 grading		 Ghana operates one of the more stringent refrigerator MEPS regimes in West Africa, administered by the Energy Commission under the Energy Efficiency Regulations (the appliance/refrigerator regulations made under the Energy Commission Act, 1997 (Act 541)).
