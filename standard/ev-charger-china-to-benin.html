Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Benin, the confirmed practical gaps are: connector hardware redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) / CCS2 (DC), since Benin follows Francophone IEC alignment and GB/T connectors are physically incompatible with IEC 62196 vehicle inlets; IEC 61851-based safety evidence required for ANM conformity and ARE / SBEE project coordination; EMC evidence per IEC 61000 series; SBEE grid-connection coordination for any grid-connected installation (220/380 V, 50 Hz); and high-temperature and dust derating documentation for Benin's West African tropical climate. Critically, Benin's EV market is at an extremely early stage: four-wheel EV penetration is negligible, grid electrification is incomplete, and the most relevant near-term EV segment is electric two- and three-wheelers (e-moto / e-tricycle), not passenger-car fast chargers. Exporters must honestly assess demand, infrastructure readiness, and project finance before committing to shipment. No single official Beninese mandatory certification route for EV chargers as a product class could be confirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14; the applicable path is ANM conformity + ARE / SBEE project coordination + IEC technical standards.
