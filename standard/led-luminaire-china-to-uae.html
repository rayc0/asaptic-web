Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against UAE conformity requirements administered by the Ministry of Industry and Advanced Technology (MoIAT, incorporating the former ESMA): the Emirates Conformity Assessment Scheme (ECAS) and Emirates Quality Mark (EQM), UAE.S national standards (adopting GSO Gulf standards plus IEC/EN), GSO regional alignment, the UAE energy-efficiency label for lighting, and an in-country importer/registrant requirement, versus Chinese GB standards and CCC certification.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer entering the UAE market must register the product under the Emirates Conformity Assessment Scheme (ECAS) administered by MoIAT (incorporating the former ESMA) and obtain a Certificate of Conformity before customs clearance at ports such as Jebel Ali (Dubai) or Khalifa (Abu Dhabi); meet electrical safety to UAE.S IEC 60598 / IEC 62560 and photobiological safety to UAE.S IEC 62471; meet EMC requirements aligned with CISPR 15 (emissions) and IEC 61547 (immunity); meet the UAE energy-efficiency requirements and carry the UAE energy-efficiency label for lighting (and register where the scheme requires); appoint an in-country importer/registrant who holds the certificate and bears legal responsibility; and where a higher-tier mark is sought, apply for the Emirates Quality Mark (EQM). The product is rated for a 230/400 V, 50 Hz grid (50 Hz matches China, but the voltage differs from China's 220/380 V). None of these UAE steps are covered by existing Chinese CCC certification.
