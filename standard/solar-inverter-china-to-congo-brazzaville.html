Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Congo-Brazzaville (Republic of the Congo), the key practical gaps are: (1) ACONOQ national conformity marking requirements — China GB certificates are not automatically accepted; (2) ARSEL licensing approval for grid-tied PV installations, which constitutes a regulatory gate independent of product certification; (3) E2C (Energie Electrique du Congo) connection approval, including anti-islanding evidence per IEC 62116 and grid-parameter validation for the 220/380 V, 50 Hz network; (4) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence, which donor-funded and lender-financed projects (World Bank, AfDB) typically require; (5) IEC 62920 EMC evidence; and (6) equatorial humidity and thermal derating documentation relevant to Congo-Brazzaville's climate. China GB/T 37408-2021 and GB/T 19964-2024 baselines are not accepted as substitutes for IEC-based evidence without supplementary IEC test reports. ACONOQ and ARSEL regulatory details are not comprehensively available in public-domain English sources — verify with in-country advisers.
