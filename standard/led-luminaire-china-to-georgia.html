Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Georgia should expect a framework that is being progressively approximated to the EU under the DCFTA, administered by GEOSTM (Georgian National Agency for Standards and Metrology) with national conformity and market surveillance: electrical safety based on IEC 60598-1 / GEOSTM-adopted standards (EU LVD-style); EMC requirements aligned with IEC/CISPR 15 emission and IEC 61547 immunity bases; energy-efficiency / ecodesign programmes referencing EU light-source thresholds; photobiological safety classification per IEC/EN 62471; and EU RoHS approximation that is being phased in (not yet a fully mandatory 10-substance regime in all respects — confirm current status). Georgia's 220/380 V 50 Hz grid matches China, so no voltage/frequency redesign is needed. Smart/wireless luminaires additionally need GNCC (Georgian National Communications Commission) authorisation. An in-country importer typically bears conformity and labelling responsibility. None of this is covered by existing Chinese CCC certification; confirm the exact applicable Georgian technical regulation per product before shipment.
