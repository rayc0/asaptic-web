Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Ethiopia should demonstrate electrical safety to ESA-adopted ES / IEC 60598-1 (luminaires) and IEC 62560 (self-ballasted lamps) and, where applicable, IEC 61347-2-13 for LED drivers; meet ESA / IEC 62471 photobiological safety; comply with Ethiopian energy-efficiency labelling / MEPS where a lighting programme applies; pass CACEA / Ministry of Trade conformity assessment and import inspection for regulated products (typically a certificate of conformity / batch or consignment inspection arranged with the in-country importer); and obtain ECA (Ethiopian Communications Authority) type approval for any wireless / smart luminaire. Ethiopia is landlocked, so goods clear via Djibouti. Ethiopia mirrors China's 220 V single-phase, 50 Hz grid, so no frequency re-rating is needed. Unlike the EU, Ethiopia has no EU-style horizontal RoHS substance-restriction or REACH SVHC regime, so Chinese RoHS (GB/T 26572) documentation has no Ethiopian counterpart obligation — but contractual / buyer requirements may still ask for it. None of these Ethiopian steps are covered by existing Chinese CCC certification.
