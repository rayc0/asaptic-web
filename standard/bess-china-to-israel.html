Short answer

Chinese BESS shipped to Israel should be treated as a project-gated compliance exercise, not a simple transfer of China grid or battery certificates. The practical gates are: (1) SII certification or confirmation of the applicable mandatory Israeli Standard route, typically where Israeli Standards adopt IEC battery and energy-storage standards such as IEC 62619, IEC 62133, and IEC 62933-5-2; (2) grid-connection approval under Israel Electricity Authority rules and Israel Electric Corporation technical review, with PCS configured for Israel's 230/400 V, 50 Hz grid rather than China's 220/380 V baseline; (3) fire-safety design approval by the Israel National Fire and Rescue Authority and local building authorities; and (4) UN 38.3 transport documentation for lithium cells, modules, and packs. China-side GB/T 36558-2023, GB/T 34120-2023, and NB/T 42090-2016 evidence is useful background but is not accepted as a substitute for Israeli project, SII, grid, or fire approvals.
