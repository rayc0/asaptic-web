Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Honduras, the key practical gaps are: (1) verifying OHN-recognised conformity evidence and the specific standards named by the project owner or authority; (2) satisfying CREE rules for distributed generation/autoproductores and the ENEE/ODS interconnection process; (3) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence for the exact inverter model or family; (4) IEC 62116 anti-islanding evidence at Honduras 60 Hz grid conditions; (5) IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC and harmonic evidence; and (6) firmware validation for Honduras 60 Hz / 120-240 V service rather than China 50 Hz / 220-380 V defaults. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 documents are useful baselines but are not automatic substitutes for Honduras project acceptance.
