Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face several Cameroon market-access gaps: (1) conformity assessment under the PECAE programme (Programme d evaluation de la conformite avant embarquement) — pre-shipment inspection and a Certificate of Conformity are required for regulated products before clearance at Douala or Kribi; (2) standards alignment to ANOR-adopted NC standards based on IEC 60335-2-24 for safety, plus energy-efficiency labelling/MEPS where the national energy programme applies; (3) French-language product documentation, instructions, and labelling; (4) an in-country importer of record. R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned, and Cameroon shares 220 V / 50 Hz single-phase nominal voltage with China, reducing voltage-frequency rework versus other markets. Cameroon does not operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS, battery, or outdoor-noise regime, so those EU-specific obligations do not apply.
