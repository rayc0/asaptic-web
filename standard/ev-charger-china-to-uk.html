China-to-UK EV Charger Compliance Gap Matrix

Beta · data under verification — informational only, not a certification conclusion. Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China EV charger documentation against UK UKCA, BS EN 61851, smart-charge-point, connector, electrical-installation, and market-access expectations.

Short answer

UKCA marking, BS EN 61851 evidence, compliance with the Electric Vehicle (Smart Charge Points) Regulations 2021, and UK Type 2 or CCS connector expectations are the main gaps compared with Chinese GB/T EV-charger compliance.

No direct equivalent to OZEV approved-list or Smart Charge Points Regs		 Two distinct but related UK market-access mechanisms apply beyond product safety marking. First, the Office for Zero Emission Vehicles (OZEV) administers grant schemes — the EV Chargepoint Grant (for homeowners and renters) and the Workplace Charging Scheme (WCS) — that fund installation of approved charge points. Critically, only charge point models listed on the OZEV authorised-chargepoint model list are eligible; installers must use a listed model or the grant is refused.
