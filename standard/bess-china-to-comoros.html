Short answer

Comoros does not have a published standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to the EU Battery Regulation or a dedicated IEC 62619 certification route. The country's standards body, SONELEC, oversees conformity assessment; however, Comoros is NOT a member of the Eurasian Economic Union (EAEU), so EAC / TR CU certification does not apply and should not be assumed to confer market access. Grid-scale BESS in Comoros falls under SONELEC (the state electricity monopoly) procurement and project specifications, which are expected to reference IEC international standards as technical prerequisites. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Comoros are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline for project acceptance; (2) IEC 62933 system-level technical requirements where referenced in SONELEC or project-owner specifications; (3) thermal derating documentation — Comoros's extreme coastal climate (ambient temperatures routinely above high-temperature) makes battery thermal management and derating evidence a critical project deliverable; (4) fire-safety installation documentation aligned with IEC 63056 / NFPA 855 direction, as Chinese GB fire standards are not internationally recognised; (5) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory under international transport conventions; and (6) grid compatibility confirmation for SONELEC's 220/380 V 50 Hz state grid — voltage is nominally compatible with China's grid, but PCS settings and protection parameters must be validated for SONELEC's specific grid conditions. Chinese GB/T 36276, GB 38031, GB/T 34131, and GB/T 36558 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC evidence in SONELEC project or authority review.
