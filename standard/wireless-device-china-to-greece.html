Short answer

Greece is a full EU member state: CE marking under RED 2014/53/EU is mandatory, enforced nationally by EETT (Hellenic Telecommunications and Post Commission). Radio performance (EN 300 328 / EN 301 893), EMC (EN 301 489), electrical safety (EN IEC 62368-1:2020+A11), and cybersecurity under RED Art. 3.3(d)-(f) mandatory from 1 August 2025 (EN 18031) are the major gaps versus Chinese SRRC and CCC. An EU Authorised Representative, Greek-language labelling, RoHS 2, and WEEE registration in Greece are additional hard requirements with no Chinese equivalent. CCC and SRRC approvals are not recognised in Greece.

GB/T 26572-2011 — Requirements for concentration limits for certain restricted hazardous substances in electronic and electrical products (Chinese RoHS equivalent)		 Under RED 2014/53/EU Articles 11–14, a manufacturer established outside the EU must appoint an EU Authorised Representative (EU AR) before placing radio equipment on the EU market, including Greece.
