Short answer

Libya is a conflict-affected, dual-government market with fragmented regulatory enforcement. In principle, LNCA (Libyan National Communications Authority) under GSPLAJ oversight requires radio type approval for wireless devices, but practical enforcement varies significantly between Tripoli (GNU-controlled) and eastern Libya (GNS-controlled). CCC, CE mark, and FCC authorisation are not recognised as substitutes for Libyan type approval. Additional gaps include IEC-aligned EMC standards, 230 V / 50 Hz electrical safety with Type L (Italian three-pin) or Type C/F plug, Arabic product labelling, a locally verified importer or agent, and UN dual-use export-control screening. Logistics are disrupted; customs operations at Tripoli (Mitiga/Tripoli International) and Benghazi are subject to conflict-related interruption. Specialist legal counsel is mandatory before any export transaction.
