Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Laos, the key practical gaps are: (1) DOSM (Department of Standards and Metrology) conformity assessment — Laos adopts IEC-based standards and requires product conformity; (2) MEM (Ministry of Energy and Mines) project licensing and technical approval for grid-connected solar installations; (3) EDL (Électricité du Laos) / EDL-T grid-connection technical acceptance, which references IEC standards including IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 62109-1/-2 safety; (4) firmware and protection settings validated for Laos's 230/400 V, 50 Hz grid — not the 220/380 V Chinese grid baseline; and (5) EMC evidence under IEC 62920 and IEC 61000-3-2. Critically, although Chinese-brand inverters are widely deployed in Laos under BRI-linked projects, market prevalence does not substitute for formal DOSM conformity and MEM/EDL technical approval. China GB/T 37408 and GB/T 19964 documents do not automatically satisfy these Laos requirements.
