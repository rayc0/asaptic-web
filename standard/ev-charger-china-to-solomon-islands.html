Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Solomon Islands, the practical gaps are project engineering, utility connection, IEC safety and EMC evidence, connector conversion, and tropical marine durability rather than a confirmed charger-specific national certification route. Solomon Islands uses 230/400 V, 50 Hz supply through Solomon Power / SIEA and project power systems, so frequency matches China but nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V baseline. Public or fleet deployments should be specified toward IEC 62196 Type 2 AC and CCS2 DC, not North American J1772. GB/T 20234 and GB/T 27930 chargers need hardware, signaling, and communication redesign before use with IEC 62196 / CCS2 vehicles or networks.
