Short answer

Samoa does not have a dedicated mandatory BESS product safety standard or a strong standalone product conformity body. The Ministry of Commerce, Industry and Labour (MCIL) administers trade and product standards, but a specific mandatory certification regime for stationary BESS has not been confirmed from official sources. The Office of the Regulator (OOTR) regulates the electricity sector, and the Electric Power Corporation (EPC) is the sole grid operator and utility. BESS projects must obtain EPC grid-connection approval and satisfy any OOTR electricity regulatory conditions. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Samoa are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline for project and EPC acceptance; (2) IEC 62933 system-level technical requirements where referenced in EPC or project-owner specifications; (3) NFPA 855-aligned fire-safety installation documentation, as Samoa's building and fire codes reference IEC/Australian-NZ and in some contexts NFPA frameworks, and no dedicated BESS fire code has been confirmed; (4) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory under international transport conventions for sea-freight via Apia port; (5) voltage mismatch — China nominal is 220/380 V whereas Samoa operates at 230/400 V 50 Hz; PCS must be re-parameterised and the difference must not be dismissed; (6) cyclone-zone structural and coastal salt-mist derating requirements for outdoor or containerised BESS enclosures. Chinese GB/T 36276, GB/T 34131, GB 38031, and GB/T 36558 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC 62619 evidence in project or authority review.
