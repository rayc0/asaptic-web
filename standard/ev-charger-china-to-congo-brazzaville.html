Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Congo-Brazzaville (Republic of the Congo — NOT the DRC), the confirmed practical gaps are IEC-based safety evidence required under IEC 61851, connector redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 since Congo-Brazzaville follows the IEC/Francophone connector tradition and GB/T connectors are physically incompatible, ARSEL project-level coordination for any grid-connected installation, IP-rated enclosure protection appropriate for equatorial humidity and temperature conditions, and OCPP back-office interoperability for any networked charger deployment. Congo-Brazzaville operates on 220 V single-phase / 380 V three-phase, 50 Hz — identical nominal voltage to China's domestic baseline — but equatorial ambient conditions (sustained high humidity and heat) require thermal and moisture derating confirmation. The EV market in Congo-Brazzaville is essentially non-existent as of 2026; the economy is oil-based and private vehicle charging infrastructure is absent. Regulatory conformity assessment routes for EV chargers through ACONOQ are unconfirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14. Exporters should verify current conformity assessment requirements, ARSEL licensing, E2C grid-connection procedures, and applicable IEC standard editions before shipment or installation.
