Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Kyrgyzstan, the confirmed practical gates are EAEU conformity for in-scope electrical products because Kyrgyzstan is an EAEU member, including TR CU 004/2011 low-voltage safety, TR CU 020/2011 EMC, and EAC marking; Kyrgyzstandart / GOST / IEC documentation where adopted or requested; project-level coordination with NEHC and the relevant grid subsidiary for 220/380 V, 50 Hz connection; and cold-climate / high-altitude operating confirmation. Kyrgyzstan's EV market is still nascent, and GB/T charging interfaces may appear because of China proximity and Chinese vehicle imports, but IEC 62196 Type 2 AC and CCS2 DC alignment is the safer internationally interoperable route for public or mixed-fleet projects. A China GB/T-only charger using GB/T 20234 connectors and GB/T 27930 DC communication is physically and protocol-incompatible with IEC 62196 / CCS2 deployments and needs hardware, firmware, labelling, and test-documentation changes before it can be treated as Kyrgyzstan-ready.
