Short answer

The main Iraq gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular, or IoT wireless device are: (1) CMC mandatory type approval — the Communications and Media Commission requires type approval for all radio and telecom terminal equipment sold or imported into Iraq; (2) EMC testing to ITU or ETSI-referenced standards as part of the CMC application dossier; (3) electrical safety compliance for 230 V / 50 Hz with plug Type D (old Indian 3-round-pin, prevalent in older buildings) and Type G (UK 3-pin, prevalent in newer buildings) — both are in common use; (4) a local Iraqi importer or authorized CMC-registered agent is required; (5) product labelling in Arabic is expected; and (6) customs and import compliance including Iraq Ministry of Trade oversight, and due diligence on US/EU sanctions exposure for the specific product and supply chain. Chinese SRRC, CCC, CE, and FCC approvals are not recognized by CMC as substitutes. The Iraqi Kurdistan Region (KRG) falls under the same federal CMC rules.
