Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Djibouti, the key practical gaps are: (1) Djibouti's national standards and conformity infrastructure is comparatively limited, so project-owner, Ministry of Energy, ANGMV, customs, and EDD connection conditions must be confirmed case by case; (2) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence is the practical international baseline for bankable PV inverter projects; (3) IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 utility-interface evidence should be prepared for EDD grid review; (4) IEC 62920 and IEC 61000-series EMC evidence should supplement China GB/T EMC files; and (5) Djibouti uses 230/400 V at 50 Hz. The 50 Hz frequency aligns with China, but the voltage differs from China's 220/380 V, so firmware protection thresholds, reconnection settings, thermal derating, and site commissioning must be validated for Djibouti conditions.
