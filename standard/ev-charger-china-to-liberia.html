Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Liberia, the practical gates are not a mature EVSE certification scheme but basic market-access and engineering validation: confirm the National Standards Laboratory of Liberia (NSL) conformity route for imported electrical equipment, coordinate any grid-connected installation with the Liberia Electricity Regulatory Commission (LERC) and Liberia Electricity Corporation (LEC), validate operation on Liberia's 120/240 V, 60 Hz service rather than China's 220/380 V, 50 Hz baseline, and redesign GB/T 20234 connector hardware for the locally applicable ecosystem. Given Liberia's 60 Hz, US-influenced electrical context, SAE J1772 (Type 1) for AC and CCS1 for DC are plausible for imported EV infrastructure, but a public Liberia EV connector mandate could not be confirmed as of 2026-06-14. Exporters should treat IEC 61851-1, IEC 61851-23, IEC 61000 EMC evidence, humidity and corrosion derating, and project-specific LERC/LEC approval as required diligence before shipment.
