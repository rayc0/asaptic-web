Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Samoa, the practical gaps are IEC-aligned product evidence, connector conversion from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC, EPC project-level grid connection acceptance, OOTR electricity-sector oversight, IEC 61000 EMC and power-quality evidence, and environmental derating for coastal salt mist, cyclones, humidity, heat, corrosion, and sea-freight handling via Apia. Samoa uses 230 V single-phase / 400 V three-phase at 50 Hz. The frequency matches China, but the nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V baseline, so input-voltage range, protection settings, cable ratings, and thermal derating must be documented. Samoa's EV market is essentially non-existent or very early-stage, and a dedicated EVSE national certification rule or strong dedicated standards body could not be confirmed from official public sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters should verify the current MCIL import route, OOTR/EPC connection requirements, HS code, and project specification before shipment.
