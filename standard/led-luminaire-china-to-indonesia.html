Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Indonesia's mandatory SNI certification (SPPT-SNI and the SNI mark via an accredited LSPro, regulated by Kemenperin / BSN), ESDM energy efficiency labelling (label hemat energi), SDPPI radio/telecom approval for smart lighting, and restricted-substance / B3 hazardous-material and e-waste rules, versus Chinese GB standards and CCC certification.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Indonesia must obtain mandatory SNI certification (SPPT-SNI plus the right to apply the SNI mark) issued by an accredited LSPro through type testing and an audit of the factory quality management system, against the applicable SNI such as SNI IEC 60598 for luminaires and the SNI for self-ballasted LED lamps; comply with ESDM energy efficiency labelling (label hemat energi / star rating) where the product is in scope; obtain SDPPI radio/telecom approval for smart lighting with Wi-Fi or Bluetooth; cover restricted-substance, B3 hazardous-material, and e-waste obligations (Indonesia has no single horizontal RoHS-style law); and route the goods through an in-country importer holding an NIB / API-U, with goods entering via Tanjung Priok or Tanjung Perak. None of these are covered by existing Chinese CCC certification or GB test reports, which are not recognised in Indonesia.
