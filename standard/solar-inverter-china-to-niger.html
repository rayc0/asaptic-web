Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Niger, the key practical gaps are: (1) Niger national conformity and standards-adoption review, because IEC evidence is normally the usable technical language but local acceptance must be confirmed; (2) ARSE licence or authorisation treatment for regulated generation, distribution, mini-grid, and sale-of-electricity activities; (3) NIGELEC connection approval for grid-tied systems on the 50 Hz / 220-380 V network; (4) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence; (5) IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 grid-interface evidence; (6) IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC evidence; and (7) extreme Sahel heat, dust, and derating evidence, including operation at 45 C or higher where project specifications require it. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence is useful background but does not automatically satisfy Niger national, ARSE, NIGELEC, or donor/lender project acceptance.
