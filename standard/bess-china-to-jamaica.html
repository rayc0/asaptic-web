Short answer

Jamaica does not yet have a comprehensive standalone mandatory BESS product safety code, and regulation is still developing. Compliance is operationalized through BSJ conformity pathways for standards-referenced documentation, OUR utility interconnection control for project operation, and JPS technical review for grid connection. As an island grid importing BESS exports from China, the practical compliance gaps are: (1) IEC 62619 safety evidence for cells and modules required by BSJ-style conformity assessment — Chinese GB 38031 / GB/T 36276 are not accepted as substitutes for IEC evidence; (2) IEC 62933 system-level requirements for utility interconnection; (3) IEC 63056 where second-life cells are used; (4) NFPA 855 fire-safety installation provisions for buildings and enclosures; (5) UN 38.3 and IEC 62281 transport documents for international sea shipping; (6) voltage configuration conversion because Jamaica uses 110/220 V 50 Hz while China uses 220/380 V 50 Hz; and (7) local import and port administration using Kingston as the principal entry point. Chinese GB/T 36276, GB/T 34131, GB 38031, and GB/T 36558 remain domestic baselines but are not accepted as standalone substitutes in Jamaica review.
