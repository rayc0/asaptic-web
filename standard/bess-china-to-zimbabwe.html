Short answer

Zimbabwe does not have a standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to the EU Battery Regulation or Saudi SABER route. The Standards Association of Zimbabwe (SAZ) adopts IEC standards, and IEC 62619 is the internationally expected safety baseline for lithium BESS cells and modules in project specifications. The Zimbabwe Energy Regulatory Authority (ZERA) regulates energy generation and storage through licensing and net-metering registration; ZESA/ZETDC governs grid connection. Zimbabwe's grid operates at 230/400 V 50 Hz — voltage levels differ from China's 220/380 V, requiring PCS reconfiguration. Chronic load-shedding driven by Kariba hydro shortfalls has accelerated solar-plus-storage uptake across C&I, mining (gold, platinum, lithium), and utility segments. The practical compliance gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Zimbabwe are: (1) SAZ conformity assessment recognising IEC certification as the applicable evidence pathway — Chinese GB/T standards are not accepted as substitutes; (2) ZERA generation/storage licence and net-metering registration before grid-connected operation; (3) ZETDC grid-connection agreement with PCS configured and validated for Zimbabwe's 230/400 V 50 Hz grid; (4) local fire authority approval for BESS enclosure installation aligned with IEC 62933-5-2 or equivalent fire-safety evidence; (5) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory under IATA/IMDG for the Durban (South Africa) or Beira (Mozambique) import corridors; and (6) MHTE mining regulations for captive systems at mining project sites. Chinese GB/T 36558-2023 and GB/T 34120-2023 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as equivalents in SAZ, ZERA, or project-owner review.
