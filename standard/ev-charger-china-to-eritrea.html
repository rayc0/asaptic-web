Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Eritrea, the honest baseline is uncertainty: Eritrea has no clearly confirmed strong national standards body for EVSE, the EV charging market appears essentially non-existent, and installations should be treated as project-specific electrical infrastructure reviewed with the Eritrean Electricity Corporation or the responsible public authority. Eritrea's low-voltage grid context is commonly 230/400 V at 50 Hz, while China is 220/380 V at 50 Hz, so frequency matches but nominal voltage differs. IEC-family design evidence should be prepared: IEC 61851-1 / -23 safety, IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC, IEC 61000 EMC, OCPP for any networked charger, and heat/dust derating for Horn of Africa conditions. China GB/T 18487.1-2023, GB/T 20234, and GB/T 27930 are useful domestic baselines but are not accepted substitutes for IEC connector and protocol readiness.
