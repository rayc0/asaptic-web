Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Niger, the practical gaps are not a mature national EVSE certification pathway but project-specific ARSE / NIGELEC approval, IEC-family safety and EMC evidence, hardware conversion from China GB/T 20234 connectors to IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC, and deployment limits caused by Niger's low electrification and grid capacity constraints. Niger's nominal low-voltage grid is commonly stated as 220/380 V, 50 Hz, so the China voltage-frequency baseline is close, but site capacity, protection settings, harmonics, earthing, metering, and commissioning must be confirmed with NIGELEC. As of 2026-06-14, a single official nationwide Niger EV charger whole-unit certification rule could not be confirmed from public sources; exporters should treat four-wheel public charging as extremely nascent and evaluate smaller 2/3-wheeler charging, solar-hybrid, and off-grid use cases with severe heat, dust, service, and spare-parts constraints.
