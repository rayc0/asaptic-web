Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Gabon, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirming whether AGANOR conformity assessment or Gabon-adopted IEC standards are required for the product or project; (2) obtaining ARSEE licensing or authorisation for electricity generation where the PV project is grid-connected or commercial; (3) securing SEEG technical connection approval for utility-interconnected systems on the 50 Hz / 220 V / 380 V network; (4) providing IEC 62109-1/-2 safety, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 utility-interface, and IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC evidence; and (5) validating firmware, enclosure protection, corrosion resistance, ventilation, and derating for Gabon's equatorial high-humidity climate. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence is useful background but is not automatically accepted as Gabon conformity, ARSEE licensing, SEEG connection, or lender documentation.
