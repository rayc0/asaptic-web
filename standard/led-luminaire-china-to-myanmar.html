Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer entering Myanmar should prepare MSTRD conformity documentation referencing MS/IEC 60598 (luminaire safety) and MS/IEC 62560 (self-ballasted LED lamps); ensure products meet Myanmar's 230 V / 50 Hz grid (note: China's nominal voltage is 220 V — the 10 V difference may require verification); obtain an MIC import permit if required under the relevant HS code; seek MPT type approval for any smart or wireless-enabled luminaire; note that Myanmar has no horizontal RoHS law and no mandatory energy labelling scheme as of 2026 — these rows are mapped as absent; and note that Myanmar standards are largely voluntary with conformity documentation serving primarily as a customs and trade facilitation tool rather than a market prohibition trigger.
