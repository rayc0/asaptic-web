Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Georgia (Caucasus country, not the US state), the confirmed practical gaps are: connector hardware redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) / CCS2 (DC) because Georgia's EU-aligned IEC infrastructure is physically incompatible with GB/T connectors; IEC 61851-1 and IEC 61851-23 safety evidence required under GEOSTM-adopted IEC standards; EMC conformity to IEC 61000 series; GNERC licensing and grid-connection coordination with the distribution-network operator (Telasi in Tbilisi, Energo-Pro Georgia in most other regions); and GEOSTM conformity marking to demonstrate compliance with adopted IEC/EN standards. Georgia is not a member of the EAEU — the EAC/TR CU certification route does not apply and must not be claimed. Georgia operates on 220 V single-phase / 380 V three-phase, 50 Hz, aligned with the European and IEC baseline. OCPP back-office interoperability is expected by major Georgian charge-point operators (e.g. E-Space, Tesla destination). As of 2026-06-14, a single mandatory whole-unit EVSE product certification regulation published by GEOSTM could not be confirmed from official sources; exporters should verify the current GEOSTM conformity assessment scope, HS code, charger type, and distribution-operator connection approval before shipment.
