Short answer

Cyprus is a full EU member state: CE marking under RED 2014/53/EU is mandatory, enforced nationally by OCECPR (Office of the Commissioner of Electronic Communications and Postal Regulation). Radio performance (EN 300 328 / EN 301 893), EMC (EN 301 489), electrical safety (EN IEC 62368-1:2020+A11), and cybersecurity under RED Art. 3.3(d)-(f) mandatory from 1 August 2025 (EN 18031) are the major gaps versus Chinese SRRC and CCC. An EU Authorised Representative, Greek-language labelling (Greek is an official language of Cyprus), plug type G (UK 3-pin BS 1363 — retained from British colonial period, not Schuko), RoHS 2, and WEEE registration with Green Dot Cyprus are additional hard requirements with no Chinese equivalent. CCC and SRRC approvals are not recognised in Cyprus. Note: compliance applies only in the Republic of Cyprus; Northern Cyprus (TRNC) is outside EU jurisdiction.
