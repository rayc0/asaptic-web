Short answer

The main Dominican Republic compliance gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT device are: INDOTEL mandatory homologación under Ley General de Telecomunicaciones 153-98 and INDOTEL resolutions — all radio and telecom terminal equipment requires an INDOTEL homologación certificate before import or sale, with no recognition of CCC or SRRC approvals; FCC and CE test reports may be accepted as a supporting basis within the INDOTEL technical dossier but do not substitute for the Dominican certificate; an authorized Dominican importer with RNC registration through DGII is required; DIGENOR NORDOM electrical safety and EMC compliance (NORDOM standards harmonised with IEC/ISO/COPANT); product compatibility with the Dominican Republic's 110–120 V / 60 Hz power system and Type A/B (US-style) plugs; and Spanish language labelling. The INDOTEL homologación number must appear on the product label. Cybersecurity certification under Ley 53-07 and CNCS is currently advisory. China established diplomatic relations with the Dominican Republic in 2018, and Chinese electronics exports via Port of Haina and Santo Domingo are growing, but Chinese CCC approval carries no weight with INDOTEL.
