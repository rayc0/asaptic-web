Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer entering the Japan market must comply with the DENAN (Electrical Appliances and Materials Safety Act): determine whether the product is a Specified product (requiring the diamond ◇ PSE mark via third-party certification by a Registered Conformity Assessment Body such as JET or JQA) or a Non-specified product (round ◯ PSE via self-declaration with self-testing); meet the applicable JIS technical standards (JIS C 8105 series for luminaires, JIS C 8155 for general lighting LED modules and lamps); satisfy Top Runner Program energy-efficiency targets for LED lamps and luminaires; apply J-Moss (JIS C 0950) hazardous-substance marking (orange / green) where in scope; meet VCCI EMC requirements for emissions; obtain MIC Giteki (技適) certification if the product incorporates radio (e.g. Wi-Fi / Bluetooth smart lighting); and account for Japan's 100/200 V, 50/60 Hz grid (different voltage and a split frequency versus China) — none of which is covered by existing CCC certification.
