Short answer

Lithuania fully applies the EU Radio Equipment Directive (RED 2014/53/EU); CE marking under RED is the primary gate enforced by RRT (Ryšių reguliavimo tarnyba). Key gaps versus Chinese SRRC/CCC include: radio performance testing to EN 300 328 / EN 301 893 (with mandatory 5 GHz DFS), EMC to EN 301 489 series, electrical safety to EN IEC 62368-1:2020+A11 (230 V/50 Hz, plug type C/F Schuko), cybersecurity under RED Art. 3.3(d)-(f) via EN 18031 (mandatory from 1 August 2025), EU Authorised Representative, Lithuanian-language labelling for consumer products, and WEEE registration in Lithuania via GROTA or EEPA. CCC and FCC are not recognised. Important market context: Lithuania's National Cybersecurity Centre (NCSC) published a 2021 report recommending exclusion of Huawei and ZTE equipment from 5G networks, and Lithuania is generally cautious about Chinese telecom equipment — this heightens regulatory scrutiny and reputational risk for Chinese-branded wireless infrastructure products in particular.
