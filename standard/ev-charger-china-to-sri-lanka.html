Short answer

For EV chargers exported from China to Sri Lanka, IEC 61851-1 charging system compliance is the primary safety baseline expected by SLSI and project stakeholders. A critical connector mismatch exists: Sri Lanka follows IEC 62196 connector standards while Chinese domestic chargers use GB/T 20234, making direct deployment of China-spec connectors incompatible with IEC 62196-compliant vehicles. CEB grid-connection review is required for public or commercial chargers. Sri Lanka's National Electric Vehicle Policy creates demand but does not yet specify a domestic certification scheme — importers should confirm current SLSI registration or type-approval requirements before shipment.
