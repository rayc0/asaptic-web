Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Brunei, the key practical gaps are: (1) Brunei's published PV-specific grid interconnection code is limited — exporters must obtain DES (Department of Electrical Services) connection approval and confirm inverter technical requirements directly with DES before shipment; (2) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence from an ILAC-accredited laboratory is expected for grid-connected inverters; (3) IEC 62116 anti-islanding compliance is a standard requirement for grid-tied PV inverters operating on Brunei's 50 Hz grid; (4) IEC 62920 EMC evidence is expected by project engineers and DES technical review; (5) inverter firmware must be validated for Brunei's 230/400 V, 50 Hz grid — not China's 220/380 V settings; and (6) tropical high-humidity climate (Brunei's equatorial climate) requires attention to IP rating and derating documentation. China GB/T 37408 and GB/T 19964 baselines are not accepted as equivalent to IEC-referenced requirements and do not substitute for DES connection approval.
