Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Nauru, the practical gaps are IEC-based safety and EMC evidence, IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 connector hardware, NUC project and grid-connection acceptance, and coastal salt-mist / humidity derating for island installations. Nauru uses 240 V single-phase / 415 V three-phase at 50 Hz: the frequency is the same as China, but the nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V baseline. A GB/T-only charger using GB/T 20234 connectors and GB/T 27930 DC communication is not physically or protocol compatible with IEC Type 2 / CCS2 deployments without hardware and firmware redesign.
