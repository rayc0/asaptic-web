Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face four major Bangladesh compliance gaps: (1) BSTI mandatory certification mark — products on Bangladesh's compulsory-certification list require a BSTI Certification Mark (CM) licence before import and sale, assessed against the relevant Bangladesh Standard (BDS), which typically adopts IEC 60335-2-24; Chinese CCC/GB 4706.13 certification is not accepted; (2) energy efficiency — Bangladesh operates a star-rating energy label programme under SREDA (Sustainable and Renewable Energy Development Authority) distinct from China's GB 12021.2 grades; (3) electrical interface — Bangladesh uses 220 V / 50 Hz (same 50 Hz as China; China's single-phase nominal is similar but its three-phase is 380 V), plus type-D/G/K-style sockets, requiring plug and labelling adaptation; and (4) an in-country importer/Letter of Credit and import-permit chain is required. R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned but charge documentation against IEC 60335-2-24 must be verified. Bangladesh has no EU-style horizontal RoHS, battery, or outdoor-noise regime.

It is mandatory (GB), enforced by SAMR, with the China Energy Label (CEL) administered by NDRC/SAMR and the China National Institute of Standardization (CNIS). The CEL must be displayed before sale in China.
