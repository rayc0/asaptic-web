Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Malaysia, the key practical gaps are: (1) obtaining a Certificate of Approval (CoA) from the Energy Commission (Suruhanjaya Tenaga / ST) under the Electrical Supply Act 1990 — regulated electrical equipment must have ST CoA before supply or installation; (2) SIRIM QAS International conformity assessment based on Malaysian Standards that adopt IEC, principally MS IEC 62109-1/-2 for safety; (3) IEC 62116 anti-islanding compliance per TNB grid-connection and SEDA NEM technical requirements; (4) EMC evidence per IEC 62920 and MS IEC 61000-series; (5) firmware validation against Malaysia's 50 Hz / 240 V single-phase / 415 V three-phase grid parameters. China GB/T 37408 and GB/T 19964 certificates are not accepted as Malaysian ST or SIRIM compliance evidence and cannot substitute for IEC-based Malaysian Standards assessment.
