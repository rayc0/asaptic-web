Short answer

The main Cambodia (MPTC / TCDB) gaps for a China-made wireless, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular, LoRa, or IoT device are: (1) mandatory TCDB type approval for all radio and telecom terminal equipment — SRRC, CCC, FCC, and CE approvals are not recognised as substitutes; (2) TCDB conformity label on the product or packaging; (3) appointment of a locally registered Cambodian authorised importer or agent for TCDB applications and customs clearance; (4) EMC conformity in line with TCDB technical requirements (Cambodia references ASEAN harmonised and ITU-R standards for frequency management; CISPR-based standards referenced for emissions); (5) electrical safety compliance appropriate for 230 V / 50 Hz mains supply (IEC 62368-1 basis); and (6) Khmer-language labelling strongly preferred for consumer products. Cybersecurity requirements are developing as of 2026-06-17. CCC/CE/FCC are not recognised alone by TCDB.
