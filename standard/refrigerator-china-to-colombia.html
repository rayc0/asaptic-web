Short answer

For refrigerators, Colombian entry is governed by ONAC-linked safety and conformity pathways aligned with RETIE and RETILAP, plus RETIQ product energy-labelling and SIC enforcement. The biggest practical differences from China are that CCC is not accepted as the destination-market conformity route, electrical safety is assessed against Colombia's adopted IEC basis under NTC/RIE references, the in-country importer must carry the product-file ownership duties, and port clearance is typically handled through Cartagena or Buenaventura. The refrigerator anchor R-600a is common and generally workable, but charge/documentation verification remains mandatory. Colombia uses a 110/220 V, 60 Hz nominal electrical supply, which differs from China and may require design or markings updates when models are currently built for 220/380 V, 50 Hz. Colombia does not have a broad EU-style battery-regulation or outdoor-noise regime for household refrigerators.
