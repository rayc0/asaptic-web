Short answer

Georgia (the Caucasus country, not the US state) does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to the EU Battery Regulation or comparable mandatory certification regimes. Georgia's standards body GEOSTM adopts IEC and EN standards under its EU Deep and Comprehensive Free Trade Area (DCFTA) alignment trajectory — EAC/TR CU (EAEU) certification does not apply because Georgia is not an EAEU member. The energy sector regulator is GNERC (Georgian National Energy and Water Supply Regulatory Commission); the transmission system operator is GSE (Georgian State Electrosystem); distribution is served by Telasi (Tbilisi) and Energo-Pro Georgia (rest of country). Georgia's grid operates at 220/380 V, 50 Hz — the same nominal voltage as China — reducing PCS voltage re-parameterisation risk, though project-specific grid-connection conditions must still be confirmed with GSE or the relevant distribution operator. The practical compliance gates for Chinese BESS exported to Georgia are: (1) GEOSTM conformity evidence aligned with IEC 62619 and IEC 62933 series, as China's GB/T standards are not accepted as equivalents in project or authority review under Georgia's IEC/EN-aligned framework; (2) GNERC licensing and GSE or distribution-operator grid-connection approval with applicable technical interface requirements; (3) local fire and building authority installation approval with documentation aligned with IEC or EN fire-safety standards; (4) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory under international transport conventions for shipments via Poti or Batumi ports, or overland; and (5) awareness that Georgia's grid is hydro-dominant with seasonal dependence on imports, making BESS an emerging but strategically important balancing and renewable integration technology.
