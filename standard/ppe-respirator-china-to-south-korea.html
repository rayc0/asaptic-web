Short answer

Anti-dust masks (방진마스크) used as occupational respiratory protective equipment in South Korea are 의무안전인증대상 (mandatory safety-certification target) protective equipment under the Industrial Safety and Health Act, requiring KOSHA/KMOEL Type Approval (안전인증) to the standard KS M 6673 with classes 특급/1급/2급 (special / class 1 / class 2) — self-certification is not permitted. Chinese KN95 (GB 2626) test reports do not satisfy the Korean type-approval requirements. A critical boundary: medical KF masks (보건용 마스크, KF80/KF94/KF99) are 의약외품 (quasi-drugs) regulated by MFDS, not occupational PPE — the wrong classification triggers a completely different regulatory route.

CRITICAL BOUNDARY: Anti-Dust Mask (PPE) vs. Medical KF Mask (Quasi-Drug) — Which Korean Regime Applies? China uses a three-track classification system for respiratory masks: (1) INDUSTRIAL / NON-MEDICAL: GB 2626-2019 (KN90/KN95) — particle respirators for occupational use, mandatory CCC, administered by SAMR; (2) MEDICAL-PROTECTIVE: GB 19083-2010 (≥95% filtration, medical-grade) — for use in highly infectious environments (e.g., SARS, COVID wards), administered by NMPA as Class II medical device; (3) SURGICAL MASK: YY 0469-2011 (Medical surgical mask — Technical requirements) — for surgical use, bacterial filtration efficiency ≥95%, NMPA Class II medical device.
