Short answer

FFP respirators entering Jordan fall under JSMO (Jordan Standards and Metrology Organization) mandatory technical regulations, which adopt GSO/EN 149 (FFP1/FFP2/FFP3) as the reference standard, and require a JSMO conformity certificate before customs clearance and market placement (typically via Aqaba port). Market access is importer-based: a Jordan-registered importer must hold the conformity documentation. The Ministry of Labour provides the occupational safety framework for workplace respirator use. Chinese KN95 (GB 2626) test reports do not by themselves satisfy the JSMO/EN 149 conformity route. Medical/surgical masks are handled through the national health regulator, not the PPE route.

Filtering Facepiece Respirator Safety — Jordanian Standard Adopting GSO/EN 149 Performance China's primary standard for non-powered air-purifying particle respirators is GB 2626-2019, with classes KN90 and KN95. It is a mandatory national standard enforced by SAMR. KN95 requires ≥95% filtration efficiency against NaCl particles at 85 L/min. Key differences from the EN 149 basis adopted by JSMO: GB 2626 uses sodium chloride （NaCl） aerosol only （EN 149 uses both NaCl and paraffin oil）, does not require the practical performance / simulated workplace test, and lacks the dolomite clogging resistance test. EN 149 specifies three performance classes: FFP1 （≥80% filtration）, FFP2 （≥94% filtration, ≤8% total inward leakage）, and FFP3 （≥99% filtration, ≤2% total inward leakage）.
