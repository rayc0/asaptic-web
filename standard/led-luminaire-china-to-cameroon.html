Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Cameroon must obtain ANOR (Agence des Normes et de la Qualite) conformity assessment for regulated products, with mandatory pre-shipment / import inspection under the Cameroon conformity programme; demonstrate electrical safety against NC/IEC 60598-1 (luminaires) and IEC 62560 (self-ballasted LED lamps) plus IEC 61347-2-13 for LED control gear; address photobiological safety via IEC 62471 where adopted as an NC; meet any applicable Cameroon energy-efficiency / energy-label programme requirement for lighting (an energy programme exists but is far less prescriptive than the EU Ecodesign / EPREL regime); obtain ART (Agence de Regulation des Telecommunications) radio type approval for smart/wireless luminaires; supply French-language documentation, marking and instructions; and appoint an in-country importer for customs clearance at Douala or Kribi. Cameroon adopts IEC standards as Normes Camerounaises rather than running an EU-style horizontal Ecodesign, RoHS, or REACH regime — there is no horizontal RoHS substance-restriction law in Cameroon comparable to EU RoHS, and no REACH-equivalent SVHC notification duty. Existing Chinese CCC certification does not substitute for ANOR conformity assessment.
