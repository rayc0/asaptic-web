Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Hungary, practical gaps are: confirm the applicable MSZT route and project-level approval path through MEKH and MAVIR; validate that IEC 62109-1/-2 safety testing, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 utility-interface evidence, and IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC documentation are accepted for Hungarian installations; and prove 230/400 V 50 Hz performance, protection thresholds, and reconnection timing per MAVIR grid-code requirements. Hungary is landlocked — import logistics run via Koper (Slovenia), Rijeka (Croatia), or Hamburg (Germany). Hungarian-language documentation is often required for local approval. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 documents are useful background but are not automatically interchangeable with Hungary/EU evidence.
