Short answer

The main Jordan gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular, or IoT wireless device are: (1) TRC mandatory type approval under Jordan Telecommunications Law No. 13 of 1995 (as amended) — the TRC conformity mark and TRC approval number must appear on the device and packaging label before import or sale; (2) EMC testing to ETSI EN 301 489 series or equivalent JISM/JS standards submitted as part of the TRC type approval application; (3) JISM Jordan Standards (JS) conformity assessment for regulated electrical and electronic product categories; (4) electrical safety to IEC 62368-1 under JISM standards (230 V / 50 Hz; Jordan uses a mixed plug environment with Type B, C, and G sockets); (5) a locally registered importer or authorized agent plus mandatory Arabic-language labelling under the Jordan Consumer Protection Law; and (6) awareness of Jordan NITC/NCSC cybersecurity guidelines and the Personal Data Protection Law No. 24/2023 for connected services. Chinese SRRC approval, CCC certification, CE DoC, and FCC ID are not recognized by TRC or JISM alone. Jordan Free Zone (ASEZ, Aqaba) procedures streamline import logistics but do not waive TRC type approval for domestic sale.
