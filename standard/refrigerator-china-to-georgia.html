Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face a Georgia market-access framework that is approximating EU technical regulations under the DCFTA but is lighter-touch than full CE today. Key points: (1) Electrical safety follows IEC 60335-2-24 (the same IEC base as China's GB 4706.13) administered via GEOSTM and national conformity/market surveillance — a genuine technical advantage versus the EU, though Chinese CCC test reports are not automatically accepted and may need re-issuance by an ILAC MRA laboratory. (2) Energy requirements run through national energy programmes that are aligning toward EU energy-labelling methodology; Chinese GB 12021.2 grades do not map directly. (3) Market access requires an in-country importer and, where radio/wireless features exist, GNCC type approval. (4) Grid voltage 220/380 V 50 Hz matches China exactly, removing the voltage/frequency redesign burden. R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned but charge documentation against IEC 60335-2-24 must be verified.
