Short answer

NCRTC mandatory type approval is the primary gap for any wireless or telecom terminal device exported from China to Sudan. CE mark, FCC authorization, and CCC are not recognized by NCRTC; SRRC approval does not satisfy NCRTC requirements. Tests must be conducted at NCRTC-accredited or ILAC/MRA-recognized laboratories. A local Sudanese authorized importer or agent is required. Electrical supply is 230 V / 50 Hz with Type C and D plugs most common (Type G in some older buildings). Arabic-language documentation is often required. Critical note: Sudan is subject to international sanctions — exporters must verify current US, EU, and UN sanctions status and obtain export-control legal advice before proceeding.
