Short answer

Lebanon does not have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation comparable to Saudi Arabia's SABER/IEC 62619 route or the EU Battery Regulation. The country's practical context is distinctive: Lebanon's chronic grid collapse has driven a massive boom in residential and commercial battery storage — predominantly private off-grid and hybrid systems paired with rooftop solar — where formal utility grid-connection by EDL applies only to a minority of projects. The practical compliance gates for Chinese BESS exported to Lebanon are: (1) LIBNOR product conformity — LIBNOR (Lebanese Standards Institution) adopts IEC standards and serves as the national conformity body for regulated products; importers and distributors bear primary responsibility for ensuring products meet applicable IEC-based requirements; (2) IEC 62619 and IEC 62133 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected technical baseline; (3) IEC 62933-5-2 system-level safety where referenced in specifications; (4) Lebanese Civil Defence fire-safety approval — critically important given the rapid, often informal scale-up of battery installations in Lebanon, which has created documented fire safety risks; (5) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory under international transport conventions; and (6) grid voltage and frequency confirmation: Lebanon operates at 220/380 V 50 Hz — the same nominal voltage as China, eliminating the voltage reconfiguration gap present in other markets. Chinese GB/T 36558-2023, GB/T 34120-2023, and NB/T 42090-2016 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC-standard evidence in project or authority review.

UN 38.3 Transport Safety Testing — Mandatory for Lithium Battery Imports to Lebanon Chinese BESS cell and module manufacturers are required to comply with UN 38.3 for export shipments under international transport conventions. The UN 38.3 Test Summary (required since January 1, 2020) must cover the specific cell or battery type being shipped. A Chinese-origin UN 38.3 test summary from a genuinely accredited laboratory is acceptable for Lebanon-bound shipments — the key gap is ensuring the test summary covers the specific cell model, chemistry, capacity, and configuration of the BESS units being shipped, and that it is maintained current with any cell design changes.
