Short answer

For Belgium-bound EV chargers, the most critical gaps are Type 2/CCS2 connector alignment under IEC 62196, compatibility with AFIR obligations as implemented through member-state practice, CE conformity evidence under LVD/EMC/RED where in scope, RCD/RDC protection per Belgian installation practice, and CREG/Elia/regional DSO (VREG/CWaPE/BRUGEL) installation and grid-operator coordination. 230/400 V 50 Hz parameters are required for project readiness. Dutch and French-language technical documentation is commonly required by Belgian installers and project owners. Antwerp and Zeebrugge are the primary entry ports.
