Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Togo, the practical gaps are ARSE / CEET coordination for any grid-connected installation, IEC 61851 and IEC 61000 evidence where required by conformity reviewers, project owners, utilities, or donor-funded tenders, connector conversion from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 for IEC-aligned vehicles, and firmware / protocol work for OCPP-managed chargers. Togo operates on a 220/380 V 50 Hz grid, close to China's domestic voltage baseline, but site capacity, voltage stability, load-shedding risk, high temperature, humidity, dust, and limited EV service infrastructure materially constrain deployment. A blanket Togo EVSE whole-unit mandatory certification rule could not be confirmed from official public sources as of 2026-06-14. Exporters should treat 2/3-wheel and low-power AC charging as the more realistic near-term market and verify ARSE, CEET, customs, and project-owner requirements before shipment.

Connector Interoperability — GB/T 20234 vs IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 China AC chargers use GB/T 20234.2 couplers and DC fast chargers use GB/T 20234.3 couplers. The GB/T AC connector differs from IEC Type 2 in connector gender, signaling, and contact arrangement; the GB/T DC connector differs geometrically from CCS2 and uses GB/T 27930 CAN communication rather than the CCS2 / IEC 61851-24 / ISO 15118 communication stack.
