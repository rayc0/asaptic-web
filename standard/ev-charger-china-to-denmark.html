Short answer

For Denmark-bound EV chargers, the most critical gaps are Type 2/CCS2 connector alignment under IEC 62196, compatibility with AFIR obligations as implemented through member-state practice, CE conformity evidence under LVD/EMC/RED where in scope, RCD/RDC protection, and Denmark/Energinet installation and grid-operator coordination. 230/400 V 50 Hz parameters are required for project readiness. Denmark's high penetration of wind power and advanced smart-charging ecosystem (including V2G pilots) means OCPP and smart-charging functionality are often expected even for commercial deployments. Port of Aarhus and Port of Copenhagen are the primary entry ports.
