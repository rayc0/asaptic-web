Short answer

The main Laos (MICT / NRTC) gaps for a China-made wireless, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular, LoRa, or IoT device are: (1) mandatory MICT type approval under the Lao Telecommunications Law for all radio and telecom equipment — Chinese SRRC, MIIT/NAL, CCC, and CE approvals are not recognised and have no regulatory standing in Laos; (2) NRTC radio frequency management compliance — Laos partially adopts ASEAN harmonised bands but 5 GHz sub-band assignments and other specific allocations must be verified against the Lao national frequency allocation table administered by MICT; (3) appointment of a local Lao importer or authorised agent as a practical prerequisite for MICT type approval and customs clearance through Lao Customs Department (key China-Laos border: Boten/Mohan via the China-Laos Railway corridor); (4) DOSM IEC-based electrical safety standards for a 230 V / 50 Hz grid with plug types A/B/C — China is 220 V, so equipment must be rated 220-240 V for grid compatibility; and (5) cybersecurity oversight by MICT Digital Economy and Society Department under Lao IT Law No. 20/NA (2012), currently advisory with no mandatory product-level certification as of 2026-06-17. Lao-language labelling is preferred for consumer products and regulatory requirements are actively developing under the ASEAN Digital Masterplan.
