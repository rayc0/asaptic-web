Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Ghana market-access requirements: Ghana Standards Authority (GSA) mandatory conformity assessment and destination inspection, GS standards adopting IEC 60598 / 62560 / 62471, Energy Commission appliance labelling and MEPS, and NCA type approval for wireless smart lighting, versus Chinese GB standards and CCC certification.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Ghana must meet Ghana Standards Authority (GSA) mandatory conformity-assessment / destination-inspection requirements for regulated lamps and luminaires; demonstrate electrical safety against GS standards adopting IEC 60598 / IEC 62560; meet Energy Commission appliance labelling and minimum energy performance standards (MEPS) requirements where the product is a regulated lighting appliance; obtain NCA (National Communications Authority) type approval for any wireless/smart lighting functionality; and route the shipment through an in-country importer for destination inspection at Tema or Takoradi. Ghana's grid is 230 V / 50 Hz — the 50 Hz frequency matches China but the nominal voltage differs from China's 220 V, so product rating must be verified. Note: Ghana does not operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS, battery, or outdoor-noise regime, so EU RoHS-style substance restrictions do not map across — China GB/T 26572 and GS/IEC safety standards are the relevant baselines. None of these Ghana obligations are satisfied by existing CCC certification alone.
