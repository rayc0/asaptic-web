Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Uganda, the key practical gaps are: (1) UNBS PVoC pre-export conformity for electrical/electronic goods including solar equipment and the related Certificate of Conformity; (2) ERA licensing, permit, guideline, or project approval requirements where the inverter is part of a generation, mini-grid, off-grid, C&I, or grid-connected project; (3) UMEME/UEDCL/UETCL connection review and commissioning evidence for grid-tied projects; (4) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 utility-interface evidence, and IEC 61000 / IEC 62920 EMC evidence; and (5) firmware validation against Uganda's 50 Hz, 240/415 V grid. China GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 baselines are useful technical references but are not automatically accepted as Uganda import, ERA, or utility connection approvals.
