Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Mongolia, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirming whether the exact inverter model, HS code, and voltage class require MASM conformity assessment or MNS standards documentation; (2) mapping China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence to MNS/GOST/IEC expectations instead of assuming automatic acceptance; (3) obtaining NDC and relevant distribution-company grid-connection acceptance for anti-islanding, protection, power-quality, and firmware settings; (4) confirming ERC licensing or project approval implications; and (5) documenting cold-climate operation and derating for Mongolian winter conditions, including sites that may reach approximately -40°C.
