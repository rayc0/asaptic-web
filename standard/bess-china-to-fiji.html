Short answer

Fiji does not have a comprehensive standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to mature certification regimes. BESS projects in Fiji operate under a framework involving: the Department of National Trade Measurement and Standards (DNTMS) for product conformity; the Fijian Competition and Consumer Commission (FCCC) for product safety and market conduct; and Energy Fiji Limited (EFL) for grid-connection technical requirements. Fiji is IEC-aligned with strong Australian/New Zealand standards influence given Pacific regional ties. The key practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Fiji are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline — Chinese GB 44240-2024 and GB/T 36276 are not accepted substitutes; (2) IEC 62933 system-level requirements where referenced in EFL connection agreements or project specifications; (3) NFPA 855-aligned fire safety installation design, as Fiji lacks a dedicated BESS fire code and NFPA is the primary international reference; (4) UN 38.3 transport test documentation, mandatory under international transport conventions; (5) voltage re-parameterisation — Fiji operates at 240/415 V 50 Hz, whereas China's nominal LV is 220/380 V, sharing 50 Hz frequency but differing in nominal voltage; and (6) coastal tropical derating — Fiji's cyclone zone and salt-mist environment require IP-rated, corrosion-resistant enclosures and structural compliance beyond Chinese domestic standards.

Chinese manufacturers typically hold UN 38.3 test reports and test summaries from CNAS-accredited testing laboratories such as UL, SGS, Bureau Veritas, TÜV, or CAICT.
