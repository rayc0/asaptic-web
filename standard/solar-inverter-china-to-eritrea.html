Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Eritrea, treat the exporter-facing market as essentially non-existent, project-led, and extremely early-stage rather than a mature national certification regime. The practical gaps are EEC or project-owner connection review, IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 utility-interface evidence, IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC and power-quality evidence, and documented derating for Horn of Africa heat and dust. Eritrea is a 50 Hz market but should be treated as 230/400 V, not China's 220/380 V; Chinese GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 evidence is not automatically accepted.
