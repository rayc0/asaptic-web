Short answer

Mauritius does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation directly equivalent to Saudi SABER/IEC 62619 or the EU Battery Regulation. BESS projects in Mauritius operate primarily under IEC-aligned international standards referenced in CEB grid-scale storage tenders and connection agreements, with overarching regulatory oversight by URA and MSB. The practical gaps for Chinese BESS exported to Mauritius are: (1) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline for project acceptance — MSB aligns with IEC and British Standards (BS) legacy, not Chinese GB; (2) IEC 62933 system-level technical requirements where referenced in CEB tender specifications; (3) fire-safety installation documentation aligned with NFPA 855 — a dominant BESS fire-installation code in IEC-aligned jurisdictions; (4) UN 38.3 and IEC 62281 transport documentation mandatory for lithium battery imports; (5) 50 Hz grid configuration for BESS power conversion systems (Mauritius and China both operate at 50 Hz, but voltage is 230/400 V in Mauritius versus 220/380 V in China — minimal but confirmable gap); and (6) coastal derating for cyclone exposure and salt-mist environment as required by CEB site acceptance criteria. Chinese GB/T 36276, GB/T 34131, GB 38031, and GB/T 36558 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC evidence in project or authority review.
