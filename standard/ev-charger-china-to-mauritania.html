Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Mauritania, the confirmed practical gap is not a mature public EVSE certification pathway; it is a layered project-risk problem. Exporters should verify the current DNPQ standards and conformity route, coordinate grid connection and operation with ARE and SOMELEC, map China GB/T 18487 evidence to IEC 61851-1 / IEC 61851-23, redesign GB/T 20234 connectors to IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 where IEC-aligned vehicles or tenders are expected, and derate electronics for Sahara heat, dust, weak-grid voltage variation, and low-electrification operating conditions. Mauritania's EV market appears extremely early-stage; broad public charging deployment should not be assumed without project-specific demand, grid capacity, and operating support.
