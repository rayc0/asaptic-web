Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Thailand requirements — mandatory TISI / TIS certification and the TIS mark (e.g. TIS 2780 LED lamp safety), EGAT Energy Label No.5 with DEDE MEPS, and NBTC type approval for smart lighting — versus Chinese GB / GB/T standards and CCC certification.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Thailand must obtain TISI mandatory TIS certification and apply the TIS mark (e.g. TIS 2780-2561 for LED lamp safety) through a licence held by an in-country importer or manufacturer; meet EGAT Energy Label No.5 efficiency criteria and DEDE Minimum Energy Performance Standards before the No.5 label can be displayed; obtain NBTC type approval where the luminaire has wireless/smart functions (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth); design to Thailand's 230 V / 50 Hz grid (same 50 Hz as China, but nominal voltage differs from China's 220 V); and address restricted-substance and hazardous-waste expectations even though Thailand has no horizontal RoHS in force (a WEEE/RoHS-style law has been in draft). None of these are covered by existing China CCC certification or GB-standard test reports, which Thailand does not recognise for TIS licensing.
