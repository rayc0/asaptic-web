Short answer

Armenia is a member of the Eurasian Economic Union (EAEU), which means EAEU Technical Regulations (TR CU / TR EAEU) and the EAC mark apply to in-scope products — this is a genuine certification pathway for Chinese BESS exporters and is materially different from non-EAEU markets such as Uzbekistan, Azerbaijan, or Georgia. The practical compliance gates for Chinese BESS exported to Armenia are: (1) EAEU TR CU 004/2011 (Low Voltage Equipment Safety) and TR CU 020/2011 (Electromagnetic Compatibility) where in scope — EAC certification from an EAEU-accredited certification body is required and Chinese GB certifications are not accepted as substitutes; (2) IEC 62619 cell and module safety evidence as the internationally expected baseline, and SARM may adopt IEC standards via its national adoption process; (3) PSRC (Public Services Regulatory Commission) grid-connection approval and ENA (Electric Networks of Armenia) interconnection requirements for grid-connected installations; (4) local fire and building authority approval for installation; (5) UN 38.3 transport test documentation mandatory under international transport conventions — Armenia is landlocked and shipments typically route via Georgia/Poti port or Iran, adding cross-border dangerous-goods documentation requirements; and (6) 50 Hz grid configuration — Armenia and China share the same nominal 50 Hz, 220/380 V grid, reducing voltage reconfiguration burden but not eliminating PSRC/ENA-specific protection parameter requirements. Chinese GB/T 36558-2023, GB/T 34120-2023, and NB/T 42090-2016 represent the manufacturing baseline but are not accepted as substitutes for EAEU TR CU EAC certification or IEC 62619 evidence.

Chinese manufacturers typically hold UN 38.3 test reports and test summaries from CNAS-accredited testing laboratories such as UL, SGS, Bureau Veritas, TÜV, or CAICT.
