Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Burkina Faso, the confirmed practical gaps are: connector incompatibility between GB/T 20234 and the IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 ecosystem that Burkina Faso follows as a Francophone IEC-aligned country; IEC 61851-1 and IEC 61851-23 safety evidence required in lieu of GB/T 18487 documentation; ABNORM conformity assessment coordination and ARSE regulatory approval for grid-connected installations; SONABEL grid-connection approval for the 220/380 V 50 Hz national grid, with severe electricity-deficit constraints that fundamentally limit 4-wheel fast-charging deployment; EMC evidence per IEC 61000 series; and high-ambient-temperature and dust derating for Sahel conditions (regularly exceeding 40 °C, heavy harmattan dust). Critically, Burkina Faso's 4-wheel EV market is extremely nascent — the primary e-mobility segment is 2/3-wheelers, and national electricity access remains very limited outside Ouagadougou. Any deployment project must engage SONABEL and ARSE early and budget for significant lead time on conformity assessment and grid-capacity review. As of 2026-06-14, a single published mandatory EVSE certification rule through ABNORM could not be confirmed; exporters must verify the current conformity route with ABNORM, ARSE, and local partners before shipment.
