Short answer

Eritrea does not have a confirmed national BESS product safety regulation, a recognised national standards body with BESS-specific technical regulations, or a mandatory product conformity scheme for energy storage equipment. The Eritrean Electricity Corporation (EEC) is the sole utility, operating an extremely low-electrification grid at 230/400 V 50 Hz. The BESS market in Eritrea is at an embryonic stage — essentially non-existent for commercial utility-scale projects — with activity limited to small donor-funded and off-grid solar-plus-storage installations. The practical compliance gaps for Chinese BESS exported to or installed in Eritrea are: (1) no national standards body or mandatory product certification route exists — project acceptance relies on internationally referenced standards (IEC 62619, IEC 62933, IEC 63056) required by donor agencies or project specifications; (2) grid voltage differs — Eritrea is 230/400 V whereas China is 220/380 V; both share 50 Hz but Chinese PCS protection settings must be reconfigured for 230/400 V; (3) no confirmed BESS-specific fire-installation authority or code adoption — NFPA 855 is the internationally referenced standard for donor-funded and project-level fire-safety design; (4) UN 38.3 transport compliance is mandatory under international conventions for lithium battery shipments to Massawa port — Chinese-origin test summaries from accredited laboratories are accepted if covering the specific cell model; (5) extreme heat and dust conditions in Eritrea require derating and environmental hardening beyond Chinese domestic design assumptions. Chinese GB/T standards (GB/T 36276, GB/T 34131, GB 38031, GB/T 36558) are not accepted as substitutes for IEC-series evidence in project or donor-agency review.
