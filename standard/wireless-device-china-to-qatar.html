Short answer

The main Qatar gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT wireless device are: (1) CRA mandatory type approval under Law No. 34 of 2006 on Telecommunications — the CRA type approval certificate number must appear on the device and packaging label in Arabic and English before import or sale; (2) EMC testing to QSMO/QS standards (harmonised with CISPR / ETSI EN 301 489) submitted as part of the CRA type approval application; (3) electrical safety to IEC 62368-1 under QS standards (240 V / 50 Hz / Type G British plug); (4) a Qatari authorized dealer or registered agent required for CRA registration; and (5) awareness of Qatar NCC cybersecurity guidelines under the National Cyber Security Framework (advisory for consumer IoT, mandatory for critical-infrastructure products). Chinese SRRC approval, CCC certification, CE DoC, and FCC ID are not accepted by CRA. The GCC Mutual Recognition Arrangement exists but CRA type approval remains nationally enforced in practice.
