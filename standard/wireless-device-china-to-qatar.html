Short answer

The main Qatar gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular, or IoT wireless device are: (1) CRA Qatar mandatory type approval — the CRA approval certificate and approval number must appear on device and packaging labels (Arabic and English) before import or sale; (2) EMC testing per CISPR-based GSO standards submitted as part of the CRA type approval application; (3) a 5 GHz indoor-use restriction applies to W52 and W53 sub-bands; (4) electrical safety to IEC 62368-1 under GSO standards at 240 V / 50 Hz / Type G (BS 1363) — the same mains standard as Kuwait; (5) a Qatari authorized importer or distributor must be appointed; and (6) awareness of Qatar cybersecurity obligations under Law No. 14 of 2014 on Cybercrime and NCSA guidance (no mandatory pre-market IoT device certification as of 2026). The GCC Mutual Recognition Arrangement creates a strong incentive for a GCC-wide type approval strategy: a CRA Qatar certificate may be leveraged for recognition by CITRA Kuwait, TRA UAE, and NTRA Bahrain under bilateral GCC MRA arrangements. Chinese SRRC approval, CCC certification, CE DoC, and FCC ID are not directly accepted by CRA Qatar.
