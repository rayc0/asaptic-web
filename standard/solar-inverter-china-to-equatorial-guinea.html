Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Equatorial Guinea, the key practical gaps are: (1) no confirmed strong national standards body route for inverter product certification, so acceptance is normally project-, utility-, customs-, or lender-led; (2) SEGESA grid-interface review and project owner requirements must be treated as the real connection gate; (3) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 utility-interface, IEC 62920 EMC, and IEC 61000 evidence should be prepared because China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 are not automatically accepted; (4) firmware must be validated for Equatorial Guinea's 50 Hz, 230/400 V grid, which shares China's 50 Hz frequency but differs from China's 220/380 V nominal voltage; and (5) tropical humidity and Spanish-language documentation should be addressed for this small, nascent grid-tie market in an oil/gas economy.
