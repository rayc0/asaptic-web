Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Cambodia, the three mandatory compliance gates are: (1) ISC (Institute of Standards of Cambodia) conformity — CS standards adopting IEC 62109-1/-2 for safety; (2) EAC (Electricity Authority of Cambodia) licensing and grid-connection approval, including IEC 62116 anti-islanding compliance and EDC utility-connection acceptance for the 230/400 V, 50 Hz Cambodia grid; and (3) EDC (Electricité du Cambodge) connection requirements for utility-scale and rooftop C&I projects. China GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 baselines are not automatically accepted in Cambodia and cannot substitute IEC-referenced evidence without supplementary IEC test documentation. EAC rooftop-solar policy, capacity caps, and technical requirements are actively evolving — verify current rules directly with EAC before project commitment.

IEC 62109-1 / IEC 62109-2 Safety Evidence — ISC (CS Standards) and EAC Cambodia Grid-Connection Requirements China's primary voluntary standard for PV grid-connected inverter safety is GB/T 37408-2021 (Technical Requirements for Photovoltaic Grid-Connected Inverters), which covers classification, environmental conditions, safety, electrical performance, EMC, and markings.
