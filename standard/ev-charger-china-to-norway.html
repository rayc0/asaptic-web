Short answer

For Norway-bound EV chargers, the most critical gaps are Type 2/CCS2 connector alignment under IEC 62196, compatibility with AFIR obligations as implemented via the EEA Agreement, CE conformity evidence under LVD/EMC/RED where in scope, RCD/RDC protection (noting Norway's historically mixed IT/TN earthing environment requiring Type B RCD compatibility verification), and NVE/DSO grid coordination. 230/400 V 50 Hz parameters are required for project readiness. Norway's world-leading EV market means AFIR and connector compliance requirements are enforced rigorously.
