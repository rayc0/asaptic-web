Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Armenia, the confirmed practical gaps are EAEU Technical Regulation and EAC conformity for in-scope products, IEC-family EVSE safety evidence, connector conversion from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC, PSRC / ENA coordination for grid-connected installations, EMC evidence under IEC 61000 and TR CU 020/2011, and OCPP or project-specific back-office interoperability for networked chargers. Armenia is an EAEU member, so TR CU 004/2011 for low-voltage equipment, TR CU 020/2011 for EMC, and the EAC mark are a genuine pathway for in-scope EV charging equipment. This is materially different from non-EAEU markets where EAC does not apply. Armenia's grid is commonly treated as 220/380 V, 50 Hz, which is close to China's nominal 220/380 V, 50 Hz baseline, but Chinese domestic GB/T 18487.1-2023, GB/T 20234 connector conformity, and GB/T 27930 communication evidence are not accepted as substitutes for EAEU / SARM conformity, IEC connector compatibility, or PSRC / ENA project acceptance.
