Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Qatar, the confirmed practical gaps are IEC-based safety and EMC evidence required by Kahramaa guidelines (Section 07 / IEC 61851), connector redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 since Qatar's IEC-aligned infrastructure is physically incompatible with GB/T connectors, Kahramaa Tarsheed project-acceptance for grid-connected installations, IP65 / IP54 enclosure protection for Qatar's desert climate, and OCPP back-office interoperability for networked chargers. Qatar operates on 240 V / 50 Hz, versus China's 220 V / 50 Hz domestic baseline, which requires voltage-range and derating confirmation. A blanket Qatar whole-unit mandatory EVSE certification rule through QGOSM could not be confirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters should verify the current conformity assessment route, HS code, charger type, and project approval path before shipment.

Connector Interoperability — GB/T 20234 vs IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 China AC chargers use GB/T 20234.2 couplers and DC fast chargers use GB/T 20234.3 couplers. Although the GB/T 20234.2 AC coupler has a similar overall shape to the IEC 62196 Type 2, they differ in connector gender (GB/T uses male connector at the charger and female vehicle inlet, opposite to Type 2), signaling protocol (CC/CP versus PP/CP), and contact arrangement, making them physically and electrically incompatible.
