Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Cameroon, the confirmed practical gaps are: (1) connector hardware redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) / CCS2 (DC), as Cameroon follows the IEC 62196 ecosystem and GB/T connectors are physically incompatible with IEC 62196 vehicle inlets; (2) IEC 61851-1 / IEC 61851-23 safety evidence from an accredited laboratory, since GB/T 18487 test reports are not accepted as direct IEC equivalents; (3) ANOR pre-shipment import conformity (PECAE programme) for electrical equipment; (4) ARSEL authorisation for charging-station installation and operation; (5) ENEO grid-connection coordination for installations drawing significant load; (6) EMC compliance to IEC 61000 series; (7) bilingual French/English documentation as required in Cameroon. Grid supply is 230/400 V 50 Hz, identical to China's frequency but with slightly higher nominal voltage (220/380 V China domestic), requiring voltage-range confirmation. Cameroon's EV market is very nascent and grid reliability is uneven; exporters should assess deployment feasibility and after-sales support realistically. A blanket mandatory whole-unit EVSE certification route through a single ANOR programme could not be confirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14; exporters should verify the current conformity assessment route with ANOR before shipment.

Connector Interoperability — GB/T 20234 vs IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 China AC chargers use GB/T 20234.2 couplers and DC fast chargers use GB/T 20234.3 couplers. Although the GB/T 20234.2 AC coupler has a similar overall shape to the IEC 62196 Type 2, they differ in connector gender (GB/T uses male connector at the charger and female vehicle inlet, opposite to Type 2), signaling protocol (CC/CP versus PP/CP), and contact arrangement, making them physically and electrically incompatible. GB/T 20234.3 DC couplers are geometrically different from CCS2 and use a nine-pin configuration with CAN bus via GB/T 27930 communication, incompatible with the CCS2 / IEC 61851-24 communication stack.
