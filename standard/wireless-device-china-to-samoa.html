Short answer

The main Samoa gaps for a China-made wireless or IoT device are: (1) OCC (Office of the Communications Commissioner) mandatory type approval for radio and telecom terminal equipment under the Communications Act 2016 — SRRC, MIIT NAL, CE, FCC, and CCC are not recognised; (2) electrical compliance for 230 V / 50 Hz and Type I (Australian AS/NZS 3112) plug — Chinese CCC electrical approval is not recognised; (3) appointment of a local Samoan importer or agent, mandatory for market access; (4) EMC alignment with SAME / AS/NZS-referenced standards, with AS/NZS accredited lab reports accepted by OCC; and (5) English and/or Samoan labelling required. CE, FCC, and CCC marks are not recognised in Samoa.
