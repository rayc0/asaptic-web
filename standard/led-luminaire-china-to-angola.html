Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Angolan market-access requirements: IANORQ conformity assessment and adopted NA/IEC standards (IEC 60598 luminaire safety, IEC 62560/62471 lamp and photobiological safety), the national energy-labelling programme, INACOM radio approval for wireless products, Portuguese-language documentation, and the in-country importer requirement, versus Chinese GB/GB-T standards and CCC certification.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Angola should expect requirements driven by IANORQ (Instituto Angolano de Normalizacao e Qualidade), which adopts IEC standards as Angolan NA standards and operates conformity and import-inspection processes for regulated products: electrical safety to NA/IEC 60598 (luminaires) and NA/IEC 62560 (self-ballasted lamps); photobiological safety to NA/IEC 62471 where applicable; and the national energy-labelling programme for lighting. Wireless/smart luminaires additionally require INACOM (Instituto Angolano das Comunicacoes) radio approval. Technical and safety documentation, labels, and instructions generally need to be in Portuguese, and a local in-country importer/representative is required to clear and place goods. Angola does NOT have an EU-style horizontal RoHS, REACH-SVHC, or Ecodesign regime — there is no direct Angolan equivalent restricting the 10 RoHS substances or mandating SVHC supply-chain notification; the China RoHS disclosure label and CCC certification do not by themselves satisfy Angolan conformity, and the 220 V / 50 Hz grid is similar to China's single-phase nominal.
