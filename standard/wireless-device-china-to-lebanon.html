Short answer

The main Lebanon gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular, or IoT wireless device are: (1) TRA mandatory type approval under Lebanon Telecom Law 431/2002 (هيئة ناظمة للاتصالات) — a TRA type approval certificate is required before import or sale; technical file and accredited-lab test reports must be submitted; (2) EMC testing to LIBNOR national standards (NL series, aligned with ETSI/IEC); CE test reports may be submitted as a basis but are not directly recognised — TRA makes its own determination; (3) electrical safety to IEC 62368-1 or equivalent NL standard under Lebanese infrastructure (220 V / 50 Hz; mixed Type C, G, and some Type D sockets — note frequent power outages due to Lebanon's ongoing economic crisis); (4) a locally registered Lebanese authorized importer or agent is required; bilingual Arabic/French documentation is strongly preferred by TRA and customs; (5) cybersecurity awareness under Law 81/2018 on Electronic Transactions and Personal Data and the developing Lebanese Cybercrime Law for connected services. Chinese SRRC approval, CCC certification, CE DoC, and FCC ID are not accepted as substitutes by TRA. Lebanon has no mutual recognition arrangement with China, the EU, or the US that would waive TRA type approval.
