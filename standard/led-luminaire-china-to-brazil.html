Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Brazil's INMETRO compulsory conformity assessment under Portaria INMETRO 389/2021, ABNT NBR IEC 60598 / 62560 safety standards, ANATEL homologation for wireless functions, and PBE/Selo PROCEL energy labelling versus Chinese GB standards and CCC certification.

Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer entering the Brazil market must obtain INMETRO compulsory certification through an accredited certification body (OCP) under Portaria INMETRO 389/2021, with testing to ABNT NBR IEC 60598-1 (luminaires) and ABNT NBR IEC 62560 (self-ballasted LED lamps); design products for the 127/220 V, 60 Hz grid rather than China's 220/380 V, 50 Hz; register the product and obtain the INMETRO registration number and compliance seal; meet PBE/Selo PROCEL energy labelling administered by INMETRO and Eletrobras; obtain ANATEL homologation for any luminaire with wireless functionality; and appoint an in-country importer or representative holding a Brazilian CNPJ to act before INMETRO — none of which is covered by existing Chinese CCC certification.
