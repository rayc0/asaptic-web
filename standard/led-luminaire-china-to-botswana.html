Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Botswana should expect conformity assessment and import inspection administered by the Botswana Bureau of Standards (BOBS) for regulated electrical/lighting products, against BOS standards that adopt IEC (and frequently SANS) requirements: electrical safety to BOS/IEC 60598 (luminaires) and IEC 62560 (self-ballasted lamps); photobiological safety referencing IEC 62471; and EMC/radio-disturbance to the IEC/CISPR-aligned BOS standard. Botswana operates a national energy-efficiency labelling programme for lamps (mapping to what the EU would handle via Ecodesign/energy label) — confirm current scope and registration with BOBS. Smart/wireless luminaires additionally require BOCRA (Botswana Communications Regulatory Authority) radio-type approval. An in-country importer of record is typically needed, and goods route via Durban or Walvis Bay as Botswana is landlocked. Note the 230 V / 50 Hz grid (50 Hz matches China; nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V). Crucially, Botswana has NO EU-style horizontal RoHS, REACH/SVHC, or battery/outdoor-noise regime — those EU obligations have no Botswana counterpart, so China GB/CCC documentation must be re-mapped to the BOBS conformity route rather than assumed equivalent.
