Short answer

For BESS exported from China to Ethiopia, the main gaps are ECAE PVoC import conformity where product categories are regulated, IEC 62619 battery safety and IEC 62933 system-level evidence, UN 38.3 transport testing for lithium cells, Ethiopian Energy Authority approval for storage connected to the EEU grid, and fire-safety and installation requirements. China GB 44240-2024 and GB/T 36276-2023 provide useful technical baseline documentation but do not replace Ethiopian conformity assessment or grid approval.

UN 38.3 (required for international transport regardless of domestic standard)		 UN 38.3 (Part III, Section 38.3 of the UN Manual of Tests and Criteria) specifies eight tests for lithium metal and lithium-ion cells and batteries to be transported safely by air, sea, or land. Passing UN 38.3 is mandatory for international transport of all lithium batteries under the IATA Dangerous Goods Regulations (DGR), IMDG Code, and ADR. Since January 2020, a UN 38.3 Test Summary document must be provided along the supply chain. BESS lithium battery modules and cells shipped from China to Ethiopia must comply with the applicable dangerous-goods transport regulations for the chosen shipping mode.UN Manual of Tests and Criteria — Part III, Section 38.3 (UN 38.3)
