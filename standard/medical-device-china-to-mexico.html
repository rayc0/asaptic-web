Short answer

Mexico generally requires COFEPRIS sanitary registration before sale or import of medical devices, local Class I/II/III classification, applicable NOM compliance including NOM-241-SSA1 GMP and Spanish labelling under NOM-137-SSA1, plus a Mexican registration holder or licensed importer. FDA, EU, Canada, or Japan approvals may support an expedited equivalence route but do not replace COFEPRIS authorization.

COFEPRIS medical device sanitary registration procedures		 NMPA approval is not a Mexican market authorization.
