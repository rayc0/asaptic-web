Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Antigua and Barbuda, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirming ABBS standards or conformity documentation requested for the shipment; (2) obtaining APUA project-specific grid-connection acceptance; (3) preparing IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 utility-interface, and IEC 61000 / IEC 62920 EMC evidence; (4) validating firmware for Antigua and Barbuda's 230/400 V, 50 Hz grid. China also uses 50 Hz, but China's nominal 220/380 V voltage is different and should not be treated as a voltage match. China GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 baselines are useful engineering background only and are not automatically accepted by ABBS, APUA, or project owners.
