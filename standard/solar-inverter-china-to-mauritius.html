Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Mauritius, the main practical gaps are: (1) MSB conformity and IEC-aligned evidence expectations in a BS/IEC-influenced standards system; (2) URA and CEB approval for SSDG/MSDG distributed-generation projects before connection; (3) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence; (4) IEC 62116 and IEC 61727 grid-interface evidence for a 230/400 V, 50 Hz network; (5) IEC 61000 / IEC 62920 EMC evidence; and (6) coastal cyclone and salt-mist derating documentation. China GB/T 37408-2021, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 are useful engineering baselines but are not automatically accepted for Mauritius MSB/URA/CEB gates without supplementary IEC evidence and local project approval.
