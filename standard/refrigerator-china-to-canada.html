Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face five major Canada compliance gaps: (1) NRCan Energy Efficiency Regulations, 2016 minimum energy performance requirements and reporting before import or interprovincial shipment; (2) required Canadian EnerGuide household appliance label content and format; (3) Canadian electrical product certification/approval through an SCC-accredited certification body to applicable CSA C22.2 standards such as CSA C22.2 No. 63 and CSA C22.2 No. 120, where applicable; (4) refrigerant and HFC controls under Canada's Ozone-depleting Substances and Halocarbon Alternatives Regulations; and (5) English/French bilingual consumer labelling and documentation. Chinese CCC, GB 4706.13, and GB 12021.2 files help technical preparation but do not replace Canadian regulatory filings, Canadian energy labels, or Canadian electrical approval marks.
