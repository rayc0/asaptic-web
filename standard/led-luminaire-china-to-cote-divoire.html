Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Côte d'Ivoire should expect to meet CODINORM national standards (NI), which adopt the IEC base (IEC 60598 for luminaires, IEC 62560 for self-ballasted lamps, IEC 62471 for photobiological safety) — the same IEC family that underlies China's GB standards, so the technical content is largely shared. The key differences are procedural and documentary: regulated electrical products typically pass through a pre-shipment Verification of Conformity (VoC) programme administered under the Ministry of Trade with CODINORM involvement, requiring a Certificate of Conformity per shipment or product registration; an energy-efficiency label may apply; smart/wireless luminaires require ARTCI radio approval; all documentation should be available in French; and an in-country importer is needed for clearance at Abidjan or San-Pedro. Côte d'Ivoire does not operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS hazardous-substance regime, so there is no standalone RoHS substance-restriction obligation equivalent to the EU directive — verify current product scope before each shipment. None of these steps are covered by existing Chinese CCC certification.
