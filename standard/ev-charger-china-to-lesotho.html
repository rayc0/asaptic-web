Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Lesotho, the unusual positive point is that Lesotho's 220 V single-phase / 380 V three-phase, 50 Hz grid genuinely matches China's nominal 220/380 V 50 Hz design baseline. That does not make a China domestic charger Lesotho-ready. The main practical gaps are: (1) LSI / project acceptance of IEC-based safety and EMC evidence, especially IEC 61851-1, IEC 61851-23, IEC 62196, and IEC 61000; (2) connector redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC, consistent with South African / IEC regional practice; (3) LEWA and LEC approval for grid-connected installations and any commercial electricity service; (4) site-level power-capacity assessment because Lesotho imports most electricity from South Africa through Eskom interconnections and supplements with Muela hydro; (5) OCPP-ready networking where a public or fleet charging project requires remote monitoring, payment, or load management. A dedicated mandatory Lesotho EVSE product certification route could not be confirmed from official sources as of 2026-06-14, so exporters should verify the exact LSI, LEWA, LEC, customs, and project route before shipment.
