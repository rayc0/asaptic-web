Short answer

The main Tonga gaps for a China-made wireless or IoT device are: (1) TAL (Tele-Communications Authority of Tonga, under the Ministry of Meteorology, Energy, Information, Disaster Management, Environment, Climate Change and Communications) mandatory type approval for radio and telecom terminal equipment — SRRC and MIIT NAL are not recognised; (2) electrical compliance for 240 V / 50 Hz and Type I (Australian AS/NZS 3112) plug — Chinese CCC electrical approval is not recognised, but AS/NZS test reports are accepted; (3) appointment of a local Tongan importer or agent, which is practically required in this very small market (~100K population); (4) English and/or Tongan-language labelling; and (5) basic cybersecurity obligations under the Computer Crimes Act 2003 where the device connects to Tongan networks. CE, FCC, and CCC marks are not recognised in Tonga.
