Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer entering the South Korea market must obtain the KC Mark under the Electrical Appliances and Consumer Products Safety Control Act (administered by KATS under MOTIE), with the applicable conformity route — KC Safety Certification, Self-regulatory Safety Confirmation, or Supplier's Declaration of Conformity — depending on the product's risk class; meet electrical safety under KS C 7653 / IEC 60598 (EN 60598-1 equivalent) verified at a KATS-designated lab such as KTC, KTL, or KTR; satisfy KC-EMC requirements administered by the RRA (covering both emissions and immunity for lighting equipment, CISPR 15 / IEC 61547-aligned); register for the Korean energy efficiency grade label (에너지소비효율등급, 1–5 grades) via KEA/KEMCO where the product is a designated energy-using product; document photobiological safety to IEC 62471; comply with the RoHS-equivalent restrictions under the Act on Resource Circulation of Electrical and Electronic Equipment and Vehicles; and track K-REACH chemical obligations — none of which are covered by existing CCC certification. Note: Korea's grid is 220 V / 60 Hz versus China's 220 V / 50 Hz, so frequency-sensitive components and any energy/EMC test conditions must be re-verified.
