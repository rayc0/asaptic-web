Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Mauritius should expect the Mauritius Standards Bureau (MSB) to be the central reference body — MSB adopts Mauritius Standards (MS), typically aligned with IEC, and operates conformity and import-inspection arrangements for regulated products. Practical compliance points: confirm electrical safety to the MSB-adopted MS/IEC 60598 series (luminaires) and IEC 62560 (self-ballasted LED lamps); supply EMC evidence aligned with CISPR 15 (the IEC base shared by China GB 17743); document photobiological safety to IEC 62471 where applicable; engage with the Mauritius energy-efficiency labelling programme for lamps where in scope; and obtain ICTA (Information and Communication Technologies Authority) type approval for any smart luminaire with wireless functionality. Design for a 230 V / 50 Hz grid (50 Hz matches China; nominal voltage differs from China 220 V). Mauritius does not operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS substance restriction or a REACH SVHC supply-chain notification regime — those EU-only obligations have no direct Mauritius counterpart, though China RoHS disclosure practice still applies for the China market. None of the Mauritius market-access steps are covered by existing Chinese CCC certification, which is not recognised in Mauritius. Verify all requirements with MSB and an in-country importer before shipment.
