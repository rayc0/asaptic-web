Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China LED luminaire documentation against Qatar market-access requirements: QGOSM conformity and registration under MOCI, GSO/IEC lighting safety standards (GSO IEC 60598, GSO IEC 62560, GSO IEC 62471), GSO energy-efficiency labelling, and CRA type approval for smart/wireless luminaires — versus Chinese GB / GB/T standards and CCC certification. Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Qatar must meet QGOSM (Qatar General Organization for Standards and Metrology, under MOCI) conformity and registration requirements for regulated products; comply with GSO/IEC lighting safety standards (GSO IEC 60598 for luminaires, GSO IEC 62560 for self-ballasted LED lamps, GSO IEC 62471 for photobiological safety) rather than the GB/EN equivalents; meet GSO EMC requirements (GSO CISPR 15 family); affix the GSO energy-efficiency label per the GCC energy-efficiency labelling scheme for in-scope lighting products; obtain CRA (Communications Regulatory Authority) type approval for any luminaire with wireless functions; design for a 240 V / 50 Hz grid (50 Hz matches China, but the 240 V nominal voltage differs from China's 220 V); and ship through a licensed in-country importer. Qatar has no horizontal RoHS analogous to the EU; hazardous-substance control is addressed through specific GSO substance rules and product safety standards where applicable. None of these are covered by existing CCC certification or China Energy Label registration.
