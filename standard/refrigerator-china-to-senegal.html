Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face several Senegal market-access gaps: (1) a pre-shipment Programme for Evaluation of Conformity (PEC / Verification of Conformity, VoC) inspection administered for the Ministry of Trade is typically required for regulated imported products, with a Certificate of Conformity needed for customs clearance at the Port of Dakar; (2) electrical safety must be demonstrated to NS/IEC 60335-2-24 (ASN adopts IEC standards as NS), and CCC/GB 4706.13 certification is not directly accepted, although IEC 60335-2-24-based CB Scheme reports ease re-use; (3) an energy label / minimum energy performance regime is being rolled out for refrigerators in Senegal/West Africa and should be verified for the specific model; (4) all documentation, instructions, and labelling must be available in French; and (5) an in-country importer is required. R-600a refrigerant is well-positioned, but charge documentation against IEC 60335-2-24 must be verified. Senegal does not operate EU-style horizontal RoHS, battery, or outdoor-noise regimes.
