Regulatory comparison of lithium battery and power bank compliance requirements for export from China to the United States. Covers NRTL safety listing (UL 1642, UL 2054, UL 2056), DOT/PHMSA transport rules (49 CFR 173.185, UN 38.3), FCC authorization for wireless products, CPSC market access requirements, and state/local rules including NYC Local Law 39 and California.

Short answer

To export lithium batteries or power banks from China to the United States, you need: (1) NRTL safety listing under UL 1642 (cells), UL 2054 (packs), or UL 2056 (power banks) — there is no single federal mandatory mark, but NRTL listing is required by Amazon, major retailers, and Authorities Having Jurisdiction; Chinese CCC/GB 31241 certification does not substitute; (2) UN 38.3 test report plus 49 CFR 173.185 (DOT/PHMSA) compliance for transport — packaging, Class 9 labelling, state-of-charge limits; (3) FCC authorization under 47 CFR Part 15 if the product has any wireless function (Bluetooth, NFC, Wi-Fi, Qi); (4) CPSC compliance and readiness for recall obligations; (5) awareness of state/local rules — NYC Local Law 39 bans non-UL e-mobility batteries; California has additional application requirements.
