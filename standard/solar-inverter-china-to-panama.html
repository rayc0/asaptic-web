Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Panama, the main practical gaps are: (1) COPANIT / DGNTI conformity and any locally adopted IEC standard references; (2) ASEP plus EDEMET, EDECHI, or ENSA distributor interconnection review for grid-tied or net-metered projects; (3) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence; (4) IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 utility-interface, and project-specific protection settings; (5) IEC 61000 / IEC 62920 EMC and power-quality evidence; and (6) reconfiguration and retesting for Panama's 120/240 V, 60 Hz grid. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 evidence is useful background, but it is 50 Hz China-grid evidence and should not be treated as automatic Panama acceptance.
