China-to-Ghana Wireless / IoT Device Compliance Gap Matrix (NCA / GSA)

Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and IoT device documentation against Ghana National Communications Authority (NCA) mandatory type approval under the Electronic Communications Act 2008 (Act 775), Ghana Standards Authority (GSA) technical standards, electrical safety requirements (230 V / 50 Hz, Type G plug), local importer obligations, Ghana Revenue Authority / GCNet customs processing, and the Cyber Security Authority (CSA) under the Cyber Security Act 2020 (Act 1038).

Short answer

NCA mandatory type approval (with NCA sticker and certificate number on product) under the Electronic Communications Act 2008 (Act 775), GSA-based technical standards compliance (GS standards adopting IEC/ETSI), electrical safety compliance (230 V / 50 Hz, Type G plug), engagement of a local Ghanaian importer or dealer, and GCNet customs processing are the major Ghana compliance gaps compared with Chinese SRRC, MIIT network access licence, CCC, and GB-standard documentation. CE and FCC marks are not recognised by NCA. The Cyber Security Act 2020 (Act 1038) is in force but mandatory IoT device certification under CSA is still developing.
