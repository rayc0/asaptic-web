Public-interest, source-linked comparison of common China Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and IoT device documentation against Ghana National Communications Authority (NCA) mandatory type approval under the Electronic Communications Act 2008 (Act 775), Ghana Standards Authority (GSA) conformity assessment under GS standards for EMC and electrical safety, 230 V / 50 Hz Type G supply requirements, local Ghanaian importer obligations, and Cyber Security Authority (CSA) obligations under the Cybersecurity Act 2020 (Act 1038).

Short answer

NCA mandatory type approval (with NCA certificate and label on product) under the Electronic Communications Act 2008 (Act 775), GSA conformity assessment under GS standards for EMC and electrical safety (230 V / 50 Hz, Type G plug), engagement of a local Ghanaian importer or distributor, and awareness of the Cybersecurity Act 2020 (Act 1038) are the major Ghana compliance gaps compared with Chinese SRRC, MIIT network access licence, CCC, and GB-standard documentation. CE and FCC marks are not recognised by NCA. A 5 GHz indoor-use restriction applies. The ECOWAS harmonised type approval framework is under development and may reduce per-country costs in West Africa in future.
