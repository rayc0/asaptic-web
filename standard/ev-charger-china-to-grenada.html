Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Grenada, the practical gaps are grid-voltage confirmation, IEC connector localisation, IEC safety and EMC evidence, and site approval with the utility and regulator. Grenada uses a 230/400 V, 50 Hz electricity system, while China domestic chargers are commonly documented around 220/380 V, 50 Hz; frequency is the same, but nominal voltage differs and input range, protection, metering, and thermal ratings must be confirmed. Grenada's UK/IEC legacy points toward IEC 62196 Type 2 for AC and CCS2 for DC, not the Americas J1772 / CCS1 ecosystem. GB/T 20234 connectors and GB/T 27930 DC communication are physically and protocol incompatible. The local EV market is essentially non-existent or very early-stage, so exporters should treat each shipment as a project-specific GDBS / PURC / GRENLEC review, with added hurricane, salt-mist, and sea-freight packaging controls for St George's import logistics.
