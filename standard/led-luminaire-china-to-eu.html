Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer entering the EU market must obtain CE marking under LVD (EN 60598-1), EMC (EN 55015 / EN 61547), and RoHS directives; comply with Ecodesign minimum efficacy, CRI, lifetime, and power factor requirements (Reg EU 2019/2020); ensure EU energy labelling and EPREL registration obligations are met for the light source component (Reg (EU) 2019/2015 applies to the light source / containing-product — a luminaire with a non-replaceable integrated LED source falls in scope as a light source; verify product configuration); add a rescaled A-G energy label and blue light hazard class to packaging where applicable; test the 4 EU-added phthalates not covered by China RoHS; appoint an EU Authorised Representative; and track REACH SVHC obligations biannually — none of which are covered by existing CCC certification.

The energy class is calculated from the Energy Efficiency Index (EEI) derived from the product's luminous efficacy relative to a reference value. Scope note: the energy labelling obligation under Reg (EU) 2019/2015 applies to the light source (and separate control gear), not automatically to a whole luminaire as such.
