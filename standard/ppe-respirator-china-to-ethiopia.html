Short answer

Filtering facepiece respirators (FFP1/FFP2/FFP3) entering Ethiopia are assessed against Ethiopian Standards (ES) that adopt EN 149, with conformity and import inspection administered by ESA and CACEA / Ministry of Trade, framed by the Ministry of Labour and Skills occupational-safety regime. Chinese KN95 (GB 2626) test reports do not directly satisfy the adopted ES/EN 149 requirements. A local importer of record is required for customs clearance and conformity. A critical boundary: medical/surgical masks are regulated by the Ethiopian Food and Drug Authority (EFDA) as medical devices, not as occupational PPE — the wrong classification triggers a different regulatory route.

Filtering Facepiece Respirator Safety — Ethiopian Standard Adopting EN 149 FFP Performance China's primary standard for non-powered air-purifying particle respirators is GB 2626-2019 （Respiratory protective equipment — Non-powered air-purifying particle respirator）, with two performance classes: KN90 and KN95. GB 2626-2019 is a mandatory national standard （GB, not GB/T） enforced by SAMR. KN95 requires ≥95% filtration efficiency against NaCl particles at 85 L/min flow rate. Key differences from EN 149: GB 2626 uses sodium chloride （NaCl） aerosol only （EN 149 uses both NaCl and paraffin oil）, does not require the practical performance test （simulated workplace test）, and lacks the dolomite clogging resistance test. EN 149 specifies three performance classes: FFP1 （≥80% filtration）, FFP2 （≥94% filtration, ≤8% total inward leakage）, and FFP3 （≥99% filtration, ≤2% total inward leakage）.
