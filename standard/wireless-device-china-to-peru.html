Short answer

The main Peru compliance gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT device are: MTC mandatory homologación — all radio and telecom terminal equipment must obtain an MTC homologation certificate and display the approval number on a Spanish-language label before import or sale, with no recognition of FCC, CE, SRRC, or CCC approvals; INACAL NTP EMC standards (IEC/CISPR-aligned) and electrical safety standards (IEC 62368-1-aligned, 220 V / 60 Hz); SUNAT RUC importer registration; and INDECOPI consumer data guidelines (advisory only, not a mandatory product certification) for connected devices. Peru operates at 220 V / 60 Hz with Type A/B/C plugs — Chinese hardware typically requires a power adapter or power supply rated for 220 V / 60 Hz.
