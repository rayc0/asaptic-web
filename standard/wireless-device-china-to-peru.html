Short answer

The main Peru compliance gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT device are: MTC mandatory homologación under Ley de Telecomunicaciones DL 702 administered by DGAT — all radio and telecom terminal equipment requires an MTC homologación certificate before import or sale, with no recognition of FCC, CE, SRRC, or CCC approvals; a local authorised importer with a valid Peruvian RUC (Registro Único de Contribuyentes) required for customs clearance; compliance with INDECOPI NTP electromagnetic compatibility and electrical safety standards harmonised with IEC and CISPR; adaptation to Peru's 220V/60Hz power system and Type A/C sockets — the 60Hz frequency at 220V is uncommon globally and may affect device and charger compatibility; Spanish language labelling; and awareness of Ley N° 30096 (Ley de Delitos Informáticos) and PECERT guidance for connected devices. The Peru–China FTA (TLC) reduces import tariffs but does not waive homologación. CE, FCC, and CCC are not recognised substitutes for MTC type approval.
