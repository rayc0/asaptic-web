Short answer

Angola does not yet have a confirmed standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to an IEC 62619 mandatory certification regime. The primary BESS deployment context in Angola is solar-plus-storage EPC projects financed by the Millennium Challenge Account (MCA) and development finance institutions (e.g., Sun Africa / MCA-funded plants), as well as hybrid mini-grids for rural electrification in interior provinces. The practical gates for Chinese BESS exported to Angola are: (1) IANORQ (Instituto Angolano de Normalização e Qualidade) conformity — Angola's standards body adopts IEC standards and may require product conformity evidence; importers must verify current IANORQ conformity requirements for BESS directly; (2) IRSEA (Instituto Regulador do Sector Eléctrico de Angola) energy-sector licensing — IRSEA regulates electricity generation, transmission, and distribution and must approve BESS installations in licensed projects; (3) ENDE (Empresa Nacional de Distribuição de Electricidade) grid-connection approval — ENDE is Angola's national distribution utility and sets grid-connection technical requirements; (4) EPC and lender specifications — IEC 62619, IEC 62933-5-2, and IEC 62133 are the internationally expected safety evidence standards referenced in MCA, development bank, and EPC project specifications; (5) fire and building authority approval — local Angolan fire and building authorities review installations; (6) UN 38.3 transport test documentation — mandatory for all lithium battery exports via Luanda (Porto de Luanda) and Lobito ports under international shipping conventions; and (7) Portuguese-language documentation — all regulatory submissions and project documentation must be in Portuguese. Chinese GB/T 36558-2023, GB/T 34120-2023, and NB/T 42090-2016 represent the manufacturing and grid-connection baseline in China but are not accepted as substitutes for IEC-based evidence in Angola project, authority, or lender review.

Chinese manufacturers typically hold UN 38.3 test reports and test summaries from CNAS-accredited testing laboratories such as UL, SGS, Bureau Veritas, TÜV, or CAICT. The UN 38.3 Test Summary (required since January 1, 2020) must cover the specific cell or battery type being shipped. A Chinese-origin UN 38.3 test summary from an accredited laboratory is acceptable for Angola imports — the key gap is ensuring the test summary covers the specific cell model, chemistry, capacity, and configuration of the BESS units being shipped, and that it is maintained current with any cell design changes.
