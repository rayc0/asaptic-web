Short answer

Mali does not have a standalone mandatory BESS product safety regulation equivalent to mandatory certification regimes in larger markets. BESS projects in Mali operate under a layered framework: (1) AMANORM (Agence Malienne de Normalisation et de Promotion de la Qualité) adopts IEC standards for conformity and may require conformity assessment for electrical equipment; (2) CREE (Commission de Régulation de l'Électricité et de l'Eau) licensing is required for power generation and distribution activities, including storage integrated with generation; (3) EDM-SA (Énergie du Mali) connection agreements govern utility-scale and C&I BESS grid connection — Mali operates at 220/380 V 50 Hz, the same nominal voltage as China, but EDM-SA grid-code technical requirements and project-specific connection agreement terms must be verified directly; (4) AMADER (Agence Malienne pour le Développement de l'Énergie Domestique et de l'Électrification Rurale) governs off-grid and mini-grid electrification projects, where IEC TS 62257 series standards apply; (5) donor and lender project specifications — World Bank, AfDB, EU, and bilateral-financed projects typically impose IEC 62619 and IEC 62933 evidence requirements that take precedence over AMANORM's own adopted standards. Chinese GB/T 36558-2023, GB/T 34120-2023, and NB/T 42090-2016 are not accepted as substitutes for IEC evidence in project financing or donor procurement specifications. Additional Mali-specific constraints include: severe high-temperature and dust operating environment (ambient ≥45°C requiring derating analysis), acute electricity deficit and EDM-SA load-shedding driving solar-hybrid mini-grid deployment, and landlocked transport routing requiring UN 38.3 documentation for sea transit (Dakar, Abidjan, or Tema port corridors) plus inland road transport to Bamako.
