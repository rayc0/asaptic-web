Short answer

For China-made electric or cordless power tools entering Uganda, the mandatory baseline is UNBS conformity through the Pre-Export Verification of Conformity (PVoC) programme, leading to a Certificate of Conformity and, for locally certified products, the Q-Mark; Uganda commonly adopts US and IEC standards, including IEC 62841 for tool safety. Cordless tools with radio functions also need UCC type approval. There is no EU-style outdoor-noise product-marking regime in Uganda, so noise is handled through general standards and workplace safety rather than a dedicated product directive.
