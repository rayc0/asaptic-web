Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer entering the Colombian market must obtain a Certificate of Conformity through an ONAC-accredited certification body demonstrating compliance with RETILAP (the mandatory lighting and public-illumination technical regulation) and RETIE (the mandatory electrical installations regulation) where applicable; test to NTC/IEC 60598 (luminaires), IEC 62560 (self-ballasted lamps), and IEC 62471 (photobiological safety) as adopted by ICONTEC; apply the RETIQ mandatory energy-efficiency label for in-scope lighting products; design for the Colombian 110/220 V, 60 Hz grid rather than China's 220/380 V, 50 Hz; obtain CRC/ANE radio approval for smart/wireless luminaires; and work through an in-country importer who registers the Certificate of Conformity and is subject to SIC market surveillance. Colombia has no horizontal RoHS, no REACH-style SVHC supply-chain regime, and no horizontal energy-using-products Ecodesign directive — energy efficiency is handled through RETIQ labelling, not minimum-efficacy market bans. None of these obligations is satisfied by an existing Chinese CCC certificate.
