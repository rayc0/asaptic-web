Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Honduras, the practical gaps are 60 Hz / 120-240 V grid suitability, CREE electricity-sector requirements, ENEE utility coordination for grid-connected installations, OHN or project-level conformity documentation, IEC 61851 safety and IEC 61000 EMC evidence, and connector conversion from China GB/T 20234 to the locally used Americas connector ecosystem. Honduras appears to follow an Americas-style vehicle and electrical environment where SAE J1772 Type 1 AC and CCS1 DC are the likely interoperability targets, but an official Honduras EVSE connector mandate could not be confirmed as of 2026-06-14; confirm the connector, approval route, and utility interconnection requirements with CREE, ENEE, the importer, and the site owner before shipment.
