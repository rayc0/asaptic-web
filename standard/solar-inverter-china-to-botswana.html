Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Botswana, the key practical gaps are: (1) BOBS (Botswana Bureau of Standards) conformity — Botswana Standards (BOS) generally adopt IEC; exporters must confirm whether the specific inverter model falls within any mandatory BOBS conformity assessment scheme and obtain applicable conformity evidence; (2) BERA (Botswana Energy Regulatory Authority) licensing — grid-tied solar PV generation requires a BERA generation licence or exemption; equipment must meet BERA technical conditions; (3) BPC (Botswana Power Corporation) grid-connection — BPC sets network access and connection conditions including anti-islanding (IEC 62116), safety (IEC 62109-1/-2), and grid parameter compliance for 230/400 V 50 Hz; (4) IEC 62920 EMC evidence for power conversion equipment; and (5) firmware validation against Botswana's 230/400 V, 50 Hz grid — not the 220/380 V parameters of the Chinese grid. China GB/T 37408 and GB/T 19964 baselines do not satisfy BERA / BPC IEC-referenced grid-connection requirements and cannot be substituted without supplementary IEC test evidence.
