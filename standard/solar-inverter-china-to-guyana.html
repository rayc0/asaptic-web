Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Guyana, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirming the current GNBS standard, conformity, and product-inspection route for the inverter and any bundled electrical equipment; (2) obtaining IEC 62109-1/-2 safety evidence for the exact inverter model or family; (3) preparing IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 utility-interface evidence for GPL/GEA grid-tied review; (4) preparing IEC 62920 and IEC 61000-series EMC and harmonic evidence; and (5) reconfiguring and retesting firmware, protection, and anti-islanding settings for Guyana's 60 Hz / 120-240 V grid, not China's 50 Hz / 220-380 V baseline. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence is useful background but should not be treated as automatic GNBS, GEA, or GPL acceptance.
