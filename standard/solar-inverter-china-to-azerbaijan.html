Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Azerbaijan, the key practical gaps are: (1) verifying whether the inverter falls within AZSTAND's current mandatory conformity certification scope for regulated electrical products — Azerbaijan is NOT an EAEU member, so EAC TR CU certification does not apply; the domestic route is AZSTAND conformity; (2) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence, which large-scale project lenders (EBRD, IFC) and project-owner specifications require regardless of local minimums; (3) IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 grid-characteristics compliance; (4) firmware and protection settings validated against Azerbaijan's 50 Hz / 220 V (single-phase) / 380 V (three-phase) CIS-legacy grid — which is nominally the same as China's domestic grid, reducing re-parameterisation risk but not eliminating the need for Azerbaijan-specific grid-operator approval; and (5) China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964-2024 are not automatically accepted in Azerbaijan and require mapping to AZSTAND conformity and project/lender specifications.
