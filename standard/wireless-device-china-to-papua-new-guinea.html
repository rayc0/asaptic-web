Short answer

The main Papua New Guinea gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT device are: mandatory NICTA type approval certificate under the NICTA Act 2009 for all radio and telecom terminal equipment; no recognition of CE, FCC, or CCC marks (though Australian ACMA / AS/NZS test reports may be accepted as supporting technical evidence); electrical safety alignment to the Australian 240 V / 50 Hz / Type I (AS/NZS 3112) standard; appointment of a local PNG authorized importer or agent; and awareness of the Cybercrime Code Act 2016. There is no active Pacific Islands Forum harmonized type approval scheme.
