Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Uruguay, the confirmed practical gaps are IEC-family safety and EMC evidence aligned with UNIT standards practice, connector redesign from GB/T 20234 to IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 for European-style public infrastructure, UTE grid-connection and charging-network coordination, URSEA-regulated electrical-service context, and OCPP/back-office interoperability where chargers are networked. Uruguay operates on 230 V / 400 V, 50 Hz, so frequency is aligned with China but voltage range, three-phase ratings, protective devices, and installation documentation still require confirmation. Uruguay's public EV direction is helped by UTE's charging network and very high renewable-electricity share, but that market context does not make GB/T connector chargers plug-compatible or waive conformity, utility, tender, or site-acceptance requirements.
