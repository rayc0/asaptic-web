Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Madagascar, the key practical gaps are: (1) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence — Madagascar through BNM adopts IEC standards; Chinese GB/T 37408 alone is not accepted as equivalent; (2) IEC 62116 anti-islanding compliance for any grid-tied installation connecting to JIRAMA's 220/380 V 50 Hz network; (3) ORE licensing — any electricity generation project above threshold capacity requires an ORE licence; (4) JIRAMA grid-connection technical review for utility-interconnected systems; (5) for off-grid and rural mini-grid projects, ADER programme requirements and donor/lender quality standards (e.g. VeraSol/Lighting Global for smaller systems) apply as separate gates. Madagascar's national grid voltage (220/380 V, 50 Hz) is nominally the same as China's, reducing firmware re-parameterisation risk, but IEC compliance evidence — not Chinese GB/T certificates — is the accepted documentary basis for regulatory submissions and donor-financed projects.
