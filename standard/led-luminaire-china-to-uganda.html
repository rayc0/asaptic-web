Short answer

A Chinese LED luminaire manufacturer exporting to Uganda must meet the UNBS Pre-Export Verification of Conformity (PVoC) programme: each regulated consignment requires a Certificate of Conformity (CoC) issued by a UNBS-appointed inspection body before shipment, obtained via Route A (consignment testing), Route B (registration), or Route C (licensing) — without a valid CoC the goods can be detained, fined, or re-exported at the Mombasa or Dar es Salaam port of entry (Uganda is landlocked). LED luminaires are assessed against US/IEC-based safety standards (IEC 60598 luminaire safety, IEC 62560 self-ballasted LED lamps, IEC 62471 photobiological safety) rather than EN harmonised standards; the product must be rated for the 240 V / 50 Hz Uganda grid (50 Hz matches China, but nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V). An in-country importer of record handles clearance and may need the UNBS Q-Mark / import inspection for products on the regulated list. Energy-efficiency labelling applies under Uganda's national programmes where in scope, and any radio-enabled smart luminaire (Wi-Fi/Bluetooth) additionally needs UCC type approval. Uganda does NOT operate an EU-style horizontal RoHS, REACH-SVHC, or Ecodesign regime — those obligations have no direct Uganda counterpart. Existing CCC certification does not satisfy the UNBS PVoC pathway.
