Short answer

The main Singapore gaps for a China-made Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or IoT device are: mandatory IMDA equipment registration under the Telecommunications Act (Cap. 323) and Radio Communications Act (Cap. 270) with the IMDA registration number on the label; IMDA EMC technical standards compliance (CE DoC not automatically accepted, no MRA with China); electrical safety under the Consumer Protection (Safety Requirements) Regulations to SS IEC standards; IMDA dealer registration for the importing entity; and the voluntary CSA Cybersecurity Labelling Scheme (CLS), which is increasingly expected by enterprise and government buyers. CCC is not recognised by IMDA. SRRC and MIIT NAL have no equivalence under Singapore law.
