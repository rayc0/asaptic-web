Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Nauru, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirm NUC and project standard requirements and any product conformity route before import; (2) obtain ministry and NUC project-level grid-connection requirements before shipment; (3) provide IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence; (4) provide IEC 62116 anti-islanding and IEC 61727 utility-interface evidence; (5) provide IEC 62920 and IEC 61000-series EMC evidence; and (6) validate firmware, protection thresholds, reconnection delays, thermal ratings, and corrosion controls for Nauru's 240/415 V, 50 Hz coastal island grid. Nauru shares China's 50 Hz frequency, but the nominal voltage differs from China's 220/380 V baseline. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence is not automatically accepted as Nauru evidence without supplementary IEC and project-specific review.
