Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Uganda, the practical gaps are UNBS PVoC pre-export conformity evidence and Certificate of Conformity planning, ERA and utility coordination for grid-connected installations, Uganda 240 V single-phase / 415 V three-phase 50 Hz input-range confirmation, IEC 61851 safety evidence, IEC 62196 Type 2 / CCS2 connector hardware where the project follows the international IEC direction, and IEC 61000 EMC documentation. A China GB/T-only charger is not connector-ready for Uganda IEC 62196 deployments: GB/T 20234 couplers are physically and electrically incompatible with Type 2 / CCS2, and GB/T 27930 DC communication does not substitute for CCS2 communication or network back-office requirements. Uganda has visible local e-mobility activity, including electric buses and two/three-wheeler charging or swapping pilots, but public charging infrastructure is still early; exporters should verify the current UNBS PVoC route, HS code, charger type, ERA or utility requirements, and site approval path before shipment.
