Short answer

For EV chargers exported from China to Ghana, GSA conformity assessment applies to imported electrical equipment and a Certificate of Conformity is required at customs. The Energy Commission has issued a directive requiring prior authorization before installing or operating EV charging stations in Ghana. Ghana has aligned with IEC 61851 (GS IEC 61851 series) as the national standard for conductive EV charging. Connector interoperability is an active policy consideration: Ghana's Energy Commission has signalled an IEC 62196 (Type 2 / Type 2 Combo CCS) direction to avoid technology lock-in, creating a gap against the GB/T 20234 connectors standard on Chinese domestic chargers. The grid operates at 50 Hz, 230/400 V. Chinese GB/T 18487 documentation does not substitute for Ghana IEC 61851-based conformity evidence.
