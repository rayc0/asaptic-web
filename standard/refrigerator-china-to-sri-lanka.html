Short answer

Chinese household refrigerator manufacturers face four main Sri Lanka compliance gaps: (1) electrical safety certification under the SLSI scheme to SLS/IEC 60335-2-24 — Chinese CCC/GB 4706.13 certification is not automatically accepted, though IEC 60335-2-24 alignment and an IECEE CB test report can ease conformity; (2) SLSEA energy labelling and Minimum Energy Performance Standards (MEPS) — the China Energy Label (GB 12021.2) does not satisfy the Sri Lanka energy label, which requires a star rating registered with the Sri Lanka Sustainable Energy Authority; (3) SLSI mandatory import certification and inspection plus affixing the SLS mark before customs clearance via a Sri Lanka-resident importer; and (4) refrigerant documentation — R-600a (isobutane) is widely used and compatible, but charge and flammability documentation to IEC 60335-2-24 must be confirmed. Sri Lanka has no EU-style horizontal RoHS, battery, or outdoor-noise regimes for refrigerators.
