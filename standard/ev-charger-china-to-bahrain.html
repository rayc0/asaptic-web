Short answer

For EV chargers exported from China to Bahrain, the critical connector incompatibility must be addressed first: Bahrain vehicles and infrastructure follow IEC 62196 Type 2 (AC) and CCS2 (DC fast charging), which are physically incompatible with China's GB/T 20234.2-2015 (AC) and GB/T 20234.3-2023 (DC) connectors. Chargers designed for the Chinese domestic market cannot be sold in Bahrain without hardware redesign for IEC 62196-compliant connectors. Additionally, Bahrain introduced a comprehensive EV charging regulatory framework through MEWA in November 2025, granting EWA authority to set technical and safety standards, approve grid connections, and maintain a national register of all EV charging equipment. IEC 61851 conductive charging system compliance and 50 Hz grid settings are expected.
