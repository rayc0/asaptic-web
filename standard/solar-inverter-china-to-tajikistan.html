Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Tajikistan, the key practical gaps are: (1) Tajikstandart conformity certification is the mandatory domestic route — Tajikistan is NOT an EAEU member, so EAC/TR CU marking does not by itself satisfy Tajikstandart requirements; (2) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety type-test evidence is required by Barqi Tojik grid-connection and donor/lender (World Bank, ADB) project specifications; (3) IEC 62116 anti-islanding compliance for the 50 Hz grid; (4) IEC 61000-series EMC evidence; (5) high-altitude derating documentation for Tajikistan's mountainous terrain (many sites above 1,000–2,000 m), including thermal and output power derating per IEC 62109-1 and manufacturer specifications; and (6) cold-climate operating range validation (winter temperatures can fall below −20 °C at altitude). China GB/T 37408 and GB/T 19964 baselines do not satisfy Tajikstandart conformity or Barqi Tojik/donor-lender grid-connection requirements and cannot be substituted without supplementary IEC test evidence.
