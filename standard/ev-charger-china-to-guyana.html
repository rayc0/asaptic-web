Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Guyana, the practical gaps are GNBS conformity and importer/manufacturer registration for electrical equipment, GPL grid coordination for installations on a 120/240 V, 60 Hz Americas-style supply, IEC 61851 and IEC 61000 evidence for safety, EMC and power quality, and connector hardware changes from China GB/T 20234 to the locally deployed or project-specified connector. Guyana's EV charging market is still nascent and official sources reviewed as of 2026-06-14 did not confirm a single national EVSE connector mandate; because Guyana is an Americas-grid market, exporters should expect SAE J1772 Type 1 AC and CCS Combo 1 DC in many deployments, but must confirm the exact J1772/CCS1 requirement with GEA, GNBS, GPL, the vehicle importer, and the site owner before quoting or shipping. GB/T-connector chargers are physically incompatible with J1772 / CCS1 vehicle inlets without hardware redesign.
