Short answer

Filtering respirators entering Mozambique are typically assessed against Mozambican national standards (NM) that adopt EN 149 (FFP1/FFP2/FFP3), under conformity and import-inspection oversight by INNOQ (Instituto Nacional de Normalizacao e Qualidade). Documentation must generally be provided in Portuguese, a registered local importer is needed, and occupational-use respirators sit within the national occupational-safety framework, while medical/surgical masks fall to the national health regulator. Chinese KN95 (GB 2626) test reports do not by themselves satisfy the NM/EN 149 route adopted in Mozambique.

Filtering Facepiece Respirator Safety — NM/EN 149 FFP Performance Requirements (Mozambique) China's primary standard for non-powered air-purifying particle respirators is GB 2626-2019 (Respiratory protective equipment — Non-powered air-purifying particle respirator), with classes KN90 and KN95.
