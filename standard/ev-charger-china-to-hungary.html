Short answer

For Hungary-bound EV chargers, the most critical gaps are Type 2/CCS2 connector alignment under IEC 62196, compatibility with AFIR obligations as implemented through Hungarian and EU member-state practice, CE conformity evidence under LVD/EMC/RED where in scope, RCD/RDC protection per IEC 60364-7-722, and MEKH/MAVIR installation and grid-operator coordination. Hungary operates on 230/400 V 50 Hz; Chinese charger firmware and protection thresholds calibrated to China's 220/380 V baseline must be revalidated. Hungary is landlocked — EVSE import logistics run via Koper (Slovenia), Rijeka (Croatia), or Hamburg (Germany). Hungarian-language documentation is expected for local installation permits.
