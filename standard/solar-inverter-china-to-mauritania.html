Short answer

For solar PV inverters exported from China to Mauritania, the key practical gaps are: (1) confirming any national conformity or import file required through Mauritania's standards/quality administration, commonly cited as DNPQ but not independently verified here; (2) ARE electricity-sector licensing or delegated-service requirements where the project is a public-service, mini-grid, or IPP installation; (3) SOMELEC technical acceptance for grid-tied projects; (4) IEC 62109-1/-2 safety, IEC 62116 anti-islanding, IEC 61727 utility-interface, and IEC 62920 / IEC 61000 EMC evidence; and (5) validation for Mauritania's 50 Hz, commonly reported 220/380 V grid plus extreme heat, dust, sand, humidity, and coastal salt derating. China GB/T 37408, NB/T 32004, and GB/T 19964 evidence is useful background but should not be treated as automatic Mauritania acceptance.
