Short answer

For China-made EV chargers entering Panama, the practical gaps are 120/240 V, 60 Hz input and distributor coordination; COPANIT / DGNTI standards or conformity review where applicable; ASEP and EDEMET / EDECHI / ENSA project coordination for grid-connected installations; and connector hardware conversion away from GB/T 20234 to the locally required connector. Because Panama is an Americas-style 60 Hz market, SAE J1772 Type 1 AC and CCS1 DC are likely practical connector expectations, but exporters should confirm the required connector with ASEP, the distributor, site owner, tender, or charge-point operator before quoting. GB/T connectors are physically incompatible and GB/T 27930 DC communication is not a substitute for CCS communication or network OCPP requirements.
