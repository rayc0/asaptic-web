Short answer

Netherlands is a full EU member state: RED 2014/53/EU applies without national derogation, enforced by RDI (Rijksinspectie Digitale Infrastructuur). CE marking covering radio performance (EN 300 328 / EN 301 893), EMC (EN 301 489), electrical safety (EN IEC 62368-1:2020+A11), and cybersecurity under RED Art. 3.3(d)-(f) (EN 18031, mandatory from 1 August 2025) are required. An EU Authorised Representative is mandatory for Chinese manufacturers. Rotterdam (Europe's largest port) is a documented high-risk entry point: RDI conducts active customs inspection programs targeting Chinese-origin wireless electronics at Rotterdam — non-compliant goods are detained or destroyed at port.

Radio Performance & CE Marking — RED Art. 3.2 (EN 300 328 / EN 301 893) — Netherlands / RDI In China, wireless transmitters (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth) must obtain SRRC Type Approval from the National Radio Administration (NRA/MIIT) under the MIIT/SRRC framework. The primary domestic technical standard for 2.4 GHz spread-spectrum devices is YD/T 1127 (series), and for Wi-Fi equipment GB 15629.11 (equivalent to IEEE 802.11). RF power limits and 5 GHz channel plans differ between China and the EU: China restricts 5 GHz outdoor use more tightly and does not align to the same DFS requirements as EN 301 893. SRRC type approval is a mandatory pre-market licence by administrative decision, not a self-declaration. It does not satisfy the EU RED Art. 3.2 or CE marking conformity assessment pathway.
